# First Words — The Cold Open

*The session's very first beat, before the boat. A storyteller-in-a-tavern open (the Brennan Lee Mulligan homage), reworked for the Mire with **Remy as the storyteller** telling the local kids the spirit-world story.*

**How to run it:**
- **Different gear from Remy's usual fast patter** — this is slow, warm, wistful. He's a man who loves a story.
- **Let the kids' questions pace it.** Don't deliver it as a monologue — bounce off the children's interruptions (improvise more if the table's into it, cut to the chase if not).
- **It seeds the campaign's player-facing cosmology** (spirit world, thin veil, honored guests) — *not* the secret. Remy has no idea about the binding or the prison. He believes every reverent word.
- **The irony is yours to savor:** he's telling kids how close the spirits are in the Mire, minutes before he rows the party down into the charged water that will tether them. Don't wink at it.
- **Flows straight into** [[session-01-read-aloud#1 · The Tavern|Read-Aloud · Beat 1]] — when the story ends, he turns to his passengers and the night begins.

---

> A tavern on the water. Lamplight and woodsmoke, the black Mire lapping at the stilts outside. And in the warm middle of it — a knot of kids cross-legged on the floor, and Remy, sleeves rolled, a bowl of stew going cold in his hands because he can't stop talking.

> One of the little ones pipes up — *"Remy, is it true the water sings?"*

> And he grins. *"Out where I take folk? Oh, it sings, love. But if you want to know* why *it sings — you've got to go back. Way back. Before this world."*

> A kid, scandalized — *"Before the* world*??"*

> *"Before* this *one."* [he leans in] *"You didn't think this was the first world, did you? No, no. There was a world before ours. The world of spirits. And great they were, some of 'em — great and mighty as the dawn. The sun and the moon. The storm up high and the dark down deep. It was them that made this world. Ours. And then..."* [softer] *"...some of them came to live in it."*

> The kids go quiet.

> *"But don't go thinking they're all great, mind. Most aren't. Most are small. Humble as a blade of grass, an acorn, a little fish in the reeds. And they're all around you — above, below, in the water, in the dark. Most folk never know they've shared a table with one."*

> [quieter still, the part he believes most] *"And there's nowhere in all the wide world they come closer than right here. The Mire. That's why we don't take more than we're given, and we don't go naming things that haven't told us their names. We just set a good table, and we call whatever comes our* honored guests."

> [a beat — and then he's all the way back, bright and brisk, setting the bowl down and clapping the stew off his hands] *"Right! Enough of that. Story's a story."*

> He stands, and turns to you — his passengers.
