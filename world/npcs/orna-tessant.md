# Grandma Orna

**Role:** Resident genius and director of [[The Verdant Margin]]; the most gifted alchemist in Sallowreach, and easily its worst teacher
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — [[The Verdant Margin]]
**Faction:** Independent (the Alchemists' Consortium would kill to have them and have stopped trying)
**Tier:** 1

*The Witch's first real ally — and the mirror they'll least want to look into. A firbolg who is cheerfully drinking themselves into a swamp-thing meets a clank who'd give anything to drink their way back to a body that was stolen. Same axis, opposite directions.*

---

## Appearance
They were a firbolg — tall, broad, soft-eyed, the gentle-giant build of forest-folk. They are *becoming* something else, and not slowly anymore. There's a creep of pale barnacle-crust along one forearm; a fringe of fine green filament where hair used to be at the temple; skin that's gone faintly translucent-brackish at the knuckles, lit wrong in the evening like the rest of the deep Mire. Davy Jones halfway through, if the sea were a swamp. They do not groom it, hide it, or remark on it. A new growth appears and they look at it the way you'd look at an interesting weather front, then forget it.

They smell of crushed green, ozone, and the specific mineral funk of something fermenting that probably shouldn't be. Their clothes have burn-holes and stains from a hundred reactions. There is usually something half-finished in their hand that they have forgotten they are holding.

## Personality & Mannerisms
A genuine genius, and exhausting in exactly the way genius is. Orna is brilliant with potions and reagents on a level no one else in the region approaches — and **they have never written a single step of it down.** It's all in their head, in a private associative tangle that makes perfect sense to them and is illegible to everyone else.

**They are a catastrophe of a teacher.** Not from impatience or contempt — they *like* people, vaguely, at a distance — but because they think ten steps ahead of whoever they're talking to and answer the question you'll have in twenty minutes instead of the one you just asked. *"Right, so obviously you'd skip the second decoction — no, don't ask why, you'll see — and then it's just the thing with the salt, you know the thing,"* and they've wandered off. Aloof, disorganized, perpetually mid-thought, three projects deep on every surface.

**They self-test compulsively.** Every promising brew goes down their own throat first. This is why they're transforming — not decades of passive osmosis, but *years of drinking their own experiments on purpose.* They consider their own body the only honest laboratory they've got. They are aware this is what's turning them into swamp. They genuinely, maddeningly **do not care.** *"Fascinating data, actually."* They will keep testing.

## What They Want
**The all-cure elixir.** A single panacea that cures *anything* — disease, poison, rot, wound, maybe worse. The holy grail of their craft. They don't know if it's possible. They are betting their body and their mind that it is, one self-administered dose at a time. It is the most ambitious and least documented research program in Sallowreach, and it exists entirely inside a head that is turning into a swamp.

## What They're Hiding — The Interesting Secret
**They did it once.** Years ago, Orna brewed something that *worked* — cured a person of something that does not get cured (a child of the grey-rot; pick your incurable). They gave it away, moved on, delighted — and have **never been able to make it again.** Because they never wrote it down, and they don't fully remember how, and the memory gets fainter every year. The grand "all-cure" crusade is the rationalization of a smaller, unbearable thing: *they performed a miracle and lost the recipe.*

And the part they will not say, maybe can't admit: **every dose they test on themselves washes a little more of the only place that knowledge lives.** They are not a serene genius indifferent to their own dissolution. They are *racing* it — trying to rebuild a miracle in a mind that's actively draining out — and they've dressed the panic up as "not caring." The person they cured is still alive in Sallowreach: living proof the impossible was real once, and a quiet clock on how much is already gone. *(GM: a thread the Witch or party can pull. If Orna finishes transforming, the miracle is lost for good — and they're the only one who ever held it.)*

## The Mirror *(GM)*
Sharper than ever. The Witch had a self *stolen* and would do anything to recover it. Orna is *giving theirs away on purpose,* one beaker at a time, and calls it research. One grieves a dissolution that was done to them; the other embraces a dissolution they're doing to themselves. And the cruelest contrast: **the Witch is a compulsive documentarian** — a meticulous formulary, every step recorded, because they know what it is to lose what was never preserved — while Orna, who could fill libraries, writes *nothing,* and is letting the most valuable mind in the region wash out untranscribed. The Witch will find this unbearable. That's the relationship.

## The Light Quest — "Persona Non Grata" *(the Witch, solo, in town)*
Orna needs a reagent they've run out of, and it's a controlled one: a dram of **gloamcap distillate** — a deep-Mire fungal extract too potent and too toxic to keep on an open shelf. The only source in the city is the **Alchemists' Consortium** chapter house. And Orna is *banned* from it. They mention this with cheerful unconcern, as though it has just become the Witch's problem: *"Oh — you'll have to fetch it. They won't see me. Long story. Boring story. Tell them it's for the garden."* It is not a boring story, and they will not tell it.

The obstacle is a door with a person behind it, not a fight — ideal for a solo Witch with a real formulary (and a shapeshifter's options if they'd rather):
- The distillate is restricted; you don't buy it off a shelf. **Magister Auguste Lorne**, keeper of the stores, decides who gets it.
- Saying Orna's name cuts both ways — *"You work with* Tessant?" *—* contempt, curiosity, or grief, depending how the Witch reads the room.
- Approaches (player's pick, all in-character): present as a serious alchemist — the Consortium *respects documented work,* and the Witch has the immaculate formulary Orna never kept (the front door); negotiate a trade or a favor; charm; or shapeshift in and lift it (fast, risky, torches the relationship if caught).

**The reveal — why Orna's unwelcome:** Lorne tells it, and it's the human story under the eccentricity. Orna was Consortium once — the most gifted alchemist any of them ever saw — and would not share a method, would not write down a step, would not be governed. It ended with a self-test gone wrong on the premises *(ruined a shared decade-long project / nearly killed a colleague / turned the reading room into a fungal bloom — pick one)*, and an expulsion. Lorne hasn't forgiven it, and can't hide that they're also *grieving:* *"Best I ever saw. Out there killing themselves in that garden, and they won't write down one word of what dies with them. You can't help someone who won't be helped."* Lorne is also the outside witness to the secret: *"They cured something once that none of us could explain — and never could again. Typical."* *(Plants the lost miracle from a second source.)*

**The price (optional, and on-theme):** Lorne releases the distillate only if the Witch will do the thing no one's managed — get Orna to **write down the recipe for *Lastlight*.** Lastlight is Orna's reagent-fixative: a few drops and a cut Mire reagent keeps its potency for months instead of spoiling in days. In a city whose entire fortune rides on exporting reagents that rot on the way out, it's the most commercially valuable formula in the region — and Orna makes it casually, has never shared the method, and it exists nowhere but in a head that's turning into swamp. The documentarian and the genius again, but now the whole Consortium is leaning on it: the writing isn't a puzzle, it's the *ransom* for the cure-ingredient, and proof the Witch can reach Orna where no one else can. *(And a quiet tragedy in miniature — the one recipe that would secure the Consortium's trade for a generation is dying, undocumented, inside someone who won't stop drinking themselves away.)*

**Payoff:** the Witch gets the reagent (and, by delivering it, Orna's trust + standing reagent access); a richer, sadder read on Orna from the outside; and the **Alchemists' Consortium** as an ongoing contact and resource. **Orna's carrot (offered up front, session 3):** help me get the gloamcap, and I'll help *you* get a **familiar** of your own — Mire-born, bound properly. *(For a witch with no coven and no past, a familiar is the first tie that's actually hers. Orna has no idea how much that lands.)* *(Quiet cross-link if you want it: the Consortium's stores are stocked partly by poachers — [[Gault]]'s buyers include them — so a sharp Witch might notice the "ethically sourced" specimens aren't. A thread toward [[Fletcher]] and [[Brin Sevarre]]'s plot.)*

## What They Know (useful to the party)
- **Mire-reagent mastery,** the deepest in the city — all of it verbal, none of it filed. If it grows within a day's punt, Orna knows it cold and can't tell you how they know.
- **They diagnose the Witch in one careless glance:** *"You're not sick. You're* bound. *No elixir touches that — wrong department entirely. You want a—"* and they trail off, already three thoughts away. A genius breadcrumb that points the Witch off the potion-path and toward [[the-tidebreaker|the thing that actually breaks bindings]] — without Orna ever finishing the sentence.
- **The garden answers the city.** The lower platform's bioluminescent beds flare on Celebration nights, *nearly* syncing to the drums. Orna has noticed (of course they have) and never recorded it, and has half a brilliant theory they never finished. *(A live Turn clue — see [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|The Turn]] — that a Witch elbow-deep in those beds will catch. Orna holds the observation; they cannot assemble it. Keep them a fragment-holder.)*

## Dialogue Hook
They don't look up from whatever's bubbling:
*"You're after something specific. Everyone who finds the Margin is."* A beaker gets swirled, sniffed, set down wrong. *"Most of them want the impossible ones — the reagents that shouldn't work. Those I have."* Now they look at the Witch, and the wrong light moves in their eyes. *"You, though. You want the impossible* done *to you. Different shelf. Sit, sit — no, not there, that's alive."*
