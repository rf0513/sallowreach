# Wandering Sights — What the City Shows You

*For when a PC drifts with no goal. Wandering should reward curiosity — drop 2–3, see which one they walk toward, let that become the scene. All read-aloud ready, morning-after-the-Celebration flavor. (Origin: improvised in Session 2.) See also the d6 quick-table in [[session-03-run-sheet]].*

---

## 🤍 Warm & Alive
- **The cleanup feast.** A communal table being scrubbed from last night's Celebration — a woman catches your eye and presses a heel of bread and a sticky golden **lantern-fruit** into your hands: *"You didn't get any last night, did you. Here. Don't argue."*
- **The domino war.** Two old men playing on an overturned crate, arguing in rapid trade-cant, *neither one looking at the board.* They pause to assess you, decide you're alright, go right back to it.
- **The canal jam.** A punt traffic snarl in a narrow channel below — cheerful, profane negotiating — and a kid leaping boat to boat to hand-deliver a covered pot of something that smells incredible.

## ✨ Wondrous *(the bayou showing off)*
- **Light under the boards.** In the shadow between stilts where the water's blackest, the surface still glows faint blue-green. A child lies on their belly trailing a finger through it, making it swirl like ink.
- **Fritters that glow.** A cart of **lantern-root fritters** glowing pale gold *before* they hit the oil. The vendor flips you one: *"First one's free. Then you're mine forever."*
- **The heron nobody disturbs.** A heron the size of a man, dead-still on a piling. People give it room — and one passerby murmurs a polite *good morning* to it without breaking stride.
- **The runaway lantern.** A single Celebration lantern that never made it out to the Mire, drifting down the channel still lit, turning slow circles — a small girl chasing it along the boards with a net, *scolding it* like a pet that got loose.

## 🌑 Uncanny *(light, eerie, no danger)*
- **The offering door.** A door with fresh green Mire-grass knotted to the handle and a bowl of rice on the threshold. The house behind it is dark. Everyone steps *over* the threshold, never on it.
- **The boardwalk that ends.** A walkway that just… stops, mid-plank, where black roots have split the timber. Someone's hung a lantern and a sign: **NOT THIS WAY NO MORE.** *(The Mire growing — the city losing ground.)*
- **The reflection that lags.** You pass a cafe window and for half a second the old man at the corner table *casts no reflection,* though he's plainly sitting there. Then he does. He smiles like he knows you saw. *(A small spirit passing as human. Free, eerie, never explained.)*
- **The three-second hush.** Every insect goes silent at once for three full seconds, then starts again. A local touches two fingers to their lips and keeps walking. *(An honored guest passing through.)*

## 🧵 Threads *(drop if you want them tugging a string)*
- **Third notice.** A relocation notice nailed to a stilt-house door on a sagging boardwalk — the water's taken the floor, the family's still inside, a neighbor quietly moving pots to higher boards. *(the failing southwest edge)*
- **The drummer at the water's edge.** Out past the last boards, a young woman drumming to the open channel — and the glow *answers her.* A little crowd, half-delighted, half-nervous. *([[Fen]] — quiet Celebration/Turn seed; don't explain.)*
- **The homesick queue.** A hand-painted sign: **THE LONG ROOM — WORD SENT ANYWHERE, EVEN WHERE YOU CAN'T GO.** A line waiting to send letters out. *(A gut-punch for someone who now literally can't leave.)*
- **Dock gossip.** Two dockhands, low: *"…the imperial one. Bought a whole table breakfast just to ask about that wreck."* *"Lonely, maybe."* *"…Maybe."* *([[Caius Maren|Caius]], simmering.)*

## If They Keep Drifting With No Bite
The city adopts them anyway — someone learns their name, presses food on them, asks after the arm they didn't say was hurt. Hand them **1d4 Stress / Hope** and let the warmth be the scene. That's Sallowreach.
