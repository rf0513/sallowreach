# The Verdant Margin

**Type:** Community garden / botanical research station
**Region:** [[Sallowreach]] — eastern boardwalk edge, where the city's cultivated land meets the Mire
**Controlled By:** [[Professor Orna Tessant]] (directorship); nominally affiliated with the Mages Guild's research arm

## Description

The Verdant Margin exists in the uncomfortable space where Sallowreach decided it was possible to garden next to a swamp and proceeded to prove itself right, mostly. It spans three interconnected platforms of cultivated growing space, two enclosed greenhouse structures, and a lower platform that is only reliably accessible at low tide — and which contains, by local consensus, the most interesting specimens and the worst footing.

During daylight it smells of turned earth, damp wood, and something sweet that shifts depending on which beds are blooming and which are fermenting. At night, especially in the hours around and after the Celebration, the lower platform produces its own bioluminescence — not dramatic, not the rays' full glow, but a persistent blue-green shimmer in three of the specimen beds that [[Professor Tessant]] has noticed for years, never written down, and has half a brilliant theory about that they've never bothered to finish.

The work is communal: anyone in Sallowreach can claim a bed, maintain it, and take the harvest. Tessant's formal research occupies the greenhouse structures and the lower platform. The upper beds are neighborhood life — people come to dig and talk and bring their children. The garden is, genuinely, a community space that also happens to contain some of the most interesting alchemical specimens on this side of the Mire. The two functions coexist without obvious conflict and with some productive tension.

## Key Features

- **The Greenhouse (North)** — Tessant's working chaos: reference specimens going unlabeled, three experiments running at once, burn-marks, and not a single page of notes anywhere, because they don't keep any. A visitor with serious alchemical intentions ends up here eventually. The bench nearest the window is where Tessant self-tests — the row of half-finished doses they've been drinking themselves into a swamp with.
- **The Lower Platform** — accessible by a ladder that the tide occasionally disagrees with. The most Mire-influenced specimens live here: plants that grow in directions light doesn't explain, reagents whose properties shift by week rather than season, three beds that bioluminesce. The handrails are rope, not wood. Watch where you step.
- **The Community Beds** — upper platforms, wide and well-maintained. The social heart of the garden: a place where people from the outer boardwalks come together, grow food, argue about technique, and occasionally discover that their neighbor has been quietly doing something interesting with fermentation. A good place to overhear things.

## NPCs Present

- **[[Professor Orna Tessant]]** — director; the most gifted alchemist in Sallowreach and its worst teacher; a firbolg drinking themselves into a swamp-thing via compulsive self-testing, chasing an all-cure elixir, writing nothing down; the Witch's first credible ally and most complicated mirror
- Regular community gardeners (background NPCs) — a cross-section of Sallowreach's outer boardwalk residents; good sources of neighborhood information and rumors about the southwest edge's deterioration

## Tensions & Hooks

The lower platform is getting closer to the Mire each season — the rate is accelerating. The community gardeners are the ones keeping count and petitioning the city (and getting told the garden isn't a priority); Tessant can't be bothered with reports, but they *have* quietly started moving their most prized specimens up to the greenhouse, and won't say why. *(They've noticed the encroachment is faster here than it should be. They have a theory. They haven't finished it.)*

The three bioluminescent beds do something on Celebration nights: they pulse — not consistently, not in perfect time with the city's drums, but *nearly.* Tessant has watched it happen for years, finds it the single most interesting thing in the garden, and has done nothing rigorous about it (no notes, no models — they think they'll "just remember the pattern," and the pattern keeps slipping). The correlation is real and unproven and sitting there for a sharper, more methodical mind to catch — like a Witch elbow-deep in the beds.

Someone from the outer boardwalk recently brought Tessant a plant they found growing under their home's foundation — deep blue-black leaves, a root system that had pushed thirty centimeters through treated wood. Tessant got fascinated for about four minutes, said something cryptic and ten steps ahead, pocketed a cutting, and almost certainly lost it on a bench somewhere. They know it's significant. They have not done one practical thing about it.

## Secrets

- **The lost miracle:** years ago Tessant brewed something that cured a person of the incurable — and has never been able to remake it, because they never wrote it down and the memory's fading with every self-test. The "all-cure elixir" crusade is the rationalization of that one unbearable failure. The person they cured is alive in Sallowreach. *(See [[Professor Orna Tessant|Tessant's file]] — a thread the Witch or party can pull, on a clock: when Tessant finishes transforming, the recipe is lost for good.)*
- The lower platform bioluminescence matches, in color and frequency, the lanterns floated during the Celebration. Tessant noticed years ago, never recorded it, and has half a theory they never finished. A live [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|Turn]] clue, held by someone who can't assemble it.
- Three of the community-bed regulars have noticed that plants grow faster when Tessant tends them than when anyone else does. They haven't said anything to Tessant. They've said it to each other.

## Dungeon Alchemist Notes

A multi-level outdoor space with two enclosed greenhouse structures. For play: the upper community beds are open and social — wide platforms, tables, people present throughout the day. The greenhouse structures are smaller, more intimate, appropriate for private conversations. The lower platform is a distinct environmental space: slightly unstable footing, unique lighting conditions, the Mire visibly close. Build the lower platform with the feel of a threshold — you're still in Sallowreach but the margin is doing something. Good for a quiet revelation or a moment of unexpected encounter. No expected battle map.
