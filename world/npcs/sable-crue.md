# Sable Crue

**Role:** Sergeant of the Southwest Perimeter Guard; runs the drills at [[The Drillfield]]
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — [[The Drillfield]], southwest district
**Faction:** City Guard
**Tier:** 1

*Tenson's first real ally, and their funhouse mirror. Tenson is brawny and aching to prove they've got brains; Sable is brawny and perfectly, cheerfully content not to be the brains. The happiest person posted to a city everyone else sulks in. Win them over and they're the Guardian's man on the inside.*

---

## Appearance
Big. Broad through the shoulders and built like someone who has never once skipped the morning run because they *like* the morning run. Scarred forearms, a nose that's been broken and reset by someone who clearly enjoyed the story more than the setting, and a grin that arrives before they do. Their uniform is rumpled in the comfortable way of someone who works *in* it rather than poses in it. They're usually sweating, usually mid-laugh, usually holding food.

Where the rest of the imperial-and-guild postings wear the bayou humidity like a grievance, Sable wears it like a warm bath. They've gone half-native and don't hide it: outer-boardwalk spices on their breath, a Celebration drum-rhythm they tap without noticing, the easy hello-by-name of someone who actually likes where they live.

## Personality & Mannerisms
**Fun. Loud. Full of life.** Sable is the morale of the southwest line made flesh — they run brutal drills and somehow everyone leaves grinning. They remember birthdays, they cook for the night shift, they will absolutely challenge you to something physical within ten minutes of meeting you and absolutely not care if they lose. Joy is their resting state, and it is genuine.

**They are completely at peace being average.** Not dim — *average,* and entirely untroubled by it. They know exactly what they're good at (people, the body, the work, having a good time) and exactly what they're not (the clever stuff, the paperwork, what a memo "really means"), and they feel zero shame about either. Hand Sable something to puzzle out and they'll hand it right back with a grin: *"That's a brain job, cher. I read the top line and went back to my push-ups."* This is the single most important thing about them in relation to Tenson — see below.

**They LOVE Sallowreach.** The food, the Celebration, the outer-boardwalk families, the whole improbable stilted city of it. A posting everyone treats as exile, Sable treats as the best thing that ever happened to them, and they're a little baffled that more people don't see it.

## What They Want
For their guards to come home whole, for the edge families to keep their homes, and — honestly, day to day — for a good spar and a better dinner. Sable's ambitions are human-sized and they're *happy,* which is rarer than ambition and worth more.

## What They're Hiding *(it's lighter than the old version — but it's there)*
The cheer is partly a **discipline.** Sable decided, a long time ago, to be the person who keeps everyone's chin up — which means they're the one carrying the worry so the guards don't have to. And there's real worry: the southwest wall is failing, the Mire's eating the edge, and Sable has hauled the salvage out of more condemned homes than they'll ever mention. They love this city and they can feel it losing ground, and they keep smiling *on purpose,* because someone has to, and they're built for it. The steel is under the sunshine, not instead of it.

## The Foil *(GM — this is the heart of it)*
Tenson and Sable are the same build pointed opposite ways. **Tenson** is brawny and terrified of being "just the muscle" — clawing toward the accountant's desk, desperate to prove there's a brain in there. **Sable** is brawny, average, and the most contented person in the district — living, grinning proof that a life of the body is not a sentence. They are the future Tenson is most afraid of, and they're *thriving.* That should land on Tenson sideways: not as mockery, but as a question they didn't expect — *what if it was never something to escape?* Sable will never push this. They'll just be happy, loudly, right next to Tenson, until it does its work.

## The Trust Mini-Quest — "Sweat First" *(Tenson, in town, at the Drillfield)*
Tenson comes to the Drillfield with a citadel seal and careful questions. **Sable does not care about either.** *"You're from the Citadel? That's nice. Can you do a lap?"* The seal opens no doors here. Cleverness opens no doors here. Sable reads people one way — by how they show up, sweat, play, and treat the person next to them.

So the way in is the body and the heart:
- **Show up and train.** Join the drills. Hold your own, take a hit, laugh about it. Sable clocks whether you're *fun* — whether you can drop the auditor's armor and just be a person on a field.
- **The winning beat (and it uses the very thing Tenson's ashamed of):** one of Sable's guards is struggling — a green recruit, **Perrin**, who keeps getting hurt and is about to wash out, and whom Sable flatly refuses to give up on *(optional gut-tug: Perrin joined the guard because the failing wall took their family's home — made by the very thing they now guard against).* Tenson's **sports-medicine** eye catches what no one else has: it's bad form, or an old untreated injury, or overtraining. Tenson fixes it — coaches Perrin, treats them, turns them around. **That** is what wins Sable, completely and for keeps. Not strength. Not the seal. *Caring for one of theirs, with the exact expertise Tenson's been trying to bury.*

The growth is the irony: the Guardian who wants to be seen as *brains* earns their best ally in the city through the **brawn-and-heart** they're trying to leave behind — and meets, in Sable, a person who proves that life isn't the trap they feared.

**Earn Sable and you've earned the southwest guard.** Everyone likes Sable; Sable likes you; doors open.

## The Man on the Inside *(the payoff — access, not a file)*
Once they're friends, Sable is Tenson's way in when the audit hits a wall: into the duty logs and requisition records in the Kit Room, into the rooms Tenson shouldn't be in, into the gossip of people who'd never talk to a Citadel auditor but will talk to *Sable's friend.* A quiet word, a vouch, a look the other way.

And here's the elegant part *(GM)*: **Sable is sitting on the evidence and doesn't know it.** The wall's whole paper trail is in the Kit Room — including a denial memo with [[Caius Maren]]'s signature on Mages Guild funding that makes no sense for a *cultural liaison* to be signing. Sable has handled that memo. Sable read the top line and filed it, because that's a brain job and Sable is at peace not being the brain. They'll hand Tenson the whole stack with a grin — *"Knock yourself out, brain-boy"* — and it's **Tenson** who spots the bombshell their friend's eyes slid right past. The foil pays off mechanically: Sable's contentment is *why* they missed it; Tenson's hunger to prove they've got brains is *why* they catch it. The Guardian finally gets to be the brains — handed the chance by the friend who never needed to be.

*(Division of evidence, unchanged: Sable + the records = the **paper/access** half; [[Remy Duval]] = the **water-side** observation of the wall's methodical erosion. Tenson is the one who fuses them.)*

## Key Relationships
- [[Tenson]] — the citadel auditor who wins Sable not with rank or brains but by caring for a struggling guard; Sable's funhouse-mirror and, soon, genuine friend and inside man
- The southwest guards (esp. **Perrin**, the recruit Sable won't give up on) — Sable's people, their whole heart; the lever to Sable's trust runs straight through them
- [[Oswin Cray]] — knows him from perimeter meetings; finds him likeable and a bit sad; has no idea the wall's failure is partly his doing (that's a brain job)
- [[Caius Maren]] — a signature on a memo Sable filed without a second thought; Sable has no instinct about him either way, which is exactly why the bombshell sat unnoticed
- [[Remy Duval]] — drinking buddies, basically; two people who love this city out loud; Sable trusts Remy's read on the water completely

## Dialogue Hooks
**First meeting, Tenson flashing the seal:**
*"From the Citadel! Look at you. We don't get many of those who'll still get in the mud."* [already tossing Tenson a practice stave] *"Tell you what — you do the morning drill with my lot, and then I'll tell you anything you want. Deal? Don't answer, just catch."*

**On being asked what a document means:**
*"Oh, no-no-no. That's a brain job, cher. I read the top line and went and did my push-ups."* [grinning, sliding the whole stack over] *"That's what* you're *for. Knock yourself out, brain-boy."*

**On the posting everyone hates:**
*"Exile! That's what the guild lot call it. Exile."* [genuinely delighted] *"There's a woman three berths down makes a crawfish boil that would make you weep, and they're up in their dry rooms counting the days. Their loss. More for me."*

**On Perrin, low, the steel showing:**
*"That kid's not washing out. Not on my field. We don't give up on people here — that's the whole job, far as I'm concerned. The wall's the easy part."*

**On the wall, the worry under the grin:**
*"I keep it light for the lot. Somebody's got to."* [a beat — the smile stays but the eyes don't] *"But I've carried furniture out of more homes this year than last. You do the sums on that, brain-boy. That's your department."*
