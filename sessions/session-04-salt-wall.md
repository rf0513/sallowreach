# Session 4 Set Piece — The Salt Line

*Isotta's arc-opener ([[isotta-arc|The Uncollected]]). Three parts spread across the session: a **strong start** (2 min), the **wall scene** (the centerpiece, ~45–60 min), and a **night button** (2 min, end of session). Designed dice-forward — four real rolls in the centerpiece — and every key clue fires **twice through different channels** (shown once, said aloud once), because subtle clues die at the table.*

---

## Where it slots in Session 4

- **Strong start:** the heron, first thing, before any errands. Points her at the wall like an arrow.
- **Centerpiece:** the wall, whenever she goes — works solo-with-witnesses or with tagalongs (hooks for Fletcher and Tenson below).
- **Night button:** the dream, as the session's last beat.

---

## Strong Start — The Heron at the Foot of the Bed

Isotta wakes on the boat (or wherever she anchored for the night). Read:

> Isotta, you wake up and do the thing you always do now — check your hands first, make sure all of you came back. It did. And then you realize what actually woke you: something's watching you. Has been for a while.
>
> There's a grey heron standing on the rail, maybe three feet from your feet. Which is wrong — herons are skittish, they bolt if you drop an oar. This one's just... standing there. Dead still. And it's looking at *you*. Not the fish basket, not the water. You.
>
> It holds your eyes for a second. Then it dips its head at you — politely, like an old man saying good morning to someone he knows — spreads its wings, and flies off southwest. Toward the wall.

**Fletcher's clue (hand it to the Ranger — let another player deliver it):** if Fletcher is anywhere nearby, he gets this for free, no roll — it's his whole skill set:

> "That's wrong, and I'll tell you exactly how it's wrong. Herons hunt alone and they hold a territory. That's the *same bird* I saw at the eel-smoker's yesterday, and outside the cat café before that. One heron doesn't range like that. It's not hunting a territory. It's following **something.**"

*(Fires [[isotta-arc#Secrets & Clues — the Tallykeeper file|Secret #1]]. If Fletcher's player hesitates, an NPC dockhand says it cruder: "Third day that bird's been on your boat, salt-eyes. You feedin' it?")*

## Before the Wall — Vael's Protocol

If she stops at [[The Still Cup]] first (recommended — he's expecting her). Vael doesn't give her a mystery. He gives her an **experiment**, in blood-hunter language, which is exactly the detective frame her player wants:

> "The wall was raised to push spirit-stuff out. Two hundred years, no exceptions — salt doesn't make exceptions. So here's the assignment, and you'll do it like Order work, not like a pilgrimage: **walk the line. Watch what it does to the dead. Then watch what it does to you. Write both down. Come back and read me the difference.**"

**The grounding technique.** Before she goes, he teaches her the anchor-move: a named thing, spoken aloud — her own name, a held hand, lantern-light — to haul herself back if the veil pulls.

- **Roll:** Knowledge or Instinct, Difficulty 12 `[VERIFY]`. Success banks the technique: at the wall she can invoke it for advantage on the Reaction roll, or to clear a tick of the [[isotta-arc#The Slipping Clock|Slipping clock]].
- Even on a failure she has it — it just costs a Stress to use under pressure (learned imperfectly).

**Vael's last line, as she leaves** *(plant it; it pays off in the debrief)*:

> "One more thing. If you see a heron out there — you leave it be. That's not Order advice. That's Mire advice."

## The Wall — beat by beat

### Beat 1 — The Weeping Face

> The wall is bigger than you expected. A huge rampart of grey salt, taller than a house, running off into the haze in both directions. Two hundred years of people packing it back together. And this stretch? This stretch is losing. The face is soft and wet, salt weeping down it in dark streaks, green stuff pushing up through the cracks.
>
> And right underneath it, there's a family stripping a drowned house. Pulling the windows out, stacking shutters in a barrow. The water already owns the place — they're just taking what it'll let them keep. There's a woman in a patched work coat doing it fast and quiet, like she's done this before and figures she'll be doing it again.

- The woman is **Solène Thouvenel** — steady, dry-humored, too tired to be bitter *out loud*. The Mire took the house; the ward-fund petition to shore this stretch was **denied** months ago.
- **Tenson hook (if present):** Solène has the denial notice, creased from handling. The signature line will mean nothing to her and everything to him — this is what the Embassy's opulence costs, with a face on it. *(Feeds his Caius/Oswin thread; don't reveal Caius's signature yet unless he digs — `[GM call]`.)*

### Beat 2 — The Control Group

*(This is the demonstration. Isotta gets to watch the wall work on a real ghost before testing herself — clean experimental method. Note: Solène has no relation to [[odile-verret|Odile Verret]], and the heron is **not** [[old-patience|Old Patience]] — see [[the-tallykeeper]] for the disambiguation and its six titles.)*

> Isotta — out past the salt line, where the yard turns into open water, there's a man standing on the surface of the water. Poler's oilskins. Hat in his hands, like he's at a door he can't bring himself to knock on. He's watching that woman carry his windows away.
>
> And every few minutes he tries to come closer — and the air just *shoves* him back. No sound, no flash. Like pushing two magnets together the wrong way. He staggers back across the water, waits, tries again. There's a worn path out there. He's been trying for a long, long time.

- He is **Anselme Thouvenel** — Solène's father, drowned poler, and (recast per the arc doc) **her first Lantern Docket case.**
- **Roll — Presence, Difficulty 12 `[VERIFY]`:** approach without spooking him. He hasn't been *seen* since he drowned; being seen nearly undoes him. On a hit, his case comes out plainly:

> "I built Solène that boat so she'd never owe the Mooring a copper. And she sold it — sold it *to the Mooring* — to pay for burying me. Everything I meant is turned inside out and I can't… I can't go while it's like this. I've tried. Believe me, I've tried."

*(Docket case opened: put the boat — or what it stood for — right, and Anselme can cross. Routes: buy it back, lever Fletcher's Gault/Mooring evidence, or something the players invent. Don't solve it tonight; open it. **If she tries to usher him across on the spot:** it fails informatively — he steps toward wherever she points and the anchor snaps him back like a mooring line. See [[isotta-arc#How a Crossing Works (when she tries to help one over)|How a Crossing Works]] for all three attempt-outcomes and the collection scene when the case eventually closes.)*

### Beat 3 — The Experiment (obvious clue, spoken aloud)

Now she does Vael's assignment: she walks up to the salt line herself.

- **The sensation:** it *drags* — at her edges, her outline, the parts of her that are spirit-stuff. It costs to approach. But —
- **She can cross.** Mark **1 Stress** stepping over the line; the drag is pain, not prohibition `[VERIFY vs your ward rules]`. She can stand on ground that two hundred years of salt keeps every ghost off of.
- **Anselme delivers the clue — baffled, out loud, unmissable:**

> "How are you *standing there?* I've thrown myself at that line a hundred times — it's like throwing yourself at the tide. And you just— you're one of us, I can *see* you're one of us, but the salt let you through. **What in the deep water are you?**"

*(Fires Secret #6, said in plain words: the wall keeps out the dead — and it doesn't keep out her. First casebook entry practically writes itself. If her player wants the deduction seeded harder, Solène adds the mortal-side echo: "Salt don't make exceptions, love. Never has.")*

### Beat 4 — The Heron on the Wall

> And then you spot it. Up on top of the wall. On the salt itself — the one place in this whole Mire where nothing dead, nothing spirit-touched, can stand. The heron. Just standing there. Comfortable. Watching you, same as it did this morning.

Anselme's whole manner changes. Hat pressed to his chest. Reverent, quiet:

> "Don't chase it off. Whatever you do. That's the **ferryman** — it comes when your crossing's ready, and it hasn't come for me because I'm not done. Two hundred years it's kept every account in this Mire — *the Tallykeeper*, my gran called it — and it's never missed a one."
>
> He goes quiet a moment. Then, softer, and this is the part he doesn't understand:
>
> "…But it's not looking at *me*, is it. It's looking at **you.**"

*(Fires Secret #3 — the dead revere the tallykeeper — and points the arrow straight at her. Two channels: shown by where it stands, said by what Anselme calls it. This is the beat the session exists for; give it air.)*

### Beat 5 — The Reach (optional push — her choice)

Offer it, don't force it: she can open her second sight right here, at the worn stretch, with the ferryman watching.

- **Roll — Instinct + *Connects with Spirits*, Difficulty 15 `[VERIFY]`.** Spend Hope for the Experience; the banked grounding technique helps.
- **On a hit, veilside for one held breath:**

> For one breath, you're seeing the other side. The wall's still a wall over here — but it glows, like a scar. And the heron on top of it is not a heron. It's *huge* — the size of a house — feathers like wet slate. And around its neck there's a cord, hung with knots. Hundreds of them. Thousands. Every death in this Mire, tied off. Finished. Done.
>
> And one cord with no knot in it at all. Worn smooth, like somebody's been handling it for years. And while you're looking at it... that cord lifts. Slowly. Like a compass needle finding north. And it points at *you*.
>
> One more thing, way out past the wall, out in the dark water: a pressure. Something enormous. Patient. Sad. You don't know what it is — and tonight, you don't have to.

*(The ledger is Secret-#7-made-visible. The last line is the great binding held to strand-length — one sentence, no names, per the arc's guardrail. **The Tallykeeper does not speak first — tonight or ever.** It inclines its head once, courteous as this morning, and lets the veil close. **But** it will not refuse a direct question twice: if Isotta insistently addresses it, the introduction — collection notice, grace negotiation, the cord — can fire tonight. Have [[the-tallykeeper#The Grace Period]] ready.)*

- **The pull:** reaching that deep, the worn veil works her loose. **Reaction Roll — Presence, Difficulty 14 `[VERIFY]`.** On a failure or with Fear: 1–2 Stress, her outline flickers, tick the **Slipping clock** — and the grounding technique is how she hauls back (say her name, grab a hand, find the lantern). *If a party member is there to be her anchor, let that be the save. Party glue.*

### Beat 6 — Debrief at the Still Cup

She reads Vael her casebook entries. He listens the way he does. Then — mentor rule: **he names the question, never the answer** (he genuinely doesn't have it; this is past even him):

> "Read those two lines back to back. The wall turned him away and let you through. Salt. Doesn't. Make. Exceptions. So it wasn't built to keep out whatever you are — which means whatever you are isn't in the Order's book. I've hunted forty years on that book, girl. You just walked off the edge of it."
>
> On the heron, he gets quieter than she's ever heard him:
>
> "I've cut down everything this Mire ever sent at me. I have never once raised a blade at a heron. Neither will you. Beyond that — I've got nothing. Nobody living does."
>
> And the assignment that hands her player the whole detective frame, explicitly:
>
> "So run the protocol. The one they taught you for hauntings — manifestations, anchor, **what the spirit is owed.** Run it on the one subject you've never dared put in the casebook. *You.*"

*(Also live here if wanted: his "Domino man" ask — Basile — as the next favor. Don't stack it if the night's already full.)*

## Night Button — the Dream

Last beat of the session, lights-down voice:

> That night, you dream about cords. A whole ledger of them, hundreds, each one tied off in a neat little knot. Finished lives. And one cord with no knot at all — smooth, like riverglass.
>
> In the dream, you reach out and take it. And it fits around your wrist. Perfectly. Like it was measured for you.
>
> You wake up gripping your own wrist — hard enough to leave marks. And out on the rail, in the dark, the heron is standing watch. And here's the thing: that's not the scary part. The scary part is that you slept better than you have in weeks.

*(Fires Secret #7. The last line is the wonder-note — her player's lane — and quietly true: the debt-holder's presence steadies the fraying. File that away; it matters at the parley.)*

## GM quick-reference

- **Rolls (4):** grounding lesson (Knowledge/Instinct 12) → parley Anselme (Presence 12) → the reach (Instinct + Experience 15) → the pull (Presence Reaction 14). All `[VERIFY]` vs SRD difficulty bands.
- **Stress economy:** 1 to cross the salt; 1–2 on a bad pull; Slipping clock debuts here.
- **Party jobs:** Fletcher = the wildlife clue + anything trail-craft; Tenson = Solène's denial notice (ward-fund rage fuel); Leavy = she *feels* the worn stretch but cannot see what Isotta sees — second sight stays Isotta's alone.
- **Guardrails:** the Tallykeeper never speaks *first* — but if Isotta insists, the introduction fires early (be ready with [[the-tallykeeper#The Grace Period]]); no names for the pressure beyond the wall; Vael confirms evidence but has no answers; the family's grief stays quiet, not milked.
- **Threads opened:** the Anselme docket case (boat ⇄ Mooring/Gault), Solène + the denied ward funds (Tenson), the Slipping clock, Basile on deck.
