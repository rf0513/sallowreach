# Isotta

> ✅ **ARC REDESIGNED — see [[isotta-arc]] ("The Uncollected").** Her death was never collected; the heron psychopomp — **the Tallykeeper** — holds the open line, and the **Lantern Docket** (the dead's cases) is her sidequest engine. Tuned to her player's confirmed leans: **mystery + wonder.** The old investigator plan in [[isotta-arc-brainstorm]] is superseded (salvage only the salt-wall set piece). Session 3's threads — *"wrong-shaped,"* [[Vael Miroux]] as kin, the dark pulling at her — are all folded in.

**Class:** Bloodhunter — Order of the Ghost Slayer subclass
**Ancestry:** Katari
**Community:** Orderborne
**Tier:** 1

## Experiences
- *Animal Whisperer* — an ease with creatures that reads less like training and more like fluency
- *Connects with Spirits* — perceives and communicates with presences most people can't sense

## The Shape of Her
Isotta grew up in the orbit of a famous family of bloodhunters. She is not one of them — not by blood — but through a chain of events she'll describe at length if you ask and immediately wish she hadn't, she ended up in the profession anyway. She's genuine about it in a way the famous family sometimes isn't. She hunts because the thing she now *is* happens to be the thing the Order hunts, and that demands a reckoning she hasn't finished having.

Because, clinically: **she is dead.** She died on the table during the bloodhunter transformation ritual and came back as a ghost wearing a ghost's body that happens to look like hers. She is a sworn member of the **Order of the Ghost Slayer** who is, herself, a ghost. She finds this considerably less funny than it sounds, and the campaign should agree with her — it's an irony that curdles into grief by the third session if you play it with weight instead of a wink.

**The condition, lived from inside:** she stands a little in both worlds at once. Spirits notice her. Animals read her as *other* — not a threat, just wrong, a still pool in moving water. The wrong thing catches at the edge of her vision; a sound arrives that no one else hears. She has been documenting all of it with the Order's clinical detachment, which is the box she keeps the fear in.

## What She Wants
To be cured — to be *only* one thing again, on one side of the line. Every serious source points the same direction: the veil between the living world and the spirit world is unnaturally thin in Sallowreach, something in the Mire charges the boundary itself. If a cure exists anywhere in reach, it's here.

**First stop in Sallowreach:** the bloodhunter cafe, [[The Still Cup]] — a gathering-place for the profession run like a quiet rotary club by [[Vael Miroux]], who has connections, resources, and a way of looking at her that says he already knows.

## The Spear Is Her Shot
*(GM — this is now a burning, concrete motive.)* [[the-tidebreaker|The Fishing Spear]] is a powerful artifact that **breaks curses,** and Isotta's ghost-condition is squarely within its power. Vael will tell her the cure is "a *parley* with the source, not an exorcism" — and the spear is exactly that bridge. Once she learns it exists, *this* is the thing she'll cross the city, deal with [[Marcel Dray]], and brave the deep Mire to get. Her ghost-curse breaks when they have the spear — a clean, earned, personal payoff. *(Same for [[the-witch|the Witch]]: two PCs now burn for one artifact, fusing the party's personal stakes onto Marcel's deal and the spear-quest. See [[the-tidebreaker|The Fishing Spear]].)*

**Distinct from the tether.** The spear breaks her *ghost-curse* — but the *tether* (the thing all four share) is a different order of problem: not a curse you cut, but the great binding at the city's heart, which only the finale resolves. So Isotta can get her personal cure mid-campaign and *still* be bound to the Mire with the others. "Taste now, cure at the finale" — except for her, the personal taste is a full meal, and the tether is the part that waits.

## What She Learns About Herself — "Old Basile"
*(GM — Vael's in-town quest; the clean introduction to the spirit world, built around Isotta.)* There's a **spirit world** alongside this one; the veil between is thin in the Mire; spirits cross, come in every temperament (not good or evil by nature), and the small ones can pass as human. [[Vael Miroux]] proves it by introducing Isotta to **Basile** — a beloved old Still Cup regular who is, and has secretly long been, a small spirit, now *slipping* as the veil strains. Helping Basile teaches Isotta the mechanics that bear directly on her: how a spirit is **anchored** to this world, how a **parley** works (the exact skill her own cure needs), and — the gut-punch — that she is the *inverse* of Basile: a spirit clinging to humanity vs. a human cursed into spirit-shape. Vael's white eye reads her plainly: *she didn't cross, she was* **tied** *— that's a curse, not a haunting* (confirming the ghost-state is a curse the spear can break, and pointing her down the [[Delphine]] → [[Caspar Renne]] → deep-Mire chain toward it). Full quest in [[Vael Miroux]].

## Deepest Lore Conduit
*(GM — handle with the cosmology's restraint.)* Her *Connects with Spirits* + the ghost condition make her the one PC who should be receiving impressions before anyone else has a clue there's anything to receive — but she **perceives, she doesn't understand** (hold the line in [[Cosmology#The Recognition Reveal — what they can know, and when|the reveal rules]]). She is also, of everyone who washed up on that bank, the **most entangled in the place:** the charged water that tethered the party is the same boundary-magic that anchors her ghost. She didn't just wash up in Sallowreach — Sallowreach is *built on the answer to her.*

## What She's Carrying
The Order's ritual tools, quietly adapted for a practitioner who is also a valid subject of her own discipline. A casebook tracking her condition's progression in the Order's flat, observational prose. Underneath the flat prose, in the margins and the things left unwritten, something far less organized.

## Key Relationships
- [[Vael Miroux]] — a former blood hunter (Order of the Ghost Slayer), owner of [[The Still Cup]]; one white eye that **sees spirits for what they are**, including the ones wearing human faces; the city's quiet authority on the spirit world. Isotta's first true teacher about what she is — and the one who reads her and says the thing no one else can: *you weren't haunted, you were* bound
- [[Delphine]] — the healer who reads things she can't name; she and Isotta will register each other as kin — two people who carry fragments of something the city won't say out loud
- The famous bloodhunter family she grew up around — *(needs a name from Isotta)* — not her teachers exactly, but the people who shaped what she believed was possible; complicated, unfinished
- The Mire's spirits — they will find her. So, in their way, will whatever is bound in the [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|black water]].

## The Tether
She came to cure a condition that left her between alive and dead. The tether, it turns out, is also a between — the charged water that might hold her cure is the very thing now holding *her.* She is more woven into this place than anyone else on that ship, and the longer she's here, the more it answers her. She of all of them should feel the recognition-click coming before her mind admits it.

## Notes for the GM
- **She's the early-warning system.** Let her get the first fragments — a feeling near the water, a wrongness on Celebration nights, a sense that the city's lanterns are *familiar.* But hold the cosmology's line: she perceives, she does not *understand.* No NPC and no instinct of hers should connect the Celebration to the curse before the earned recognition beat (see [[Cosmology#The Recognition Reveal — what they can know, and when|the reveal rules]]).
- **Order of the Ghost Slayer, is a ghost — play it for tragedy.** It's funny once. Then it's the loneliest thing at the table.
- **The "followed the light up" beat is strongest on her.** She has already died once. A [[lantern-rays|lantern ray]] handing her a *second* second chance underwater is exactly the layered, aching image this campaign rewards — strong candidate for the personalized cold-open moment in [[session-01-prep]].
- **Animal Whisperer + the Mire's fauna** is a built-in bridge to [[Remy Duval]]'s reverence and the deep channels. Her read on a creature should sometimes carry an extra layer no one else gets.
- **Backstory gut-punch available:** the famous family. *Did they know the ritual could do this? Did anyone warn her? Whose idea were the "shenanigans" that got her in?* Give it a name and a face — someone who will eventually have to answer for what she became.
- `[VERIFY]` Bloodhunter class + Order of the Ghost Slayer mechanics, and how a ghost-condition PC interacts with the Mire's thin veil — worth a ruling before it comes up at the table.
