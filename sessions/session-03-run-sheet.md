## 🎲 Dice First
*Last session had ~2 rolls in hours. **The reflex:** the moment a PC tries to* get *something — read a person, find a thing, push past a "no," impress someone, hold their nerve — call the roll* before *you narrate the result.*

**Action roll** = 2d12 (Hope die + Fear die) + trait vs **Difficulty.** Hope die high → gain a **Hope.** Fear die high → **you** gain a **Fear.** Matching dice = **Crit** (auto-succeed, gain Hope, clear a Stress).
- **Spend a Hope → +2** from a relevant Experience.
- **Help an Ally** → the helper **spends 1 Hope** and rolls **+d6** (if several help, only the **highest** d6 applies).
- **One PC rolls, others Help** — don't gang-roll the same thing.
- ⚠️ **Reaction rolls** (resisting a hazard) generate **no Hope/Fear** and **can't be Helped.** *(Matters for the wall — see that scene.)*
- **Gate the stakes, not the fun:** they always learn *something*; the die decides the **cost** and the **bonus.**

**Diff benchmarks (SRD):** 10 = moderate · **11 = Tier-1 baseline** · 13–14 = hard · 15 = a real reach.

### 0 · Too Many Teeth *(strong start)*
> Morning on the boards, and it's the good kind — the cleanup feast still going from the Celebration, somebody pressing warm bread into hands, the smell of frying lantern-root. You're three bites into the best welcome a city ever gave anyone. And then a woman screams. She comes down the boardwalk at a dead run, and she's holding — you see it before your brain wants to — a little dog, or what's left of one, its leash chewed clean through, blood on her forearms. People scatter. She's not screaming for help. She's screaming at the *city.* *"It came back! In daylight — it came BACK! Eyes like coins under the boards. And a sound — it sounded like a* child. *Somebody do something before it takes one of ours."* And behind you the mood's already turning — a man pulling a gaff-hook off a wall, somebody shouting a number like a bounty. And every face that was smiling at you a second ago is now turned your way, waiting.

*GM — the door into the whole day. The "haunt" is the amber civet; the crowd's the real hazard. **Open on the crowd's eyes, not dice.** If Fletcher doesn't bite, the civet stays loose and escalates toward dinner.*

### 🌿 Delphine's Hand-Off *(launches the curse path)*
**Walk-in:** drying herbs and beeswax, the mineral smell of deep Mire water; bundles from every rafter; she's already looking at whoever's most worn-down before anyone speaks.

**How to play:** gets **very close and very quiet** when she examines. Blunt but never cruel — what's wrong, what she can do, what she can't, what to do next, *in that order.* **The tell:** her eyes drift a half-second *left,* she goes still a beat too long, then she's back like nothing happened. Bristles at being treated like an oracle.

> *"I've turned this over since you left, and I'll tell you plain: this isn't new. What's tied to you is* old *— older than me, maybe older than the stories this city tells about itself. That's past my shelf. But I know a man who chases things that old for a living. Caspar Renne — the museum, up in the old quarter. Tell him I sent you. He'll actually listen."*

🎲 **Read what she's not saying** — *Instinct 13* → catches the drift-left; *something hit her* she won't name. Don't disclose the vision — *"Old habit. The water's loud today."* · **Press her on how old** — *Presence/Knowledge 12* → old, deep, past her shelf → Caspar.
> **Guardrails:** she doesn't know about any binding/prison/Tree Spirit. The "it's like the Celebration" line is a *return-visit* reward, not now.

### 🏹 The Hunt — "The Haunt of Thornside" *(Fletcher; night beat)*
**Tracking read-aloud:**
> Down through a gap in the planks, the world goes close and dark and dripping — a forest of stilts furred with weed, the water a foot below you black as tar. Up here it smelled like bread. Down here it smells like fear: a sharp, musky, animal reek, fresh. And the sign's everywhere — claw-scores too high and too neat for any Mire creature, a *climber's* marks. A half-eaten cache of stolen larder-food, untouched fish right beside it *(this thing doesn't even know what's food here).* And snagged on a nail, a tuft of ringed, dry-country fur — and under it, a smear of old blood that isn't the dog's. The animal's own. It's hurt. It's been hurt a while.

**Cornered read-aloud:**
> You corner it where a broken crate's wedged between two pilings, and for one second your blood goes cold — the eyes *do* catch the light, two coins in the dark — and then it moves into a shaft of daylight and the monster falls apart into what it actually is. A cat-sized thing, ringed tail, a sleek climber built for dry cliffs a thousand miles from here. One claw clipped to a stub. Sores worn into its neck the exact shape of a cage. Ribs you can count. It makes that sound again — the one that scared a whole neighborhood, the one that sounds almost like a child crying — and you realize it's not a threat. It's terrified. It has been terrified since the day somebody put it in a box.

🎲 **Track** — *Instinct + Eye for Danger/Swamp Specialist* → flips predator to prey. · **Corner without butchering** — *Agility/Finesse,* civet **Diff 12,** flees not fights, *not a damage race.* · **Chase the cause** — *Knowledge/Instinct* → Mooring crates → **Gault's** marked cages.
*Point: the monster in the dark is trafficked, starving, far from home — the campaign's thesis in Fletcher's hands. Pass → snake intel + Brin ally; clean kill → **Thornside Amber.** (Optional heavy: a hidden litter.)*

### 🧂 The Salt Wall *(Isotta's re-entry & arc-opener — your 4-roll showcase)*

**How to play Vael:** calm the way deep water is calm; listens far more than speaks; sets a coffee down with no ceremony. **The white eye** drifts to something past your shoulder, then the brown one finds you. Flat "I don't know" when he doesn't; dry humor at the bottom of a long silence.
> *"I've served a few like you. Died in the transformation, came back still attached. The Order says that's not supposed to survive. But you're not quite that either, are you. Sit. Drink."*

**Approach read-aloud:**
> Vael walks you out past the last lit windows — and there it is. Bigger than the stories. A rampart of grey salt taller than a stilt-house, crusted and re-packed over two hundred years, running into the haze both ways. And this stretch is *losing:* the southwest face soft, weeping, green pushing through every crack, a family lifting the windows out of a condemned house in the half-dark. Vael stops ten paces back — and you feel why before he says it: the closer you get, the more the salt *pulls* at you. Not your body — your *edges.* *"This is as far as I go. The salt was raised to push out things like what's pressing on the far side. Trouble is — it doesn't know the difference between that, and you. The rest is yours alone."*

**Contact read-aloud *(hold this one):***
> You push past where the living can stand, to the worn place where the wall's gone to gauze — and something on the far side feels the opening and *leans toward it.* It is enormous. It presses on you the way deep water presses when you dive too far, except the pressure is *grief* — old, patient, bottomless. And here's the thing you'll write down later, when your hands stop shaking: it isn't reaching for *you.* It's reaching *past* you — through the salt, through the city, toward something held deep in the middle of all those lights — and for one breath you feel both ends at once. The vast thing in the dark, straining in. And a small, fixed, buried point in the heart of the city, straining back. Two hundred years, and never touching. Like a held note. Waiting to land.

🎲 **Grounding lesson** (*Knowledge/Instinct*) → banks advantage for the wall · **read the line** (*Instinct*; push to the salt costs **1–2 Stress**) · **THE REACH** (*Instinct + Connects with Spirits* vs **Diff 15**; spend Hope for the Experience) · **parley the dead** (*Presence* → "the one they keep") · **the pull** (*Presence* vs **Diff 14** — run it as a **normal action roll** so it swings Hope/Fear; on Fear she half-crosses, the grounding move hauls her back).
> **Guardrail:** she feels the **shape, never the answer.** No Tree Spirit, no Celebration-as-cage, no founders. Direction and dread only. **Leaves her 3 leads:** Caspar · Vael's training (Basile) · Oswin/the breach.

### 🥊 The Drillfield — Sable *(Tenson's mirror; dice goldmine)*
**Walk-in:** a pounded-flat training yard on the southwest line, loud with grunts and laughter at once — recruits running brutal drills and *grinning.* The big sergeant in the middle sweats hardest and enjoys it most, usually mid-laugh, usually holding food.

**How to play:** the **grin arrives before they do.** Taps a Celebration drum-rhythm without noticing. Tosses you a stave mid-sentence. Loud, warm, *cher* and *brain-boy;* at peace being average — hands anything clever right back. Steel only shows when they talk about their people.
> *"From the Citadel! Look at you. We don't get many who'll still get in the mud. You do the morning drill with my lot, then I'll tell you anything you want. Deal? Don't answer — just catch."*

🎲 **The spar** — *Strength/Agility 12;* **fail forward:** eat a stave, mark 1 Stress, but Sable likes that you got up grinning. · **Spot Perrin's old injury** — *Knowledge/Instinct 13* (the winning beat — Tenson's buried sports-medicine eye). · **Coach him through it** — *Presence 12* → wins Sable for keeps.

### 🏹 The Ranging House — Brin *(if they wander)*
**Walk-in:** three storeys of waxed timber at the city's wet edge; inside, rendered fat and solvent and river-mud worn into the thresholds. A contract board color-coded by danger. In the corner, a table buried in three-color channel-maps and a wall of tagged shed-skins.

**How to play:** moves quietly even indoors; reaches for her own map *before you finish talking.* Precise, no ornament; asks three sharp questions before she answers one; never raises her voice.
> *"Two-headed pattern. Southwest junction. Three months old, give or take. I've got four more of these in a drawer. What I don't have is a reason to trust the man holding the fifth."*

🎲 **Read the contract board** — *Knowledge/Instinct 10* → what the Mire's doing (contracts thinning, two hunters lost). · **Make your case** — *Presence 14* (not required; warms her faster) — the pitch: *"I hunt to remove a threat, not for glory or coin."*
**Send-off:** *"There's a haunt loose in Thornside. Go handle it — and how you do it is the whole answer."* → the civet job (night); snake intel **gated** behind passing it.

### 🏛️ The Embassy *(the showpiece — don't fumble it; two movements)*
**Movement I — wonder *(let them love it):***
> There's a door at the end of the inner boardwalk, and you step through — and you step *out of Sallowreach.* The heat goes first — two hundred years of wet bayou air, gone — and you're breathing something dry and clean and faintly sweet, like the first cool morning of an autumn you haven't felt since you got here. There's *grass.* Gravel paths. Apple trees in full white blossom *and* heavy with red fruit at once. A gold butterfly drifts past. Birdsong up in the blossoms. It is, honestly, beautiful. It feels like *home.*

**Movement II — the seams *(drip, don't dump):***
> And then it starts to come apart at the edges. You go to brush the butterfly away and your hand passes *through* it — no weight, the same lazy pattern, because it isn't flying, it's being *drawn.* You pick one of those impossible apples and it's flawless and it has no *smell* — and when you bite it there's no crunch, no juice, your teeth close on cool air and a fading image. And now you can't stop seeing it: no insects in this grass. No frogs. No gnats. The birdsong loops. The breeze pulses on a schedule. And half-hidden behind a blossoming hedge, three people in plain robes sit very still with their eyes shut and their hands open and **real sweat** running down their faces — the only real sweat in this whole garden — from the effort of holding all of it up.

*GM — **the Empire's thesis vs. Sallowreach's, in one room.** The swamp's magic is alive and asks nothing; the Empire's is illusion maintained by hidden labor. Caius doesn't see the irony — to him this IS "better infrastructure." Let them love it, then let the seams teach them what kind of man hosts a dinner inside a lie. **File it, don't underline it.** If a PC pities the straining mages, the scene's working.*

### 🍷 The Dinner — Caius *(the centerpiece; SOCIAL, not combat)*

**How to play:** imperial true believer; warm, delighted, genuinely charming — younger than expected, dry and pressed and at ease in his impossible garden. Every warm question is quietly **filed as testimony.** **Tell:** always too put-together for the humidity.

**The host read-aloud:**
> In the middle of his impossible garden, at a table set with the best of everything, Caius Maren stands to greet you — *delighted.* He's poured the wine himself. *"You came. Good. I've read three incident reports out of this Mire in two years, and not one had survivors. And here you are, at my table. Sit. Eat something that hasn't been fried in swamp-fat."* [wonderful company — asks about the city, your trades, the Celebration, listens like every word's a gift. Then, second course, gentle, almost an afterthought:] *"Can I ask — and stop me if it's too soon — where exactly did she go down? Out past the southwest channels, was it? And there were the — what, the four of you, and your guide? How long were you in the water, before the light?"* [and he waits. And listens. Like he'll remember all of it.]

🎲 **Catch the slip** — *Instinct/Knowledge* → he knows a detail he shouldn't (*"past the southwest channels" / "the four of you" / "before the light"*). A hit plants *"this man already knew about our wreck"* — **not** that he did it. **For Tenson:** this is the man who was told the Guardian died. Let the divination itch; don't pay it off. · **Decline the written report** — *Presence* → his real ask (a formal incident report = document fourteen in his annexation file) turned down without burning the bridge.
> **Vivienne's armor:** if she dressed them, they arrive in **Sallowreach finery, not imperial fashion** — a quiet "he doesn't own you." Caius feels it and is mildly, genuinely put out. **He is NOT a fighter** (two guards, an unused knife) — keep it social; the payoff is the *realization later.*
