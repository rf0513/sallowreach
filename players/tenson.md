# Tenson

**Class:** Guardian — Stalwart subclass
**Ancestry:** Faun
**Community:** Orderborne
**Home:** the capital *(confirm scale with player — big-city, citadel-adjacent)*
**Tier:** 1

## Experiences
- *Intense Discipline* — the body and habits of someone trained hard, young, and never quite allowed to stop
- *Budding Bureaucrat* — a genuine appetite for procedure, documentation, and how institutions actually move money and blame

## The Shape of Them
Tenson is the kid who played the sport all through school, took the scholarship, earned the degree in sports medicine because it was the logical next rung — and graduated into a career they were excellent at and quietly couldn't stand. Personal guard to a high-ranking citadel wizard. Very good at it. The discipline is real. But the part of the job that actually lit them up turned out to be the *paperwork* — the rosters, the incident logs, the way a well-kept ledger makes a chaotic thing comprehensible.

They want to be an accountant. Not a euphemism for it — not "treasurer," not "administrator." An accountant. Numbers. Books. The clean satisfaction of a column that balances. It's the least romantic dream at the table and they hold it with total sincerity, and a campaign that takes it seriously will find it quietly moving: the brawn-over-brains tank who just wants to be left alone with a ledger.

So [[Magistra Silence]] — the citadel wizard they guarded, who is genuinely fond of them and not blind to the mismatch — did them what she called a favor. She sent them to Sallowreach to investigate something that needed *both* halves of Tenson: a security question and a financial irregularity in one knot. Why does [[Oswin Cray]] keep requesting funds for the salt wall? Why does [[Caius Maren]] keep denying them? Find out.

Tenson has not fully sat with the fact that "go audit a possible corruption scandal in a city with no roads in or out" *was the kindness.*

## What They Want
Two things, pulling against each other:

1. **The assignment.** Understand the Oswin/Caius funding dispute. The wall matters to someone who matters at the citadel — enough to spend a person on it. Why is it underfunded? Who benefits? What does the Imperial Liaison know that the paperwork doesn't say? *(This is, functionally, a citadel-sanctioned audit dropped directly onto the campaign's central fault line — see below.)*
2. **The pivot.** Permission — somehow, from someone, maybe just from themselves — to stop being the thing they were built to be. Do this job well, on their own terms, with research and documentation instead of a shield, and maybe that's evidence they could be something else. Maybe a clean ledger from Sallowreach is the first page of a different life.

**First stop in Sallowreach:** [[The Drillfield]], the southwest perimeter training ground. Partly old reflex — bodies that trained that hard don't sit still easily. Partly instinct: people who work with their bodies gather there, and people who gather there *talk.* It's also, not coincidentally, where [[Sable Crue]] runs the guard that watches the failing wall.

## The Auditor on the Fault Line
*(GM — this is the hook the table built on purpose, and it's load-bearing.)* Tenson arrives pre-aimed at **Front 2 (the wall), Front 6 (Caius), and Front 7 (Marcel/the embezzlement)** — see [[world-in-motion]]. They are the one PC the city's political players will treat as a *piece on the board* from day one: a credentialed outsider asking exactly the questions the powerful have reasons to deflect.

- [[Oswin Cray]] is skimming the maintenance budget to pay a gambling debt to [[Marcel Dray]]. The embezzlement *is* the answer to "why does Oswin keep asking for money."
- [[Caius Maren]] is denying the budget *on purpose* — he wants the wall to fail so he can manufacture the crisis that justifies imperial annexation. The denial Tenson is investigating is an act of sabotage dressed as fiscal protocol.
- A **budding bureaucrat** is the perfect instrument and the perfect victim here: Tenson will want to see the ledgers — and the ledgers, followed honestly, lead to a broke wizard begging and a true-believer imperial agent who arranged the party's *own shipwreck.* The audit ends in two gut-punches the campaign already has loaded.

## What They're Carrying
A letter of introduction from [[Magistra Silence]] — official enough to open doors, vague enough not to spook anyone. Their own personal ledger, kept immaculately, not because anyone requires it but because they *like* it. And a body that is, unfairly, still built like a career guardian even though its owner would rather be at a desk.

## Key Relationships
- **[[Magistra Silence]]** — the citadel wizard who assigned the job; fond of Tenson, still their patron, still expecting a report; she knows Sallowreach's politics — she does not know the Mire can *keep* people
- **[[Sable Crue]]** — sergeant of the southwest perimeter guard at [[The Drillfield]]; brawny, sunny, gloriously at peace being average — Tenson's funhouse mirror and, once earned, their man on the inside. Won not by the seal or by cleverness but by caring for one of Sable's struggling guards (Tenson's buried sports-medicine skill). Opens the door to the records; the memo inside is Tenson's to find
- **[[Oswin Cray]]** — the wizard whose requests keep getting denied; Tenson doesn't yet know whether he's a victim, a crook, or both *(he's both)*
- **[[Caius Maren]]** — the liaison denying the funds; Tenson has no reason yet to suspect the denial is sabotage

## The Tether
Tenson came on official business with the exit built into the brief: investigate, report, return. The tether cuts off *return.* Now it's an indefinite audit in a city they can't leave, on behalf of a patron who will, eventually, want answers and updates. The Magistra knows the city's politics. She presumably does not know it can trap people. Whether she'd consider Tenson's tether a tragedy or an *asset* — a permanent, deniable agent inside Sallowreach — is a question worth letting Tenson lie awake over.

## Notes for the GM
- **They'll want the ledgers — give them ledgers.** Documentation is how this PC engages the world. The Oswin/Caius story is, underneath, a paper trail, and Tenson is built to read it. [[Sable Crue]] is the *access* (the inside man who waves them into the records); [[Oswin Cray]]'s document box ([[Oswin Cray#The Evidence In His Office|the evidence]]) is a later payoff.
- **Let Tenson be the brains — that's the whole arc.** The Caius memo sits unread in the Drillfield files precisely because [[Sable Crue|Sable]] is content not to be the brain ("that's a brain job, cher"). Sable hands Tenson the boring stack with a grin; *Tenson* spots the bombshell. Don't let an NPC pre-chew it. The Guardian who's desperate to prove they have brains should be the one who *uses* them here.
- **Sable is the foil, not a second documentarian.** [[Remy Duval]] observes the wall from the *water* (the methodical, rhythmic erosion = spirit pressure); the *records* (and Sable's access to them) are the paper half; Tenson fuses the two. And Sable personally is the joyful, at-peace-with-brawn life Tenson's terrified of becoming — played as a quiet challenge to the whole pivot, never as mockery.
- **The Magistra is a powerful off-screen face of urgency.** Her letters can arrive (the campaign's "forwarded after the ship was reported lost" device, see [[session-01-prep]]). Decide early how much *she* knows and what she'd do with a trapped Tenson — that ambiguity is a backstory gut-punch waiting to be triggered.
- **The pivot is the soft target.** A character who secretly wants out of who they are is begging for a moment where they have to *be* the guardian to save someone — or where the desk-life they want is dangled and costs something. Honor the accountant dream; then make it expensive.
- **Backstory gut-punch available:** *was it really a favor?* The cleanest version: the Magistra meant kindness and is horrified to learn Tenson is trapped. The sharper version: she suspected the risk and spent them anyway. Hold both live; let the table's relationship with her letters decide.
