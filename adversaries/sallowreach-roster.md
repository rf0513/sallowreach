# Sallowreach Roster

*The people who might stand in the party's way. Daggerheart SRD adversary format (Tier 1: Difficulty 11, Thresholds 7/12, ATK +1, damage 1d6+2–1d12+4; Tier 2: Difficulty 14, Thresholds 10/20, ATK +2, damage 2d6+3–2d12+4).*

*A note that matters in this campaign: per [[Sallowreach (Campaign Frame)#GM Principles|Complications Have Faces]], almost none of these want a fight. The bandits want a toll, the enforcers want you gone (not dead), the marshals want order, the guards want to follow orders, and the Tessak want their children to live. Lead with the want. The stat block is what happens when the want isn't met.*

*Used by: [[The Wending]], [[The Pale Room]], [[The Celebration]], [[The Hall of the Mire]], [[The Lantern-Ray Shallows]], [[The Deep Mire]].*

## Wending Runner

**Tier 1 Standard**

*Punt-pirates and channel-runners who make their living on the one waterway you didn't know was there. They don't want blood — blood is bad for repeat business. They want the toll.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Levy a toll, control the channel, withdraw if it gets expensive

**Difficulty:** 12 | **Thresholds:** 7/13 | **HP:** 5 | **Stress:** 2
**ATK:** +1 | **Gaff Hook:** Melee | 1d8+1 phy  ·  **Sling:** Far | 1d6+1 phy
**Experience:** Knows the Channel +2

**Features:**
- **Reads the Water - Passive:** A Wending Runner is never Lost in the Mire and ignores its difficult terrain. In a chase or a navigation contest, they roll with advantage.
- **The Toll, Not the Blood - Passive:** They open with a demand, not an attack. If paid (coin, cargo, a favor, a name), they let the party pass and may even guide them. Violence is their second choice, always.
- **Cut and Run - Reaction:** When a Runner marks HP, they may mark a Stress to move up to Close and take cover behind reeds, a hull, or a companion.

## Wending Runner Lackey

**Tier 1 Minion**

*Younger, hungrier, in the boat because the alternative was worse. Dangerous in a pack, gone the moment it turns.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Back up the crew, look tough, bolt when it sours

**Difficulty:** 10 | **Thresholds:** None | **HP:** 1 | **Stress:** 1
**ATK:** -1 | **Boat Knife:** Melee | 2 phy
**Experience:** —

**Features:**
- **Minion (4) - Passive:** The Lackey is defeated when they take any damage. For every 4 damage a PC deals to the Lackey, defeat an additional Minion within range the attack would succeed against.
- **Swarm the Punt - Action:** Spend a Fear to spotlight all Lackeys within Close range of a target. They close to Melee and make one shared attack roll; on a success they deal 2 physical damage each, combined.

## Pale Room Enforcer

**Tier 2 Skulk**

*Marcel's idea of a problem solved. Quiet clothes, quiet voice, cold hands like his. They don't threaten — they describe what's about to happen, and then it does. They would much rather walk you to the door than hit you.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Keep the room calm, remove the problem, never escalate first

**Difficulty:** 14 | **Thresholds:** 9/17 | **HP:** 6 | **Stress:** 3
**ATK:** +2 | **Blackjack:** Melee | 2d6+2 phy *(nonlethal by preference)*
**Experience:** Reads the Room +3

**Features:**
- **Nothing Personal - Passive:** The Enforcer never makes the first move. Against a target who drew a weapon or struck first, their attacks deal an extra 1d6 — they are very good at finishing what someone else started.
- **Escort Out - Action:** Mark a Stress and attempt to remove a target. On a success, the target is Grappled and moved up to Close toward the exit; they break free with a Strength or Finesse Roll. No damage — the point is the door, not the floor.- **Marcel Is Watching - Reaction:** The first time a PC threatens real violence inside [[The Pale Room]], the whole room goes still. Every PC marks 1 Stress under the weight of [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]]'s attention, and they all understand, wordlessly, that this is the worst possible place to do this.
> **They do not fight to kill** unless Marcel's own life is on the line. If a brawl turns lethal, that is the campaign telling you something has gone badly wrong — see [[Marcel Dray]].

## Imperial Attaché Guard

**Tier 2 Standard**

*The two soldiers who stand outside [[Caius Maren|Caius]]'s door and shadow him through the market. Drilled, courteous, and entirely willing — the danger isn't malice, it's obedience.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Protect the attaché, enforce the order, hold the line

**Difficulty:** 14 | **Thresholds:** 10/19 | **HP:** 6 | **Stress:** 2
**ATK:** +2 | **Issued Blade:** Melee | 2d6+3 phy  ·  **Crossbow:** Far | 2d6+1 phy
**Experience:** Drilled +2

**Features:**
- **By the Authority Vested - Passive:** While acting on a lawful imperial order, the Guard rolls with advantage to intimidate, detain, or command civilians — and most of Sallowreach knows better than to test the seal.
- **Disciplined Line - Reaction:** When another Guard within Close range is targeted by an attack, this Guard may mark a Stress to step in and grant that ally resistance to the damage.- **Make the Arrest - Fear:** Spend a Fear to formally declare a target detained in the Empire's name. Until the end of the scene, any ally who helps that target resist or flee marks a Stress for defying imperial authority. *(This is the move that turns [[Caius Maren|Caius]]'s cornered endgame from a conversation into a crisis.)*

## Celebration Marshal

**Tier 1 Standard** *(also runnable as Minions — see below)*

*Stewards of the Musicians' Accord and [[Adaline Roux|Adaline]]'s civic marshals — the people who keep sixty thousand celebrants safe and the [[The Celebration|Celebration]] moving. Local, known, and absolutely uninterested in hurting anyone. They restore order; they do not wound.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Restore order, separate the fighters, protect the ritual, hurt no one

**Difficulty:** 11 | **Thresholds:** 6/12 | **HP:** 4 | **Stress:** 2
**ATK:** +1 | **Restraining Hold:** Melee | 1d6 phy *(nonlethal)* + Grappled
**Experience:** Keeps the Peace +2

**Features:**
- **Restore Order, Not Harm - Passive:** A Marshal never deals damage that would drop a PC to 0 HP. Their aim is to remove, separate, and calm — to get the problem out of the crowd, not to put it on the ground.
- **Part the Crowd - Action:** The Marshal pulls a target up to Close out of the press of celebrants; the crowd closes behind, blocking pursuit. Useful for splitting up a fight before it spreads.
- **We All Know Each Other - Passive:** A Marshal can be talked down by anyone with standing in Sallowreach — a vouching local, an honored-guest claim, an appeal to the night itself. Roleplay first; rolls only if that fails.

> **As Minions:** to handle a surging crowd, run a line of stewards as **Minion (3)** — Difficulty 10, HP 1, ATK +0, **Shove:** Melee | 1 (nonlethal). They herd and contain; they never strike to harm.

> ## ⚠ Canon note — the Tessak are man-eaters *(GM decision)*
> The sympathetic "dying children / they're just parents" framing in `world/creatures/lantern-rays.md` is **superseded.** The Tessak are a **predatory deep-channel clan** for whom the warm-blooded peoples are simply the finest prey the Mire offers — no malice, you don't hate a fish. **Retained:** their `tessavrak` restraint-ethic ("taking beyond the season") and the keystone-ray collapse plot — predators with a strict sustainable-take code, *corrupted by Gault into excess.* **Re-cut:** why they serve Gault — from "medicine for the sick" to a **predator-supply deal** (he stirs the channels and sinks ships; the wrecks feed the clan). The full `lantern-rays.md` / `gault.md` Act 2 rewrite is **pending** (see `STATUS.md`). Until it lands, run the clan as predators, not victims.

## Tessak Scout

**Tier 1 Skulk** *(Session 1 **Encounter B** — see [[session-01-prep#Potential Adversaries|Session 1 Prep]])*

*A lone outrider of the deep-channel clan — the people-eaters the city pretends aren't there. It came to the wreck for the meal the Mire delivered and found some of the meat crawled off onto the bank. Lean, patient, no malice in it whatsoever: to a Tessak you are not an enemy, you are food that got up and walked.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Cut out the weakest, snare it, drag it to deep water, slip away before the rest organize

**Difficulty:** 12 | **Thresholds:** 7/14 | **HP:** 6 | **Stress:** 3
**ATK:** +1 | **Barbed Harpoon:** Close | 1d8+2 phy  ·  **Weighted Bola:** Far | 1d6 phy + target Restrained**Experience:** Hunts the Channels +3

**Features:**
- **Of the Deep Channels - Passive:** Never Lost, ignores the Mire's difficult terrain and currents, can stay submerged as long as it likes. In the water it has advantage on nearly everything.
- **Haul It Home - Action:** Mark a Stress and attack a target in Close. On a success, deal 1d8+2 and the target is **Grappled** on the harpoon-line; on each of the Scout's turns it drags the Grappled target a Close distance toward deep water. Escape with a Strength or Finesse Roll, or an ally deals 6 damage to the line. *(The threat is the **dragging-off,** not the bite — it means to take them home.)*- **Food That Walks - Passive:** The Scout always works the most wounded or most isolated target (already marked HP, alone, or in the water). Not cruelty — economy. Its first attack each scene against such a target is made with advantage.
- **Sound the Channel - Fear:** Spend a Fear. The Scout trills a low note through the water and a fresh group of **[[mire-bestiary#Mire Gar|Mire Gar]]** boils up at the blood. *(The clan and its scavengers share a table.)*
- **Slip the Net - Reaction:** When the Scout marks HP, it may submerge and move Close, vanishing until it chooses to surface. **It does not die for one meal** — if the fight turns costly it flees, *with its catch if it has one,* back to the deep channels. **If it escapes clean, the clan and [[Gault]] learn that outsiders survived the wreck.**
> **The Scout's craft (the party's ride to town).** The Scout works the surface in a low, flat, hide-and-salvage boat it uses to haul catches home — beached in the reeds during the fight. Whether the party kills it or it dives away, **the boat is left behind,** and it's how they reach Sallowreach (Remy's own boat is gone with the wreck). It's a **stopgap:** fine for limping into the [[The Outer Market|Outer Market]], useless in the deep channels, and Remy won't trust it past the shallows — so the **boat-fund arc ([[The Mooring]]) is untouched.** *(Optional chill: there's old catch in the bottom — bones, a previous traveler's belongings — quiet proof the clan has done this before, and that wrecks feed them.)* See [[session-01-read-aloud#10 · The Crossing|Read-Aloud · Beat 10]].

## Tessak Diver

**Tier 1 Standard**

*The deep-channel clan proper — they have hunted these black channels longer than Sallowreach has stood, and move through them the way locals move through a kitchen. To a Tessak the warm-blooded peoples are the finest prey the Mire offers: soft, slow, obligingly delivered by every wreck. Right now they're poaching [[lantern-rays|lantern rays]] for [[Gault]] — see the **canon note above** for the why.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Cut out the weak, take the meat to deep water, never over-hunt, never come up into the city

**Difficulty:** 13 | **Thresholds:** 7/14 | **HP:** 5 | **Stress:** 3
**ATK:** +1 | **Net & Fishing Spear:** Close | 1d8+2 phy
**Experience:** Lives in the Channels +3

**Features:**
- **Of the Deep Channels - Passive:** A Tessak Diver is never Lost, ignores the Mire's difficult terrain and currents, and can stay submerged as long as they like. In the water, they have the advantage on nearly everything.
- **Weighted Net - Action:** Mark a Stress and throw a net at a target within Close range. On a success, the target is Restrained until they break free (Strength or Finesse Roll, or 6 damage to the net).- **Never Beyond the Season - Passive:** The Tessak are apex predators bound by a strict code of restraint — *tessavrak,* the take that doesn't exhaust the channel. They take only what the Mire delivers, never raid the boardwalks, and break off a fight that turns too costly *(a lost meal isn't worth a dead hunter).* **The Act 2 lever isn't pity — it's the code and the math:** sever Gault's easy-supply deal, or convince the clan the city is worth more as a trade-partner than a pantry. See [[lantern-rays|Lantern Rays — Separating the Tessak from Gault]] *(pending rewrite)*.

> **Drevath** leads the clan — run them as a Tessak Diver with one extra Experience (*Speaks for the Clan +2*) and the final word on the clan's deal with Gault. Not a villain and not a victim: an apex predator with a worldview, who weighs the party as threat, trade-partner, or food and acts on the math. If this clan grows into a recurring thread, give Drevath their own NPC file.
