# Brin Sevarre

**Role:** The Hunters' Guild's balance-keeper — population control and invasive-species management for the Mire
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — [[The Ranging House]]
**Faction:** Hunters' Guild (Sallowreach chapter); the conscience of it
**Tier:** 1

*Fletcher's anchor — and his hardest first audience. She does the same work he does, with a creed he hasn't been tested against yet: you cull to keep the balance, never for glory, never for coin. She will not take him to the snake until he proves which kind of hunter he is.*

---

## What She Is
Brin isn't a bounty hunter and she isn't a trophy-tracker. She's a **steward with a culling knife.** Sallowreach's whole economy rests on a healthy Mire — the reagents, the lumber, the fish — and a healthy Mire is a *balanced* one. Brin's job, the one the Guild quietly depends on and rarely thanks her for, is to keep that balance: thin a species that's booming, protect a nesting ground that's collapsing, and above all **hunt the invasives** — the things that don't belong, that arrived from elsewhere and eat through native populations with no predator to check them.

She reads the Mire as a system, the way [[Remy Duval]] reads it as a conversation and [[Gault]] reads it as a ledger. She kills, sometimes a great deal, and she has never once enjoyed it. The creed underneath everything: *take only what the balance needs taken; understand the animal before you raise a hand to it; and never, ever do it for an audience.*

## Appearance
Late forties, built the way the Mire builds the people who outlast it — lean, economical, nothing on her that doesn't earn its place. Hair pinned under a waxed canvas hat that has survived more expeditions than most hunters attempt. The backs of her hands are mapped with old scars; she can tell you what each one taught her. She moves quietly even indoors. Her vest has seventeen pockets and she knows what's in every one without looking.

The one tell of the work's weight: she keeps a small tally-book, and it isn't of kills. It's of *populations* — counts, season over season, of the things she's watching. She'd rather show you a recovering heron colony than anything she's ever put down.

## Personality & Mannerisms
Precise rather than cold. She gives information whole, without ornament, and expects the same back; she'll ask three questions before she answers one, and hers will be sharper. She respects competence the way other people respond to warmth — and she has a clean, unbothered contempt for two kinds of person: the tourist who thinks the swamp is scenery, and the hunter who thinks it's a purse.

That second contempt has a name now, and it's why she's hard on her own kind. **She vets hunters like she's deciding whether to hand someone a loaded weapon — because, to her, that's exactly what trusting a hunter is.** A person who hunts for glory or money is a person who will, eventually, take the shot that shouldn't be taken, strip the ground that shouldn't be stripped, or get themselves and others killed reaching for it. She has watched it happen. She does not intend to watch it again.

She doesn't raise her voice. She has never once needed to.

## What She Wants
To keep the Mire in balance against a tide that's turning the wrong way — and right now two things are out of balance and beyond her to fix alone:

1. **The amphisbaena.** An invasive apex with no business in the Mire, eating native young faster than the system can absorb. It's squarely her job. It's also in the deep southwest channels, it's killed at least two hunters she knows of, and *she cannot cull it alone.* She needs a partner with the skill — and the right reasons. She has been waiting for the right hunter and turning away the wrong ones.
2. **The poaching.** Someone is extracting live specimens out of the deep Mire and running them through the city to buyers elsewhere — gutting populations she's spent years stabilizing. She's been ringing this bell to the Guild for years. Like everyone in this city who's right too early, she's been thanked and ignored.

## What She's Hiding — The Wound
Three years ago she vouched for a young hunter she'd half-trained — eager, gifted, broke. He wanted the fast money. Against her advice he took work with a deep-channel extractor — *[[Gault]]* — as a hired hand, because the pay was triple and the man asked no questions. He went into the Mire on Gault's skiff and he did not come out. Ruled an accident, the way all deep-Mire deaths are ruled. (Gault has gone through eleven hired hands in eight years. He has no feelings about this. Brin has nothing but.)

She doesn't talk about it. It is the entire reason she watches new hunters for the glint — the one that says *this one's in it for the prize* — and shuts the door the moment she sees it. When she looks at Fletcher, fresh off a shipwreck and burning to kill a specific creature, the glint is exactly what she's afraid she's looking at.

## Gault — Her Antithesis
Where Brin is the steward, [[Gault]] is the strip-miner: he reads the Mire as inventory, harvests nesting grounds to ruin, sells live and dead specimens through port-city intermediaries, and burns through disposable hired hands without a backward glance. He is everything her creed exists to oppose, and he is the man who got her hunter killed.

She can't prove enough to make the Guild move, and he's never in the city — he's out in the channels, constantly moving. But she knows his *method* on sight: the specific way a population gets worked, the cage-and-transport signs, the cruelty that's purely practical. **When Fletcher's trust-test turns up exactly that method inside Sallowreach (below), Brin goes very still — because it means Gault's chain reaches into her city, and someone finally has the evidence.** *(GM: this is an early, low-stakes planting of the main villain — see [[Gault]]. Fletcher brushes the edge of the operation in town long before the party meets the man in the deep Mire.)*

## The Trust-Test — "The Haunt of Thornside" *(Fletcher solo; in-town; the proof Brin requires)*
Brin will **not** hand over the deep-channel intel to a hunter she doesn't trust. Instead, when Fletcher comes asking about the amphisbaena, she gives him something else: *"There's a thing loose in the Thornside boardwalks. Killing pets, getting into pantries, scaring people badly. The neighborhood's screaming for it dead and somebody's about to do something stupid. You want me to take you into the deep Mire? Show me how you handle this first. I'll hear about how you do it."*

It's a real problem, it's genuinely useful, and it's a test. *(Designed to run solo or with one tag-along — it keeps Fletcher busy in town without dragging the whole party into the deep Mire. See [[#Stat Block]] for the creature.)*

**The surface:** a "demon" or "haunt" is terrorizing the [[Sallowreach|Thornside]] boardwalks at night — reflective eyes in the dark, a cry that sounds almost human, raided larders, a savaged dog. There's a bounty forming and a frightened mob mood. The glory move is obvious: kill the monster, publicly, collect the credit.

**The truth Fletcher uncovers** (Eye for Danger, Swamp Specialist, tracking):
- It's an **amber civet** — a sleek, ring-tailed, nocturnal climber from the dry uplands, *not a Mire animal at all.* Invasive. It shouldn't be here.
- It's here because it **escaped a smuggler's crate.** The signs are all over it and its trail: cage-sores, a clipped claw, malnourishment, the panic of an animal loose in an environment it can't survive in. It isn't hunting Thornside for sport — it's *starving and terrified,* lashing out in an alien place.
- The civet is trafficked **alive** for the amber-scented musk in its glands — a prized alchemical fixative and perfume base. It's caged and milked so wealthy people elsewhere can smell expensive. Its whole existence is suffering for a luxury good.
- The trail leads back to a holding cache among the **night berths at [[The Mooring]]** *(canon-consistent: that's where the Wardens look the other way and [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]]'s edge-trade moves what it moves).* A crate broke; the civet got loose. There are *other* crates — and some of them hold caged *Mire* creatures bearing the unmistakable marks of [[Gault]]'s extraction. One cache, two directions.

**Who brought it, and why** *(short)*: a night-berth fence — **Cauvin** — runs live contraband through the Mooring's dark end. He shipped the civet *in* from the uplands to sell its musk to a city perfumer (the Alchemists' Consortium pays well for the fixative); the same cache stages Gault's Mire haul going *out.* The broken crate is just bad luck. Pull the thread and you get the handler, the buyer, and Gault's method all in one room — which is exactly the proof Brin's been missing.

**What Brin is actually watching for** (she'll hear the whole story from Fletcher, and from her own quiet asking-around):
- Did he kill cleanly and only because it had to be done — an invasive genuinely can't be loosed into the Mire — *without cruelty, without grandstanding, without playing to the bounty?* Or did he make a show of it?
- Did he protect the Thornside people and the native creatures in the process?
- **Did he chase the *cause*?** The trophy was the civet. The real quarry was the pipeline. A glory hunter takes the kill and the credit and goes home. A balance-keeper follows the trail to the crates and asks who's running them.

There's no clean answer baked in, which is the point — even Brin sometimes has to put an animal down. What she's judging is the *manner* and the *follow-through.* (If the table wants to twist the knife: make the civet a mother with a hidden litter. Now "invasive, must be removed" collides with "and the young?" — exactly the kind of impossible the campaign runs on. Optional; heavy.)

**The thematic payoff** *(GM)*: this little quest teaches Fletcher — and the player — the campaign's whole thesis in miniature. *The monster is a victim of displacement and cruelty.* The terrifying thing in the dark turns out to be frightened, trafficked, and far from home. That is the [[Mangrove Spirit]]'s entire story, scaled down to one civet on a boardwalk. Fletcher learns it early, with his hands, on a side quest — and it will echo when the real "monster" turns out to be grief.

**The Amber — Fletcher's prize** *(not coin)*: assume Fletcher harvests the musk-gland — and that he does it *cleanly,* using all of a kill he had to make, which is the opposite of the poachers who milk the thing caged for luxury (Brin notices; it's points in his favor). It yields a few doses of **Thornside Amber**: a thick, resinous, amber-scented oil. Smeared on, it makes a creature read as *wrong* to Mire predators — beasts overlook you for a scene; predator encounters can be avoided or slipped. *(A clean, earned, temporary version of the very thing [[Gault]] has become permanently — predators ignore him because the deep water made him wrong; Fletcher gets the ethical, limited echo of it.)* It doubles as a scent-**lure** to draw or tag an animal — quietly perfect for the amphisbaena hunt later. [VERIFY: stat as a Tier 1 consumable — suggest 3 doses; "beasts must succeed against your Difficulty to detect you for one scene, or you gain advantage to avoid/evade a Mire predator encounter."]

**If Fletcher passes:** Brin trusts him — visibly, and for keeps. She gives him the amphisbaena intel (below), and she gains an ally against the poaching she's been fighting alone. She also, quietly, stops seeing the dead hunter when she looks at him.
**If he fails** (goes glory-hunter): she's coldly courteous, withholds the deep intel, and he earns it another way — investigation, the party vouching, time. Recoverable, but he'll feel the door close.

## What She Knows (the amphisbaena intel — gated behind the trust-test)
- **It's invasive.** Two-headed, not native to the Mire, an egg-and-young raider throwing the southwest balance off. Her professional read and Fletcher's personal vendetta point at the same animal for different reasons — and once she trusts him, that shared target is a bond.
- **The nest:** the **Double Knot**, a channel-junction root-tangle in the southwest deep channels, three to four hours of hard punting past the city's edge — *deep enough you want a real boat, not a rented skiff* (see [[The Mooring]]). She'll map the approach.
- **The shed-skin trail:** she has four castings of her own to lay beside Fletcher's; matched, they confirm the same animal, same territory.
- **The warning, flat and serious:** *"Two hunters went past the Double Knot after it and didn't come back. I won't go alone, and I won't send you alone. That's not caution. That's arithmetic."* She can't go *with* him into the deep channels easily (her work, and it's a party-and-boat expedition by then) — but she gives him everything, and she means it.

## Key Relationships
- [[Fletcher]] — the hunter who arrived for the exact creature she most needs culled; she will not trust him on sight, and the trust, once earned, is total; she's the first person to ask him whether his fire is discipline or recklessness, and to *care* about the answer
- [[Gault]] — her antithesis and her grief; the extractor who got her hunter killed and whose chain, she'll learn, reaches into her own city
- [[Remy Duval]] — kindred, in their way: two people who love the Mire and are tired of being the only ones who'll say it's in trouble; reverence (his) and stewardship (hers) get along fine
- The Hunters' Guild / [[The Ranging House]] — her institutional home; she's its conscience and a thorn in its bounty-board side
- The hunter she lost — *(needs a name)* — three years dead on Gault's skiff; the reason she vets

## Dialogue Hooks
**First meeting, when Fletcher shows the castings:**
She pulls her own channel-map across the table before he's finished reaching for his journal. *"Two-headed pattern. Southwest junction. Three months old, give or take."* Then she looks at *him,* not the skin. *"I've got four more of these in a drawer. What I don't have is a reason to trust the man holding the fifth. Convince me you want this thing dead because it's killing what shouldn't be killed — and not because of how it'll feel to be the one who did it."*

**Sending him to the test:**
*"There's a haunt loose in Thornside. City wants it dead. Go handle it. I'll hear how you did — and how you did it is the whole answer."*

**Her creed, if pressed on what kind of hunter she is:**
*"I've put down more animals than you've seen alive. Not one of them for a trophy, and not one of them I didn't understand first. That's the line. Hunters who can't see it end up working for men like the one in the deep channels — and that ends one of two ways, and both of them are graves."*

**On Gault, low and even:**
*"I know how he works the way you know a man's handwriting. I've been telling the Guild for three years. They like the reagents he brings to market more than they like me saying where they came from."*
