# The Mooring

**Type:** District / transit hub (the docks)
**Region:** [[Sallowreach]] — Mire-edge, southwest boardwalk, near [[Remy Duval]]'s inn
**Controlled By:** the **Punt Wardens** (the punt operators' bloc), under [[#Sabine Aucoin|Sabine Aucoin]]; [[Marcel Dray]]'s Courtesy reaches the berths too

*Everything that enters or leaves Sallowreach passes through here, and everything that passes through here pays. The Mire is the wall; the Mooring is the only gate; the Wardens hold the key and rent it by the hour.*

---

## Description

A forest of black pylons at the city's southwest edge, where the boardwalks give way to open channel and the Mire breathes in and out with the brackish tide. Punts are racked three deep along the mooring posts — fishing skiffs, cargo flats, the long lacquered guide-punts — and the air is rope, tar, fish, and the green smell of the water. Polemen call rates across the channel in a clipped trade-cant half the city uses without thinking. Goods come off trade vessels and onto flats here; news comes with them, a day stale and twice marked-up. It is the most crowded, most surveilled, most *transactional* place in a city that prides itself on hospitality — because hospitality is for guests, and the Mooring is for business.

It is also where the party first learns the shape of their cage. **Every road out begins here**, and so this is where they'll try to leave — hire a flat, push for open water, and feel the tether close its hand. Everyone tries once. They try it at the Mooring.

## The Rental Economy

*The campaign's standing money-sink. [[Remy Duval|Remy]]'s boat went down with the ship (sabotage — [[Caius Maren|Caius]]'s doing, though no one knows yet). Until the party can buy a boat, they **rent** to move through the Mire — and a tethered, penniless party feels every coin of it. This is how gold stops being fake: it finally has somewhere to go.*

**Use the optional coins tier** (10 coins = 1 handful) [VERIFY: SRD optional rule] so the bleed is granular and survivable rather than instantly ruinous. PCs start with **1 handful each** — pooled, a fresh party holds maybe **3–4 handfuls**, less after a shipwreck. Prices are GM-set (the SRD gives none); these are tuned for *significant but reachable.* Adjust to your table.

### Renting

| Craft | Per trip | Deposit | Good for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tired flat-bottom skiff** | ~3 coins | 1 handful | Inner [[The Wending]] only. Leaks, slow. **Disadvantage / genuinely dangerous in the Deep Mire.** |
| **A proper guide-punt** | ~1 handful | 2–3 handfuls | The full Wending. Still not built for the deepest channels. |

**The deposit is the hook.** The Mire chews up boats — on a navigation **Fear** result, a bad [[mire-complications|complication]], or any trip that turns rough, the punt comes back damaged and the Wardens keep the deposit. Sometimes a trip *costs more than it pays.* That sting is the system working.

### Buying — the goal

| Boat | Cost | What it unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| **A used skiff of their own** | ~1 Bag (10 handfuls) | The rental bleed **stops.** Freedom on the inner Wending. Still limited in deep water. |
| **A proper Deep-Mire guide-boat** | ~2 Bags | What Remy needs to do his real work — and the craft that can carry them to [[The Mangrove Spirit's Heart]]. **Gates true Deep Mire access.** |

A Bag is a serious climb for a party earning in coins and handfuls — which is the point. Reaching the used skiff is an **early-Act-1 milestone**; the proper boat is the **Act 1 → Act 2 gateway.**

### Don't let it deadlock (the bootstrap)

If they're too broke to rent the skiff, they can't take the Mire jobs that pay for the skiff. So the first money is **in-town or fronted:**
- **Remy fronts the first rental** on the last shred of dock credit he has left — Sabine extends it *once,* pointedly, and lets him feel it.
- **Sell wreck salvage** at the pawnbroker (~1–2 handfuls, one-time).
- **[[Caius Maren|Caius]]'s "incident testimony"** pays a couple of clean-looking handfuls (it's document fifteen in his annexation file).
- Small dockside tasks — load a flat, find a missing crate, vouch in a dispute.

### Where the lump sums come from — the fork

The boat is reachable in **two or three meaningful scores, not twenty errands.** Each is a *choice with a cost*, which is what makes the money pressure produce story instead of grind:

- **[[Théo Rouchard|Théo]] — honest, lean.** The brightfin specimen commission (~3 handfuls + his goodwill). Clean money, but it won't buy a boat alone. The slow, decent road.
- **[[Marcel Dray|Marcel]] — high pay, no questions.** A Mire salvage run for premium coin — a *visible chunk of a Bag in one score.* The fast road, and the first thread of *owing Marcel.* Doubles as the on-ramp to the spear deal. **This is the seduction the money pressure is built to create.**
- **[[Caius Maren|Caius]] — clean-looking, poisoned.** Generous imperial pay for reports and testimony. They won't feel the cost for a long time. *(The knife: Caius sank the boat they're raising money to replace. If they take his coin for it, that lands in Act 2.)*

### Money still matters after the boat

A mercantile city should give coin somewhere to go, or it goes fake again. Keep these live: rental deposits forfeited to the Mire, [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]]'s monthly Courtesy, bribes and informants, **medicine for the [[lantern-rays|Tessak]]'s sick young**, lodging once Remy's charity quietly wears thin, the pawnbroker, boat repairs, and premium guiding into territory no one sane visits.

## The Boat Fund — Arc Beats

1. **The wound.** Remy's boat is gone. He's functional but gutted (he keeps his *first* punt over the bar; he knows what a lost boat means). He scrapes a loaner for the first trip and hates it.
2. **The bleed bites.** Every Mire trip costs. The deep channels — where the cure lives — are locked behind a boat they can't afford. Being stuck gets a price tag.
3. **The fork.** Whose money do they take? (above) The squeeze is what makes Marcel tempting.
4. **Milestone — a skiff of their own.** The rental bleed ends. A real, earned win. Deep Mire still closed.
5. **Milestone — the proper boat.** Act 2 opens. They name it. It becomes *theirs* — and it's the boat that reaches the Heart.
6. **The reveal.** What sank the first boat was no accident. Depending on whose money they took to replace it, that knife cuts deeper.

**Remy's pride runs under all of it.** He will not take charity — he'd rather earn it *with* them than be handed it. He's mortified coming to the Mooring hat in hand, and [[#Sabine Aucoin|Sabine]] enjoys the famous guide's comedown more than she lets on. Let the party be the ones who restore him, not rescue him.

## Sabine Aucoin

**Role:** Berth-mistress of the Mooring; ranking voice of the Punt Wardens
**Tier:** 2 *(social — she does not fight; she sets prices, which is worse)*

A broad, unhurried woman in her sixties with a ledger-memory and forearms like mooring rope, third generation on these pylons. She calls everyone *cher* and means none of it warmly. She is not cruel — she is *exact.* The Wardens hold the only transit through the Mire and she has spent forty years making sure the city remembers what that's worth.

**What she wants:** to keep the Wardens' grip on transit absolute and quietly profitable, and to never, ever let an outsider — or a sentimental frog who gives strangers rides — forget who controls the water.

**What she's hiding:** the Wardens skim for [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]] and look the other way at what moves through the edge berths at night; she'd call it good business, not corruption, and she'd rather the party not look too hard.

**On Remy, privately:** she respects him and would never say so. She thinks his civilian-guiding is foolish and his reverence for the Mire is sentiment a working operation can't afford. Watching him rent is a small, daily satisfaction she's mildly ashamed of.

**Voice:** *"You can leave whenever you like, cher. The water charges the same going out as coming in."* · *"A deposit. Because the Mire keeps what it takes, and I am not the Mire's creditor."* · *"Remy Duval, on my dock, renting. Sit down, this is the best part of my month."*

**As a lever:** she's the recurring face of the money pressure — gouge them, warm to them slowly if they're straight with her, or (optional, very on-theme) offer a **rent-to-own** deal that is quietly a [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]] arrangement: climb out of the rental and into a worse debt.

## Key Features

- **The Rental Berth** — Sabine's shack, the racked punts, the chalkboard of rates that changes with the weather and her mood.
- **The Channel Mouth** — where the boardwalk ends and open water begins; the threshold where the tether bites.
- **The Trade Flats** — cargo vessels unloading; goods, news, and the occasional new outsider arriving the way the party didn't.
- **The Night Berths** — the eastern pylons where the Wardens don't keep close books, and Marcel's people move what they move.

## NPCs Present
- **[[#Sabine Aucoin|Sabine Aucoin]]** — runs the rentals; the gate's gatekeeper
- **[[Remy Duval]]** — reduced to renting where he used to own; comes here often and quietly hates it
- Punt Wardens, polemen, dockhands — run as [[sallowreach-roster#Wending Runner|Wending Runner]] stats if it ever comes to it (it shouldn't)

## Tensions & Hooks
- The party tries to leave from here and learns the tether is real (Session 1 / early).
- Every Deep-Mire goal is gated behind a boat they can't yet afford — pressure toward the job-fork.
- The Night Berths are a thread to pull toward [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]]'s operation.
- Selling wreck salvage here connects them to the pawnbroker and the city's underground economy.

## Secrets
- The Wardens skim for Marcel; the edge berths move bootleg [[Marcel Dray|Mire Spirits]] and worse.
- Sabine has standing the party will need later — if the city ever has to decide whether to trust outsiders, the Wardens' voice matters, and she remembers who paid honestly.

## Dungeon Alchemist Notes
Stilt-dock over black water: a forest of mooring pylons, punts racked along them, a weathered rental shack with a chalkboard, stacked crates and barrels on the trade flats, a lantern-strung boardwalk edge opening onto an open channel mouth. Night-berth variant: same map, fewer lights, fog on the water. Medium size; the playable focus is the berth shack and the channel mouth.
