# Session 2 Prep — Honored Guests

*The first day in the city. Session 1 was a straight line — drown, survive, arrive. **Session 2 is a sandbox,** and that's a different muscle: the party will almost certainly **split**, each PC drifting toward the place they came here for. The job tonight is **three things, in this order** — let them understand the curse, let the Empire come knocking, then let the city open up and adopt them. Plot stays a background hum (see [[world-in-motion]]); **wonder and pathos** stay in the foreground, exactly like Session 1. See [[session-02-read-aloud]] for the spoken blurbs and [[session-02-cheatsheet]] for the one-pager you run from.*

> **The dramatic irony that powers the whole session:** the city calls the spirits it can't see *honored guests* — presences you welcome and never command, who are free to come and go. The party are now, unknowingly, the city's newest honored guests: welcomed, fed, fussed over — and the one kind of guest the Mire will not let leave. Nobody in Sallowreach can see the joke yet. You can. Let it sit under everything warm.

## How to Run a Split Party *(the only new skill this session asks of you)*

Session 1 kept everyone in one boat. Tonight they scatter. Don't fight it — *design for it.*

- **Round-robin in short laps.** Give each PC a tight 8–12 minute scene, then **cut — preferably on a hook** (a question asked, a door opened, a test offered) — and jump to the next PC. Momentum comes from the *cutting,* not the lingering. The player you just left should be a little impatient to get back.
- **"Meanwhile" is your friend.** *"We'll come back to that. Meanwhile, across the city, the Witch is climbing a ladder a tide is arguing with…"* Hard cuts feel cinematic, not disjointed.
- **Let them tag along.** Two PCs at one anchor is fine and often better — give the tag-along something to *do* (Isotta reads the spirits in Orna's garden; Tenson spots the books are cooked at the Ranging House). The anchor still belongs to its PC.
- **Bank the warmth between cuts.** Each transition can pass through [[The Outer Market]] — a free bowl of gumbo, a rumor offered warmly, the Stress-clear of **The Table That Adopts You**. The market is the connective tissue *and* the engine of investment.
- **Reconverge on purpose.** Beat 4 (the echo) and/or the dinner (Beat 5) pull them back together. End the day in one room, even if the day happened in four.

## Beat 1 — The First Morning *(the curse beat)*

**Open with warmth, then take it out from under them.** They went to sleep half-drowned strangers in [[Remy Duval|Remy's]] rooms. They wake *dry,* fed, blanketed — and the city is already roaring outside the window: stilts creaking under sixty thousand feet, cook-smoke, a vendor singing a price, a Celebration drum somebody's already practicing. For one beat it's the best morning of their lives. **Then they remember.**

*(Read-aloud: [[session-02-read-aloud#1 · The First Morning|Beat 1]].)*

**The curse becomes undeniable — in daylight.** Last night, on the bank, the tether was a feeling in the dark. This morning it's a *fact.* However you want to stage it: a PC steps to the edge of a boardwalk facing open water and feels it again — the wall that isn't there, the resistance, then the wrongness — and this time the sun is up and there's no adrenaline to blame it on. **There are no marks.** Nothing to see, nothing to bandage, nothing torn. That's the horror of it: a wound would at least make sense. *(Canon: the tether is purely relational — them and the Mire. See [[STATUS]]. Ignore the older "marks on your skin" line in the [[sallowreach-frame|frame]].)*

**Then Remy takes them to [[Delphine]].** He doesn't make a big deal of it — *"Before you do anything, you see Delphine. Everybody sees Delphine."* This is the curse-explainer, and it's the city loving them first, not an info-transaction.

**What Delphine gives them** *(see [[Delphine#First Visit — What She Tells Them|her file]] — run it blunt-but-warm, never a lecture):*
- **It's a knot, not a wound.** Her exam finds nothing to heal. They weren't *injured* — they were *tied.* The black water did it. There's no physical cure because there's nothing physical to cure.
- **She can't lift it — flat, no false hope.** *"No. But I know what it is, and I know who might know more. That's not nothing."*
- **It's spirit-water magic, and it's real.** Sixty-two years of feeling the presence in that water; this is that, concentrated.
- **The referral, warm:** to **[[Caspar Renne]]**, the museum curator in the old quarter — *"He studies what the Mire makes and why. Tell him I sent you; he'll actually listen."* *(This is the party's first thread toward the curse's real answer — and, much later, the spear. Don't push it; just leave the door open.)*

**What she shows but will NOT say — plant it.** The instant she touches the first of them, her eyes drift a half-second to the left and she goes still a beat too long, then carries on. *(GM: she's getting the vision — cold black water, two lights converging, a long-suspended chord resolving. It's the whole campaign in four seconds.* **Do not disclose it.** *If a player calls the tell: "Old habit. The water's loud today.")* She is **clue #5** of [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|The Turn]]: the tether *rhymes* with something the city does every week — she starts *"It feels like—,"* stops herself, *"…no. Go see Caspar."* **Never let her finish that sentence.**

> **⚠ If you already ran Delphine in Session 1:** then Beat 1 is the *re-grounding* instead — the curse settling into daylight, a return question (*"Has it changed?"* — *"No. They don't."*), and her quiet investment. The work of the beat (they understand the curse is real, relational, and uncurable-by-her, with a thread to pull) is the same. Adjust the read-aloud accordingly.

## Beat 2 — The Invitation *(the Empire knocks)*

**Caius moves the day after they arrive.** He heard the Mire produced something it never produces — *survivors* — and tethered ones at that: credible, sympathetic, no local agenda. Exactly the witnesses his two-year file needs. *(He arranged the wreck. He didn't expect anyone to live. He's adjusting, and he's fast. See [[Caius Maren#The Plan|his file]] — GM only.)*

**Stage it small and warm.** An **aide** finds the party — at Delphine's door, or in the market, wherever the morning has carried them. Polished, imperial, faintly too dry for the humidity. A handwritten card on heavy cream paper, sealed in imperial wax. *(Read-aloud: [[session-02-read-aloud#2 · The Invitation|Beat 2]] — read the card itself aloud; it's a prop.)*

**What the invite is** *(player-facing):* a dinner, *tonight,* at the Imperial Attaché Quarters — Caius Maren, Cultural Liaison, heard what happened and wishes to extend "a proper imperial welcome." Nothing elaborate. The city's best provisions. No agenda mentioned.

**What the invite is** *(GM):* document fourteen. He wants their account of the wreck *in writing,* through proper imperial channels, framed as helping future travelers. He is collecting testimony. He will not appear to be collecting testimony.

**The note in the room:** Caius should read as *pleasant and one degree off* — a low hum of wrongness the party can't place. Let an NPC half-confirm it: a market line like *"The imperial one — Maren — he's been asking after that shipwreck. Bought a whole table breakfast just to chat."* The party files it. They won't know why for a long time. *(See the overheard lines in [[improv-ammo]] §4, #6.)*

**Where it goes:** the dinner is Beat 5 — run it tonight as a capstone, or cliffhang on the invitation and open Session 3 at his table. Either way, the invite **arrives early** so it hangs over the whole day's exploration: a dinner with the Empire, coming.

## Beat 3 — Free Exploration *(the body of the session — four doors)*

*This is the heart of the night. Each PC has a place they came here for and a person standing in it. Run these round-robin (see above). For each: the **scene**, what it **establishes**, the **gate** on the personal quest, the **wonder/pathos** beat, and the **RP grounding** so the NPC arrives fully voiced. Locations are written to feel **alive** — five things you notice, who else is there, and what's already happening when the PC walks in.*

### ↳ Fletcher → [[Brin Sevarre]] @ [[The Ranging House]]

**The scene.** Fletcher walks into a working hall that smells of rendered fat and river mud, finds the corner table buried in three-color channel maps and tagged shed skins, and lays down his castings. Brin pulls her own map across the table *before he's finished reaching for his journal* — *"Two-headed pattern. Southwest junction. Three months old, give or take."* Then she looks at the *man,* not the skin, and the door doesn't open.

**Establishes:** Brin (the steward with a culling knife), the Guild, the southwest as the campaign's hot quadrant, and the creed Fletcher hasn't been tested against — *cull for balance, never for glory.*

**The gate:** she will **not** hand over the deep-channel intel to a hunter she can't read. She gives him a test instead — *"the Haunt of Thornside,"* a thing loose in the boardwalks the neighborhood wants dead. *How* he handles it is the whole answer. *(Full design in [[Brin Sevarre#The Trust-Test — "The Haunt of Thornside"|her file]]. You can set it up tonight and run it next session, or kick it off if there's time — it's built to run solo.)*

**Wonder/pathos:** the man who nearly drowned checks for his *journal* before himself; the trail outlived the drowning. And the quiet ache — he came across the world to kill one specific thing, and the first professional who's seen its sign looks at him like she's deciding whether to hand him a loaded weapon.

**Location alive — five things you notice:** the contract board, color-coded and ruthlessly updated, telling you exactly what the Mire is doing this week · a wall of tagged shed skins, four of them two-headed · the stairwell window facing southwest, the canopy pressing the city's edge *patiently* · margin notes in fauna books from trackers fifty years dead · a thinning southwest contract list nobody will explain. **Who's here:** the archivist (protective of the old materials), a hunter or two not taking the deep contracts anymore. **What's happening:** Brin is mid-count in her tally-book — *populations,* not kills — and would rather show you a recovering heron colony than anything she's put down.

### ↳ The Witch → [[Professor Orna Tessant]] @ [[The Verdant Margin]]

**The scene.** The Witch climbs into a community garden balanced on the city's edge — three platforms of cultivated chaos, a greenhouse of running experiments and zero notes, and a lower platform the tide keeps arguing with. Orna doesn't look up from whatever's bubbling: *"You're after something specific. Everyone who finds the Margin is."* Then the wrong light moves in their eyes and they *see* it. *"You, though — you want the impossible **done to you.** Different shelf. Sit, sit — no, not there, that's alive."*

**Establishes:** Orna (the firbolg genius drinking themselves into a swamp-thing, the best alchemist in the city and its worst teacher), the garden, and — gently — the campaign's central knife.

**The gate:** Orna can't be hired so much as *survived.* The "Persona Non Grata" quest: they need a controlled reagent (**gloamcap distillate**) from the Alchemists' Consortium — where Orna is *banned.* Someone has to fetch it. *(Tell it: "Oh — you'll have to fetch it. They won't see me. Long story. Boring story." It is not boring. Full design in [[Professor Orna Tessant#The Light Quest — "Persona Non Grata"|their file]].)*

**Wonder/pathos — the breadcrumb that recolors everything:** Orna diagnoses the Witch in one careless glance and points them *off* the potion-path: *"You're not sick. You're **bound.** No elixir touches that — wrong department entirely. You want a—"* and trails off, already three thoughts away. *(This is the [[The Fishing Spear|thing that breaks bindings]] foreshadowed — without Orna knowing it. Don't let them finish the sentence.)* And the mirror: a being who is *throwing their self away on purpose* sitting across from one who had their self *stolen.* The Witch — a compulsive documentarian — watching the most valuable mind in the region wash out unrecorded. That's the bond, and it aches.

**Location alive — five things you notice:** turned earth and damp wood, something sweet that shifts as you walk past blooming versus fermenting beds · the greenhouse bench with its row of half-finished doses Orna keeps drinking · the lower platform's rope handrails and tide-wet ladder · plants growing in directions the light doesn't explain · **three beds that shimmer faint blue-green even by day, and pulse — *nearly* in time with the Celebration drums — at night.** **Who's here:** community gardeners (a cross-section of the outer boardwalk, full of rumors about the failing edge), someone who brought Orna a blue-black plant that pushed thirty centimeters of root through treated wood. **What's happening:** the lower platform is closer to the Mire than last season, and Orna has *quietly started moving their prized specimens up* — and won't say why. *(GM: the bioluminescent beds are a live [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|Turn]] clue. A Witch elbow-deep in that soil might catch what Orna never recorded. Plant; don't assemble.)*

### ↳ Isotta → [[Vael Miroux]] @ [[The Still Cup]]

**The scene.** Isotta steps into the best coffee in a city with opinions about coffee — dark wood, good light, the specific quiet of a room that runs deep. Vael sets a cup in front of her with no ceremony and the brown eye finds her while the white one drifts to something past her shoulder: *"I've served a few like you. Died in the transformation, came back still attached. The Order says that's not supposed to survive."* A beat. *"But you're not quite that either, are you. Sit. Drink."*

**Establishes:** Vael (retired Ghost Slayer, the white eye that sees what's wearing a human face), the spirit-world cosmology made *warm* and ordinary, and Isotta's first true teacher about what she is.

**The gate / the gift:** Vael decides she's *ready,* and *kin,* and opens the door — but the teaching runs through a *quest,* not a lecture. **"Old Basile":** the gentle old man who's played dominoes in the corner *longer than anyone's grandparents remember* is a small spirit who crossed for love of an ordinary life — and lately he's **slipping** (no reflection in the urn some mornings; his shadow falls wrong; he calls a regular by their dead grandmother's name). Isotta notices first, because he reads as *kin.* Helping him teaches her **anchoring** and **parley** — the exact skill her own cure will require. *(Full design in [[Vael Miroux#The Quest — "Old Basile"|their file]].)*

**Wonder/pathos — the read no one else can give her:** once the white eye has her, Vael says the thing: *"The ones I see came across on their own. You didn't. Someone **did this to you,** child. That's not a haunting. That's a **curse.**"* The Order of the Ghost Slayer trains her to hunt the thing she now *is* — and here's a retired master telling her she was never the monster; she was a *victim.* Let it land as relief and grief at once. And the mirror in the corner: Basile is a spirit clinging to humanity; Isotta is a human cursed into spirit-shape. Two beings on opposite banks of the same veil.

**Location alive — five things you notice:** the name carved into the door-frame, not hung above it · twelve tables, half-full, warm-but-never-loud · the back corner where serious conversations go to be sheltered · Vael making every single cup like it matters, because they've decided it does · an old man in the corner, dominoes, everyone's honorary grandfather. **Who's here:** the Thursday Order regulars (bloodhunters at every career stage — textured spirit-and-Mire lore), a young recently-credentialed Ghost Slayer itching to go *deal with* the spirit agitation in the deep channels (Vael is quietly discouraging it). **What's happening:** Basile sets out a domino that isn't there, and for half a second the room doesn't notice and Isotta *does.*

### ↳ Tenson → [[Sable Crue]] @ [[The Drillfield]]

**The scene.** Tenson comes to the largest flat deck in the southwest district with a citadel seal and careful questions, and Sable — big, grinning, mid-laugh, holding food — does not care about either. *"From the Citadel! Look at you. We don't get many of those who'll still get in the mud."* A practice stave comes flying. *"You do the morning drill with my lot, and then I'll tell you anything you want. Deal? Don't answer — just catch."*

**Establishes:** Sable (brawny, sunny, gloriously at peace being average — the happiest person in a posting everyone else treats as exile), the wall *you can see losing ground from the deck,* and Tenson's funhouse mirror.

**The gate:** the seal opens no doors here. Cleverness opens no doors here. **"Sweat First":** show up, train, take a hit, laugh about it — and then the winning beat, which uses the very thing Tenson's ashamed of. A green recruit, **Perrin**, keeps getting hurt and is about to wash out; Sable flatly refuses to give up on them. Tenson's buried *sports-medicine* eye catches what no one else has — bad form, an old untreated injury, overtraining — and turns Perrin around. *That* wins Sable, completely and for keeps. *(Full design in [[Sable Crue#The Trust Mini-Quest — "Sweat First"|their file]]. The records — and the unread Caius memo — come **after** the friendship, not tonight.)*

**Wonder/pathos — the question Tenson didn't expect:** Sable is the future Tenson is most afraid of — a life of the body, no ledger — and Sable is *thriving.* Not as mockery. As a quiet, sideways question: *what if it was never something to escape?* And the steel under the sunshine, if you want it: *"I keep it light for the lot. Somebody's got to."* — the smile stays, the eyes don't — *"But I've carried furniture out of more homes this year than last. You do the sums on that, brain-boy. That's your department."*

**Location alive — five things you notice:** salt-grey planks re-leveled so often no two agree on the horizon · the smell of sweat, rope, wet wood, and the mineral bite of salt · racks holding *more rescue gear than weapons* (which tells you what the job actually is) · recruits drilling with their backs to the wall *on purpose* (it doesn't help) · the southwest horizon reading as *encroaching green,* the Mire pressing the frame. **Who's here:** Perrin (the recruit Sable won't lose), a long-serving guard who grew up on a boardwalk already gone under and volunteers every time to post the relocation orders. **What's happening:** the morning drill — brutal, and somehow the best hour of everyone's day — and at the Long Bench, off-shift guards eating something Sable cooked and saying, unfiltered, exactly what they think is happening to their city.

## Beat 4 — The Echo *(reconvergence; quiet and load-bearing)*

However the day ends, get them back in one room — Remy's table, a market bench, the walk to the dinner. Let them **trade what they learned,** and let the word surface on its own:

- Delphine: *a knot.*
- Orna: *you're not sick, you're **bound.***
- Vael: *you weren't haunted, you were **tied.***

Three strangers, three rooms, one word none of them planned. **Don't have an NPC point it out.** Let a *player* notice. That's the [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|Turn]] clue-chain doing its slow work — recognition, not deduction, weeks ahead of the click. If nobody catches it tonight, fine; it compounds. *(And the rule that protects the reveal: no one in this city can yet connect any of it to the Celebration. Hold that line.)*

This is also where the **"these strangers aren't friends yet"** principle earns its keep — they don't have to like each other to notice they're carrying the same wound. Let trust be a thing they're *surprised* to feel starting.

## Beat 5 — *(Optional capstone)* The Embassy Dinner

*Run it tonight if the table's got the energy; otherwise cliffhang and open S3 here. Full scene + dialogue in [[Caius Maren#The Dinner|Caius's file]]; read-aloud open at [[session-02-read-aloud#11 · The Embassy|Beat 11]].*

The Imperial Attaché Quarters are the nicest rooms in the building — he had them reassigned on arrival. His own furnishings, brought from the capital, arranged so the desk faces the door. He always knows who's coming in. **The dinner is genuinely excellent;** he has the city's best provisions and uses them.

**The shape of it:**
- **Warmth and specific flattery.** He's done his research — knows which families have been here longest, which dishes to order. Every compliment is also an appraisal.
- **Genuine attention to the wreck.** *Where exactly. What the water felt like. How long under.* He is collecting testimony and does not appear to be.
- **The ask, somewhere in the second course:** would they put their account *in writing?* A formal incident report, through proper channels — *"Future travelers deserve safer waterways. You'd be doing them a service."* He means it to be document fourteen.
- **The exit:** he pays, thanks them, hopes they find their situation resolved soon — and *"if there's anything the imperial offices can do, my door is open."* He means that last part. Useful things should be maintained.

**Run him as pleasant and one degree wrong.** The menace is sub-perceptual. When they look back at this dinner *weeks* from now, they'll realize how specific his questions were. *(Do NOT tip the sabotage. He's charming, helpful, genuinely fond of the city, and quietly weighing them as assets.)*

## Secrets & Clues

*Ten short truths. Abstract from discovery — drop each wherever it fits. Expect to reveal about half; carry the rest. Several roll forward from Session 1.*

1. The tether leaves **no mark** — there's nothing to heal because nothing was injured. They weren't wounded; they were *tied.* The cure isn't medicine; it's understanding what the water did.
2. **[[Caspar Renne]]**, the museum curator in the old quarter, "studies what the Mire makes and why." Delphine trusts him. He is the next real thread on the curse.
3. Three different experts reach for the same word about three different conditions: **bound.** None of them can say what binds.
4. The garden's three glowing beds **pulse on Celebration nights** — nearly in time with the city's drums. [[Professor Orna Tessant\|Orna]] has watched it for years, never written it down, and has half a theory they never finished. *(Plant quietly.)*
5. There is a **spirit world** alongside this one; the veil is thin in the Mire, and small spirits can pass as human. You have almost certainly already met one. *(Vael; the honored-guest etiquette is built on this.)*
6. The veil has been **straining for months** — more crossings, agitated spirits, quiet ones who passed as human for years suddenly slipping. Something is pulling at the boundary. *(Vael is moving from attentive toward alarmed.)*
7. The imperial liaison **Caius Maren has been asking about the shipwreck** all over the city — buying strangers breakfast to do it. *(Background hum. The party files it; the meaning comes much later.)*
8. The southwest **wall is losing**, the guards know it, and the official line ("performing within acceptable parameters") is a lie they can feel in their bodies — they just don't know *whose* lie. The Watch Post's informal logbook and the filed reports **don't match.**
9. Someone is **poaching the deep Mire to ruin** — extracting live specimens, gutting populations. [[Brin Sevarre\|Brin]] has a name she can't prove ([[adversaries/gault|Gault]]), and the pipeline *stages through the city.*
10. **PC-personalized — word travels slowly to a city with no road, but it travels.** **Tenson's is live:** [[Magistra Silence]] was told the ship was lost with no survivors and refuses to believe it; her first letter is already coming — forwarded into a city she doesn't know can *keep* people. ⟦For Fletcher and Isotta: as you get the names, the same dread seeds — a grieving town that lost its hunter; a bloodhunter relative who knows the ritual shouldn't have left someone walking around. For the Witch, it points *inward* — the faint wrongness that the trail loops back toward their own hand.⟧

## Important NPCs *(this is your RP grounding — voice + lines you can say)*

*Each entry: who they are in a breath, what they want **tonight**, how they **talk**, lines ready to steal, and the one thing to remember. Lean on [[improv-ammo]] for any face you need on the fly.*

### [[Delphine]] — the healer who reads more than she says
- **In a breath:** sixty-two years of Mire medicine and visions she can't control; blunt, warm underneath, hates being treated as an oracle.
- **Wants tonight:** to help, to be honest, and to *not* say the thing the touch showed her.
- **Voice:** plain to the point of bluntness, never cruel. Short sentences. Tells you what's wrong, what she can do, what she can't, what to do next — in that order. Dry wit that lands after a pause.
- **Say:** *"Sit. All of you — sit down. You're carrying it up in your shoulders, and that's doing you no good."* · *"You're not sick. Nothing to flush, nothing torn. It's a knot. You went under, and the water tied you to this place."* · *"No, I can't lift it. But I know what it is, and I know who might know more. That's not nothing."* · *(if pressed on her tell)* *"Old habit. The water's loud today."*
- **Remember:** she starts *"It feels like—"* and **stops herself.** Never finish it.

### [[Caius Maren]] — the hungriest man in the room, smiling
- **In a breath:** an imperial true believer building an unauthorized case for annexation; charming, genuinely fond of the city he's trying to take.
- **Wants tonight:** their testimony, in writing — and to keep them as managed assets.
- **Voice:** warm, present, eye contact *just* long enough. Listens more than he speaks; means everything he says; never says anything he doesn't intend to. Compliments are appraisals.
- **Say:** *"I'm glad you survived. I mean that — I've read three incident reports from this Mire in two years and none of them had survivors."* · *"Extraordinary city. It deserves so much better infrastructure than it has. That's what I keep thinking. It deserves better."* · *"Nothing formal — just a written account of what you observed. Future travelers deserve safer waterways. You'd be doing them a service."* · *(parting)* *"If there's anything the imperial offices can do, consider my door open."*
- **Remember:** pleasant and **one degree wrong.** The menace is sub-perceptual tonight. Don't tip the sabotage.

### [[Brin Sevarre]] — the steward with a culling knife *(Fletcher's)*
- **In a breath:** the Hunters' Guild's conscience; kills only for balance, never for glory; vets hunters like she's handing out loaded weapons, because a mentee she vouched for died working a poacher's skiff.
- **Wants tonight:** to read what kind of hunter Fletcher is before she trusts him with anything that matters.
- **Voice:** precise, unornamented, never raised. Asks three sharp questions before she answers one. Clean contempt for the tourist and the trophy-hunter.
- **Say:** *"Two-headed pattern. Southwest junction. Three months old, give or take."* · *"I've got four more of these in a drawer. What I don't have is a reason to trust the man holding the fifth."* · *"Convince me you want this thing dead because it's killing what shouldn't be killed — and not because of how it'll feel to be the one who did it."* · *"There's a haunt loose in Thornside. Go handle it. How you do it is the whole answer."*
- **Remember:** when Fletcher's test later turns up the poaching method *inside her city,* she goes very still. That's the villain planting early.

### [[Professor Orna Tessant]] — the genius dissolving into the swamp *(the Witch's)*
- **In a breath:** the best alchemist in the city, its worst teacher, drinking their own experiments into a slow transformation and chasing an all-cure they once made and can't remember how.
- **Wants tonight:** a reagent they've run out of — and, vaguely, an audience that can keep up.
- **Voice:** brilliant, exhausting, ten steps ahead — answers the question you'll have in twenty minutes, then wanders off mid-thought. Cheerfully unconcerned about the barnacle-crust on their arm.
- **Say:** *"You're after something specific. Everyone who finds the Margin is."* · *"You want the impossible **done** to you. Different shelf. Sit, sit — no, not there, that's alive."* · *"You're not sick. You're **bound.** No elixir touches that — wrong department entirely. You want a—"* *(gone, three thoughts away)* · *"Oh — you'll have to fetch it. They won't see me. Long story. Boring story. Tell them it's for the garden."*
- **Remember:** they write **nothing** down — and they will *not* finish the sentence that points at the cure.

### [[Vael Miroux]] — the calm deep water with one white eye *(Isotta's)*
- **In a breath:** a retired Ghost Slayer who learned most spirits don't need slaying; the one person in the city who can see which neighbors aren't human, and won't tell.
- **Wants tonight:** to understand what's straining the veil — and, seeing kin walk in, to take Isotta in and teach her.
- **Voice:** calm the way deep water is calm. Listens far more than speaks. Precise when they know, flat *"I don't know"* when they don't. Dry humor at the bottom of a long silence.
- **Say:** *"I've served a few like you. The Order says that's not supposed to survive."* · *"You're not quite that either, are you. Sit. Drink."* · *"The ones I see came across on their own. You didn't. Someone **did this to you,** child. That's not a haunting. That's a **curse.**"* · *"Exorcism kills what you are. What you'd need is to **talk** to whatever's holding you, and get it to let go."* · *"There's a man in the corner I think you ought to meet."*
- **Remember:** Vael can't bring the *vast, held* thing in the deep water into focus, and never connects anything to the Celebration. Fragment-holder only.

### [[Sable Crue]] — the happiest person in the worst posting *(Tenson's)*
- **In a breath:** brawny, sunny, completely at peace being average; runs brutal drills everyone leaves grinning; carries the worry so the guards don't have to.
- **Wants tonight:** for Tenson to drop the auditor's armor and just be a person on the field. *(A good spar and a better dinner.)*
- **Voice:** fun, loud, full of life; calls you *cher* and *brain-boy;* food on his breath, a Celebration rhythm tapping without noticing. The steel only shows for half a beat, then the grin's back.
- **Say:** *"From the Citadel! Look at you. You do the morning drill with my lot, and then I'll tell you anything you want. Don't answer — just catch."* · *"That's a brain job, cher. I read the top line and went and did my push-ups."* · *"Exile! That's what the guild lot call it. There's a woman three berths down makes a crawfish boil that'd make you **weep,** and they're up in their dry rooms counting the days. Their loss."* · *(low, the steel showing)* *"That kid's not washing out. Not on my field. We don't give up on people here."*
- **Remember:** he's *sitting on the Caius memo* and doesn't know it — because reading it closely is "a brain job." That's Tenson's win to find, later.

### Quick faces you may need
- **The aide** *(Caius's)* — imperial, dry, faintly bored, too clean for the humidity. *"Liaison Maren sends his compliments, and this."* Hand over the card; do not wait for an answer; leave.
- **Basile** — the slipping spirit in the Still Cup corner. Gentle, beloved, *of another decade.* *"You're new. Sit — Cosette, bring the child a cup. …No, that's not right. Cosette's been gone twenty years. Has she?"*
- **Perrin** — Sable's green recruit, getting hurt, about to wash out, trying so hard. *(Optional gut-tug: joined the guard because the failing wall took their family's home.)*
- **Remy Duval** — still around the edges of the morning: warm, fast, reverent, gutted about his boat. Pushes them to Delphine; offers the rooms a little longer than he can really afford. *(His charity should quietly start to wear thin — a soft money pressure. See [[The Mooring]].)*

## Fantastic Locations *(three aspects each — built to feel alive)*

- **[[The Ranging House]]** — a contract board that reads the Mire's mood like a pulse; a wall of two-headed shed skins; a southwest-facing window where the canopy presses the city *patiently.*
- **[[The Verdant Margin]]** — a garden balanced on the city's dissolving edge; beds that glow and *nearly* keep the Celebration's time; a genius quietly carrying their treasures uphill and refusing to say why.
- **[[The Still Cup]]** — the best coffee in a city that argues about coffee; a back corner where serious things get said quietly; an old man in the corner who's been there longer than anyone's grandparents, and is starting to come apart.
- **[[The Drillfield]]** — salt-grey planks that can't agree on the horizon; more rescue gear than weapons; a wall you can watch lose ground, and recruits who drill with their backs to it on purpose.
- **[[The Outer Market]]** *(the connective tissue)* — a city that feeds you before it asks your name; rumor as the city's nervous system; a single warm seam of grievance under the cook-smoke. *(Run **The Table That Adopts You** — actually hand out the Stress/Hope. See below.)*
- *(Around the edges)* **[[Delphine|Delphine's rooms]]** *(brackish medicine, drying herbs, a woman who looks too long)* and the **Imperial Attaché Quarters** *(immaculate, imported, the desk facing the door)*.

## Potential Adversaries

**This is a warm town day. Keep the teeth in the swamp.** No combat in Sallowreach by design. If the party manufactures violence, the boardwalk answers with embarrassed crowd-control, not blades (rice-guild porters, off-duty polemen, a market warden — run non-lethally; see [[The Outer Market]]). Save real threats for the Mire.

**The one live candidate** — *only if Fletcher's trust-test runs tonight:*
- **The amber civet** *(the "Haunt of Thornside")* — a **Tier 1 Skulk** that wants to flee and hide, not fight. The encounter is about *cornering it without butchering it,* reading that it's terrified and trafficked rather than hunting, and choosing how to end it — **not** a damage race. *(Reskin a light Tier 1 statline; lower its damage, lean on evasion. Full context + the "Thornside Amber" reward in [[Brin Sevarre#Stat Block|Brin's file]].)* Recommend setting it up tonight and **running it next session** unless the table's hungry for it.

## Potential Treasure & Items *(tied to story, not gold)*

- **The city's hospitality, made mechanical.** A shared, unhurried meal clears **1d4 Stress** or grants a **Hope** *(see [[The Outer Market]] → The Table That Adopts You; [[improv-ammo]] §5)*. The warmth is real. Hand it out freely tonight — it's the investment everything later spends.
- **Caspar's name** — Delphine's referral is the session's most valuable "item": the next thread on the curse.
- **A standing cup at the Still Cup** — if Isotta lands with Vael, she's got a place that's hers now, and a teacher. Worth more than loot.
- **Standing reagent access / Consortium contact** — the *payoff* of "Persona Non Grata," not tonight's gift. Tonight the Witch just gets the *errand* and Orna's flicker of trust.
- **The dinner's provisions** — the best food they've had, served by the man who tried to kill them. Let that irony be invisible for now and devastating later.
- **NOT here:** [[The Fishing Spear]] is Act 2+. The "bound" breadcrumbs *point* toward something that breaks bindings; don't hand out the thing itself.

## Running Notes *(the guardrails)*

- **Pace for belonging, not plot.** Tonight's win: they understand the curse is real and uncurable-by-the-obvious-means, the Empire has its hand warmly on their shoulder, and four people in four rooms have started to matter to them. That investment is the most valuable thing you build all campaign.
- **Never let an NPC connect the curse to the Celebration.** Delphine stops herself. Orna and Vael say *bound* and cannot name what binds. The garden beds and the lantern-echo are **planted, not explained.** The party *recognizes* the truth later (the second Celebration) — they never get told. See [[Cosmology#The Recognition Reveal — what they can know, and when|the reveal rules]].
- **No marks.** The tether is purely relational. Nothing to see, nothing to heal. *(Supersedes the [[sallowreach-frame|frame]]'s older "marks on your skin" lines.)*
- **The devotion is real; the warmth is not a trick.** When NPCs are kind, they mean it. The horror later only lands because the love now is authentic.
- **Complications have faces.** Every obstacle tonight is a *person* doing right by someone else — Brin protecting the Mire and the memory of a dead hunter, Orna racing their own dissolving mind, Vael guarding a slipping friend, Sable carrying the worry for his guards. Negotiate with the person, not the problem.
- **Let them split, and let trust be slow.** They share an affliction, not a history. Don't skip to found family. Let them be *surprised* to find they're starting to care.
- **Soft money pressure** begins now: Remy's charity wearing thin, the rental bleed at [[The Mooring]], wreck salvage to convert to coin. Don't foreground it — just let "we need money, and the deep channels need a boat" start to hum.
- **Carry-forward:** unrevealed secrets roll to Session 3; if you cliffhang the dinner, *that's* your strong start next time. Track whether the Tessak scout escaped Session 1 — and whether it escaped *with someone* — because the clan and [[adversaries/gault|Gault]] knowing outsiders survived is a live Act 2 seed.
