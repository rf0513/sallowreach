# The Deep Mire

**Tier 2 Traversal/Exploration**

*"The compass needle has been pointing at your sternum for an hour now. The water glows in a color you have no name for, and it is brightest behind you — exactly where the channel you came in by used to be."*

**Impulses:** Turn the lost in circles, gather its attention into a single thread, draw the seeker toward the [[Mangrove Spirit's Heart]], reward reverence and punish the careless.

**Difficulty:** 14

This is the deep water past [[The Wending]] — beyond the fishing grounds and the named channels, where even [[Sallowreach]] locals don't go casually. [[The Wending]] is the safer approach: known routes, manageable rolls, a guide who can get you home. The Deep Mire is its higher-Tier cousin, and it is not safe. Navigation here is made at Disadvantage [VERIFY]. Compasses spin. The bioluminescence stops following wind or current and starts following *you*. This is the approach route to [[The Mangrove Spirit's Heart]], and the spirit's grief — Front 1, *The Grief Rises* — is no longer diffuse here. It has become directional. Something pays attention. Run this environment as the place where the party first feels, in their bodies, that the swamp is not weather but *will*.

## Potential Adversaries

- **[[Gault]]** (Tier 2 Solo) — this is his hunting ground. He moves through the deep channels alone, reading them by a thread of cold attention he won't admit he's following, extracting from pools the party came to revere. The Deep Mire is the most natural place in the campaign to stage Gault's third encounter (see [[Gault]]) — he is here for the wrong reasons, tracking concentrations he thinks are lantern rays. Predators ignore him. The water lets him through. The party should find that infuriating.
- A glass snake (SRD) or a [[mire-bestiary#The Gloamjaw|Gloamjaw]] — the deep-channel apex — summoned by *Patient Hunter* below
- A Minor Chaos / spirit-touched aberration in the deepest reaches near [[The Mangrove Spirit's Heart]] [VERIFY: SRD adversary]

## Features

**The Mire Pays Attention - Passive:** This is the defining feature. All navigation and Instinct rolls to find a route, hold a bearing, or escape *Lost* are made at **Disadvantage** [VERIFY: Disadvantage representation in Daggerheart]. On any roll, track a **Progress Countdown (6)** toward the party's intended destination; advance it on a success, hold on a failure. The catch: every roll made *with Fear*, success or failure, draws the spirit's attention a notch tighter — the GM gains a held marker, and at three markers the next feature the GM triggers costs no Fear. The deeper the party goes, the more the place is *looking at them.*

**Lost in the Deep - Action:** When the party fails a navigation roll, or on a failure with Fear at any time, they become **Lost** [VERIFY: Lost condition mechanics]. While Lost they cannot benefit from a Long Rest [VERIFY], and each hour Lost the GM may spend a Fear for a Mire encounter. To stop being Lost, succeed on a second navigation attempt — at Disadvantage, like everything here. Spend a Fear to make the way out *move:* the channel they remember is gone, and the only open water leads deeper, toward the [[Mangrove Spirit's Heart]], not back toward [[Sallowreach]].

**Mire Complications - Action:** Draw from [[Tables/mire-complications|the Mire Complications table]] (the Deep Mire d12) when the party is Lost, or when the GM spends a Fear to twist the journey. A few of that table's deep entries, inline for convenience:
1. **The channel closes behind you.** The way back is choked with new roots that weren't there an hour ago. Returning the way you came is no longer an option.
2. **The light leads.** Bioluminescence brightens along one route and dims along all others. Following it is *easier* (clear the next navigation roll's Disadvantage) — but it leads deeper, toward the Heart.
3. **The water remembers.** Someone in the party briefly sees a drowned thing that looks like them — an echo of the wreck, the [[The Tether|tether]], the black water. Mark 1 Stress, or 2 Stress and gain a flicker of true insight about the spirit.
4. **Wrong animals.** Creatures that should flee don't; creatures that should be here aren't. A heron watches too long. (If [[Gault]] is near, they're avoiding *him* — a tell.)
5. **The compass lies, then tells the truth.** The needle spins, settles, and points at one party member's heart. It will keep pointing at whoever the spirit has decided is *looking for it.*

**Undertow Roots - Action:** A character at Close range to open black water makes an Agility Reaction Roll [VERIFY] or a root-mass drags them under: 1d8+3 physical and **Submerged** (cannot act until they surface, costing their action) [VERIFY]. **Spend a Fear** to have the same pull take *two* targets, or to keep a Submerged character down a second round as the water decides whether to let them go.

**Patient Hunter - Action:** **Spend a Fear** to summon a Tier 2 Mire predator (Glass Snake or equivalent [VERIFY]) within Close range. The Deep Mire does not send hunters at [[Gault]] — only at those who read as prey. If Gault is present in the scene, narrate the predator passing him to reach the party. That is the whole horror of him, delivered without a line of dialogue.

**The Welcome Channel - Reaction:** The Deep Mire is not only a threat. When the party behaves with reverence — leaves an offering at a root, names nothing presumptuously, moves quietly, honors the *honored guest* — a channel opens that wasn't there. Clear the next navigation roll's Disadvantage, or remove the GM's accumulated attention markers from *The Mire Pays Attention.* The swamp following its own logic includes following it *toward* those who treat it as a guest rather than a resource. This is the mechanical seed of the campaign's central reframe: the Mire's attention is grief, not malice, and grief can be met with grace.

## Feature Questions

*Which PC has the compass pointed at — and what is it about them the spirit recognizes? (The [[The Tether|tether]] makes every PC a candidate.)*

*What did the party take, kill, or disturb on the way in — and is the Deep Mire's tightening attention a response to that, or to something one of them is quietly carrying?*

*If [[Gault]] is in this scene, what does it cost the party — in Stress, in time, in faith — to watch the swamp refuse to touch the one person who deserves it most?*

*When the party finally meets reverence with reward and a channel opens for them, who at the table understands first that the Mire was never the enemy?*

## Running This

The Deep Mire is a **mood made mechanical.** Three dials carry it:

1. **Disadvantage is the theme.** Every navigation roll at Disadvantage is the swamp saying *you don't belong here yet.* Don't soften it — let them feel outmatched, then offer *The Welcome Channel* as the way through. The lesson is not "navigate better." It's "stop treating the Mire like terrain."

2. **Fear tightens the thread.** Lean on *The Mire Pays Attention.* Narrate the attention as **directional** — never *toward* the party so much as *through* them, toward the city, exactly as Front 1 describes. The party is a splinter the spirit barely registers; what it's reaching for is on the far side of them. Drop that tell early and often.

3. **Gault is the cruelty engine.** If you're running his third encounter (see [[Gault]]), set it here, in spirit-adjacent dark lit by something that has nothing to do with lantern rays. Use *Patient Hunter* and *Wrong animals* to make *Wrong to the Mire* visible without exposition. After he's dead, the bioluminescence disperses and the Mire goes still — don't comment on it. Let it land.

This is the **approach route to [[The Mangrove Spirit's Heart]].** Whenever the party pushes deeper instead of retreating, reward it with proximity to the campaign's emotional core — more light, more attention, more of the sense that the grief has a *shape* and they are getting closer to it.

## Dungeon Alchemist Notes

Mostly theater-of-the-mind — describe the deep channels rather than mapping them. If you need a set-piece (Gault's third fight, a *Patient Hunter* ambush): narrow black-water channels with single-file punt approaches, dense canopy blocking sky, root-masses breaking the surface for *Undertow Roots*, and low blue-green ambient light from the water itself — pulsing wrongly, brightening along one exit to sell *The light leads.* No fixed walls; the channels should feel like they could close behind the party at any moment.
