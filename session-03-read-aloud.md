# Session 3 — Read-Aloud Blurbs

*Spoken, not written — paced like you're improvising it at the table. Read it or riff off it. One image per beat. The load-bearing ones: the strong start (1), the civet's truth (3), the salt wall's Contact (6), and **the Embassy (8)** — the showpiece. Full prep in [[session-03-prep]]; one-pager in [[session-03-cheatsheet]].*

### 1 · Too Many Teeth *(strong start)*
> Morning on the boards, and it's the good kind — the cleanup feast still going from the Celebration, somebody pressing warm bread into hands, the smell of frying lantern-root. You're three bites into the best welcome a city ever gave anyone.
> And then a woman screams.
> She comes down the boardwalk at a dead run, and she's holding — you see it before your brain wants to — a little dog, or what's left of one, its leash chewed clean through, blood on her forearms where she's clutching it to her chest. People scatter out of her way. She's not screaming for help. She's screaming at the *city.*
> *"It came back! In daylight — it came BACK!"* [she catches your eye, all of you, the strangers] *"Eyes like coins under the boards. And a sound — it sounded like a* child. *Somebody do something before it takes one of ours."*
> And behind you, already, the mood's turning — a man's pulling a gaff-hook off a wall, somebody's shouting a number like a bounty, somebody else is just shouting. A frightened animal somewhere under your feet, and a frightened crowd up here with hooks. And every face that was smiling at you a second ago is now turned your way, waiting.

*GM — this is the door into the whole day. The "haunt" is the amber civet; the crowd's the real hazard. **Don't open with dice — open with the crowd's eyes on the party.** What they do here decides whether Fletcher's hunt is a chase or a riot. If Fletcher's player doesn't bite, the civet stays loose and escalates toward dinner; the scream still establishes the city scared.*

### 2 · Under the Boards *(the hunt — tracking)*
> Down through a gap in the planks, the world goes close and dark and dripping — the underside of the city, a forest of stilts furred with weed, the water a foot below you black as tar. Up here it smelled like bread. Down here it smells like fear: a sharp, musky, animal reek, fresh.
> And the sign is everywhere, once you drop into it. Claw-scores too high and too neat for any Mire creature — a *climber's* marks. A half-eaten cache of stolen larder-food, untouched fish right beside it *(this thing doesn't even know what's food here).* And then, snagged on a nail, a tuft of ringed fur, dry-country fur, and under it — you go still — a smear of old blood that isn't the dog's. The animal's own. It's hurt. It's been hurt a while.

*GM — Fletcher's read (Instinct + Eye for Danger / Swamp Specialist) flips the story: this isn't a predator, it's prey loose in a place that's killing it. **Let the truth land before the confrontation** — that's the whole point. The trail leads to the Mooring crates and Gault's marked cages. Full design: [[session-03-prep#The Hunt|the prep]] / `session-02-live.md`.*

### 3 · The Thing in the Corner *(the civet — cornered)*
> You corner it where a broken crate's wedged between two pilings, and for one second your blood goes cold because the eyes *do* catch the light, two coins floating in the dark — and then it moves into a stray shaft of daylight and the monster just… falls apart into what it actually is.
> A cat-sized thing, ringed tail, a sleek climber built for dry cliffs a thousand miles from here. One claw clipped to a stub. Sores worn into its neck the exact shape of a cage. Ribs you can count. It's backed itself into the corner with nowhere left to go, and it makes that sound again — the one that scared a whole neighborhood, the one that sounds almost like a child crying — and you realize it's not a threat.
> It's terrified. It has been terrified since the day somebody put it in a box.

*GM — Tier 1 Skulk, Diff 12, **wants to flee, not fight.** The scene is cornering without butchering (Agility/Finesse), reading scared-not-savage. Not a damage race. This is the campaign's thesis in Fletcher's hands: the monster in the dark is a displaced, trafficked victim. (Optional knife-twist: a hidden litter.) If he kills it, he does it cleanly → Thornside Amber.*

### 4 · The Museum *(Caspar — the curse)*
> Three crooked stories of stilted timber up in the old quarter, leaning companionably on its neighbor, every window stuffed with the silhouettes of *things* — and a hand-lettered sign that just says what it is, no hours, no fuss. Inside it's cedar oil and old paper and dust held in slow shafts of light. Cases everywhere. A wall of channel-maps showing the Mire crawling closer, generation by generation.
> And right by the door, where you almost walk straight past it: a life-sized wax figure of an old harbor-master, punt-pole in one hand, ledger in the other, candles burning at its feet. It's been there so long nobody really sees it anymore.
> Then a small man comes down a ladder backwards, talking before he's even turned around — ink-stained fingers, spectacles shoved up into wild hair, a man clearly mid-thought about six other things — and when he hears the word *Delphine,* and then the word *bound,* he stops dead. Goes completely still. And for the first time all morning, somebody looks at you like you're the most interesting thing that's happened to him in years.
> [Caspar] *"Spirit-water binding. Yes. That's — yes, I have three accounts of something very like it. Sit down, please — all of you. ...This is going to take a moment."*

*GM — he gives the lore freely (he's been* waiting *for listeners). He will NOT give the* name *of the man with the spear — that's fear, not ignorance (Roll: Instinct to clock it). The trust path is returning Vane's journal; plant it, don't resolve. The wax Warden only wakes if they push to coercion or the archive stairs — it's a relationship cost, not a fight. Lines in `world/npcs/caspar-renne.md`.*

### 5 · The Salt Wall — The Approach *(Isotta; Vael walks her out, then stops)*
> Vael walks you out past the last lit windows, to where the boardwalk runs thin and the houses give up — and there it is. The wall's bigger than the stories made it. A rampart of grey salt taller than a stilt-house, crusted and re-packed and crusted again over two hundred years, running off into the haze both directions with no end you can see.
> And this stretch is *losing.* The southwest face is soft, weeping, slumping at the seam, and green is pushing up through every crack in it. A family is working a condemned house nearby in the half-dark — quiet, no fuss, lifting the windows out of the frames to take with them, because the water already owns the floor.
> Vael stops. Plants his feet a good ten paces back, and you feel why before he says it: the closer you get to that salt, the more it *pulls* at you — not your body, your *edges,* like a hand smoothing at the line where you stop and the air begins.
> [Vael, quiet] *"This is as far as I go. The salt was raised to push out things like what's pressing on the far side."* [and his white eye finds you] *"Trouble is — it doesn't know the difference between that, and you. The rest of the way is yours alone."*

*GM — **the salt-and-her detail is load-bearing: the thing that keeps the city safe is the thing that would shove her out.** She's on the wrong side of its mercy, same as whatever's beyond it. Roll: Instinct to read the line (find the worn point); pushing to the salt costs 1–2 Stress. [VERIFY] Then the reach: Instinct + Connects with Spirits vs Diff 15. [VERIFY] Let her choose how far.*

### 6 · Contact *(the salt wall — the centerpiece; hold this one)*
> You push past where the living can stand, to the worn place where the wall's gone to gauze — and something on the far side feels the opening and *leans toward it.*
> It is enormous. It presses on you the way deep water presses on your ears when you dive too far, except the pressure isn't water — it's grief. Old grief. Patient, and bottomless, and so much larger than a person that for a second you forget you are one.
> And here's the thing you'll write down later, when your hands stop shaking: it isn't reaching for *you.* It isn't even reaching for the wall. It's reaching *past* you — through the salt, through the city, toward something held deep in the middle of all those lights — and for one single breath you feel both ends of it at once. The vast thing out here in the dark, straining inward. And a small, fixed, buried point somewhere in the heart of the city, straining back. The two of them pulled toward each other across two hundred years and never, ever touching. Like a held note. Waiting to land.

*GM — **the shape of the whole campaign, with no names attached.** Something outside reaching for something bound inside. First casebook entry. **Guardrail: she feels the shape, NEVER the answer** — no Tree Spirit, no Celebration-as-cage, no founders. Direction and dread only (`cosmology.md`). Then: the parley (Presence) with a lingering dead soul → "the one they keep"; then the pull (Presence reaction vs Diff 14) → her slipping clock. [VERIFY] Four real rolls in one scene — this is the dice fix.*

### 7 · The Casino *(optional tease — Sinope's tip)*
> You smell it before you see it: lamp-oil, spilled rum, and money. A great flat-bottomed barge lashed permanent to the pilings, lit up gold while the rest of the quarter goes dark, and the noise rolling off it is the particular noise of people losing things and laughing about it. Inside, the whole floor tilts a hair with the tide, and nobody at the tables so much as spills a drink — they've all got their sea-legs for it.
> And somewhere in the warm crush, a dealer's voice, and a man's laugh that's pitched a little too high — the laugh of somebody who's already in deeper than he can climb out of, and has decided the only way left is down.

*GM — atmosphere + a thread, not a set piece tonight. The man laughing too high is the Oswin/ward seed (someone tied to the failing wall gambles here and owes badly). Quick Finesse/Presence/Knowledge gambling rolls if they want more dice. Maps: `maps/The Stranded Casino *`.*

### 8 · The Embassy *(the showpiece — stepping out of Sallowreach and into the Empire)*

*Run this in two movements. First the wonder — let them love it. Then the wrongness — let them notice, one detail at a time, that none of it is real.*

**Movement I — the threshold *(wonder):***
> There's a door at the end of the inner boardwalk, and an aide holds it, and you step through — and you step *out of Sallowreach.*
> The heat goes first. Two hundred years of wet bayou air, gone, just like that — the humidity drops, the sweat cools on your neck, and you're breathing something dry and clean and faintly sweet, like the first cool morning of an autumn none of you have felt since you got here. There's *grass,* real green lawn-grass that has no business growing on black water. Gravel paths. And trees — apple trees, in neat rows, in full white blossom *and* heavy with red fruit at the same time, which some quiet part of you knows a tree is not supposed to do.
> A butterfly drifts past your face. Then another. Pale gold, lazy, catching a light that's softer and goldener than the green Mire-light you've gotten used to. Birdsong, somewhere up in the blossoms. Apple-sweetness and cut grass and cool dry air. It is, honestly, beautiful. It feels like *home* — somebody's home, anyway. It feels like a held breath of somewhere that isn't trying to kill you.

**Movement II — the seams *(the wrongness — drip these, don't dump):***
> And then — if you're paying attention — it starts to come apart at the edges.
> The butterfly. You go to brush it away and your hand passes *through* it, no weight, no flutter, and it doesn't startle the way a living thing startles. It just keeps drifting its lazy pattern, the exact same lazy pattern, because it isn't flying — it's being *drawn.*
> You reach up and pick one of those impossible apples and it's perfect, flawless, and it has no *smell* — and when you bite it there's nothing there, no crunch, no juice, your teeth just close on cool air and the image of an apple, already fading.
> And now you can't stop seeing it. There are no insects in this grass. No frogs. No gnats in the cooling air — and you have not been somewhere without gnats in a long time. The birdsong loops. The breeze comes in soft even pulses, on a schedule. And off to the side, half-hidden behind a blossoming hedge, there are three people in plain robes sitting very still with their eyes shut and their hands open and sweat — *real* sweat, the only real sweat in this whole garden — running down their faces from the effort of holding all of it up. The cool. The apples. The season that isn't. A little corner of the capital, conjured over a swamp and kept aloft by three mages working themselves grey, so that one man never has to feel like he left home.

*GM — **this is the Empire's thesis vs. Sallowreach's, in one room.** The swamp's magic is alive — Chausson, the lantern-rays, genuine wonder that asks nothing. The Empire's magic is illusion: beautiful, controlled, hollow, and maintained by labor nobody's supposed to look at. Caius doesn't see the irony — to him this IS better, this is what "infrastructure" looks like. Let the party feel the wonder honestly first (it's a real relief from the heat), then let the seams teach them what kind of man hosts a dinner inside a lie. **It's also a clue:** the Empire makes things that look perfect and aren't real. File it; don't underline it. If a PC pities the straining mages, that's the scene working.*

### 9 · The Host *(Caius at the table — the slip)*
> And in the middle of his impossible garden, at a table set with the best of everything, Caius Maren stands to greet you — and he is *delighted.* Genuinely. Younger than you expected, open-faced, dry and pressed and perfectly at ease, and he's poured the wine himself and he means every warm word of it.
> [Caius, glass up] *"You came. Good — good. I've read three incident reports out of this Mire in two years, and not one of them had survivors. And here you are, at my table. Sit. Please. Eat something that hasn't been fried in swamp-fat — I had it brought in."*
> [and he is wonderful company. He asks about the city, your trades, what you make of the Celebration — and he listens like every word is a gift. And then, somewhere in the second course, gentle, almost an afterthought:]
> [Caius] *"Can I ask — and stop me if it's too soon — where exactly did she go down? Out past the southwest channels, was it? And there were the — what, the four of you, and your guide?"* [a warm, sympathetic beat] *"How long were you in the water, before the light?"*
> [and he waits. And he listens. Like he's going to remember all of it.]

*GM — **he names a detail he shouldn't have.** "Past the southwest channels" / "the four of you" / "before the light" — pick the one that bites. He's checking the party's account against the hit he ordered. Roll: Instinct/Knowledge to catch it → it doesn't reveal HE did it, it plants "this man already knew about our wreck." **For Tenson:** this is the man who was told the Guardian died. Let the divination itch; don't pay it off. The real ask — "would you put it in writing?" — comes later in the meal, framed as helping future travelers. He is NOT a fighter; keep it social. Full scene: `world/npcs/caius-maren.md`.*
