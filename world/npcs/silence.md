# Magistra Silence

**Role:** Senior Mages Guild magistra at the capital citadel; [[Tenson]]'s former principal and the patron who sent him to Sallowreach
**Location:** The capital — the citadel *(off-screen; reaches Sallowreach only by letter and the [[The Long Room|magical courier]])*
**Faction:** Mages Guild (capital establishment)
**Tier:** 3 *(institutional weight, not combat — she is a force exerted at a distance)*

*Tenson's quest begins with her favor. Whether it was a favor is the question the campaign gets to answer. She is a face of the urgency the tether creates: a powerful person expecting a report, who will eventually notice it isn't coming.*

> **On the name.** High-powered wizards in this world are named for spells — or the spells are named for them; no one quite remembers which came first, and the ambiguity is the point. A magistra called **Silence** is a person you have heard *of* and rarely *from*: someone whose attention is a quiet that falls over a room. The name is a credential. It is also a warning.

---

## What She Is to the Table
Silence almost certainly never appears in person — Sallowreach has no road, and a sitting magistra does not punt through a swamp. Her presence in the campaign is **letters,** delivered late and forwarded through [[The Long Room|the Long Room]] after the ship was reported lost. That makes her a precision instrument for the GM: a way to reach into Tenson's chest from a thousand miles off, at exactly the moment it will cost the most.

Use her sparingly and well. Each letter should do one of three things: tighten the screw (she's expecting that report), humanize the stakes (she's *fond* of him, and worried), or turn the knife (she knows more than she's saying — or learns Tenson is trapped, and decides what to do about it).

## Appearance *(for the rare flashback, dream, or sending)*
Late fifties, silver hair kept short and practical, Guild robes of a rank that doesn't need ornament to announce it. She has the unhurried economy of someone whose time is other people's most valuable commodity. When she's fond of you, you know it — the attention is total and genuinely warm. When she's deciding something about you, you usually don't know it at all. People who've worked under her say a room goes quiet when she starts to listen. They mean it as praise. They also mean it.

## Personality & Mannerisms
Brilliant, busy, and genuinely kind in the way of powerful people who've learned that a little real warmth buys more loyalty than a lot of money. She liked Tenson immediately and never stopped — not as a servant, as a *person,* which is exactly what made the mismatch between Tenson and the job unbearable for her to watch. She's the rare patron who noticed her guard hated his life and decided to do something about it.

She writes the way she thinks: precise, affectionate, a half-step ahead. Her letters open warm and close with a request that is never quite an order and is always, somehow, complied with.

## What She Wants
**On the surface:** an answer to a problem that has stopped being tidy. [[Oswin Cray]] has petitioned five times for ward-maintenance funds; the imperial channel — [[Caius Maren]] — has denied him five times; and on the fifth denial Oswin did something a quiet bureaucrat almost never does: he went *over Caius's head,* straight to the Citadel, warning in plain language that **without the funds, the ward will fail.** That warning landed on Silence's desk. A failing ward means the Mire's growth, the lumber tribute, and a Guild posting two centuries old all in question. Someone reliable, discreet, and good with *both* a sword and a ledger should go find out where the truth actually stands. Tenson is perfect — and it gets him out from behind her, doing real work on his own terms.

**Underneath *(GM eyes)*:** Silence is one of the very few at the Guild who has actually *read* Oswin's quarterly reports — not the budget lines, the **observations.** The note, repeated for four years and ignored above and below her, that the Mire's great spirit moves toward Sallowreach with *direction, not randomness,* from the southwest, "as if something inside the city has its attention." She has cross-referenced it against the gap in the archive where [[The First Maintainer's Journal|Aldric Vane's original field journal]] should be. She doesn't have a theory. She has a *curiosity* — the patient, dangerous kind that built her career. Oswin's escalation is a real crisis and also her pretext: she wants eyes inside Sallowreach, and trusts exactly one person not to tell her what he thinks she wants to hear.

She did not, and does not, know the Mire can *keep* a person. That part she will have to learn.

## What She's Hiding
That the errand was never only an errand. She sent Tenson toward something she finds genuinely fascinating and cannot investigate herself without alerting people at the Guild she'd rather not alert — including, though she doesn't know his name, the channel [[Caius Maren]] operates through. She framed it as a kindness because it *is* one, and because a kindness asks fewer questions than an assignment.

She is not a villain. She is a good person who used a person she loves as an instrument and told herself the two weren't in tension. Whether she's right is the campaign's question to put to her.

## The Gut-Punch *(GM — hold both, let play decide)*
When word reaches Silence that Tenson is *tethered* — that her favor became a prison — the campaign forks. Keep both tines live until the table's relationship with her letters tips it:

- **The kind version:** she is horrified. She spends real Guild capital trying to help from afar — pressure, resources, a letter that finally moves the funding — and carries the guilt openly. Her affection was the whole truth and the trap was an accident. This makes Tenson's eventual choices about *her* ache.
- **The sharp version:** she grieves, and then she calculates. A trusted agent permanently embedded in the one city the Guild has never been able to see inside is not a tragedy — it's an *asset.* Her letters get warmer and her requests get larger. Tenson realizes, slowly, that he was spent, and that the person who spent him still signs her letters with love. This is the knife, and it should only land if the table earns the suspicion.

Don't pre-decide. Let Tenson's player read the letters and tell you, by how they react, which Silence they're more afraid of.

## Key Relationships
- [[Tenson]] — her former personal guard; she's fond of him, proud of him, and the author of the assignment that stranded him; the most important off-screen relationship in his story
- [[Oswin Cray]] — a name in her files, a maintainer whose reports she actually read when no one else would; she's never met him and has, unknowingly, more sympathy for him than anyone else at the Guild; his over-her-rank warning is what set Tenson in motion
- [[Caius Maren]] — unknown to her by name; the imperial channel denying Oswin's funds runs through territory she's wary of; she sent Tenson to find out who's behind it, not knowing she'd aimed him at the man who arranged his shipwreck
- The Mages Guild — her power base and her cage of professional caution; she works *around* it more than through it

## Dialogue Hooks *(from her letters)*
**The assignment, before the wreck:**
*"You'll hate that I'm right about this, so let me be right quickly: you are wasted standing behind me. There's a question in Sallowreach that needs a careful eye, a steady hand, and someone who can read a ledger without falling asleep in it. That's you. Go. Send me what you find — and Tenson, see the place. They say it's extraordinary. Let yourself see it."*

**The first letter to reach him after the wreck (forwarded, late):**
*"They told me the ship was lost. They told me there were no survivors. I have spent a week being a person who believes that, and I find I refuse. Write to me the moment this reaches you. Whatever has happened, whatever that place has done — write to me."*

**Later, the screw tightening — read the tone the table has earned:**
*"I'm glad you're alive. Gladder than I can put in a letter that goes through that ridiculous singing room. Now. Tell me about the wall. Tell me everything about the wall. You have no idea — or perhaps by now you do — how much depends on what you've seen."*

## Stat Block
Not a combat NPC; she will never be in the room. If her institutional weight ever needs to manifest as threat or ally, express it through resources, authority, and Guild leverage, not a fight. [VERIFY: only if a sending/projection scene ever requires mechanics.]
