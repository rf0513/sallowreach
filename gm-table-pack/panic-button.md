## 🆘 WHEN YOU FREEZE — do ONE of these

1. **Throw it back.** Describe the situation, end on *"What do you do?"* Hand them the spotlight.
2. **Ask a player a question** and build on the answer: *"Why does that matter to you?" · "What were you hoping to find?" · "What does your character notice first?"*
3. **Make a soft move** — telegraph trouble (see moves, bottom).
4. **Cut away** — to another PC, the B-plot, or an NPC doing their own thing. Scenes can be short.
5. **Drop a secret/clue** from your prep. You wrote ten; reveal one *now.*
6. **Make a Fear move** — you've been banking it. Spend it (menu below).
7. **Advance a front** off-screen: *"While you were here, the southwest wall…"* (see [[world-in-motion|the fronts]]).
8. **Call for a roll** — only if there's real uncertainty *and* real stakes.
9. **End the scene** on a strong image and smash-cut.

> **The move that never fails:** *describe what's happening → "what do you do?"* When truly lost, just do that.

## 🔥 THE FEAR MENU

*Your stated worry, solved. This is the heart of the page.*

**When you GAIN Fear:** every time a player rolls **with Fear** (the Fear die comes up higher), you bank **1**. Some adversary and environment features hand you more. Pool caps at **12**. [VERIFY: exact Fear triggers/cap vs. core book]

> **THE ONE RULE: SPEND IT.** Hoarded Fear is wasted Fear — a big pile means your scenes are too safe. You *cannot* spend it "wrong"; the worst case is a slightly bigger moment. Aim to spend **something in every tense scene.** It refills on its own. Stop guarding it.

**The rhythm:** a **Fear result** hands you the spotlight *for free* — make a move **and** bank 1. Then **spend 1–3** to escalate, twist, or move the world. Spend in **bursts at the peak** of a scene, not a nervous trickle.

### Spend 1 Fear →
- **Seize the spotlight** when it isn't yours — interrupt with a hard move.
- **Spotlight an adversary for an extra action** (a second surge from the gar pack).
- **Fire an environment Fear feature** — the [[The Wending|Wending]]'s *Something Surfaces*, the [[mire-bestiary#Mire Gar|gar]]'s *Feeding Frenzy*, the [[The Celebration|Celebration]]'s hidden moves.
- **The Mire answers** — roll or pick a [[mire-complications|Mire Complication]].
- **An ignored NPC acts** (NPCs have Tuesday): [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]] makes a move, [[Caius Maren|Caius]] files another document, [[Fen]] plays at the water again.

### Spend 2–3 Fear →
- **Summon reinforcements** — more gar, a fresh threat, the [[mire-bestiary#Old Gar|Old Gar]] rising on her false light.
- **A hard reversal** — the situation gets meaningfully, visibly worse.
- **Advance a front with teeth** — the wall gives a little, an edge-family loses a home, the displacement bites ([[world-in-motion|fronts]]).
- **Stack adversary actions** for a real spike of danger.

### 🌑 Sallowreach Fear *(the campaign's own flavor)*
- **The bioluminescence does something wrong** — something vast turns its attention *through* a PC. The grief, brushing past. (1 Stress, no explanation.)
- **A tether ache** — a PC feels the pull toward the place they were going. Mark Stress; make it *personal* (use their Session Zero stakes).
- **The Mire reaches** — roots where they weren't, a channel closing behind them, the way back gone.
- **Word lands at the worst moment** — a message arrives via [[The Long Room|Lucien]] from the person waiting for a PC.
- **The hidden cost ticks** — disrupt the Celebration and the next morning the wall is thinner (the [[world-in-motion|Interlock]]).

### Worked examples
- **Gar fight, 3 Fear:** spend 1 to spotlight the pack for a *Feeding Frenzy*; on the next Fear roll spend 1 to **summon the Old Gar**; *hold* the third for the moment someone's an arm's length from the bank.
- **Quiet tea scene, 2 Fear:** spend 1 so **Caius's** dinner invitation arrives *right now*; spend 1 so the outer-boardwalk family they just met is quietly packing up — the edge moved while they sipped.

## 🎛️ GM MOVES — what a "move" even is

**Soft** *(telegraph; no immediate harm — usually free):* show signs of trouble · an NPC wants something · reveal an unwelcome truth · start a countdown · offer a hard bargain · a complication appears.

**Hard** *(it happens; often a Fear result or a Fear spend):* deal damage or a real cost · separate them · take something away · an NPC acts on their goal · turn their own move against them · make the soft thing you telegraphed come true.

> **Telegraph hard moves with soft ones first.** **Be a fan of the characters.** And **fail forward** — a failed roll moves the story (new trouble, a cost, a complication), never just *"nothing happens."*
