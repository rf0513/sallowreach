# Justice in Sallowreach

*How a city with no police, no prison, and no law but its own keeps itself. GM reference — the party **will** brush against this.*

---

## Why There's No Police Force

Sallowreach doesn't need one, for the same three reasons the party is stuck:

1. **Everyone knows who you are.** Nineteen in twenty are multigenerational, embedded from birth in family, neighborhood, guild, and Celebration-role webs. You cannot be anonymous here.
2. **You cannot leave.** The [[The Mooring|Mooring]]'s punt-Wardens control *all* transit, and the [[The Mire|Mire]] kills the unprepared. The geography is the jail wall. A thief can no more flee the city than the party can.
3. **The swamp is the final sanction.** [[Adaline Roux|Adaline]]'s creed — *"the Mire knows who belongs"* — isn't only theology. It's the spine of the justice system.

So in place of cops and a courthouse, justice runs on **reputation, restitution, and the Mire.** The community *is* the law — convened when needed, dissolved after.

---

## The Two Courts

- **The Hall of the Mire** — what the city is willing to *look at.* [[Adaline Roux|Adaline]] and the district elders arbitrate in the open. She solves a dispute the way a river finds a channel: she listens longer than anyone expects, and by the time she says what should happen, the room has usually already arrived there.
- **The Pale Room** — what the city *won't* look at. [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]] *"settles what can't go before a magistrate"* — debts, underworld disputes, and, quietly, the occasional predator the city would simply rather stopped existing. Sometimes the warm city's clean hands stay clean because the cold thing under the floor handles the worst, and nobody asks.

Most ordinary trouble never reaches either — guilds discipline their own members, families police their own, and a district elder settles the rest over a table.

---

## The Ladder of Justice

A community that can't leave each other has to keep living together — so justice is **restorative first, punitive last.** It climbs only when it has to.

**1 — Make it right, and wear the shame.** *(Most things.)* You repay what you took, you make public amends, your family bears the weight of your name and leans on you hard to reform. Then you're reabsorbed. In a city this tight, *loss of face is the punishment* — doors that were open close, neighbors trade elsewhere, your family stops being vouched for. That costs more than a fine ever could.

**2 — Exclusion and bond.** *(Serious, or repeat.)* You lose standing. You're cut from the communal tables and the Celebration roles — a kind of social death in a city built on belonging. You work a **labor-bond** to make the city whole: rebuild the boardwalk you broke, haul salt on the ward line, crew the punts that clear the channels.

**3 — Given to the Mire.** *(Severe. Rare — a handful in a generation.)* For the genuinely dangerous and unrepentant, see below.

---

## Casting Out — "Given to the Mire"

There's no prison (an isolated stilt-city won't feed and guard inmates, and there's nowhere to ship them), and the city will not keep a true predator among its families. So the worst offenders are **cast out.**

It is done rarely, and solemnly, and never called an execution. The condemned is **denied transit** by the Wardens, **struck from the tables**, walked at first light to a far channel at the city's edge, and left there with no punt, no salt, and no welcome. The city does not pronounce a sentence of death. It says **the Mire will decide** — and tells itself that what the swamp does next is the swamp's judgment, not theirs.

Most don't come back. The rare ones who do are no longer quite people — they become the ghost stories told to children, the half-seen shapes in the deep channels, the [[The Mire|Mire]]-fringe figures who learned to live in the place that was meant to take them.

> **The discomfort is the point.** A gentle, warm community does something quietly brutal and reframes it as the swamp's will so its own hands stay clean. It is the **[[The Celebration|Celebration]] in miniature** — the city's habit of not looking directly at the hard thing it's doing. Run a casting-out, if it ever comes up, with full solemnity and full warmth. Let it sit wrong.

## Who Actually Enforces

Nobody whose *job* it is.

- **The aggrieved and their neighbors** detain the offender — they're not going anywhere, and everyone knows their face.
- **[[Adaline Roux|Adaline]]'s marshals** and the Accord **stewards** keep the peace at scale (run them as [[sallowreach-roster#Celebration Marshal|Celebration Marshals]] — few, local, non-lethal, *restore order, don't wound*). They usher and separate; they are not a standing force.
- **A locked room at the Hall** holds someone until they're heard. That's the closest thing to a cell. There is no long-term jail.

---

## The Party's Exposure *(a live stake)*

The party has **none** of what makes this system gentle: no family web, no generational standing, no neighbors to vouch for them. Their only shield is the **honored-guest status** [[Adaline Roux|Adaline]] extended — real, but *thin.*

- A desperate, broke, tethered party that starts stealing is exposed to the *harsh* rungs of the ladder far faster than a local would be.
- Abusing the welcome **frays the one thing keeping the city soft on them** — and Adaline is *watching* what they do with it.
- Conversely, *making it right* the local way (restitution, public amends, a labor-bond worked without complaint) earns standing the city doesn't give outsiders lightly.

Quick rulings:
| The party… | Likely consequence |
|---|---|
| Petty theft, first time, caught | Restitution + a very public loss of face; Adaline hears about it |
| Brawling / property damage | Labor-bond to repair it; stewards separate them |
| Serious, repeated, or unrepentant harm | Honored-guest status revoked; the city turns cold; the harsh end looms |
| Crossing [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]] | Not the Hall's problem. His. |

## Hooks & Toys

- **The cast-out who lived.** Somewhere in the deep Mire is someone the city gave to the water years ago — now a Mire-fringe hermit who knows channels no one else does, carries a grudge or a strange peace, and may help or haunt the party. A great deep-Mire encounter, and a living indictment of the city's gentlest cruelty.
- **A casting-out the party can stop, witness, or be asked to carry out.** What do they do when the warm city they love walks someone to the edge?
- **The city wants a problem to "go to the Mire,"** and looks — quietly — to the party, or to [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]], to make it happen without anyone having to say so.
- **The party is accused** (rightly or framed — [[Caius Maren|Caius]] would *love* a documented incident of imperial-adjacent travelers committing crimes on Sallowreach water).
- **[[Sinope]] is the counterexample:** a harmless eccentric the city *"stopped trying to move."* Sallowreach absorbs weirdness with ease; it reserves its hardness for genuine harm. The contrast tells the party exactly where the lines are.

---

## GM Note

Keep it **mostly gentle.** Day to day, Sallowreach justice is restitution, a stern word, a family closing ranks, an elder's ruling over a shared meal — warm, embedded, forgiving, because it has to be. The brutality at the top of the ladder is *rare*, *solemn*, and *reframed* — which is exactly what makes it land when the party finally sees it. The horror isn't that the city is cruel. It's that the same people who fed you and pulled you from the water will, once in a long while and with genuine sorrow, walk a person to the edge and call it the swamp's choice.
