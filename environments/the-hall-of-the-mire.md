# The Hall of the Mire

**Tier 1 Social**

*"The whole room turns when you enter — sixty families' worth of watching — and somewhere behind the City Leader is a locked box none of them have ever asked her to open."*

[[Adaline Roux]]'s civic seat: the stilted hall at the center of [[Sallowreach]] where the city governs itself, hears petitions, administers the honored-guest tradition, and remembers itself out loud. It is older than anyone in it. The floor is dense Mire-lumber worn pale by two centuries of standing feet; the high windows let in green canal-light; the long benches fill, on a hearing day, with the old families who have held this room since the first boardwalks went down. People come here to be heard, to be weighed, and — though no one would phrase it this way — to be permitted. Run it as warmth with teeth: this is hospitality administered by people who have been administering it longer than the petitioner has been alive.

*(GM SECRET — the box: somewhere in this hall, in a locked box passed City-Leader to City-Leader, is a water-damaged fragment of the founders' record. It describes the [[Cosmology#The Celebration The Truth|Celebration]]'s establishment as a thing made to "**hold** the great presence in welcome and peace." Adaline has read it. She read "hold" as "keep," as in keep in welcome — and has chosen, for twenty-two years, not to look closer ([[The World in Motion#Front 8 — The Steward's Blindness|Front 8]]). The founders meant it the other way. The document is **The Founders' Box** feature below; it should intrigue, never confess.)*

---

**Impulses:** Weigh the stranger, keep the faith, let nothing change that hasn't earned changing, hold what was handed down.

**Difficulty:** 11

**Potential Adversaries:** None by design — the challenge here is civic authority, not violence. The threat is being found wanting, being asked to leave, watching a powerful family close ranks against you. If a hearing somehow turns to force (it should not, and the Hall will be appalled), reach for [[sallowreach-roster#Celebration Marshal|civic marshals]] to *escort out, not to harm.* [[Caius Maren|Caius]]'s [[sallowreach-roster#Imperial Attaché Guard|imperial attaché guards]] may attend a hearing to watch the party be measured by a city he wants to inherit.

### Features

- **The Weight of the Room - Passive:** This is the defining shaper, and it is always on. The party does not simply *speak* in the Hall — they are *weighed* by it. Any attempt to petition, propose, request standing, or be taken seriously by the city is a Presence Roll against the room itself, not against Adaline alone (she rarely speaks first; she lets the room arrive somewhere). On a success with Hope, the benches lean in — the party is heard *as people the swamp caught for a reason,* and one secret below opens to them. On a success with Fear, they are heard but not yet trusted: the request advances, and Adaline clocks that this stranger is *trying,* and remembers it. On a failure, the room does not turn hostile — it turns *patient,* which is worse, and the PC marks 1 Stress under the specific weight of two hundred years deciding whether you belong. [VERIFY: Daggerheart Action Roll + Presence trait]

- **Two Hundred Years of Tradition - Passive:** The Hall has inertia, and it is heavier for outsiders. Any roll a PC makes to *propose change* — to alter the honored-guest custom, to question how the Celebration is run, to ask the city to do a thing it has never done — is made with disadvantage; any roll to *work within the form,* to show respect for what was handed down, to ask the city to do more of what it already believes, is made with advantage. The Hall rewards the petitioner who honors the tradition and resists the one who arrives certain they understand it better. *"You don't honor something by deciding you understand it better than everyone who came before you."* [VERIFY: advantage/disadvantage from environmental stance]

- **The Founders' Box - Action (GM-secret clue):** If the party has earned real standing in the Hall — or has reason and leverage to ask — Adaline (or a clerk, or the room's grudging permission) may grant access to the locked box and the founders' fragment inside. A PC who studies it makes a Knowledge or Instinct Roll [VERIFY: trait]. On a success, the water-damaged hand resolves enough to read: the Celebration was made to **"hold the great presence in welcome and peace"** — and "hold" sits on the ruined vellum *ambiguously,* legible as *keep-in-welcome* and equally as *keep-imprisoned,* the ink eaten away at exactly the word that would settle it. This is a clue, not a confession: it should make the careful PC's skin prickle without telling them what they've found. On a failure, the damp has taken too much this reading — they have the shape of it, the word "hold," and the certainty there is more here than Adaline has admitted to herself. *(Honor [[The World in Motion#Why Only the Party Can Bend This|the structural blindness]]: Adaline holds this fragment and has chosen not to look at the word. The party is the first in two centuries with no reason to look away.)*

- **A Family Objects - Action:** The GM puts an old family in the party's path — not a villain, a *constituency.* When the party's request would cost someone who has been in this room for generations (an inner-district matron, a drum-keeping lineage, a name that built a boardwalk), that family speaks, and the room's sympathy tilts toward the people it has known for two hundred years over the strangers it met three weeks ago. The party must now win a person, not an argument: answer the objection, find what the family actually fears, or watch their petition stall against the soft immovable weight of belonging. [VERIFY: opposed Presence/social rolls]

- **Adaline Goes Still - Reaction (Fear-gated):** Spend a Fear when the party pushes the Hall past what it will bear in a single hearing — proposing to change the Celebration to a room that reveres it, accusing the city of a harm it cannot yet see, demanding faster than they have earned the right to demand. Adaline does not raise her voice. She goes *very still* — the stillness of someone who has been deciding not to look at something for a long time and will not be made to look at it today — and asks them, with the full and terrible politeness of civic authority, to leave. The room does not recover this scene; the party's standing in the Hall drops a notch, no further business is heard today, and they walk out past sixty families who watched it happen. *"Be careful what you assume you understand about this place."* This is reversible — earned back over time, through who the party becomes to her and to the city — but it is felt now, and it is meant to sting. [VERIFY: Fear spend; social-standing as fiction, not a tracked stat]

- **Show Me - Reaction (Fear-gated):** Spend a Fear *only* when the party brings the Hall a truth it cannot wave off, with evidence it cannot dismiss — the founders' word, the binding, the Interlock, [[Fen]]'s wrong note — and only after they have earned the right to be believed at all (see Running This). Adaline goes still in a different way: not the door-closing stillness, the *floor-opening* one. *"Show me."* Not angry. Not dismissive. The voice of a woman handed what was always going to be there the day she finally looked. This is not a feature that resolves anything. It opens the hardest conversation in the campaign — the steward, the city she has given her life to, and what it is built on. Present it as *possible.* Never script where it goes. [VERIFY: Fear spend]

## Feature Questions

*Use these to seed the room as the party works it — what the Hall wants from them, and what it costs to be weighed here.*

- *Which old family has the party already brushed against out in the city — and what happens when that family is the one in the benches when the party finally asks for something?*
- *When the room turns patient rather than hostile, what does a PC reach for — humility, leverage, the truth — and what does the swamp's choosing of them earn them that an ordinary stranger could never claim?*
- *If the party gets the founders' box open before they understand the binding, does the ambiguous word "hold" intrigue them enough to chase it — or do they file it and walk past the thing in the city's heart, the way everyone else has?*
- *What would the party have to have been, to Adaline and to Sallowreach, before "Show me" is a door they're allowed to open — and are they willing to be that, this early, for this city?*

---

## Running This

This is a **Social** environment: the challenge is being weighed by a city's authority and the weight of two centuries of inherited care, not anything you roll initiative for. Lead with **The Weight of the Room** every time the party comes to the Hall to be taken seriously — let them feel the benches deciding, let Adaline hold her counsel until the room has nearly arrived somewhere on its own. Keep the two Fear-gated reactions strictly distinct: **Adaline Goes Still** is the door *closing* on a party that pushed too fast or too certain; **Show Me** is the door *opening* for a party that brought a truth they earned the right to be believed about. Most tables will see the first long before the second, and that is correct — the second has to be paid for in relationship, across sessions, with who the party has been to her and to the city ([[The World in Motion#Front 8 — The Steward's Blindness|Front 8]]). The **Founders' Box** is a clue engine, not a reveal: the word is *hold,* the ink is gone where it matters, and Adaline herself read it the comfortable way. Let the ambiguity do the work. Above all, never make the Hall a villain. It is a room full of people doing right by the people they already love, administering a tradition they revere, in a building older than their grandparents — and that sincerity is exactly what makes proposing to change it the hardest, most earned conversation the party can walk into.
