# The Outer Market

**Tier 1 Social** *(strong Exploration texture)*

*"You came here a stranger off a wrecked ship, and by the third bell someone has put food in your hands that you did not pay for, learned your name, and asked after the shoulder you didn't tell them you'd hurt — and the worst part, the part that will undo you, is that they mean it."*

The everyday heart of [[Sallowreach]]: the wide market boardwalks where the city does its living. This is where [[Fen]] rents a room above a tannery and frames her rejection letters; where [[Sinope]] holds court from the third pillar past the rice guild's loading dock; where [[Remy Duval]] sends newcomers to be fed and the smell of the cook-stalls reaches the canal-streets before the punts do. The boardwalk runs from dawn to the third bell at full commercial roar, then loosens into something slower and warmer toward evening. Run it for exactly what it is: the city deciding it likes the party, and the party discovering they want to be liked back. **The danger here is light. The warmth is real, and it is the warmth that does the work** — this is the engine of player investment, the place that makes everything the campaign later asks of them *cost* something.

*(GM note — the surface is the substance here. There is no hidden layer to this environment. But [[The World in Motion#Front 4 — The Fracture|Front 4, the Fracture]] simmers a single degree under the cook-smoke: the seam between the old guard and the outer-boardwalk folk, between the Accord's comfortable inner districts and the holdout families on the far water. Let the party *feel* it before they understand it — a table that goes quiet, a vendor's pointed kindness toward one customer and clipped tone toward another. See **Old Argument, New Edge** below.)*

---

**Impulses:** Pull the stranger into the warmth, feed them better than they deserve, trade in rumor and name, let the old seam show for half a breath.

**Difficulty:** 10

**Potential Adversaries:** None by design — this is a Social environment, not a fight. If the party manufactures violence (starting a brawl, robbing a stall, drawing steel over a punt collision), the boardwalk answers with embarrassed crowd-control, not blades: rice-guild porters, off-duty punt operators, a market warden or two who want everyone to *calm down and sit down* (use [[sallowreach-roster#Celebration Marshal|Celebration Marshal]] stats, run non-lethally). A cutpurse working the crowd is a textural complication, not a combatant (see **The Quick Hand** below). Save real threats for the Mire.

### Features

- **The Word on the Boardwalk - Passive:** The market is the city's nervous system, and a party that lingers, buys a meal, and *listens* may make an Instinct Roll (or Presence, if they're working the crowd directly) to gather what Sallowreach is talking about this week. On a success with Hope, three rumors surface, and one of them is offered *warmly* — a vendor or a neighbor who decides the party is worth confiding in. On a success with Fear, two rumors surface, but the party is also clocked as outsiders asking questions, and someone files that away. On a failure, mark 1 Stress for the disorienting wall of a hundred conversations in a dialect half a beat off your own — and learn one rumor anyway, because the boardwalk cannot help itself. [VERIFY: Daggerheart Action Roll + trait]. Sample rumors, each pointing at a live thread — pick by what the table has brushed against:
  - *"The southwest families are losing floor to the high water. My cousin's porch is ankle-deep now and the city keeps sending paper instead of pilings."* ([[The World in Motion#Front 3 — The Edge Erodes|the eroding edge]] / [[The World in Motion#Front 2 — The Wall Thins|the thinning wall]])
  - *"You hear that drummer girl plays to the swamp now, out past the boards? The Accord won't have her. Some say good riddance. Some say she's the only honest music left in this city."* ([[Fen]], [[The World in Motion#Front 4 — The Fracture|the Fracture]])
  - *"There's a Celebration every week of your life, and you still cry at the lanterns. That's not nothing. Don't let the inner-district people tell you it's just a meal."* ([[The Celebration]])
  - *"If you need a thing that can't be gotten, you don't go to a shop. You find out who works for Marcel Dray, and you're polite."* ([[Marcel Dray]], the underground economy)
  - *"The imperial man — Maren — he's been asking after the shipwreck. Real friendly. Bought a whole table breakfast just to chat. My uncle thinks he's lonely. My uncle's an idiot."* ([[Caius Maren]], [[The World in Motion#Front 6 — The Patient Man|the Patient Man]])
- **The Table That Adopts You - Passive:** This is the defining feature, and the genuine boon: **the hospitality of Sallowreach is mechanically real.** A PC who sits at a cook-stall, accepts a stranger's offered seat, or simply lets the food and the talk land on them may, once per visit, take a downtime-style breather amid the warmth — clear 1d4 Stress, or recover Hope (their choice) [VERIFY: Daggerheart Stress/Hope recovery, akin to a Tend to Wounds / short-rest move applied socially]. There is no trick to it, no debt incurred, no hidden cost. The vendor who waves off the coin, the family that shifts down the bench, the old woman who decides a PC looks too thin — they mean it. *The warmth is not a setup.* Let the party feel cared for. The campaign will spend this later; for now, simply give it to them.
- **A Voice You Can't Ignore - Action:** The GM seats a notable into the party's afternoon, free of charge, because the market is where these people live. [[Fen]] is set up against a piling with her brass-ringed frame drum, playing an arrangement the Accord has rejected three times, to a small crowd that is half-delighted and half-nervous to be seen enjoying it — *"It's the same song. I changed eight bars. Nobody can tell me why those eight bars are sacred, just that they are."* Or [[Sinope]] from his alcove, opening a debate the way other men open shops — *"Where exactly is the sensation? Not metaphorically. Physically. Have you mapped the threshold?"* — the most annoying and most useful questions anyone will ask the tethered party. Either one is an open door into the city's social fabric. Engaging is rewarded; both of them remember who actually *listened*.
- **Eat What You're Given - Action:** A PC who hesitates at a stall — eyeing a Mire-ingredient dish they can't name, a glistening fungus, a thing that was alive an hour ago — is gently dared by the vendor to *trust the bayou.* A PC who eats with an open heart (no roll needed, just a yes) gains a Hope, and the vendor decides they're alright; word travels, and the next stall is friendlier. A PC who refuses or demands to know what's in it isn't punished — but the moment cools, and the boardwalk reads them, correctly, as someone still standing outside the city looking in. [VERIFY: Hope gain]
- **Old Argument, New Edge - Action/Reaction:** Spend a Fear to let the Fracture surface for a held breath. A brewing dispute boils over within earshot — an inner-district Accord member calling an outer-boardwalk family's grief *unseemly*; a holdout matron telling a comfortable rice-guild man that his tradition was *never hers to begin with*; a relocation-order argument that turns from policy to something older and rawer. The party can step in (broker it, take a side, defuse it with a free Presence Roll [VERIFY]), stay out (and watch the seam widen a degree — a tell that [[The World in Motion#Front 4 — The Fracture|the Fracture]] is warming), or simply witness it. **No blood is shed. Nothing is resolved.** The point is the party learning, in their bodies, that the city they're falling in love with is not at peace with itself — and that the people on both sides of the seam are people they've already shared a table with.
- **The Quick Hand - Reaction:** Spend a Fear when a PC is flush, distracted, or basking in the warmth: a cutpurse — young, fast, working the crowd to eat — lifts a coin pouch or a small carried item and melts toward the canal-streets. A PC may make an Agility or Instinct Reaction Roll [VERIFY] to feel the lift and catch a wrist. **This is texture, not a fight.** Catch them and the party holds a frightened kid whose options are the Soak or a meal — and the boardwalk watches to see which the strangers choose. Miss them and the loss is minor, but a neighbor has *seen,* and is mortified on the city's behalf: *"That's not us. That is not who we are. Come — sit, eat, let me make it right."* Either way the complication deepens belonging rather than danger.

## Feature Questions

*Use these to seed hooks as the party moves through the market.*

- *Which rumor does the boardwalk hand the party warmly — and which vendor decided they were worth confiding in, and why?*
- *Who feeds them the best meal of their lives for free, and what does it do to a person to be cared for by a city that has no reason to care?*
- *When the old seam shows — the Accord member and the edge family — which side does the party already have a face and a name for?*
- *If the party catches the cutpurse, do they hand a hungry kid to the Soak, or to a cook-stall — and who's watching them decide?*

---

## Running This

Run this early and run it slow. This is the **"let the city love them first"** environment, and its whole job is to make Sallowreach feel like home before the campaign gives the party any reason to fear for it. Lead with warmth, follow with texture, and keep the danger almost entirely off the table — a missing purse, a near-collision of punts, a heated word between neighbors is the *upper bound* of what should go wrong here. Lean hard on **The Table That Adopts You**: actually hand the party that Stress relief and that Hope, with no strings, so the generosity registers as true. The Fracture features (**Old Argument, New Edge**, and the rumors pointing at [[Fen]] and the displaced families) are the only shadow you should let fall, and even then only a degree — the party should leave the market not alarmed but *attached,* having met [[Fen]] and [[Sinope]], eaten extraordinary food, gathered the threads that will pull them deeper, and started — without quite noticing — to belong. That attachment is the most valuable thing you will build all campaign. Spend the early sessions building it generously.
