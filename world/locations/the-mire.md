# The Mire

**Type:** Wilderness / Magical Swamp
**Region:** Surrounds [[Sallowreach]]
**Spirit Presence:** [[The Mangrove Spirit]] (diffuse, vast), [[The Tree Spirit]] (trapped, suffused into wood and water)

---

## Description

Dense mangrove, black water, shifting waterways. Beautiful and dangerous. Bioluminescent at night — the water glows in patterns that don't follow wind or current, which locals accept and outsiders find unsettling. The smell is mineral-rich, organic, alive. The air is thick. Sound carries strangely.

Travel is by punt boat through narrow channels. The channels shift — what was passable last season may be choked with roots this season, and a waterway that closes sometimes opens somewhere else. Outsiders get lost. Locals get lost less often.

**The Mire is growing.** Slowly but measurably. Boardwalks that were clear last season are now choked with roots. Fishing channels are narrowing. The city's outer edges are being swallowed inch by inch. *(GM SECRET: The growth is the [[Mangrove Spirit]]'s longing — it's searching for the [[Tree Spirit]]. The ward is also undermaintained. See [[Cosmology]] and [[The Wizard]].)*

## The Wending

The navigable waterways that locals use — named channels with established routes, memorized over generations. Travel through the Wending is manageable with a guide or strong navigation. Without one, rolls are required. See [[Overview#The Tether|Mire Navigation mechanics]]. [VERIFY: confirm mechanic location once finalized]

The Wending covers the Mire's inner ring — the fishing grounds, the ingredient-harvesting areas, the channels the punt operators use for city transit. Beyond the Wending is unfamiliar territory.

## Key Locations

### The Drowning Garden

A sunken ruin deeper in the Mire — structures half-submerged, bioluminescent growth covering everything, water clear enough in places to see what's beneath. Possible location of [[the-tidebreaker|The Fishing Spear]] or a key encounter with the [[Tree Spirit]].

*To be built out.*

### The Oldstone

A shrine or landmark the locals avoid. Not with fear exactly — more the way you don't walk through someone else's private space. The oldest Sallowreacher families leave small offerings at the edge of the path that leads to it and don't go further. Possible site of the original binding, or where the binding can be broken.

*To be built out.*

### The Deep Mire

Past the Wending, past the fishing grounds. Where even locals don't go casually. Navigation fails. Compasses spin. Animals behave strangely. The [[Mangrove Spirit]]'s presence is strongest here — not threatening exactly, but undeniable. Something pays attention in a way that feels less diffuse, more directed.

The party will need to come here. Probably more than once.

## Secrets

- The black water is magically charged — people who go under in it may be tethered. *(See [[Overview]] — the why is an open question to resolve.)*
- The Mire's growth is grief, not aggression. The Mangrove Spirit is searching.
- The channels that seem to close and reopen are not random — the Mire is slow-thinking, but it thinks.
- Locals who've lived near the Mire their whole lives sometimes sense things they can't explain. The [[Old Stories Keeper]] has collected accounts.
