# The Ranging House

**Type:** Guild hall / professional organization
**Region:** [[Sallowreach]] — outer boardwalk district, southwest edge
**Controlled By:** Hunters Guild (Sallowreach chapter)

## Description

The Ranging House sits at Sallowreach's practical edge: three stories of waxed timber and iron fittings, close enough to the Mire that the bottom boardwalk tends to be damp even in dry weather. It doesn't advertise itself. The sign over the door is the Guild's mark — a knotted cord over crossed arrows — and below it, nothing. Either you know what it is or you have no business here.

Inside: the smell of rendered animal fat, solvent, and river mud that has been tracked over so many thresholds for so long that no amount of cleaning reaches it anymore. The ground floor is a working space — map tables, specimen boards, racks of equipment for hire or purchase, a long counter where contracts are registered and information is bought and sold. The atmosphere is a working library that has been converted to heavy outdoor use. Nobody is precious about the furniture.

The upper floors hold private offices for senior trackers, a decent reference collection on Mire fauna, and the records room — a locked archive of Guild contracts and field reports going back eighty years, organized by a system only the current archivist fully understands. The window at the top of the stairwell faces southwest. On clear evenings, you can see the Mire's canopy pressing against the city's edge in a way that looks patient.

## Key Features

- **The Contract Board** — a large cork board with active contracts: recovery work, pest control, exploration commissions, the occasional bounty. Organized by tier of difficulty, color-coded and ruthlessly updated. A good place to read what the Mire is doing right now.
- **Brin's Table** — a corner of the map room that is, functionally, [[Brin Sevarre]]'s permanent workspace: covered in channel maps annotated in three colors of ink, specimen jars, and a wall of tagged shed skins. She works here when she's not in the field.
- **The Reference Room** — upper floor, unlocked during business hours. Contains the best working library on Mire fauna in the city. Some of the older volumes have notes in margins from trackers who have been dead for fifty years.

## NPCs Present

- **[[Brin Sevarre]]** — senior tracker, Mire expertise lead; Fletcher's first credible ally in Sallowreach; has the amphisbaena's movement patterns mapped and has been waiting for the right person to bring them to

## Tensions & Hooks

The Guild's southwest contracts have been thinning. The deeper Mire channels are getting harder to work — not just because of the growth, but because two hunters didn't come back from past the Double Knot two months ago and nobody has a good explanation. The Guild officially considers the matter under investigation. In practice: people aren't taking contracts that go that deep.

There's a quiet rift inside the Guild itself, and [[Brin Sevarre]] is one side of it: the Guild likes the rare reagents and specimens that come across its market more than it likes asking where they came from. Brin — the chapter's balance-keeper — has been warning for three years that someone is *poaching* the deep Mire to ruin, gutting populations she's spent years stabilizing. She's been thanked and ignored. She vets every new hunter hard now (a hunter she once vouched for died working an extractor's skiff), which is exactly why she puts [[Fletcher]] through a trust-test before she'll share anything that matters. *(See her file: "the Haunt of Thornside.")*

The Guild has a complicated relationship with the Mages Guild via [[Oswin Cray]]: they share perimeter information, but the working relationship has been fraying. Oswin is harder to reach lately. The last joint patrol was three months ago.

## Secrets

- The two hunters who didn't return from past the Double Knot: the Guild officially doesn't know what happened. Brin privately suspects they went after the amphisbaena alone — exactly the kind of glory-or-coin recklessness she vets against — or that the deep channels simply took them, the way they take people. Either way she logged it and won't send anyone after it solo.
- The poaching Brin keeps warning about has a name she can't prove: [[Gault]], a deep-channel extractor who's never in the city. But his pipeline *stages* through Sallowreach — the night berths at [[The Mooring]] — and [[Fletcher]]'s trust-test will turn up the proof inside the city limits. Brin will know the method on sight.
- The Guild's records room contains an old contract — sixty, seventy years ago — for "verification of a deep channel passage to the Double Knot formation." The party signed it, got paid, and the follow-up report is a single line: *"Passage confirmed impassable. Further investigation discontinued."* No names on the follow-up entry. Someone crossed out the original signatories.

## Dungeon Alchemist Notes

Single-story layout for the ground floor is most useful for play: reception counter at the entrance, map tables in the center, specimen boards along the east wall, contract board near the door. The feel is a well-used workshop, not a showroom. Wood floors, mismatched furniture, natural light from windows that look out toward the Mire. For the reference room (upper floor): a smaller space, shelved walls, a reading table, the kind of quiet that suggests people don't bother it. No battle-map encounter expected here — this is a social/information space.
