# The Living Mire

**Tier 1 · Traversal** — *the general swamp. The default backdrop for any scene out on the black water.*

> **This is the one you share with the table.** It's a teaching environment: a clean, reusable card that shows your players what an environment *is* and why it's fun, before you ever spring a fancy one ([[the-lantern-ray-shallows|Lantern-Ray Shallows]], [[the-drowning-edge|Drowning Edge]], [[the-wending|the Wending]]). Use it whenever the party is *just in the swamp* and you want the place to feel alive. It is not a specific location — it's the Mire itself, anywhere.

---

## 📖 Read this to your players (once)

> *"In Daggerheart, the **place** is a character too. The swamp has its own stat block — a Difficulty and a set of moves — same as a monster. It can act against you. But it can also be **used**: cover to hide in, water to vanish under, terrain you can turn on your enemies. I'll usually tell you what the environment is doing and let you push back on it. When I do something and say 'that's the Mire,' that's this card."*

**Three things to remember:**
1. The Mire has a **Difficulty (12)** — that's the number to beat when you fight *the swamp itself*.
2. The Mire can **take a turn** like a monster (the GM moves it).
3. The Mire is a **toolbox** — you can use it too.

---

## Stat Block

**Impulses:** Swallow whatever stops moving · blur every landmark · shelter those who respect it · take back what's taken.

**Difficulty:** 12 *(the default number for any roll to fight the terrain: sprint across it, keep your footing, find the way, haul someone free)*

**Potential Adversaries:** [[mire-bestiary#Mire Gar|Mire Gar]] pack · [[mire-bestiary#Bloodmidge Swarm|Bloodmidge Swarm]] · glass snake (SRD) · a [[sallowreach-roster#Tessak Diver|Tessak]] in the water. *The Mire rarely needs an adversary — the water and the dark are the threat.*

---

## Features

Each one teaches a different way an environment can play. Use whichever fits the beat.

**🟫 The Mire Underfoot — Passive** *(the baseline)*
There is no solid ground — only muck, roots, and waist-deep black water. Any roll to **move fast, chase, or fight on your feet** out here is made against **Difficulty 12**. On a failure, you don't necessarily fall — but the swamp has you a beat longer than you wanted. *Lesson for the table: the place has a number, and it's working on you the whole time.*

**🌿 Reeds, Mist, and Black Water — Passive** *(the toolbox — point this one out loud)*
The Mire hides things, including the party. A PC who stops and goes still can roll **Agility (Diff 12)** to become **Hidden** in the reeds or mist — rolls against a Hidden creature have **disadvantage**, lost the moment they're seen, move into the open, or attack. A PC can also **drop under the black water** to break line of sight entirely. *Lesson: an environment isn't only a hazard. It's yours to use.*

**🕳️ The Ground Gives — Action** *(the Mire takes a turn)*
What looked like ground is bog. The targeted PC makes an **Agility reaction roll (Diff 12)** or sinks to the waist — **Restrained** (can't move, can still act) until they or an ally spends an action to haul free (**Strength, Diff 12**). *Lesson: the GM can spotlight the environment like an adversary, on the GM's turn.*

**🩸 Blood in the Water — Reaction** *(the Mire answers you)*
When a PC spills blood, makes a loud splash, or **rolls with Fear**, the swamp responds: bloodmidges boil up, or something downstream wakes and turns toward the noise. The GM may bring an adversary to **Close range**, or make every nearby roll **Vulnerable** to the bites. *Lesson: environments react to what you do — the swamp keeps score.*

**🌫️ The Mire Closes In — Action · Fear** *(the escalation, and the Fear lesson)*
**Spend a Fear.** The mist drops to arm's length and the water climbs. Sightlines collapse: any attack beyond **Very Close has disadvantage**, and **one PC is cut off** from the group, alone in the white. *Lesson: the GM's scariest moves cost a resource — and the table can watch that Fear get spent, so they feel the turn coming.*

## How to introduce environments in Session 1

You don't need all five. Drop **two** during the swim/bank fight and *name them as the Mire's moves* so the concept lands:

1. **Show the toolbox first.** When a PC wants to escape the gar, offer **Reeds, Mist, and Black Water** — "you can go under and they lose you; roll Agility." Let a player *win* using the environment. That's the hook.
2. **Then show it bites back.** Later, spend a Fear on **The Mire Closes In** and say out loud *"that's a Fear — the swamp is taking a turn."* Now they get the whole loop: the place helps, the place hurts, and the Fear is the dial.

That's the entire lesson, taught in two moves, mid-fight. After tonight, the fancy environments will just feel like *this, with a theme.*

---

## Temporary clocks live here too

Any **Countdown** you spin up in the swamp belongs to this card — like the **Breath Countdown (3)** for a PC dragged under (ticks down each action; at 0 they mark Stress per action). A clock can be printed on an environment or improvised on the spot. Both are "the environment acting." *(Official parallel: the SRD's own "Raging River" environment runs exactly this way — a passive that starts a Stress clock, an undertow action, and a Fear reaction.)*
