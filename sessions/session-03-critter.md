# Session 3 — The Critter *(the "Haunt of Thornside")*

*The "demon" on the Thornside boards is a trafficked, terrified **owlbear cub.** Not a monster — a lost, caged-scarred baby that screams like a child because that's the sound owlbears make. Fletcher baited a **smoked-eel trap** (from Corentin the eel-smoker). The tension isn't "does he catch it" — it's **"what does he do when he sees what he caught."** Related: [[session-03-run-sheet]] · [[brin-sevarre]] · [[gault]].*

---

## 🦉🐻 What it is
A trafficked **owlbear cub** — beak, downy feathers over fur, paws too big for its body, talons that scored the pilings with marks *too large for the tracks.* Owl-eyes that catch the lantern like two coins. Escaped a smuggler's crate at the [[The Mooring|Mooring]]; starving, cage-scarred, a forest baby loose in a swamp that's killing it. The neighborhood's "demon" is a **lost, terrified infant.**

---

## The Beats

### 1 · Setting the trap 🎲
Fletcher rigs a baited snare / drop-box with the smoked eel, down under the boards.
- **Set it well** — *Finesse/Knowledge + Eye for Danger/Swamp Specialist, Diff 12.* Hit: good trap, well-placed. Strong hit: set to catch *without* maiming (soft snare, not a gutting one — matters later).
- **The wait** — night under the city, black water a foot below, the eel doing its work. Let it be quiet and tense.

### 2 · It comes *(read-aloud)*
> The eel does what fresh Mire-fish never could. Somewhere in the dark, claws on wet wood — a *climber's* rhythm, coming down the pilings headfirst. Two coins of light float toward the bait, low and careful. And that sound, the one that emptied a neighborhood: a thin, rising cry, almost a child, almost a question. It's hungry — so hungry it's forgotten to be careful. It goes for the eel, and the trap takes it.

### 3 · What he caught *(the thesis — let it land)*
> It thrashes, and for one heartbeat the monster is real — talons, the coin-eyes wild, that awful screech. And then it tires, because it's got nothing left, and in the lantern-light it just… falls apart into what it is. A cub. Downy feathers over soft fur, a beak too big for its face, paws it hasn't grown into. One claw clipped to a stub. Sores worn into its neck the exact shape of a cage. Ribs you can count. It's not fighting you anymore. It's just crying — that wailing, child's-cry screech — and shaking, and waiting for the thing it's learned always comes next.

### 4 · The choice *(the real test — low combat)* 🎲
**It's caught. Now what?** This is what Brin's really judging — the *manner.*
- **Calm it** — *Presence/Instinct 12* → a gentle approach settles the cub *faster* than any cage. The best answer; reward kindness.
- **Cage/secure it** — *Agility/Finesse 12* → contain it without it hurting itself or him (it'll panic).
- **A clean, merciful kill** — if he decides it can't be loosed; done without cruelty. Brin respects a necessary, clean cull — but it's *killing a baby.*
- **What NOT to do:** a damage race. It has almost no fight in it.

### 5 · 🪶 The twist — *the mother*
An owlbear cub didn't wander here. It was **taken** — which means somewhere there's a mother owlbear, and owlbears don't forget their young.
- **The grief:** the smugglers already killed her for the cub (a pelt, parts at the Mooring). The cub is all that's left.
- **The clock:** she's *out there, and coming* — a Tier 2 storm of talons and grief following her stolen baby's scent toward the city. A future encounter that's really about *giving the cub back.*

### 6 · Aftermath — the leads he earns
- **Chase the cause** *(what a balance-keeper does)* — the crate → **Mooring night-berths → [[Gault]]'s** trafficking operation. Following the pipeline (not just taking the kill) is what wins **[[Brin Sevarre|Brin]]** for keeps.
- **The "treasure" is the cub itself.** They'll *want* to keep it — let them wrestle with it (raise it? rehome it? find its mother?). A living reminder of what the trade costs.
- **Brin's read:** how Fletcher handled a frightened baby *is* the test → her trust + the snake intel.

## 🦉🐻 Stat Block — *Owlbear Cub, Tier 1 Skulk* (FreshCutGrass homebrew)
*A downy, beaked cub, talons too big for it, cowering where the trap caught it.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Flee, cower, forage, cry for its mother
**Difficulty:** 12 | **Thresholds:** 5 / 9 | **HP:** 4 | **Stress:** 2
**ATK:** +1 | **Panicked Talons:** Melee | **1d6 phys** *(only cornered, terrified — it doesn't want to)*

**FEATURES**
- **A Cub, Not a Beast** – *Passive:* It's a baby. Tires fast, cowers. A gentle approach (**Presence/Instinct 12**) calms it faster than any cage — reward kindness over force.
- **Prey, Not Predator** – *Passive:* Won't make a standard attack while an escape route exists.
- **Scrabble** – *Passive:* Climbs any surface; rolls to strike it while it bolts have disadvantage.
- **Bolt** – *Fear Move:* Vanishes up a piling / under the boards — **unless anticipated.** *(Fletcher's baited trap pre-empts this — reward the smart play.)*
- **Screech Like a Child** – *Passive:* Its cry is wrenchingly human. A PC about to strike the cornered cub must first succeed on a roll or *hesitate.*

*Frail, fleeing baby. **Not a damage race.** HP 4 means one clean hit could end it — so keeping it alive means* not *just attacking.*

## The one line to remember
> The monster in the dark was **frightened, trafficked, and far from home** — a baby crying for its mother. That's the whole campaign, in Fletcher's hands, on a boardwalk. Let it *cost* him something to see it. 🦉🐻
