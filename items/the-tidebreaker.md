# The Tidebreaker

**Type:** Artifact (appears mundane)
**Current Location:** [[Marcel Dray]]'s private quarters, displayed as a curiosity
**Origin:** Carved by [[The Tree Spirit]] as a gift for [[The Mangrove Spirit]]

*A fishing leister that breaks curses. Named in old accounts, dismissed as fairytale by the one man who owned the proof. The single object the whole curse-path runs toward — and it's sitting in a loan shark's parlor, mistaken for a pretty antique.*

---

## What Anyone Sees

To anyone without context: **a beautifully crafted but otherwise ordinary fishing spear.** A leister — a slim, barbed fishing spear — exceptionally balanced, clearly handmade, clearly old. The grain shifts between amber, deep green, and near-black depending on the angle of light, in patterns and colors no living tree produces. The craftsmanship is beyond anything a human woodworker could explain.

Show it to a local woodworker and they'll tell you the technique is unrecognizable and the wood is impossible — and they'll be unsettled in a way they can't put into words. Show it to a collector and they'll see a curiosity worth a great deal of money. **Neither sees what it is.** [[Marcel Dray]] finds it genuinely pleasing to look at and keeps it out where he can. He has no idea what he's holding.

## What the Documentation Says *(the lore — and where the party gets it)*

The truth of the Tidebreaker isn't obvious in the object. It's in **the papers** — a chain of old accounts, provenance notes, and transcribed oral histories that [[Caspar Renne]] inherited with the museum and has accumulated over the years. According to them:

- It was **carved by one great spirit as a gift for another** — the [[The Tree Spirit|Tree Spirit]] for the [[The Mangrove Spirit|Mangrove Spirit]] — and the gift was **never delivered.**
- It is a **bridge between worlds.** It carries the Tree Spirit's own boundary-crossing nature, the same power that charges the Mire's black water.
- In the right hands, it **breaks curses** — it can sever the spirit-magic that holds a soul where it shouldn't be.

> **The irony that drives the Caspar encounter:** Caspar *has* all of this in his archive and **thinks it's fairytales.** He deals in what objects are *worth,* not in swamp-grandmother stories about spirit-gifts — which is exactly why he fenced "a very pretty old fishing spear" for a fortune without a second thought. The party gets the cure-knowledge from **his documents,** not his belief. The expert handing them the answer doesn't trust his own files. *(See [[session-03-caspar-encounter]] and [[caspar-renne]].)*

## What It Actually Does

The papers are right. The Tidebreaker is a powerful magical artifact, and what it does is **break curses.**

- **[[Isotta]]'s ghost-condition and [[the-witch|the Witch]]'s clank-curse are within its power.** Once the party has the spear, those curses can be broken — clean, earned, personal payoffs for two PCs who'll have crossed the whole city to get it. *(GM: stage these as weighty scenes, not a quiet stat change.)*
- **The tether is the exception — the final boss of curses.** The party isn't simply *cursed*; they're *caught,* tangled directly in the great binding at the city's heart (see [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|The Turn]]). The Tidebreaker can't just snip that. Lifting the tether means resolving the binding itself — freeing or reuniting the spirits — which is the finale, and where the cost to Sallowreach lives. ("Taste now, cure at the finale.")

It is also, still, **a bridge between worlds.** Carved as a gift for the Mangrove Spirit, it opens the conversation with the spirits that the central resolution requires, and in the deep Mire it becomes a **compass** toward the one it was made for (below).

## How the Party Learns About It

A chain. Nobody hands them the whole answer — each link knows one piece.

### Step 1 — Delphine *(the shape of the problem)*
[[Delphine]] examines the tether and can't cure it — flat, no false hope. But she names the shape: this is **spirit-water magic, old and ancient,** past her shelf. *"You need something that speaks the spirit's language back at it. Not a spell. Not medicine."* She points them at **Caspar Renne** — the one man who studies what the Mire has made. *(See [[session-03-run-sheet]] for the hand-off card.)*

### Step 2 — Caspar Renne *(the lore, the name, and the truth about how it got away)*
**This is now the [[session-03-caspar-encounter]] — "The Counterfeit Corpse."** The soft, slow, trust-gated version is retired. In the live encounter the party breaks in on a barricaded, terrified Caspar, survives his faked-death trap and the [[caspar-renne#The Warden|Warden]], and earns the **whole truth at once:**
- The Tidebreaker's nature and significance — **from his documentation** (he's skeptical; the papers aren't).
- That **Marcel Dray** has it.
- That it's Marcel's *because of Caspar* — he tried to fence it out-of-city, cut Marcel out of the deal, and Marcel took it as the penalty. No omission left; the encounter strips him bare.

### Step 3 — Marcel Dray *(the deal)*
See [[Marcel Dray]] for the full deal. The short version: Marcel will trade the spear for [[Oswin Cray|the wizard]]'s disgrace and removal from Guild protection. The party has to work out what that means — and what it costs — before or after they commit.

---

## After Acquiring the Spear

For **Isotta and the Witch, this is the moment their curses can finally be broken** — the artifact does what the artifact does. Give them the scenes; they earned them.

But for the **tether** — the thing all four share — it stays quiet. No hum, no glow. Because the tether isn't a curse the spear can cut; it's the great binding itself, and the spear's role there isn't to *break* it but to *reach* it. They're holding the key to a conversation they don't yet know how to have. The natural next move is back to Caspar.

### Step 4 — Back to Caspar
He sees it and goes very still — that genuine-interest stillness. *"May I?"* He holds it a long moment. Then: *"This won't lift what's tying you here. I don't mean it's powerless — gods, look at it — I mean the thing holding you isn't a curse you *cut.* It was made as a gift. A gift needs someone to receive it."* *(The cynic, finally outvoted by his own evidence.)*

He knows where the Mangrove Spirit is — roughly. Southwest Deep Mire, the direction it's drifted toward the city for two hundred years (his records; confirmed by [[the-first-maintainers-journal|Vane's journal]]). He gives them the last documented sighting, the heading, and the warning that past the **Wending** the usual navigation stops working. What he *cannot* tell them is what happens when the spirit receives it — that part was never written down. *"I don't know what it does when it reaches the one it was made for. I know it was meant to. I imagine that's the point."*

**The scene that belongs to him:** the man who sold a miracle as junk gets thirty seconds holding it, understanding at last, before the party carries it into the Deep Mire and maybe never comes back. He'll hold it a beat too long before he hands it over. He won't comment. Neither should you.

### The Spear in the Mire
Past the Wending the balance shifts — it wants to be held pointed southwest, the grain drifting slowly in the direction of travel when aimed true and stilling when it isn't. A **compass seeking its destination.** It won't lead them around obstacles or protect them. It simply knows where it's going, and if they follow it, so will they.

## GM Notes
- **The cure-knowledge now comes from the encounter, not slow trust-gating.** [[session-03-caspar-encounter]] gives the party Marcel's name and the spear's nature in one night. The "earn it over Act 1" gate is gone — the *gate* is now getting at **Marcel.**
- **Caspar believes none of the magic** until his own evidence and four cursed people wear him down. Play him as a fence who keeps catching himself taking the fairytale seriously and hating it.
- **Marcel has no idea what he owns.** When the party finally reaches the Pale Room and sees a priceless world-key kept as a parlor curiosity, that should land hard.
- When Marcel mentions "someone else has been asking about it," the click is Caspar — retroactively explaining his terror.
