## ⚡ THE WHOLE NIGHT IN 30 SECONDS

The party survived the wreck and woke up **trapped in Sallowreach** (the curse = they can't leave the Mire). Tonight does **three things, then opens up:**

1. **The curse lands** — they wake warm, then remember; the healer Delphine names it (*"a knot, not a wound"*) and can't fix it.
2. **The Empire knocks** — Caius's aide delivers a dinner invitation for tonight (warm, perfect, one-degree *off*).
3. **The city opens four doors** — they split up, each going to the place they came here for:

| PC | goes to | meets | who is… |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fletcher** (frog ranger, hunting the snake) | the **Hunters' Guild** | **Brin** | a hunter who won't trust him yet |
| **Leavy** (the Witch, stuck in a built body) | the **garden** | **Orna** | a genius dissolving into the swamp |
| **Isotta** (the bloodhunter who *is* a ghost) | the **coffee house** | **Vael** | the one who can *see* what she is |
| **Tenson** (the auditor who wants out) | the **drill-yard** | **Sable** | the happiest guard in the worst posting |

Then they **come back together** (Beat 4 / the dinner). **Town stays warm — no fights** unless they start one.

> **🚫 THE ONE HARD RULE:** No NPC ever connects the curse to the weekly Celebration. They can say *"you're bound"* — they can **never** say what binds. The players figure that out weeks from now, not tonight.

## 🎭 WING ANY NPC IN 10 SECONDS

*Pick the "play them like," do the body thing, steal a line. That's the whole character.*

- **Delphine** (healer) — *play her like* a no-nonsense grandmother who's seen everything. *Body:* gets too close, goes quiet. *Say:* "Sit. You're carrying it in your shoulders." / "You're not sick. It's a knot." / "I can't lift it. But I know who might."
- **Caius** (imperial liaison) — *play him like* the most charming man at the party who's also appraising the silverware. *Body:* eye contact just a half-second too long. *Say:* "None of the reports had survivors. Remarkable." / "This city deserves *better.*" / "Would you put it in writing?"
- **Brin** (hunter) — *play her like* a sniper who's tired of burying reckless people. *Body:* answers your question with three sharper ones. *Say:* "Two-headed pattern. Southwest. Three months old." / "What I don't have is a reason to trust you." / "How you do it is the whole answer."
- **Orna** (alchemist) — *play her like* a brilliant professor three thoughts ahead, mid-dissolve into a swamp-thing, and *delighted.* *Body:* holding a beaker she forgot about. *Say:* "Sit, sit — no, not *there,* that's alive." / "You want the impossible done *to* you. Different shelf." / "You're not sick. You're *bound.* You want a—" *(wanders off)*
- **Vael** (coffee house owner, one white eye that sees spirits) — *play them like* still, deep water that misses nothing. *Body:* the white eye drifts past your shoulder; the brown one locks on. *Say:* "You're not quite *that* either, are you. Sit. Drink." / "Someone *did this to you,* child. That's a curse, not a haunting." / "You don't exorcise it. You *talk* to it."
- **Sable** (guard sergeant) — *play them like* a big, sweaty golden retriever who's secretly carrying everyone's worry. *Body:* tosses you something; calls you *cher* and *brain-boy.* *Say:* "From the Citadel! Do a lap with my lot, then I'll tell you anything." / "That's a brain job, cher." / *(quiet)* "Somebody's got to keep it light."

> **The thread tying the whole night together:** three of them — Delphine, Orna, Vael — independently use the same word for the curse: **bound** / *knot* / *tied.* Don't point it out. Let a **player** notice at the end (Beat 4).

## ▶️ BEAT 1 — THE FIRST MORNING *(the curse lands)*

**☀️ THE WELCOME THEY ALMOST MISSED** *(open here — read aloud; the city loving them first)*

> You wake up **dry,** and warm, wrapped in a blanket someone tucked round you in the night — and the first thing you understand is that you are not alone. Light through the shutters. Low voices. And the smell — gods, the smell — woodsmoke and frying spice and something sweet, right up close.
> You come out onto Remy's little boardwalk landing and stop dead. Because overnight, somebody made this a *party.* A long table's been dragged out — mismatched, every plank of it borrowed — and it is *covered:* a black pot of gumbo still steaming, fritters that glow faintly gold, hot torn bread, a whole smoked fish, hush cakes stacked crooked. Eight, ten neighbors you have never met are crowded onto the boards in their good clothes, a little bleary — because it's barely dawn and they were up half the night making this. And when they see you, they *cheer.* Quiet, so as not to wake the babies. But they *mean* it.

> [Remy — beaming, up all night, wonderful] *"There they* are! *Right, I know — you missed it. The whole city does the welcome proper: the lanterns, the music, the lot. And you lot came in at the absolute fag-end of the Celebration looking half-drowned, and I thought — no. No, that won't do. You don't get* no *welcome. Not on my boards. So we threw you a little one."*

> An old woman — *(grab a name: Cosette? Marguerite?)* — takes your hand and ties a braid of dried Mire-grass round your wrist, murmuring something old you don't quite catch. A kid, shoved forward by their mother, holds up a drawing: four stick figures and a wobbly lantern. *"That's you."*
> And Remy presses a small lit lantern into each of your hands — blue-green, the *exact* soft glow of the things that lit the water while you were drowning. *"Now — normally we float these* out. *Out to the Mire. Open door, all that. But you're already* in, *aren't you."* [something flickers across his face, gone] *"So this morning we float 'em the* other *way. Toward the house. To call you the rest of the way home."*
> You set your lantern on the rail, facing in. The neighbors hum a few bars of something — old words, learned by the sound and not the meaning — and for just a second the whole little gathering glows.
> [Remy, quiet, just to you] *"...Welcome to Sallowreach. I know it's not how you meant to arrive. But you're here now. And here — we feed our own."*

*GM — hand out the boon: a shared meal here **clears 1d4 Stress or grants a Hope** (their pick), no strings. Let it land big — this warmth is the investment everything later spends. **Two seeds, don't underline:** (1) the lanterns are the same shape and color as the rays that saved them — a PC may clock it; (2) Remy floats them* inward *because "the swamp already let you in" — kindly meant; it lands very differently once they understand the tether. The hummed words are a fragment of the Celebration's welcome verse. Plant; never explain.*

**Then it lands** *(the turn — once the warmth has had its moment):*
> And it's perfect. The warmest you've felt in longer than you'd care to admit — a bowl in your hands, a braid on your wrist, a stranger's lantern glowing in front of you. And then it comes back. The water. The cold. The thing that closed around something behind your ribs in the dark and did not let go. *Oh. Right.* You can't leave. You are sitting in the kindest morning of your life, inside a cage.

**If a player tests the edge** (steps toward open water):

> Sun's up, head's clear, no shock to blame it on — and you take one step toward *away,* and it's still there. A wall that isn't a wall. Resistance, then something *worse* than pain, a full-body *no.* And there's no mark on you. Nothing to show anyone. Just you and the open water and the certainty it will not let you go.

**Then Remy walks them to Delphine** *(this is where they learn what the curse is):*

> Stilt-house in the Thornside quarter, door unlocked, herbs from every rafter. A woman in her sixties, grey hair cropped close — and before you say a word she points at a stool.
> "Sit. All of you — sit down. You're carrying it up in your shoulders, and that's doing you no good."
> She gets close. Quiet. Two fingers on the nearest wrist — and for half a second her eyes slide off to the left, to *nothing,* and she goes still. Then she's back.
> "Hm. You're not sick. Nothing torn, nothing to flush. It's a **knot.** You went under, and the water tied you to this place. ...It feels like—"
> *(she stops herself)* "No. I'm not cutting at a thing I can't see both ends of. But I know what it *is,* and I know who might know more — **Caspar Renne,** the museum, old quarter. Tell him I sent you." *(and softer)* "...Come back if it changes. Come back if it doesn't and you just want to sit somewhere that isn't trying to kill you."

*GM, that's the whole beat:* **Reveal** → it's a relational knot, not a wound; **no marks**; she can't cure it; go to **Caspar.** **Withhold** → she stops at *"it feels like—"* (never finish it); and whatever the touch showed her stays hidden. If a player catches the eye-drift: *"Old habit. The water's loud today."*

### 🔮 DELPHINE, ONE AT A TIME *(private divinations — the backstory spotlights)*

She won't do it in a crowd. **"One at a time. Out back. This isn't a parlor trick and I'll not have it gawked at."** She takes each PC alone — a hand, or something they carry — and the charged water shows her something. *It comes differently for each, because each of them is different underneath.* Run each as a 3–5 min two-hander. **The boxed text is your hook; the `→` question is what makes the *player* talk.** *(She hates being called an oracle — she downplays every word of it.)*

*GM — the buried thread: under **every** reading she also catches the same flicker — cold black water, two points of light drifting toward each other, a long-held chord resolving. **She says nothing.** Eyes drift left a half-second; she carries on. That's the whole campaign, and she's not ready to hand it over. It's also why she keeps reaching for the same word with all four: **bound.** Never let her connect it to the Celebration.*

**🐸 FLETCHER — "The Tasting"** *(she takes his hands, or the shed-skin journal, and tastes what he's chased)*
> She closes her hands over yours and goes still. "...Iron. And something gone bad in water. *Eggshell.*" Her jaw tightens. "Oh, child. That's no *job* you're on, whatever you call it." Her eyes open. "There's a cold place — southwest, deep, past where anybody sensible goes. That's where it's gone to ground. And the water out that way is *wrong,* in a way I've no word for."
> *Curse:* "You're tied to this swamp now, same as the rest. I can't cut it. Caspar Renne might know who can."
> **→ Ask Fletcher:** *"What did you lose that you crossed the world to set right — and what's the word you keep refusing to say about it?"*

**🍃 LEAVY — "The Empty Room"** *(she reaches for the vision the usual way — and almost nothing comes)*
> She lays a palm flat over your chest and waits for the water to show her something — and her brow creases, because it *won't.* "Huh." She shifts her hand. Tries again. "...There's a person in here. There *was.* I can feel the shape of where they stood." Her dark eyes find yours, not unkind. "You're wearing a face that isn't the one you were born in. I'll not ask. But whoever *made* this—" she turns something over "—their hand felt strange to me. *Close.* Like I'd felt it before."
> *Curse:* "You're not sick, child. You're *bound.* Two knots, even — a new one to this water, and an older one underneath I'll not pretend to read. No tea fixes that. Wrong department. ...Caspar. Or the alchemist out at the garden."
> **→ Ask Leavy:** *"You grieve someone you can't remember — yourself. What's one thing your body still does that you know belonged to* them?"
> *(GM, whisper-quiet: the maker's hand felt familiar because it was Leavy's own. Don't reveal — just let the wrongness sit.)*

**🐈‍⬛ ISOTTA — "The Flood"** *(she takes Isotta's hand and nearly lets go — not from fear, from volume)*
> The instant your skin touches hers she sucks in a breath and her grip *tightens.* "*Oh.*" The light in the room seems to lean. "You are the loudest thing that has ever sat in that chair." She holds on, riding it. "I see a table. A circle of faces around it — people who knew you, people who *did* something — and one of them looked *away.*" Her voice drops. "You came back from somewhere you weren't meant to. And you didn't come *all* the way."
> *Curse:* "You're tied here like the others — but you were half-tied long before you washed up. Someone put a knot in *you,* a long time ago. Caspar studies what this water does — start there. ...And there's a man with a coffee house you ought to meet."
> **→ Ask Isotta:** *"That circle of faces — the famous family you grew up around. Who looked away, and what do they owe you?"*

**🪓 TENSON — "The Ledger"** *(she takes his hand expecting the same knot — and finds it clean)*
> She holds your wrist, waiting for the wrongness — and instead her face does something almost like surprise. "Well, now. Isn't *that* something." She turns your hand over. "The others — there's a knot tied in each of them, old and ugly. You? Just the tether. Nothing underneath it. You came here *whole* — the only one who did." A pause, gentler. "And you're pulled two ways. Like a man standing in two doorways at once. One's a shield. The other's quiet, and full of numbers, and you want it so bad you're ashamed of it." Then her eyes drift left, and stay a beat too long. "...There's a letter on the water. Coming to you. From someone who's been told you're dead and won't *have* it."
> *Curse:* "The tether I can't lift, same as the rest. But you've the least to undo of any of them. Hold onto that."
> **→ Ask Tenson:** *"You were built to be the muscle, and you want a desk and a ledger. Who decided you were only good for the one — and what would it cost to prove them wrong?"*

*↪ If you already ran Delphine last session, this is just the curse settling in daylight — same takeaways.*

## ▶️ BEAT 2 — THE INVITATION *(the Empire knocks)*

> Someone cuts neat through the crowd toward you — and you clock him before he arrives, because he's *wrong* for here: pressed, clean, bone-dry in all this wet heat. Imperial. A little bow that costs him nothing. "Liaison Maren sends his compliments. And this." A heavy cream card, sealed in red wax. He doesn't wait for an answer.
> *(read the card aloud — heavy cream paper, an even, confident hand, imperial wax:)*
> ***To those the Mire gave back —***
> ***I hope this finds you warm, dry, and beginning to mend. Word reached me this morning that the black water returned what it almost never returns — that four souls walked off it who, by every account I have read, should not have. I have served this region some years and read more of these notices than I would wish; not one, until today, ended in survivors. That you are reading this at all is close to remarkable.***
> ***Permit me to welcome you properly. The city will tell you, rightly, that it looks after its own — allow the Empire to look after its guests. Dine with me this evening at the Attaché Quarters, on the inner boardwalk. Nothing elaborate; only the best table this extraordinary city can set, and the quiet of a room where, like you, no one quite expected to find themselves in Sallowreach. Come as you are. Come hungry. Bring whatever questions the morning has left you — I have a great many of my own, and far better wine than answers.***
> ***With sincere regard, and gladder than I can say that you are here to receive it,***
> ***Caius Maren — Imperial Cultural Liaison, Sallowreach***
> ***P.S. — I'm told you owe your lives in no small part to a local boatman. Bring him, if he's willing. I should very much like to shake the hand of the man who beat the Mire at its own game.***
> Warm. Gracious. Perfect, really. And something about it sits a half-degree off — like a painting hung slightly crooked. Then the feeling's gone.

*GM:* he **arranged the shipwreck** and didn't expect survivors; he wants their account of it *in writing.* **None of that shows tonight** — he's charming and genuinely fond of this city. The invite hangs over the whole day. Run the dinner tonight (Beat 5) **or** end on it as a cliffhanger.

## ▶️ BEAT 3 — THE FOUR DOORS *(the sandbox — the body of the night)*

> *(to the table)* You've got a day, and a city that already seems to have decided it likes you. Boardwalks stacked into the smoke, punts sliding the canals below, green light off the water, cook-smoke you can *taste,* and everyone you pass nodding like — *oh, you're the ones the swamp gave back.* Where do you go first?

**Winging a split party — 3 rules:**
1. Give each PC a tight scene, then **cut on a hook** (a question asked, a test offered) and jump to the next. Momentum is in the *cutting.*
2. *"Meanwhile, across the city…"* — hard cuts feel cinematic, not messy.
3. Between scenes, pass through the market — hand them a free bowl of gumbo: **a shared meal clears 1d4 Stress or grants a Hope.** (The warmth is real. Give it freely.)

---

### 🏹 DOOR 1 — Fletcher → Brin (the Hunters' Guild)

> A three-story hall at the city's edge. The sign is just the Guild's mark — a knotted cord over crossed arrows — and below it, nothing. Inside hits you: rendered fat, solvent, river-mud worked so deep in the threshold no scrubbing reaches it. Map tables, specimen boards, a wall of tagged shed skins. In the corner, a woman buried in channel-maps inked three colors deep, who hasn't looked up.
> You reach for your castings — and she's already dragging her own map over to meet you. "Two-headed pattern. Southwest junction. Three months old, give or take." *(now she looks at you, not the skin)* "I've got four more of these in a drawer. What I don't have is a reason to trust the man holding the fifth. Convince me you want this thing dead because it's killing what shouldn't be — and not for how it'll feel to be the one who did it."

- **What she wants:** to read what kind of hunter he is before she trusts him with anything that matters.
- **The gate (don't give it free):** she won't hand over the deep-channel intel. She offers a **test** — *"There's a haunt loose in the Thornside boardwalks. City wants it dead. Go handle it. How you do it is the whole answer."* *(Set it up tonight; run it next session. ↪ It's a frightened, trafficked animal, not a monster — that's the lesson, but you don't need it tonight.)*
- **Wonder/pathos:** the frog who nearly drowned checked for his *journal* before himself. The trail outlived the drowning.
- **Five things you notice:** a contract board reading the Mire's mood like a pulse · a wall of shed skins, four of them two-headed · a southwest window where the canopy presses the city *patiently* · margin notes from trackers fifty years dead · Brin counting in a tally-book — *populations,* not kills.

### 🌿 DOOR 2 — Leavy → Orna (the garden, the Verdant Margin)

> A garden, somehow — out on the literal edge, where the city's last dirt meets black water. Three climbing platforms, turned earth, something sweet that changes as you move. Down a tide-wet ladder, plants grow in directions the light doesn't explain, and three beds *glow* faint blue-green even now. At a bench: a tall broad firbolg, soft-eyed — except a pale barnacle-crust is creeping up one forearm and a fringe of green grows where hair should be, and they don't seem to have noticed or to care. Something bubbles in a hand they've forgotten they're holding.
> "You're after something specific. Everyone who finds the Margin is." *(a swirl, a sniff, the beaker set down wrong)* "Most want the impossible reagents. Those I have." *(now they look at you, and the wrong light moves in their eyes)* "You, though — you want the impossible done *to* you. Different shelf. Sit, sit — no, not *there,* that's alive."

- **What she wants:** a reagent she's run out of — and, vaguely, someone who can keep up.
- **The gate:** the quest **"Persona Non Grata"** — fetch **gloamcap distillate** from the Alchemists' Consortium, where Orna is *banned.* *"Oh — you'll have to fetch it. They won't see me. Long story. Boring story. Tell them it's for the garden."*
- **The breadcrumb (the gold):** somewhere in the scene, a careless glance — **"You're not sick. You're *bound.* No elixir touches that — wrong department entirely. You want a—"** and she trails off, gone. *Never finish it.* (It points at a thing that breaks bindings — for much later.)
- **The mirror that aches:** Orna is throwing her *self* away on purpose, one experiment at a time; Leavy had her self *stolen* and wants it back. Same axis, opposite directions.
- **Five things you notice:** sweet-then-fermenting air shifting bed to bed · the bench row of half-finished doses Orna keeps drinking · plants growing wrong toward the light · **three beds that pulse, *nearly* in time with the Celebration drums, at night** · Orna quietly moving her prized specimens *uphill,* away from the water, and refusing to say why.

### ☕ DOOR 3 — Isotta → Vael (the coffee house, the Still Cup)

> The name's carved into the door-frame, not hung above it. Inside: dark wood, good light, a *quiet* that's settled, not empty. Best coffee in a city with opinions about coffee. The owner clocks you instantly — tall, fifties, an old grey tattoo down one whole sleeve. One eye dark, quick, warm. The other **milky-white and very still** — and it drifts, just slightly, to a point past your shoulder while the brown one stays on you.
> *(setting a cup down, no ceremony)* "I've served a few like you. Died in the transformation, came back still attached. The Order says that's not supposed to survive." *(a beat)* "But you're not quite *that* either, are you. Sit. Drink." *(a nod to the corner, where an ancient man lays out dominoes, alone, smiling at nobody)* "There's a man over there I think you ought to meet. And then you and I are going to talk about what you actually *are.*"

- **What they want:** to understand what's straining the veil — and, seeing kin walk in, to take Isotta in and teach her.
- **The read that reframes her whole life:** once the white eye has her — **"The ones I see came across on their own. You didn't. Someone *did this to you,* child. That's not a haunting. That's a *curse.*"** The Order trained her to hunt what she is; here's a master telling her she was the *victim.* Let it be relief and grief at once.
- **The gate:** the quest **"Old Basile"** — the old man in the corner is a small spirit who's lived as human for generations, and he's **slipping** (no reflection in the urn some mornings; calls a regular by a dead grandmother's name; sets out a domino that isn't there). Helping him teaches Isotta how a spirit is *anchored* — the exact thing her own cure needs.
- **Five things you notice:** Vael making every cup like it matters · twelve tables, half-full, warm-but-never-loud · a sheltered back corner where serious things get said · Thursday's bloodhunter regulars trading Mire lore · Basile laying out a domino that isn't there — and Isotta the only one who sees it.

### ⚔️ DOOR 4 — Tenson → Sable (the drill-yard, the Drillfield)

> The biggest flat deck in the district — salt-grey planks re-leveled so often no two agree where the horizon is. Sweat, rope, wet wood, and the mineral bite of salt, because the *wall's* right there: two boardwalks out, grey and crusted along the Mire's edge, and you can watch the green pressing against it. The recruits drill with their backs to it. On purpose, you think. You come in with your seal out — and the big sergeant takes one look and grins like you handed him a gift.
> "From the *Citadel!* Look at you. We don't get many who'll still get in the mud." *(a practice stave spins at your chest)* "Tell you what — do the morning drill with my lot, then I'll tell you anything you want. Deal?" *(already turning back, delighted)* "Don't *answer.* Just catch."

- **What they want:** for Tenson to drop the auditor's armor and just be a person on the field. The seal opens nothing here; cleverness opens nothing. Heart does.
- **The gate:** the quest **"Sweat First"** — a green recruit, **Perrin,** keeps getting hurt and is washing out; Sable won't give up on them. Tenson's buried *sports-medicine* eye catches what no one else has (bad form / old injury / overtraining) and saves them. *That* wins Sable for keeps. *(The records — and a memo with Caius's name on it that Sable filed without reading — come **after** the friendship, not tonight.)*
- **The question Tenson didn't expect:** Sable is the future Tenson is terrified of — a life of pure brawn — and Sable is *thriving.* Play it as a quiet challenge, never mockery. The steel under the sunshine, if you want it: *"I keep it light for the lot. Somebody's got to. But I've carried furniture out of more homes this year than last. You do the sums, brain-boy."*
- **Five things you notice:** planks that can't agree on the horizon · more *rescue* gear racked than weapons · recruits drilling backs-to-the-wall · the southwest horizon reading as *encroaching green* · the morning drill — brutal, and somehow the best hour of everyone's day.

## ▶️ BEAT 4 — THE ECHO *(bring them back together)*

> *(end of the day — Remy's table, a bench, the walk to dinner. Let them swap what they learned, and let it land:)*
> The healer said it was a **knot.** Said you weren't hurt — you were *tied.*
> The alchemist barely looked up. Said: *you're not sick, you're* **bound.**
> The old hunter with the white eye said it plainest. Said: *somebody did this to you. You were* **tied.**
> Three people. Three rooms. None of them talking to each other. The same word every time — like it was the only word — and not one of them could tell you what you're bound *to.*

*GM: don't explain it. Let a **player** catch it. That's your slow-burn curse mystery doing its work.*

## ▶️ BEAT 5 — THE DINNER *(optional capstone — run it or cliffhang on it)*

> The Attaché Quarters are the nicest rooms in a building full of people who'd rather be elsewhere — and everything in here was shipped in from somewhere else, arranged just so, the desk facing the door. The food is *extraordinary,* even for this city.
> *(warm, glass raised, and he means it)* "I'm glad you survived. I mean that — three incident reports out of this Mire in two years, not one with survivors. Sit. Tell me everything."
> *(around the second course, easy, almost offhand)* "Where exactly did she go down? What did the water *feel* like, at the end? How long were you under?" *(and he listens like every word matters. Like he'll remember all of it.)*

**The ask** (later in the meal): would they put their account *in writing* — a formal report, "to help future travelers"? **The exit:** he pays, thanks them, *"if the imperial offices can do anything, my door is open."* He means it. Useful things should be maintained.

*GM: pleasant and one degree wrong. The menace is sub-perceptual. Do NOT tip the sabotage.*

## 🧰 WING-IT TOOLKIT *(so you never freeze)*

**Paint any scene in 4 strokes:** a *sense* (smell/sound) + a *motion* (something moving) + a *person* (doing one specific thing) + a *small wrongness.* That's a Sallowreach scene.

**Grab a name:**
- *Local:* Odette · Lucienne · Sabine · Cyprien · Émile · Gaspard · Marguerite · Florent · — *surnames:* Duval · Doucet · Boudreaux · Thibodeaux · Hébert · Lacroix
- *Outsiders (sound wrong, on purpose):* Severin · Cornelia · Galen · Marcus · — *surnames:* Vell · Crane · Holt · Stagg

**Five things you notice (city):** stilts creaking under sixty thousand feet · cook-smoke and frying spice you can taste · washing strung house-to-house over the water · a punt vendor singing a price · the whole city tilted half a degree, no one minding.

**What's it taste like (make mouths water):** black-water gumbo (smoke + something green-bitter you can't name) · lantern-root fritters (glow gold *before* the oil) · reedback crab in spirit-pepper (you'll cry and ask for more) · stilt-house bread dragged through brown butter · pepper-candied frog-leg skewers, three for a coin.

**Overheard (drop as background):** "The southwest wall's thinner than it was — my cousin's floor takes water now." · "The imperial one's been asking about that shipwreck. Bought a whole table breakfast just to ask." · "*Honored guests,* they're calling those wreck survivors. The swamp always has a reason." · "There's a frog-man at the edge takes outsiders into the swamp. Lost his boat in that wreck, poor soul." · "Don't whistle on the water tonight."

**Instant background NPC:** roll a *who* + a *want* + a *tic.* WHO: poleman / fishmonger / cook / boardwalk kid / miserable imperial clerk / lantern-maker. WANTS: to be paid · to be *heard* · to know your business · to be left in peace · to sell you one thing. TIC: never sits · counts coins as they talk · always eating · talks to the water mid-sentence · names everything.

**If players do X:**
- *"Can the healer cure us?"* → "No. It's a knot, not a wound. But go see Caspar Renne."
