# Caius Maren

**Role:** Imperial Cultural Liaison; the Empire's most patient instrument in Sallowreach
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — the Imperial Attaché Quarters, inner boardwalk district; the nicest rooms in the building, which he had reassigned from a previous occupant on arrival
**Faction:** The Empire / Mages Guild (nominally)
**Tier:** 2

*His official mandate: observe, document, report. What he is actually doing is building the case for annexation. None of it has been sanctioned. He is betting the capital ratifies success after the fact.*

---

## Appearance

Younger than the role suggests — early thirties, which is the first thing people notice and the last thing they remember to be suspicious of. He dresses in imperial formal: clean lines, good fabric, the understated quality of institutional money rather than personal wealth. He is always slightly too put-together for the bayou humidity, which is either a personal discipline or a quiet statement about his relationship with Sallowreach's climate.

He has an open face — easy to read, which is the point. He laughs readily and means it. He makes eye contact in the specific way of someone trained to make eye contact: present, warm, just long enough. Nothing about him reads as threat. Everything about him reads as opportunity.

His rooms are immaculate. He brought his own furnishings from the capital, which nobody does, and arranged them with a precision that makes the space feel like an office that happens to have a bed in it. The desk faces the door. He always knows who's coming in.

---

## Personality & Mannerisms

He is the hungriest person in any room he enters. Not visibly — the hunger is disciplined, channeled, expressed as attentiveness and charm rather than impatience. He listens more than he speaks. When he speaks, he means it. He never says anything he doesn't intend to say.

**He is a genuine true believer.** This is what makes him dangerous. He doesn't think he's doing anything wrong. He thinks Sallowreach is a brilliant, vital, underserved city that would flourish under proper imperial infrastructure. He thinks the lumber trade is squandered on local guild politics. He thinks the ward situation is exactly the kind of problem the Empire exists to solve. He thinks the people of the outer boardwalk would be safer, more prosperous, more protected under imperial management. He is completely wrong about all of this and he has never once been made to doubt it.

**He treats Sallowreach like something he already owns** — not with contempt, but with the proprietary fondness of a collector for a piece they're still negotiating for. He compliments the city constantly and specifically. He has done his research. He knows which families have been here longest, which craftspeople are considered the best, which dishes are the ones to order. Every compliment is also an appraisal.

**He is perfectly, infuriatingly polite to [[Adaline Roux]].** He respects her position, admires her commitment to the city, defers to her expertise on local matters. He has never once said anything she could object to directly. She hates him with a clarity she rarely allows herself, and he finds this mildly unfortunate.

**He finds the party genuinely interesting** — or performs interest so well the distinction is irrelevant. Tethered outsiders who shipwrecked in the Mire are exactly the kind of witness he needs: credible, sympathetic, with no apparent local agenda. He moves on them immediately.

---

## The Plan

*GM Eyes Only.*

Two years ago, Caius Maren arrived in Sallowreach with a mandate to observe, document, and assess integration potential. He assessed it within three months. The city is extraordinary, profitable, and strategically invaluable. He has spent the twenty-one months since building the case for annexation without authorization.

**The mechanism:** The Empire does not conquer visibly. It documents instability. It offers aid. It negotiates from the position of someone who arrived during a crisis, helped, and stayed. What Maren needs is a crisis.

**What he has done:**
- Arranged the shipwreck. He needed a documented incident demonstrating that unregulated Mire waterways are dangerous to imperial-adjacent travelers. He sourced a man through [[Marcel Dray]]'s network — without Marcel's knowledge or permission — and paid him to ensure the ship went down. He expected everyone to die. [[Remy Duval]] was on the ship. Remy is amphibious. The party is alive. Maren has adjusted.
- Denied [[Oswin Cray]]'s maintenance budget request. Oswin submitted a formal appeal for additional ward maintenance funds through the imperial bureaucratic channel — the Guild's relationship with the Empire means this crosses Maren's desk. Maren declined it with pleasant, well-reasoned language about fiscal protocol and existing maintenance guarantees. He does not know about the embezzlement. He simply wants the ward to fail. A Mire incursion into the outer boardwalk — property damage, displaced families, a city overwhelmed — is the crisis that completes his file.
- Built an incident file spanning two years: navigational failures, ward degradation reports, outer boardwalk displacement records, the shipwreck. Every document is real. Every document is framed.

**What he has miscalculated:** He thinks he is opening a door for a manageable flood. He does not know about the [[Mangrove Spirit]]. He does not know about the [[Tree Spirit]]. He does not know that what is pressing on the other side of [[Oswin Cray]]'s ward is two hundred years of compressed grief from something ancient and vast. He thinks he is engineering a bad season. He is engineering a catastrophe.

**What makes him vulnerable:** Everything he has done is unauthorized. His mandate is observation. The shipwreck, the budget denial, the incident file — none of it sanctioned by the capital. If he is exposed before he can file his annexation petition and receive retroactive approval, he has no imperial protection. He is a rogue agent who arranged a massacre on civilian waterways. The capital would disavow him to protect the relationship with Sallowreach. He knows this. It is why he is moving as fast as he is.

**Marcel's position:** [[Marcel Dray]] had the goon killed the moment he discovered what had been done in his city without his permission. Marcel cannot move against an imperial officer — too visible, too dangerous for his operation. But Marcel has the connection. He knows what Maren arranged. He is sitting on it, waiting for the right instrument. If the party comes to Marcel with the right question, Marcel hands them what they need without being asked twice.

---

## What He Wants

To file the petition. To be the man who delivered Sallowreach. To return to the capital with something that has never been accomplished and spend the rest of his career at the level that achievement earns.

He is thirty-two years old. He has been patient for two years. He is running out of patience.

---

## What He's Hiding

The shipwreck. The unauthorized scope of everything he's done. The petition that doesn't exist yet because he hasn't filed it yet because he needs one more incident and he denied Oswin's budget request and now he waits.

He is also hiding, from himself, that he has come to genuinely love this city. He walks the outer boardwalk markets on his evenings off. He has a favorite table at a restaurant run by a family that's been here six generations. He finds the Celebration genuinely moving, which he has not written in any report. None of this will stop him. But it is there, and occasionally, at the table he likes, with the food he has come to love, something in him goes quiet in a way that isn't calculation.

He never examines it. He has learned not to.

---

## The Dinner

He invites the party the day after they arrive. The invitation is delivered by an aide — handwritten, formal, warm. Dinner at his quarters, that evening, nothing elaborate, he simply heard about what happened and wanted to extend a proper imperial welcome.

The dinner is excellent. He has the city's best provisions and he uses them. He asks about the shipwreck with genuine attention — where exactly, what the water felt like, how long they were under. He is collecting testimony. He does not appear to be collecting testimony.

Somewhere in the second course he raises his concern: the waterways. The lack of standardized navigation. The inadequate safety protocols for imperial-adjacent travelers. He has been documenting incidents for two years. What happened to them is, unfortunately, not unusual.

He asks if they would be willing to put their account in writing. A formal incident report, submitted through proper imperial channels, would be invaluable to his ongoing assessment of the Mire infrastructure situation. He frames it as helping future travelers. He means it to be document fourteen in his annexation file.

He pays for dinner. He thanks them for their time. He hopes they'll find their situation resolved soon — and if there's anything the imperial offices can do to assist, they should consider his door open.

He means this last part. They are useful to him, and useful things should be maintained.

## Key Relationships

- [[Adaline Roux]] — his most significant obstacle and his most careful performance; every interaction with her is a kind of cold war conducted in pleasantries; she knows what he is; she cannot prove it; he finds her admirable in the way you admire an opponent
- [[Théo Rouchard]] — his most promising cultivated ally; Théo finds him charming and his interest in the lumber trade flattering; Maren has been careful not to reveal the scope of what "imperial regulation" of the trade would mean; Théo is not stupid, but he is comfortable, and comfort makes people slow
- [[Oswin Cray]] — an instrument he doesn't think of as a person; Oswin's budget request came through the proper channel; Maren denied it through the proper channel; they have met twice at civic functions and had pleasant, meaningless conversations; Oswin has no idea Maren knows who he is
- [[Marcel Dray]] — the most dangerous relationship he has, and the one he thinks about least; he believes the goon's disappearance was unrelated to his own hire; he is wrong; Marcel knows exactly what he did; Marcel is waiting
- The party — witnesses who survived when they should not have; currently being managed as assets; becoming liabilities as they ask the wrong questions

## Campaign Function

**Act 1:** The dinner. The report request. The warm, well-resourced face of imperial interest in Sallowreach. He should read as pleasant and slightly concerning — a low hum of wrongness the party can't immediately place. He is genuinely helpful in small ways, which makes the wrongness harder to name.

He is watching them. Not obviously. But when they look back at the dinner later, they will realize how specific his questions were.

**Act 2:** The threads start to pull toward each other. The party investigates the ward, the spirits, the spear — and keeps bumping into Maren-adjacent facts. The budget denial. His name on Oswin's paperwork. Remy's observations about the southwest wall and Maren's official responses to city inquiries. He starts to look like a shape rather than a person.

He also starts watching them more directly. They've been asking questions that are getting close to things he needs kept quiet. He escalates from management to monitoring.

**Act 3:** Cornered. Whatever the party has assembled — the shipwreck evidence from Marcel, the unauthorized mandate, the budget denial paper trail — Maren is out of moves. His response to being cornered is to stop being charming. He makes a play: something personal, something that forces the fight. An arrest order for someone the party loves. A fabricated charge that gets Remy detained. A move against Adaline's position. Something that takes off the diplomatic mask and shows what's underneath.

The party has been waiting for this. So has the audience.

He has two imperial guards. He is not a fighter. But desperate men do desperate things, and Caius Maren did not get this far by giving up when the situation turned against him.

## Dialogue Hooks

**At the dinner, on the shipwreck:**
*"I'm glad you survived. I mean that — I've read three incident reports from this Mire in the past two years and none of them had survivors. You were fortunate to have someone like your guide with you."* [genuine, warm, filing everything you say]

**On Sallowreach:**
*"Extraordinary city. I've been posted to six cities in eight years and I've never seen anything like it. The culture, the food, the craftsmanship — it deserves so much better infrastructure than it has. That's what I keep thinking. It deserves better."* [he means this; it is also a sales pitch]

**On the report request:**
*"Nothing formal — just a written account of what happened, where, what you observed about the navigation. It helps me make the case to the capital that this situation requires attention. Future travelers deserve safer waterways. You'd be doing them a service."*

**On Adaline, if asked:**
*"Remarkable woman. Deeply committed to this city. I have enormous respect for what she's built here."* [pause] *"We disagree about some things. That's healthy."*

**On the Empire:**
*"People hear 'imperial integration' and they imagine something being taken away. I've seen it work. What it actually looks like is infrastructure. Roads. Reliable transit. A ward that doesn't fail because one bureaucrat is having a bad year. Sallowreach deserves that. Its people deserve that."*

**When cornered, mask off:**
*"You have no idea what you've walked into. This city is already decided. The only question is whether you're here when it happens."*
