## Strong Start

**A scream, then teeth.** Open on the morning boards — warm bread, the cleanup feast still going — and a **Thornside woman's scream** cuts the market noise short. She comes down the boardwalk clutching a **savaged little dog**, half its leash gone, and the crowd parts around her. *"It came back. In daylight. It came back."* Reflective eyes, she says. A cry that sounded almost like a child.

The mob mood is already forming — somebody's got a gaff hook, somebody's shouting about a bounty. **A frightened animal and a frightened crowd, and the party standing right in the middle of it.** Whatever they do here is the door into the day: Fletcher's hunt is now urgent and *public*, and the rest of the party has seen the city scared.

*(If Fletcher's player isn't chasing the hunt, the scream still lands as texture and the civet stays loose, escalating toward the dinner hour.)*

## Secrets & Clues *(reveal ~half; drop wherever it fits)*

1. The "haunt" is no demon — it's an **amber civet**, a terrified upland animal that escaped a smuggler's crate, starving in a swamp it can't survive.
2. The civet's trail leads to **night-berth crates at the Mooring** — and *other* crates beside it hold caged **Mire** creatures, marked by a deep-channel poacher. *(Gault, planted early.)*
3. Caius **already knows specifics about the party's shipwreck** he has no clean way of knowing — he asks where exactly, how long under, *as if checking an account against his own.*
4. Someone was told the **Guardian is dead** — and that someone set the wreck in motion. *(Tenson's divination + Caius, not yet connected by the PCs.)*
5. The **salt wall is failing** on the southwest face — green pushing through, a family pulling windows out of a house the water already owns.
6. Something **vast and grieving** presses on the far side of the wall — not at the city, *through* it, reaching for "the one they keep" buried in its heart.
7. The wall's protection **rejects what Isotta is** — the salt that keeps the city safe would shove a spirit out; she stands on the wrong side of its mercy.
8. **Caspar knows exactly where a curse-breaking artifact is** — and is too frightened to say the *name* of the man who has it.
9. The **casino** (Sinope's tip) is a place where Sallowreach's debts and secrets change hands — and at least one person there owes far more than coin. *(Oswin thread.)*
10. The dressmaker dressed the party in **Sallowreach finery, on purpose** — "so you'll not go looking like you hope he *likes* you." The city and the Empire are not friends. *(Anti-Caius seed.)*

### 🏹 The Hunt — "The Haunt of Thornside" *(Fletcher; solo or one tag-along)*

**Full design is in `session-02-live.md` (lines ~122–151) and `world/npcs/brin-sevarre.md`.** Quick version:

- **Surface:** a "demon" terrorizing the boards. Bounty forming, mob forming. The glory move is obvious.
- **Truth (he uncovers by tracking — Instinct + his Experiences):** an **amber civet**, invasive, escaped a smuggler's crate, *starving and terrified,* not hunting for sport.

- **Reward (not coin): Thornside Amber** — harvest the gland *cleanly*; doses make beasts read you as *wrong* and overlook you. Doubles as a lure for the amphisbaena later. [VERIFY: 3 doses, "beasts roll vs your Difficulty to detect you for one scene."]

### 🧂 The Salt Wall — "What's Behind the Salt" *(Isotta's re-entry & arc-opener)*

**Full design in `isotta-arc-brainstorm.md`.** This is your **mechanics showcase — four rolls in one scene.** Run it as her welcome-back spotlight.

**Part 1 — The Still Cup (Vael).** She asks about ghosts/the veil; he answers straight (none of it is the secret): a spirit world beside this one, thin across the Mire; spirits aren't good or evil; small ones wear human faces. Then the white eye: *you didn't cross, you were **tied** — that's a curse, not a haunting,* which makes her the most sensitive instrument in the city. He's felt a worn-through spot on the southwest edge — *the living can't stand in it. You can.*
- He teaches **one grounding move** first (how to listen to something vast without coming loose from your own name). **Roll: Knowledge or Instinct.** Success banks advantage/a Hope for the wall.

**Part 2 — The Wall (hers alone; Vael stops at the edge of what he can stand):**
1. **The approach** — the wall is bigger than the stories, weeping green, a family evacuating. The salt drags at her edges. **Roll: Instinct** to read the line → finds the worn point; pushing to the salt costs **1–2 Stress.** [VERIFY]
2. **The reach (centerpiece)** — **Instinct + *Connects with Spirits* vs Diff 15.** Spend Hope to add the Experience; grounding move = advantage. *Let her choose how far she pushes.* [VERIFY]
3. **Contact** — something enormous and grieving leans toward the opening, reaching *past her, through the city,* toward something held deep inside. The shape of the whole campaign, **no names.** First casebook entry.
4. **The parley** — the thin spot draws the dead; another lingers here. **Roll: Presence** to reach it without spooking it → a clue in its muddled words: the big thing wants *"the one they keep,"* and it's been getting louder for years.
5. **The pull (her clock)** — the grief finds her own and the veil works her loose. **Presence reaction vs Diff 14.** On Fear: heavy Stress, her outline flickers, she half-crosses; the grounding move hauls her back. **Choice: pull out and keep what she has, or push for one more piece and risk another slip.**

> **Guardrail:** she feels the **shape, never the answer.** No Tree Spirit, no Celebration-as-cage, no founders. Direction and dread only. (See the reveal rules in `cosmology.md`.)

**Leaves her with three leads:** the pull comes from the SW and aims at the heart → **Caspar.** She needs to reach without coming apart → **Vael's training (Basile).** The wall is failing → **Oswin / the breach clock.**

### 🍷 The Dinner with Caius — *the centerpiece* *(whole party, nightfall)*

**Full design in `world/npcs/caius-maren.md` ("The Dinner").** The dramatic-irony engine of the whole campaign: **they are dining with the man who blew up their boat, learned they lived, and invited the survivors in to take their measure.** They don't know. *You* do. Play him warm, open-faced, genuinely charming — *that's* what makes it land.

**His three goals at the table:**
1. **Collect testimony** — he asks about the wreck with real attention: *where exactly, what the water felt like, how long under.* He's checking their account against the hit he ordered.
2. **The report request (his real ask):** would they put it in writing? A formal incident report, "for future travelers." → it's **document fourteen in his annexation file.** *(Roll: Presence to decline without burning the bridge.)*
3. **Maintain useful assets** — "my door is open." He means it. They're useful, and useful things get maintained.

**The slip the party can catch (Roll: Instinct/Knowledge):** somewhere in the second course, Caius knows a **detail he shouldn't** — names the stretch of channel, or the hour, or that there were *survivors plural* before they said so. A hit doesn't reveal *he* did it — it plants *"this man already knew about our wreck."* **For Tenson especially:** this is the man who was told the Guardian died. Let the divination itch without paying off.

**Vivienne's armor (if she dressed them):** they arrive in **Sallowreach finery, not imperial fashion** — the city's quiet "he doesn't own you" statement. Caius *feels* it and is mildly, genuinely put out. A small win before a word is spoken.

**Adversary note:** Caius is **not a fighter** (two imperial guards, a knife he's never used). The dinner is **social, not combat** — Presence/Instinct/Knowledge rolls, Stress and Hope swings, the slow chill of realizing how *specific* his questions were. Don't let it become a brawl; the payoff is *the realization later.*

> **Caius:** *"I've read three incident reports from this Mire in two years. None of them had survivors. You were fortunate to have someone like your guide."* [warm — filing every word]
