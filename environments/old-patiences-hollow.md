# Old Patience's Hollow

**Tier 1 Social/Exploration**

*"The water here does not glow so much as remember light. Sit at its edge and the stillness gets into you the way cold gets into stone — slowly, completely, and without your noticing the moment it began."*

**Impulses:** Reward the reverent and rebuke the presumptuous, offer the deep truth of the Mire to those who wait, soften the [[The Tether|tether]] for guests who behave as guests, withdraw entirely from those who command or take.

**Difficulty:** 12

This is the Still Pool — the dwelling-place of [[Old Patience]], an ancient lesser Mire spirit, in an unnamed break in the mangroves off the southwest Wending channel. It is the quietest place in the campaign and the most sacred. Nothing here wants to hurt the party; nothing here will, unless they earn it. The challenge is **conduct**, not combat: this is the [[Cosmology|honored-guest]] framework made into a single encounter, where how you behave matters more than anything you can roll. A respectful party can earn **partial relief of the tether** — the first concrete proof the binding *can* change. A presumptuous one leaves with nothing, and the Mire becomes a harder place behind them. The pressure [[Old Patience]] feels in the deep water — Front 1, *The Grief Rises* — is the [[Mangrove Spirit]] approaching, described here from the inside by something that lives in the same water. Run this as the campaign exhaling. Long pauses. The Guide barely breathing.

**The Protocols** (see [[Old Patience]] for full text): **Wait** — do not speak first. **Do not lie** — it knows. **The offering rule** — decline the first offer (courtesy), accept the second (exchange), never see a third. **When it is done, it is done** — do not follow, do not call after it. The spirit never explains these. The party discovers them through play, or a quiet, still PC intuits them.

## Potential Adversaries

None. There is no monster here. The only thing that can go wrong is the party — and the only "defeat" is withdrawal. If a GM needs a stat block, they have already misread the scene. (If violence is somehow brought to the Still Pool, [[Old Patience]] does not fight; it simply *leaves,* and the Mire's later difficulty is the consequence. [VERIFY: no SRD adversary intended for this environment.])

## Features

**The Hush - Passive:** The Still Pool rewards reverence and punishes presumption. A character who waits, stays quiet, and behaves as a guest — not a petitioner, not a commander — gains **advantage** on rolls to perceive, intuit the protocols, or receive what [[Old Patience]] communicates [VERIFY: advantage representation]. A character who speaks first, makes demands, or treats the spirit as a resource to be questioned rolls those same checks at **disadvantage**, and the GM holds a **Presumption marker.** Track these. They are the quiet clock under the whole scene: at the GM's discretion (and at **three markers**, automatically), the spirit's attention turns from *examining* to *withdrawing* — see *The Withdrawal.* Commanding a spirit is the one unforgivable manner here. The Mire does not run on prayers or bargains; it runs on welcome.

**What the Still Water Knows - Action:** Once the party has waited correctly and the spirit has chosen to speak, it offers the Mire's deeper truth — freely, in something between language and impression. A quiet PC gets more than a talking one. The GM may reveal, in order, across the scene: *you carry two marks, the city's and the water's — you are the first in a long time;* *something in the deep water has been answering something in the city longer than I have attended to it, and it is very close now;* and finally, shown not spoken, the Mire from above — every channel and current, the southwest unmistakably **brighter,** everything flowing toward [[Sallowreach]]. This is an information feature, not a roll to "win." The party's manner (tracked by *The Hush*) decides how much arrives intact. [[Remy Duval]] translates about half; the rest lands directly.

**The Second Offering - Reaction:** The tether-relief lever — the reason this environment matters. After the protocols are honored (notably the offering rule: declined once, accepted on the **second** offer), [[Old Patience]] offers **partial relief of the [[The Tether|tether]].** It is made of the same spirit-water magic that made the mark; it understands the binding and *softens* it. Mechanically: the agony at the Mire's boundary becomes quieter, the wall less sharp — the party can travel **measurably closer to the edge** before the resistance turns impassable. Not out. Closer to out than before. This is not a cure (the underlying binding is unresolved) and not re-scripted here — see [[Old Patience#The Reward]] for the fiction. It is a concrete, permanent lever: the first sign the tether is *not* a fixed condition. Grant it once, when earned. Do not let the party negotiate for more.

**The Withdrawal - Reaction:** **Spend a Fear** when the party commands, lies to, or takes from [[Old Patience]] without asking — or trigger this for free at three Presumption markers (see *The Hush*). The light does not attack. It **disperses.** The temperature levels. The sense of being watched by something *interested* is simply gone. The party keeps whatever they had already earned, but the offer of tether relief — if not yet accepted — is withdrawn for good, and so is the Mire-from-above vision. Worse: the Mire becomes a harder place afterward. For the next stretch of play [VERIFY: GM-paced duration], bioluminescence dims, readable channels turn confusing, and navigation rolls in [[The Wending]] and [[The Deep Mire]] are made at disadvantage. This is a real consequence, not a punishment — the honored guest was disrespected, and an honored guest who is disrespected withdraws its welcome.

**The Lingering Stillness - Action:** **Spend a Fear** to let the pool's stillness reach for someone who sat too long at its edge — the way it caught the child [[Lenne]]. The target makes a Presence Reaction Roll [VERIFY] or loses the thread of time and self: not harmed, not entranced by anything hostile, simply *stilled.* They mark 1 Stress and cannot take an action until another character speaks to them, touches them, or the spirit's attention shifts. This is the gentlest hazard in the campaign — there is no malice in it. The Still Pool is just very old and very still, and stillness that deep is not safe for the living to share without care.

## Feature Questions

*Which PC understands first that you do not command an honored guest — and which one has to learn it the hard way, with a Presumption marker on the table?*

*What does a party member offer [[Old Patience]] — and is it the offering of someone who has been listening, or someone performing reverence to get the reward?*

*When the tether softens for the first time, what does it mean to the PC who has been most desperate to leave — relief, or the terrible new hope that the cage has a door after all?*

*The spirit says the last tethered ones "found what they were looking for" and will not clarify. Whose face goes still at that — and do they read it as a promise or a warning?*

## Running This

This is the campaign's **sacred scene, not a scary one.** Three principles carry it:

1. **Stillness is the mechanic.** The whole encounter runs on *The Hush.* Reward waiting, silence, and guest-manners with advantage and information; meet demands and questions-as-interrogation with disadvantage and Presumption markers. Never narrate the spirit as menacing — narrate it as *attentive,* the way deep water is attentive. The horror, if any, is the party's own fear of getting the etiquette wrong, and that fear is the point. Let them feel the weight of behaving well.

2. **The reward is earned, not given.** *The Second Offering* is the load-bearing lever — partial tether relief, the first proof the binding can change. Gate it behind the protocols, especially the offering rule (decline once, accept on the second offer, never a third). If the party blunders the etiquette badly enough to trigger *The Withdrawal* before accepting, they lose it. That is allowed. The relief means something *because* it can be missed. See [[Old Patience]] for the full fiction and what it unlocks downstream.

3. **Withdrawal, not violence.** There is no fight here. The only failure state is *The Withdrawal* — the light disperses, the Mire goes harder, [[Remy Duval]] won't speak for a while. Play that as loss, not punishment. The party found the child [[Lenne]]; that part is always complete. What they forfeit is the deeper gift. Let the silence after the light disperses do the work — do not explain it.

Tie this to the living world lightly: the pressure [[Old Patience]] feels is the [[Mangrove Spirit]]'s approach (Front 1), and the southwest-brighter vision is the same truth [[Caspar Renne]]'s tide journals point at, delivered faster. [[Delphine]] will recognize the two-lights image [[Lenne]] describes — connect it in the scene that hits hardest.

## Dungeon Alchemist Notes

No battle map needed — this is theater-of-the-mind by design, and mapping it for combat would undercut the tone. If you want a visual aid: a single small, perfectly still break in dense mangrove off a southwest channel, ringed by old roots. The water does not ripple. Low blue-green ambient light, dimmer and steadier than the rest of the Mire — bioluminescence that *organizes* rather than glows, resolving into the suggestion of a face only on the surface, never above it. A child-sized offering (a twist of dried Mire grass, a bright button) left at the water's edge. One punt, held still. Keep the space small, close, and quiet; the smallness is the sacredness.
