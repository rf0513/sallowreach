# The Field Journal of Aldric Vane

**Type:** Document (mundane appearance)
**Current Location:** Estate of Reille Preis — a box of miscellaneous papers labeled *OLD — UNCLEAR VALUE*
**Origin:** Written by Aldric Vane, first Mages Guild ward maintainer posted to Sallowreach, approximately 200 years ago
**Significance:** The earliest institutional record of the Mangrove Spirit's behavior — and the document [[Caspar Renne]] has been trying to locate for six years

---

## Description

A leather-bound field journal, water-damaged at the bottom third of every page (he lived here; it was humid). The handwriting is precise and compressed — a man trained to use paper efficiently. No illustrations. Margin notes in a different ink suggest he returned to earlier entries and added postscripts, sometimes years later.

To anyone without context: old papers. Guild notation at the front, dense technical observations, nothing exciting.

To Caspar: six years of questions answered, partially.

---

## Key Passages

The party doesn't need to read the whole journal. These are the excerpts worth staging.

---

**Early in the posting — dated about six months after arrival:**

*"The spirit does not wander. I have now observed its movement at sufficient intervals to be confident: it moves toward the city. Not randomly. Not incidentally. Toward Sallowreach, with intention that the word 'intention' barely covers — a force of nature that has chosen a direction and sustains it. My predecessors in the Guild literature called it 'encroachment.' Encroachment implies accident. This is not accident."*

---

**A frustrated entry — about three years in:**

*"Two colleagues have reviewed my observations and concluded there is no attractor. The spirit simply grows, they say, and Sallowreach happens to be in its path. I have run the trajectory calculations. I have consulted the channel-shift maps going back four generations. The spirit does not simply grow. It is pulled. Something inside the city holds its attention the way a lantern holds a moth's, and I cannot locate the source. The ward holds — the salt holds — but I am holding back a tide that wants to come in. This is different from holding back a flood."*

---

**Practical observation — undated, appears to be a reference note added later:**

*"Salt disrupts approach. Ward geometry must account for sustained directional pressure from the southwest quadrant specifically — not uniform perimeter pressure. If a future maintainer finds the ward skewing, reinforce the southwest. It will always be the southwest. The spirit is always coming from there."*

---

**Late in the posting — near the end of the journal:**

*"I have spent eleven years trying to identify the attractor. I have failed. What I am confident of: it is not us. We did not build it, and we are not maintaining it — it predates the Guild posting by at least as long as the city has records. Whatever draws the Mangrove Spirit to Sallowreach is something the city itself contains, and I believe the city does not know what it is.*

*I attended the Celebration again last week. I have attended it perhaps fifty times now. It is very beautiful. The chanting is older than the city's written history — when I asked the council where the chants originated, they had no answer. They have simply always been. I wonder, sometimes, if the answer I'm looking for is somewhere in those chants. I have not been able to read them — they are in an old dialect I can't fully parse, and Sallowreach's scholars have not seen fit to translate them for a Guild representative.*

*I am leaving these notes to my successor. The ward holds. The arrangement with the city holds. But the central question — why the spirit comes here — I cannot answer. I hope someone does, eventually. The answer matters."*

---

**The arrangement, noted in the first entry of the journal:**

*"Terms of posting, as I understand them: the Mages Guild maintains a qualified practitioner here indefinitely, responsible for salt-ward maintenance and any spiritual containment the Mire requires. In exchange, Sallowreach provides agreed quarterly shipments of Mire lumber to Guild operations in the capital. The timber they provide is extraordinary — I had not believed the reports before seeing it. The arrangement, the council told me without apology, is tribute. They pay for the protection they require and we provide the service for which we are paid. No one pretended otherwise. I respect that."*

---

## What Caspar Finds

He has read about Vane's posting in secondary sources. He has documented Vane's name, dates, and institutional role. But the primary record — Vane's own observations — has been missing from his archive for six years, and Caspar has suspected it contained something the secondary sources missed.

What stops him when he reads it: the attractor passage. Vane couldn't identify it. In sixty years of research, Caspar hasn't been able to identify it either. But Vane's certainty that it exists — that specific passage, *something inside the city holds its attention* — confirms what Caspar has believed and been unable to prove.

He reads it twice. He sets the journal down. He says, quietly:

*"He didn't know what it was. Eleven years, and he couldn't — I've been at this forty years, and I couldn't—"*

Then, longer pause:

*"You need the spear. I know you need it. The man who has it is named Marcel Dray."*

He doesn't explain how he knows. He doesn't explain the complicated feeling on his face. He just says the name.

## The Mini-Quest: Tracking the Journal

### How the Party Learns About It

Three routes in, any of which works:

**From Caspar directly** — if the party returns to the museum and engages him about spirit research rather than just the spear's location, he'll mention it in passing. He's been trying to find Vane's original field journal for years. He tracked it to an estate auction six years ago but lost the trail. *"If it still exists, it would be the earliest first-hand account we have of the spirit's behavior. Forty years I've been working from summaries."*

**From Delphine** — she and Caspar are old friends. She knows he's been preoccupied with a missing document for years. She can point the party toward him as a way to build relationship: *"Caspar's been miserable about some old journal that vanished at auction. If you could find it for him, he'd — yes, that would help."*

**From the museum itself** — attentive players browsing the archive floor might notice Caspar's research files and find a folder labeled VANE — PROVENANCE INQUIRY with six years of dead-end correspondence.

### Finding the Journal

**The trail:** Estate auction, six years ago. A lot of miscellaneous papers sold as a single lot to Natta Preis, a retired merchant collector who bought things that seemed interesting without always knowing what they were.

**Natta Preis is dead.** Died two years ago. Left the house and everything in it to a niece named **Reille Preis**, who inherited two floors of accumulated objects and hasn't finished cataloguing them and frankly isn't sure she wants to.

**Reille** is cooperative if the party is pleasant. She's not guarding the journal — she doesn't know she has it. She has, by her estimate, "about forty boxes of Aunt Natta's paper things" and would be relieved if someone took some of them off her hands with a plausible reason. A nominal payment, a favor, or just a kind explanation will get the party access to the boxes.

The journal is in a box labeled *OLD — UNCLEAR VALUE*, beneath some guild administrative correspondence and what appears to be a collection of annotated fish-market receipts.

### The Return Scene

They bring it back to Caspar.

He opens it with careful hands. Reads. Goes very still — the tell that means genuine engagement, not avoidance. Reads for what feels like longer than it is.

Then he finds the attractor passage and goes quiet in a different way.

Stage his realization. Let the players watch him work through it. Then the name.

**After he says the name:** he does not offer anything else immediately. If they press on why he knows Marcel has it, he'll answer — but briefly, carefully, without details. That conversation is for another time. What matters is the name is out.

---

## GM Notes

- The mini-quest is not about difficulty. It's about trust. The journal isn't locked up or guarded. Reille Preis is not an obstacle. The point is that the party did something for Caspar without being asked to, and that act unlocked the conversation.
- If a player asks to look through the museum archive while there on another visit, let them find the provenance folder. Reward investigation with the trail.
- The scene where Caspar reads the journal should be slow. Don't rush it. The party has been patient; let them watch the moment land.
- The fact that Vane couldn't identify the attractor is meaningful twice: once for Caspar in the moment, and again later when the party understands what the attractor IS. Vane was looking for something external. The Tree Spirit was inside the city the whole time, in the Celebration, in the wood, in the water.
