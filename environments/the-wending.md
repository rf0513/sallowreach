# The Wending

**Tier 1 Traversal**

> *"The black water remembers a road that was here last season, and forgets it the moment you need it. The Mire is not lost. You are."*

**Type:** Traversal — the network of named, shifting navigable channels that locals memorize over generations. This is the campaign's signature travel environment: the party crosses it every time they leave [[Sallowreach]]. Drop it in for any journey through the Mire's inner ring — the fishing grounds, the ingredient-harvesting basins, the punt-routes between settlement and deep water. Use [[The Mire]] for grounding and reconcile with the navigation rules in [[Sallowreach-frame#The Wending (Mire Navigation)|the campaign frame]].

**Impulses:** Reroute the unwary, swallow the road behind them, reach (always, slowly, toward the city), reward those who listen to the water.

**Difficulty:** 11 *(with a local guide such as [[Remy Duval]]). **13** without a guide — navigation rolls are made at Difficulty 13 and the Lost stakes bite harder. In the Deep Mire, past the known channels, raise to 15 and consult* [[Tables/mire-complications|the Mire Complications table]] *for what Lost means out there.* [VERIFY: Daggerheart environment Difficulty as the target number for player navigation rolls — reconcile with the frame's Instinct-roll Hope/Fear framing]

**Potential Adversaries:** [[Gault]] (Mire Extractor, Tier 2 Solo) working a stripped basin; [[mire-bestiary|Mire beasts]] — [[mire-bestiary#Ridgeback Crawler|ridgeback crawler]], glass snake (SRD), [[mire-bestiary#Bloodmidge Swarm|biting swarm]]; [[sallowreach-roster#Wending Runner|punt-pirates and channel-runners]] (and their [[sallowreach-roster#Wending Runner Lackey|lackeys]]) who know one channel the party doesn't; lantern-ray poachers in [[Gault]]'s pay (use [[sallowreach-roster#Wending Runner|Wending Runner]] stats).

## Features

**The Shifting Road - Passive:** Crossing the Wending requires the party to complete a **Progress Countdown (4)**, advanced by navigation rolls — one PC leads with an **Instinct Roll** [VERIFY: appropriate trait and Action Roll mechanics per frame] as the others read water, watch roots, and call the turns. On a success the Countdown advances 1 (advance 2 on a success with Hope). On a **roll with Hope**, the lead also learns one true thing about the route ahead — a hidden hazard, a faster channel, a sign of who passed this way. On a **roll with Fear**, the GM chooses: the party arrives late and drained (each PC marks 1 Stress), *or* a waterway that was open last season is simply gone — the Countdown does not advance and the party is now somewhere it did not mean to be. *With a guide, the guide makes this roll and the party need only handle what surfaces.*

**Lost - Reaction:** When a navigation roll fails with Fear (or whenever the GM judges the party has truly missed the way), the party becomes **Lost**. While Lost, the party **cannot complete a Long Rest** [VERIFY: Long Rest interaction] and cannot finish the journey's Countdown until they reorient — a successful navigation roll clears the Lost condition and resumes the crossing. Each hour spent Lost, the GM may **spend a Fear to trigger a Mire encounter** (see *Something Surfaces*). The deeper the party has strayed, the worse the company.

**The Hinge - Action:** At a channel that "turns in a way that makes no hydrological sense" [[Remy Duval]]'s name for it, the current grabs the boat. Each PC aboard makes an **Agility Reaction Roll** or the punt slews into the mangroves at speed: on a failure, 1d6+1 physical damage, and one carried item or piece of gear is knocked loose into the black water unless a PC spends their action to catch it. A guide who knows the Hinge lets the party roll with advantage. [VERIFY: Reaction Roll, advantage, item-loss as improvised consequence]

**Something Surfaces - Action:** Spend a Fear to summon a Mire threat within Close range — a ridgeback crawler stirred from its basin, a glass snake [VERIFY: SRD] coiled in the roots, or one of [[Gault]]'s nets cutting the water nearby. It takes the spotlight immediately. In the inner Wending these are curious or territorial; in the Deep Mire they are drawn, as if the water pointed them here.

**Root-Choke - Reaction:** When the party tries to force a shortcut, push through after dark, or move faster than the Mire allows, the channel closes on them: roots break the surface and seize the hull. The boat is **Restrained** [VERIFY: applying Restrained to a vehicle] until a PC succeeds on a **Strength** or **Agility** roll to clear it, or until someone goes over the side into the water to cut it free — and the water is not empty.

**The Reaching Dark - Action:** *(Fear-gated escalation.)* Spend a Fear when the party is Lost, deep, or moving against the Mire's grain: the bioluminescence arranges itself — holding a pattern that tracks nothing in the wind — and every PC feels, briefly and unmistakably, *examined by something very old.* Each PC marks 1 Stress; the next navigation roll this crossing is made with disadvantage as instinct and instrument both falter [VERIFY: disadvantage, compasses-spin effect]. This is the [[The Mangrove Spirit|Mangrove Spirit]]'s attention brushing past — never aimed at the party, always aimed *through* them, toward [[Sallowreach]]. Do not name it. Let them feel it.

## Feature Questions

*Who in the party is leading the navigation, and what do they have to prove — to the water, to the locals, or to themselves?*

*What did the Wending look like last time they came this way, and what's different now that the grief is rising?*

*If they're traveling with [[Remy Duval]] or another guide, what does the guide notice and quietly file away without telling them?*

*When the Reaching Dark turns its attention on them, what does each PC privately suspect it wants?*

## Running This

The Wending is the heartbeat of travel in this campaign, so make it a **texture, not a tax.** Most crossings should be a Progress Countdown (4) resolved in two or three rolls while the party talks — about where they're going, what they're dreading, who Remy is naming today. Spend your Fear on *Something Surfaces* or *The Reaching Dark* only when a crossing should mean something: a tense run to beat [[Gault]] to a basin, a panicked return after a plan went wrong, the first time they push past the known channels into the Deep Mire.

**With a guide, the Wending is wondrous.** [[Remy Duval]] makes the rolls, names the channels, stops the boat to show them a ridgeback crawler — Difficulty 11, low stakes, high beauty. The party is along for a master's-eye tour. **Without a guide, it is a cage with teeth:** Difficulty 13, Lost is a real threat, and *Lost means no Long Rest* — the single mechanic that makes a wrong turn frightening instead of inconvenient. Lean on that. A party that can't rest is a party that has to choose between pressing on hurt and bleeding Fear into the dark.

**Make the shifting channels real.** At least once, take away a route the players already used — "the cut you ran last week is roots to the waterline now." That's Front 1 (*The Grief Rises*) made tactile: the Mire isn't attacking, it's *reaching*, and the road closing behind them is the same longing that's swallowing the southwest boardwalk. The *Reaching Dark* is your tie to the world in motion — when the deep channels press in, the party is feeling the pressure that the whole campaign turns on. Use it sparingly, never explain it, and let them carry the unease back to town.
