# Session 3 — RP & Dice Cards (the afternoon stops)

*The "what do I actually SAY" page. Full lore in the NPC files; this distills each likely afternoon stop into: a **walk-in** you can read aloud, **how to play** the NPC (one body tic + one voice tic), an **opening line**, and **🎲 where the dice live.** Late-afternoon start → these stops → evening dinner ([[session-03-read-aloud]] beats 8–9). Diff benchmarks (SRD p.34): 10 = moderate, **13–14 = hard for Tier 1 (baseline Diff is 11)**, 15 = a real reach.*

> **The dice reflex:** the moment a PC tries to *get* something — read a person, find a thing, push past a "no," impress someone, hold their nerve — **call a roll before you narrate the result.** That's the whole fix for last session.

## 🌿 Delphine's Hand-Off — launching the curse path *(the spark for stop ↓)*
*This is the beat that points them at Caspar. A transition, not a full scene — keep it tight and warm, then let them move.*

**Walk-in:** Drying herbs and beeswax, the mineral smell of deep Mire water. Bundles from every rafter, a scrubbed examining table, shelves in a system only she knows. She's already looking at whoever's most worn-down before anyone speaks.

**How to play Delphine:**
- **Body:** gets **very close and very quiet** when she examines — patients tell her things they didn't plan to. Single braid of dried Mire-grass on her left wrist. **The tell:** her eyes drift a half-second *left*, she goes still a beat too long, then she's back like nothing happened.
- **Voice:** blunt but never cruel — tells you what's wrong, what she can do, what she can't, what to do next, *in that order,* no cushioning. Dry wit sideways; never laughs at her own joke. Bristles if treated like an oracle — *"I'm a healer. I heal."*

**Opening line (the "old and ancient" framing → the referral):**
> *"I've turned this over since you left, and I'll tell you plain: this isn't new. What's tied to you is* old *— older than me, maybe older than the stories this city tells about itself. That's past my shelf."* [a beat] *"But I know a man who chases things that old for a living. Caspar Renne — the museum, up in the old quarter. Tell him I sent you. He'll actually listen."*

🎲 **Dice here:**
- **Read what she's not saying** — *Instinct, Diff 13.* A hit catches the drift-left: *something hit her* when she touched them, and she's not naming it. **Don't disclose the vision** — if pressed: *"Old habit. The water's loud today."* (The two-lights vision is a reward for a later visit.)
- **Press her on *how* old** — *Presence/Knowledge, Diff 12.* A hit gets her to level: she's felt the presence in that water sixty years, and *this* is that, concentrated and ancient — which is exactly why she's sending them to the one man who studies it.

> **Guardrails:** she does **not** know about any binding, prison, or Tree Spirit — she's a feeling-holder, not the answer. The explicit *"it's like the Celebration"* articulation is a **return-visit** reward, not now. Here it's only "old, deep, past my shelf → Caspar."

**Leaves them with:** a warm, specific push to **Caspar's** — and the itch that she felt something she won't say.

## 📚 Caspar's Museum — the curse path *(most likely first stop)*
*Read-aloud arrival is [[session-03-read-aloud]] beat 4. This card = how to run him once they're inside.*

**Walk-in (if you want it fresh):** Cedar oil, old paper, dust hanging in slow shafts of light. Cases everywhere; a wall of channel-maps showing the Mire crawling closer, year by year. By the door, a life-size **wax harbor-master** with a punt-pole and a ledger, candles lit at its feet — been there so long nobody sees it.

**How to play Caspar:**
- **Body:** when *fascinated*, he goes utterly **still** (the academic twitch drops away). When *hiding*, the opposite — too much motion, straightening things on shelves, eyes finding the ceiling.
- **Voice:** interrupts himself to clarify things that don't need it. Says "fascinating" and means it. A terrible liar who *knows* he's a terrible liar and can't stop.

**Opening line (on hearing "Delphine" and "bound"):**
> *"Spirit-water binding. Yes. That's — yes, I have three accounts of something similar. Sit down, please — all of you. This is going to take a moment."*

🎲 **Dice here:**
- **Clock his tell** — *Instinct, Diff 12.* He's a bad liar, so a hit lands clean: he's hiding a **name out of fear**, not ignorance. (Miss = they just feel he's slippery.)
- **Find a thread in the gallery** — *Knowledge/Instinct, Diff 13.* Display case seven, one slot **empty six years** ("Object on Loan"). A hit connects the empty case to his evasion — *that's* the spear.
- **Push too hard** *(only if they do)* — moving toward the archive stairs / coercing him wakes **the Warden** (SRD Construct: Diff 13 · HP 9 · ATK +4 · Fist Slam 1d20 phys). He calls it off the instant they back down. Relationship cost, not a fight.

**Leaves them with:** the spear exists & breaks bindings; a **name to earn** (trust path = return Vane's journal). Don't resolve tonight.

## 🏹 The Ranging House — meet Brin *(if they wander; intro #1)*

**Walk-in:** Three stories of waxed timber at the city's wet edge. Inside: rendered fat, solvent, and river-mud worn so deep into the thresholds no cleaning reaches it. A **contract board** color-coded by danger. In the corner, a table buried in channel-maps inked in three colors and a wall of **tagged shed-skins** — somebody's life's work.

**How to play Brin:**
- **Body:** moves quietly even indoors. Reaches for her own map *before you finish talking* — she's three steps ahead. Keeps a tally-book that's **not of kills, of populations.**
- **Voice:** precise, no ornament. Asks three sharp questions before she answers one. Never raises her voice — has never needed to.

**Opening line (Fletcher shows the snake's shed-skin):**
> *"Two-headed pattern. Southwest junction. Three months old, give or take."* [then she looks at *him*, not the skin] *"I've got four more of these in a drawer. What I don't have is a reason to trust the man holding the fifth."*

🎲 **Dice here:**
- **Read the contract board** — *Knowledge/Instinct, Diff 10.* A hit reads what the Mire's *doing*: southwest contracts thinning, two hunters lost past the Double Knot.
- **Make your case to Brin** — *Presence, Diff 14.* Not required (she'll give the test regardless), but a strong roll makes her *warm* faster and is a real Hope/Fear swing. The pitch she's listening for: *"I hunt to remove a threat, not for glory or coin."*
- *(The actual trust-test — the Thornside civet — is the **night** beat. Here she just hands him the assignment.)*

**Brin's send-off:**
> *"There's a haunt loose in Thornside. City wants it dead. Go handle it — and how you do it is the whole answer."*

**Leaves them with:** the civet job (night), and the snake intel **gated** behind passing it.

## 🥊 The Drillfield — meet Sable *(if they wander; intro #2; Tenson's mirror)*

**Walk-in:** A pounded-flat training yard on the southwest line, loud with grunts and laughter at once. Recruits running brutal drills and *grinning* about it. The air's thick, everyone's sweating, and the big sergeant in the middle is sweating hardest and enjoying it most — usually mid-laugh, usually holding food.

**How to play Sable:**
- **Body:** the **grin arrives before they do.** Taps a Celebration drum-rhythm without noticing. Will toss you a practice stave mid-sentence.
- **Voice:** loud, warm, calls everyone *cher* and *brain-boy*. Completely at peace being average — hands you anything clever right back. The steel only shows when they talk about their people.

**Opening line (Tenson flashes a Citadel seal):**
> *"From the Citadel! Look at you. We don't get many who'll still get in the mud."* [already tossing a stave] *"You do the morning drill with my lot, then I'll tell you anything you want. Deal? Don't answer — just catch."*

🎲 **Dice here** *(this stop is a dice GOLDMINE — it's physical):*
- **The drill / spar** — *Strength or Agility, Diff 12.* Hold your own, take a hit, laugh. **Fail forward:** you eat a stave and mark 1 Stress, but Sable likes that you *got up grinning.* Sable reads heart, not score.
- **Spot what's wrong with Perrin** — *Knowledge/Instinct, Diff 13.* The green recruit keeps getting hurt — Tenson's sports-medicine eye catches an old injury / bad form no one else saw. **This is the winning beat** — caring for one of Sable's people with the exact expertise Tenson's ashamed of.
- **Coach Perrin through it** — *Presence/Knowledge, Diff 12.* A hit turns the kid around and wins Sable completely.

**Leaves them with:** Tenson's best ally in the city — and the man sitting (unknowing) on the wall's whole paper trail, including a Caius signature that makes no sense.

## ☕ The Still Cup — Vael & the salt wall *(Isotta, if she breaks off)*

**Full run is [[session-03-read-aloud]] beats 5–6 + [[isotta-arc-brainstorm]].** Quick voice card only:

**How to play Vael:**
- **Body:** calm the way deep water is calm. Listens far more than speaks. Sets a coffee down with no ceremony. **The white eye** drifts to something past your shoulder, then the brown one finds you.
- **Voice:** precise and generous when he knows; flat "I don't know" when he doesn't — never lets a guess pass for fact. Dry humor at the bottom of a long silence.

**Opening line:**
> *"I've served a few like you. Died in the transformation, came back still attached. The Order says that's not supposed to survive."* [the white eye, then the brown] *"But you're not quite that either, are you. Sit. Drink."*

🎲 **Dice here:** grounding lesson (*Knowledge/Instinct* — banks advantage for the wall) → read the line (*Instinct*) → **the reach** (*Instinct + Connects with Spirits, Diff 15*) → parley the dead (*Presence*) → the pull (*Presence, Diff 14*). **Four+ rolls in one scene — your dice fix, in Isotta's flavor.**

## 🎯 Pacing the afternoon
- Run stops as **short laps** — cut on the hook, don't drain a location dry.
- **Split is fine** but converge on the dinner. Pass transitions through the market (hand out 1d4 Stress/Hope).
- **Don't gate the fun behind dice — gate the *stakes*.** They always learn *something*; the roll decides the cost and the extra.
- Carry unrevealed threads to Session 4. The dinner can cliffhang into next session's strong start.

## 🌆 "They Just Wander" — Sallowreach street-sights *(when the party drifts with no goal)*
*Insurance for aimless time: every one is a small dose of **wonder or pathos** with a **die baked in,** so drifting still rolls dice and the city keeps loving them. Roll a d6 or just grab one. Default: hand out **1d4 Hope** on a warm hit; **1d4 Stress** if it costs them something. Keep each to a beat or two, then drift on.*

| d6 | The sight | 🎲 The roll |
|:--:|---|---|
| **1** | **The cleanup feast, still going.** Three days after the Celebration and a Thornside auntie is still ladling crawfish to anyone who slows down — she will *not* take no, and she wants to know your names and your business and whether you're eating enough. | **Presence** (Diff 10) to charm your way to the *good* table — a hit earns a free meal, a name to drop, and a warm rumor (seed any thread you need planted). Miss: she adopts you anyway, louder. |
| **2** | **Lantern-rays at dusk.** Out over the open channel, the lantern-rays rise to feed — slow, glowing, drifting up between the stilts like the city's lights came loose and went swimming. Locals barely look up. The party will. | **Instinct/Knowledge** (Diff 12) to read the pattern — a hit clocks they're *steering away from the southwest,* same direction everything in this city quietly leans. Pure wonder on a miss; no cost. |
| **3** | **A trinket where a sock used to be.** Someone's pocket is a little heavier than it was: a smooth warm river-pebble, a brass button, a *weirdly useful* small thing. No one saw a hand. ([[Chausson]] says hello.) | **No roll — a gift.** Hand the PC a Hope and a grin. *(If they try to catch the culprit: Agility, Diff 15 — and they still won't.)* |
| **4** | **The neighbor who isn't.** An old regular feeds pigeons on a low rail — gentle, beloved, been here "longer than anybody, if you press it." For half a breath his **shadow falls the wrong way.** Then it doesn't. *(A small spirit-among-us; soft foreshadow of [[vael-miroux\|Vael]]'s world — never name it.)* | **Instinct** (Diff 14) — only a spirit-sensitive PC (Isotta) should catch it. A hit: *something about him reads like kin, like standing between.* File it; don't explain it. |
| **5** | **The edge that's losing.** Down a side-boardwalk, a family quietly lifts the windows out of a house the water already owns — no fuss, no drama, just salvage. A kid waves at the party like it's a normal day. Because it is. | **Presence** (Diff 12) to lend a hand without making it charity — a hit earns a real friend on the southwest line and the gut-truth that *the city is losing ground here.* (Soft tie to the wall / Oswin.) Mark 1 Stress either way — it sits with you. |
| **6** | **A street game with stakes.** Polers and dock-kids run a plank-balance dare over black water for coppers and bragging rights. Somebody eyes the party: *"Outsiders want a go?"* | **Agility** (Diff 12, rises to 15 in the wind) to run the plank — a hit wins coin, a crowd, and instant local cred. **Fail forward:** a cold dunk, 1 Stress, and a *better* story. Pure dice fun, low stakes. |

*Use these to pass time between the real stops, to cool down after a heavy beat, or when a player's at a loose end. The point is the same as the whole night: **wonder + personal pathos,** with the dice doing real work.*
