# The Dinner — An Imperial Welcome

*The evening set-piece. The party steps out of Sallowreach and into a manufactured slice of the capital to dine with the warmest, most dangerous man in the city. **No combat — a tense dinner.** The whole scene is a magic trick: the wonder is real before it's hollow, the host is kind before he's a threat, and every warm question is being filed. Full villain canon in [[caius-maren]]; run it from here.*

> **The one thing to hold in your head:** Caius is the most *likable* person the party will meet — and he is the antagonist of the whole imperial thread. Play him with zero menace. The dread comes entirely from the seams the party notices on their own: the servants that don't breathe, the questions that are too specific, the garden held up by three people sweating behind a hedge. **Let them do the noticing. Never underline it.**

---

## What's Really Going On *(GM — condensed; full plan in [[caius-maren]])*

Caius Maren is a **true believer in the greedy shape of Empire** — not the whole Empire, but its hungriest instinct made into one charming man. Manifest destiny with good table manners. He looks at Sallowreach — self-sufficient, communal, thriving without imperial roads or garrison or coin — and what he sees is a problem: **the city has too much soft power.** It doesn't *need* the Empire, so the Empire has no lever. He intends to fix that.

**His plan, in one line:** engineer a crisis so total that Sallowreach has to *beg* the Empire for help — and the Empire, arriving as savior, never leaves.

**The two engines of it:**
1. **Let the wall fail.** He denied [[Oswin Cray]]'s ward-maintenance budget through proper channels with pleasant, well-reasoned language. He *wants* the southwest wall to break — a Mire incursion, displaced families, a city overwhelmed. That's the disaster that completes his file. *(He has no idea what's actually behind the wall. He thinks he's engineering a bad flood. He's engineering a catastrophe.)*
2. **Win the information war.** Everything Caius sends to the capital paints Sallowreach as beautiful, valuable, and **dangerously ungoverned** — a place of navigational hazards and failing infrastructure that needs imperial protection. The **shipwreck was a manufactured data point** (dead imperial-adjacent travelers = the Mire is deadly = the Empire must step in). It failed — the party lived. So now he converts the survivors into **testimony instead.**

**Why the report matters (his real ask tonight):** a signed, formal incident account from credible outside witnesses is **document fourteen** in an annexation file that doesn't officially exist yet. It's not paperwork. It's ammunition.

**His vulnerability:** *none of this is sanctioned.* His mandate is *observe and report.* The wreck, the budget denial, the file — all unauthorized. If he's exposed before he files, the capital disavows him. He's a rogue officer who arranged a massacre on civilian water. He knows it. It's why he's fast.

---

## Caius in a Breath
Early thirties — younger than the role, which is his best disguise. Open-faced, warm, laughs readily and *means* it. Always a touch too pressed and dry for the swamp. He has done his homework on the party and the city; every compliment is also an appraisal; he treats Sallowreach with the fond, proprietary patience of a collector for a piece he's still negotiating for. **He does not think he is the villain.** He thinks he's the one adult in a room full of people too sentimental to save themselves. Call him a colonizer and you'd genuinely hurt his feelings.

**The surprise to keep in your pocket:** his love for this city is *real.* He walks the outer markets on his evenings off. The Celebration moved him and he wrote it in no report. None of it will stop him — but it's there, and once tonight, you let it flicker.

## The Living Staff *(the surprise — design them to unsettle)*

Caius does not employ people. People talk, forget, take sides. Instead the Embassy is run by **the Staff** — conjured attendants in flawless imperial livery, solid enough to pour your wine and carry a tray, and *hollow.* The Empire's answer to hospitality: service with no servant inside it.

- **Appearance:** tall, identical, immaculate. Faces a half-degree too symmetrical, too smooth — the uncanny of a portrait that's technically perfect. They don't sweat in the heat. They don't quite *blink* on the human schedule. Each wears a small brass pin with a **number,** and Caius calls them by it — *"Six, the wine."* *(The numbers skip. There's no Four. Nobody explains this.)*
- **The tell that they're not alive:** they move in **perfect anticipation** — a glass is refilled a half-second *before* you reach for it, a chair drawn before you turn to sit. They aren't reading *you.* They're reading the *script.*
- **The horror beat (give it to a wanderer):** a PC who slips off and catches a Staff member *unneeded* finds it standing in a corner, perfectly still, eyes open, not breathing, hands folded — a doll between commands — until it registers the PC and *smoothly* comes alive: *"May I bring you something?"* It was never off. It was *waiting.*
- **They are the surveillance.** A Staff member is always within earshot. They don't report to Caius — they simply *are* the record; he reviews the evening later the way you'd re-read a letter. If the party splits off to talk privately, one drifts near to "assist." You cannot get a truly private word inside these walls.
- **The gut-punch tie (drop only if a PC is looking hard):** the Staff are sustained by the same conjuration as the garden — **the three mages sweating behind the hedge.** The wine-pouring, the flawless service, the anticipation: three exhausted people working themselves grey are *puppeteering the help.* A sharp PC might catch a Staff member's motion **stutter** at the exact instant a mage falters. The Empire's effortless hospitality is other people's unseen labor. *That's the whole thesis, poured into your glass.*

> **Contrast to hold:** [[Chausson]] the sock-spirit is warm, messy, *alive,* and chose to stay. The Staff are perfect, cold, and cannot choose anything. That's Sallowreach vs. the Empire in two kinds of magic.

## The Beats

### Beat 1 — The Threshold *(the wonder — narrate it big)*
*Let them love it before they distrust it. Take your time here; this is the "amazing and weird and magical" you get to swing for.*

> An aide holds a door at the end of the inner boardwalk, and you step through — and you step *out of Sallowreach.*
> The heat goes first. Two hundred years of wet bayou air, gone between one stride and the next — the humidity drops, the sweat cools on your neck, and you're breathing something dry and clean and faintly sweet, like the first cool morning of an autumn none of you have felt since you came here. There is *grass* — real, green, close-cropped lawn-grass that has no business growing over black water — and pale gravel paths that crunch underfoot, warm, like they've been lying in a sun that isn't this one. Apple trees in neat rows stand in full white blossom *and* heavy with red fruit at the same time. A fountain runs somewhere near — and the water in it is *clear,* clear as glass, the first clear water any of you has seen since the Mire swallowed your boat.
> And the light is *wrong* in the loveliest way: goldener, lower, a late-afternoon sun where Sallowreach outside is going to evening dark — and when you find the little stone sundial by the path, its shadow reads an hour that matches neither. Capital time. Somebody built a piece of home out here on stilts over a swamp, down to the *hour of the day,* and it is, honestly, breathtaking. Gold butterflies drift. Birdsong threads the blossoms. It smells like cut grass and apple and warm stone — like somewhere a thousand miles away that isn't trying to kill you.

*GM — this is a gift; give it fully. Every PC should feel the pull of it — especially the ones homesick or worn down. THEN let the seams arrive on their own (Beat 3-onward, or whenever a PC pokes): the butterfly your hand passes through, the apple with no smell or crunch, no insects, no frogs, the birdsong that loops, the breeze on a schedule, and — behind a blossoming hedge — the three robed mages, eyes shut, hands open, **real sweat** running down their faces, holding all of it aloft. Drip these. Don't dump.*

### Beat 2 — The Staff *(greeting)*
Two of the Staff meet them at the threshold — a bow of exactly the right depth, a warm word of welcome delivered in a warmth that doesn't reach the eyes. They take cloaks (that a PC didn't offer). They know the party's names already. They lead them in, and one is always, quietly, *near.*

🎲 **Read the Staff** — *Instinct 14* → a hit lands the wrongness: no breath, no blink-rhythm, the anticipation. *"These aren't people."* (Isotta's spirit-sight reads them instantly as **not-alive-but-not-spirit** — a made thing, which unsettles her more than a ghost would.)

### Beat 3 — Caius, Delighted *(meeting the host)*
> In the middle of his impossible garden, at a table set with the best of everything, Caius Maren stands to greet you — and he is *delighted.* Genuinely. Younger than you expected, open-faced, dry and pressed and perfectly at ease, and he's poured the first wine himself, waving the Staff off to do it — the one task he does with his own hands.
> *"You came. Good — good. Sit, please, all of you. I've read three incident reports out of this Mire in two years and not one of them had survivors. And here you are, at my table. Eat something that hasn't been fried in swamp-fat — I had it brought in."*

*GM — he is wonderful company. Warm, curious, funny, attentive. Let the players be charmed and suspicious at once (they will be). The tension is that nothing he does is wrong — yet.*

> **Vivienne's armor (if she dressed them):** they arrive in **Sallowreach finery, not imperial fashion** — the city's quiet *"he doesn't own you."* Caius *feels* it and is mildly, genuinely put out. A small win before a word is spoken.

### Beat 4 — The Interview *(the wreck, and the slip)*
He asks about the shipwreck with real, gentle attention — *where exactly, what the water felt like, how long you were under.* He listens like every word is a gift. He is collecting testimony and does not appear to be.

> *"Can I ask — and stop me if it's too soon — where exactly did she go down? Out past the southwest channels, was it? And there were the — what, the four of you, and your guide?"* [a warm, sympathetic beat] *"How long were you in the water, before the light?"*

🎲 **Catch the slip** — *Instinct/Knowledge 13* → **he knows a detail he shouldn't.** Pick the one that bites: *"past the southwest channels" / "the four of you" / "before the light" / "survivors — plural"* — before they'd told him. A hit does **not** reveal he did it; it plants **"this man already knew about our wreck."** For **Tenson especially,** this is the man someone told the Guardian was dead. Let the divination itch. Don't pay it off.

### Beat 5 — The Philosophy *(manifest destiny, with a napkin in his lap)*
Somewhere over the main course he says the quiet part warmly. He believes it completely — that's what makes it land.

> *"People hear 'imperial integration' and they picture something taken. I've seen it work. What it actually looks like is infrastructure. Roads. Reliable transit. A ward that doesn't fail because one bureaucrat had a bad year."* [he gestures at the garden, the impossible apples, the clear water] *"This city is a miracle. It is also completely alone. No road, no bank, no law but whoever's kindest — and it works, right up until the season it doesn't. And when that season comes, who does it call?"* [a warm shrug, a sip] *"I'd like the answer to be someone who already loves it. Is that so sinister?"*

*GM — this is his thesis: Sallowreach's self-sufficiency (its "soft power") is exactly what he resents, because it means the Empire has no way in. He wants the city to **need** rescuing. Don't editorialize; let the party feel the chill of a man describing their home's destruction as a favor.*

### Beat 6 — The Ask *(document fourteen)*
Dessert, or the last of the wine. Framed as an afterthought, a kindness for strangers.

> *"One thing, and only if you're willing. Would you put it in writing? Just a plain account — where she went down, what the water was like, what you saw of the navigation. It helps me make the case to the capital that these waterways need proper attention. Future travelers deserve better than what happened to you."* [he slides a clean sheet and a good pen to the center of the table, unhurried] *"No rush. Take it with you. Send it whenever."*

🎲 **See the ask for what it is** — *Instinct/Knowledge 14* → it's not for future travelers; it's **evidence** that Sallowreach is dangerous and ungoverned. · **Decline without burning the bridge** — *Presence 14* → put it off gracefully (they stay a "useful asset"); a botched refusal turns them, subtly, into a *liability* he starts watching. *(If they sign: he's genuinely, warmly grateful — and it's the best thing that's happened to his file in a year. Let them almost not notice.)*

> **The folio (dramatic irony, optional):** Caius keeps a slim leather folio at his elbow and makes the occasional warm note during dinner — *"so I don't forget."* A PC who glimpses it (Finesse/Instinct) catches a tabbed edge: *…INCIDENT XI … INCIDENT XII …* — this account will be one more.

### Beat 7 — The Crack *(Tenson's gambit — the tense turn)*
If **Tenson** (or anyone) brings up **Oswin / the ward's lack of funds** — and Tenson the auditor almost certainly will — watch Caius closely, because this is the one place he isn't ready.

> [He goes still. Just for a breath — the warmth holds on his face but something behind it *sharpens.* And then, awful to see if you catch it: for half a second he looks almost **pleased,** the way a man looks when a stranger confirms something he badly wanted confirmed. Then it's gone, smoothed over, and he's sympathetic again:] *"The ward funding — yes. A shame. I understand the Guild's petition didn't clear review. Fiscal protocol; I'm sure it'll be sorted."* [he doesn't ask how you know. He wants very much to know how you know.]

🎲 **Catch the flicker** — *Instinct 14* → a hit reads the two-beat tell: *this man was **glad** to hear the wall is starving — and he is now, quietly, alarmed that you noticed.* It plants that **Caius and the failing ward are connected,** without saying how. *(GM: he denied that budget himself. An auditor pulling this thread is the most dangerous thing that's happened all night — and he knows it while smiling.)*

### Beat 8 — The One Real Thing *(the surprise pathos — use if the moment's right)*
On a side table, apart from all the conjured perfection, sits **one real, struggling potted plant** — a scrubby capital wildflower, half-wilted, the soil freshly watered. It's the only living thing in the entire Embassy that isn't pretending to be something else, and Caius waters it himself; the Staff aren't allowed to touch it.

> [if a PC notices it] *"That? It's nothing. It doesn't do well here — wrong air, wrong water. I keep meaning to let it go."* [he doesn't. He waters it every day. For one beat, the salesman is just a thirty-two-year-old a very long way from home.] *"...Anyway. More wine?"*

*GM — don't explain it. It's the flicker of the human under the instrument. It makes him harder to hate and worse to fight, which is the point.*

### Beat 9 — The Exit *("my door is open")*
He pays it no thought that it costs; he thanks them for their time with total sincerity; he hopes their "situation" resolves soon, and if the imperial offices can ever help — his door is open. A Staff member returns their cloaks and, with a bow, presses a small imperial calling-card into each hand.

> *"Come back anytime. Truly. It's a rare thing, good company this far from home."* [and he means it, and the Staff will remember your faces forever, and both of those are true at once]

## 🎯 Hints Menu — *drop the right amount, no more*
*The dinner's real content is what the party* almost *catches. Meter it: land 2-3 of these tonight, leave the rest as itch.*

| Hint | How it surfaces | Dose |
|---|---|---|
| **He already knew about the wreck** | the slip (Beat 4) | Land it — it's the hook. |
| **He wants the city to need saving** | the philosophy (Beat 5) | Land it — said openly, warmly. |
| **The report is a weapon, not a kindness** | the ask (Beat 6) | Land it if they roll or push. |
| **Caius ↔ the failing ward** | the Oswin crack (Beat 7) | Only if they raise Oswin. Huge if they do. |
| **The Empire's perfection is hollow, maintained by hidden labor** | the seams + the three mages + the Staff | Environmental — reward attention. |
| **He's lonely / his love for the city is real** | the plant (Beat 8) | Grace note. Optional. |

> **Guardrails — do NOT reveal tonight:**
> - **He arranged the shipwreck.** The slip plants suspicion, never proof. Proof comes later, through **Marcel.**
> - **Anything about the Mangrove/Tree Spirit or the binding.** Caius doesn't know it exists; he thinks he's engineering a flood, not a catastrophe.
> - **The petition / annexation file by name.** They feel a shape, not a document.
> - Keep it **social.** He is not a fighter; there is no combat here. If the table turns hostile, see below.

## 🎭 Caius — Voice Card
- **Body:** too pressed and dry for the humidity; open, easy eye-contact held *just* long enough; pours the first wine himself and waves off the Staff; makes small warm notes in a leather folio.
- **Voice:** warm, unhurried, sincere; compliments that are also appraisals; never says anything he doesn't mean to say; the quiet part delivered as reasonable kindness.
- **Signature lines:**
  - *"It deserves so much better infrastructure than it has. That's what I keep thinking. It deserves better."*
  - *"And when that season comes — who does it call? I'd like the answer to be someone who already loves it."*
  - *"You were fortunate to have someone like your guide."* [warm, filing every word]
  - *(the mask, only if truly cornered — not tonight)* *"This city is already decided. The only question is whether you're here when it happens."*

## ⏭️ If It Goes Sideways
- **They refuse the report flatly / rudely?** He's gracious about it — *"Of course, forget I asked"* — and quietly reclassifies them from *asset* to *watch.* No scene; a cooling.
- **They accuse him (of the wreck, of anything)?** He is *wounded,* not defensive — the true believer genuinely stung. *"I'm — I'm sorry you see it that way. I only want to help."* He gives them nothing because he has nothing to confess *to their faces.* Proof lives with Marcel.
- **They snoop / wander?** A Staff member materializes to "assist." Pushing past it toward his desk/papers is a real breach — he notices, and the temperature drops ten degrees under the smile.
- **They try to start a fight?** He simply won't be in the room for it — two imperial guards, a knife he's never used, and a lifetime of never being where violence happens. Don't let the dinner become a brawl; the payoff is the *realization,* carried out into the night.
- **They sign?** Warm gratitude, a real win for him, a debt they don't feel yet. Let it sit.
