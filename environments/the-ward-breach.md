# The Ward Breach / Mire Incursion

**Tier 2 Event**

*"The salt gives way without a sound — and what comes through the gap is not a wave. It is two hundred years of someone reaching for a thing they were promised, and the boardwalk is simply in the way."*

**Impulses:** Reach for what was taken, come *through* people rather than at them, drown the edge in sorrow, force the city to look at the thing in its heart.

**Difficulty:** 14

**Potential Adversaries:** [[Gault]] (driven in from the deep channels by the rising water, out of his depth for the first time and too stubborn to run); Mire predators driven inward rather than hunting — 2–3 glass snakes (SRD), a [[mire-bestiary#Bloodmidge Swarm|biting swarm]], or a single [[mire-bestiary#The Gloamjaw|Gloamjaw]] flushed from a drowned channel; in a near-catastrophe, the displaced families themselves are the "adversary" the clock fights to save.

---

## Features

**The Wall Lets Go - Passive:** A **Progress Countdown (6)** tracking how far the breach widens and the water climbs. Advance it 1 on any roll with Fear, 2 on a roll that fails *with* Fear, and 1 each time the party leaves the southwest face to deal with something else. At 3, the outer boardwalk is ankle-deep and footing is treacherous (Agility Rolls at the edge gain disadvantage [VERIFY: disadvantage source]). At 5, whole spans are floating loose, snapping their moorings. At 6, the southwest outer ring is *gone* — see **The Edge Gives Way**. Filling a competing **Countdown** the party builds (shoring the salt-line, channeling the water, reaching the families) can halt or reverse this; this is the dial the whole scene turns on. *(GM: scale the starting value — set it lower for a partial disaster already in motion, higher for a breach the party can still avert. Honor the Interlock from [[world-in-motion]]: if the Celebration was disrupted, the wall was already humming wrong this morning.)*

**It Reaches Through You - Passive:** This is not an attack — it is presence. Any character Close to the open water that holds an attachment, a grief, or a thing they have lost makes a **Presence Reaction Roll** at the start of a scene. On a failure they mark 2 Stress as the longing pours in and finds the shape of *their* longing — a dead parent's voice in the current, a home they can't return to, a name they buried. On a success with Fear, they feel it anyway and gain a vision the GM may use as a clue (the spirit reaching for something at the city's *heart*, not its edge). This never deals HP damage. The water does not want to hurt them. It barely knows they are there. [VERIFY: Presence Reaction Roll target — suggest vs. Difficulty 14]

**The Slow Tide - Action:** The water doesn't surge; it *rises*, patient and total. Make a Reaction Roll for a target near the failing edge: on a failure they take 1d8+2 physical and are moved Close toward the open Mire, dragged not by force but by the sheer reaching weight of the water. A character who spends their action *holding onto another person* (an elder, a child, a panicking neighbor) instead auto-succeeds the grab but takes the movement themselves. The Mire would rather take you gently than at all.

**Driven Inward - Action:** *Spend a Fear.* The flood pushes the deep Mire's creatures ahead of it. Summon a fleeing Mire beast (see Potential Adversaries) within Close range of the party — it is not hunting them, it is *terrified*, and it will trample, thrash, and bolt toward solid ground through anyone standing there. It can be calmed, herded, or simply survived; killing it is the least interesting option and the GM should make that plain.

**The Edge Gives Way - Reaction:** *Spend a Fear* (free if **The Wall Lets Go** reaches its final tick). A span of the southwest outer boardwalk — a home, a market row, a procession route from last week's Celebration — tears loose and begins to founder. Every creature on it makes an Agility Reaction Roll: failure means going into the black water (1d10+3 physical and Vulnerable until pulled out), success means a desperate scramble to a still-anchored span. Anyone who chose to be there shielding a family rolls with the family's fate riding on the same dice. This is the moment the abstract becomes a great-grandmother's house, sinking.

**The Pull Toward the Heart - Reaction:** *Spend a Fear.* Once per scene, when a character resists the water — braces a wall, hauls someone clear, refuses to let go — the presence *turns its attention.* The current near them stills for a breath, the bioluminescence gathers and pulses inland (toward the city's center, toward the bound [[Tree Spirit]] in the Hall of the Mire), and every character Close marks 1 Stress from the sheer ache of it. The water is not fighting back. For one terrible instant, it is *grateful for the contact* — and then it keeps reaching past them, because what it wants was never here at the edge.

---

## Feature Questions

- *Which displaced family is on the span that gives way — and does a PC know their name before the dice are cast?*
- *What does the reaching water wear when it finds each character's grief — whose voice, whose face, which loss?*
- *Is the party here to avert a catastrophe that hasn't fully landed, or to pull survivors from one already underway?*
- *When the presence stills and turns inward toward the city's heart, who notices that it isn't attacking — and what do they start to suspect?*

## Running This

This is a **disaster payoff**, not a monster fight — the climax the fronts in [[world-in-motion]] have been bending toward (Front 2 "The Wall Thins" reaching "The Breach," coupled through the Interlock to the binding). Trigger it when the dials say the southwest salt-ward has failed; **scale it freely.** A high Countdown and a strong competing clock make it a near-catastrophe the party can avert. A low Countdown, or one already half-spent, makes it a partial disaster where the question stops being "can we stop it" and becomes "who do we save."

Do not let it read as anger. The single most important table move: every time the water does something awful, describe it as *reaching*, not *raging*. It comes through people, finds their grief, and is sorry without knowing how to be. The horror is that it isn't malicious — it is bereaved, and the city built its whole life on top of the reason. Save the **Pull Toward the Heart** for the moment a player commits everything to holding the line; that beat — the water grateful for the touch and then reaching *past* them, inland — is the campaign's thesis in one Fear.

Do **not** script who caused the breach or how it ends. [[Oswin Cray]]'s underfunded ward, [[Fen]]'s frayed binding, [[Caius Maren]]'s denied budget, and the rising grief are all upstream causes; let the table's history decide the mix. The party may shore it, channel it, reach the families, expose the cause, or stand in the water and refuse to move. Whatever they do, the grief underneath is not solved here — only met. [[The Drowning Edge]] covers the survivor-rescue scenes in detail; lean on it for the human face of the **Edge Gives Way** and **Slow Tide** features.
