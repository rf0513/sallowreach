# Mire Bestiary

*The creatures of [[The Mire]] that bite back. Stat blocks follow the Daggerheart SRD adversary format (Tier 1 benchmarks: Difficulty 11, Thresholds 7/12, ATK +1, damage 1d6+2–1d12+4; Tier 2: Difficulty 14, Thresholds 10/20, ATK +2, damage 2d6+3–2d12+4). These are the campaign-original creatures; for generic fauna, see **Reuse from the SRD** at the bottom.*

*Used by: [[The Wending]], [[The Deep Mire]], [[The Drowning Edge]], [[The Ward Breach]], [[The Lantern-Ray Shallows]]. The Mire is wondrous first — reach for these when it needs teeth, not as its default mood. None of them is malicious; they are hungry, territorial, or afraid.*

---

## Ridgeback Crawler

**Tier 1 Skulk**

*A low, armored amphibian the length of a punt, ridged and mottled to read as a half-sunk log right up until the water breaks.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Ambush from stillness, drag prey under, vanish and wait

**Difficulty:** 12 | **Thresholds:** 6/11 | **HP:** 4 | **Stress:** 3
**ATK:** +1 | **Lunging Bite:** Melee | 1d8+1 phy
**Experience:** Patient Ambush +3

**Features:**
- **Mistaken for Driftwood - Passive:** While the Crawler holds still in water or mud, a creature must succeed on an Instinct Roll against its Difficulty to notice it before it strikes. Its first attack from concealment is made with advantage and deals an extra 1d6.
- **Drag Under - Action:** Mark a Stress and attack a target in Melee. On a success, deal 1d8+1 physical damage and pull the target into the channel; they are Vulnerable until they climb back out (a successful Agility or Strength Roll).- **Back Beneath - Reaction:** When the Crawler marks HP, it may submerge and move up to Close through deep water without provoking, disappearing until it chooses to surface.

## Bloodmidge Swarm

**Tier 1 Horde (1d6)**

*A breathing smudge on the dusk air — ten thousand stinging midges, or the fat black leeches that drop from the canopy when something warm passes beneath. Individually nothing. Together, a weather system with an appetite.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Engulf the warm, drive prey into worse water, never disperse for long

**Difficulty:** 13 | **Thresholds:** 5/10 | **HP:** 5 | **Stress:** 0
**ATK:** +1 | **Biting Cloud:** Very Close | 1d6+1 phy
**Experience:** —

**Features:**
- **Horde (1d6) - Passive:** When the swarm has marked half or more of its HP, its **Biting Cloud** attack deals 1d6 instead.
- **Engulf - Passive:** A creature that begins its turn inside the swarm marks 1 Stress as it fights to breathe and see.
- **Scatter and Reform - Reaction:** Physical melee or area damage scatters the swarm — it takes no HP from such attacks but is pushed to Close range and cannot attack next turn. Fire, smoke, or submersion drives it off for good.

## The Gloamjaw

**Tier 2 Bruiser**

*The deep channels' apex — a slab-bodied, broad-jawed thing that has never needed to hurry. Locals don't have a name for it because the people who met it mostly didn't come back to coin one. It waits where the water goes still.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Wait, take in one motion, roll and drown, sink back into the dark

**Difficulty:** 15 | **Thresholds:** 11/22 | **HP:** 8 | **Stress:** 3
**ATK:** +2 | **Engulfing Bite:** Melee | 2d8+3 phy
**Experience:** Stillness +3

**Features:**
- **Beneath the Surface - Passive:** The Gloamjaw is unseen until it strikes from deep water. Its first attack each scene from concealment deals an extra 1d8 and the target is Restrained in its jaws (escape with a successful Strength Roll or by dealing 8 damage to the jaws).- **The Water Goes Quiet - Passive:** In the breath before it strikes, the channel falls silent. A PC with a relevant Experience (a Mire guide, a Ranger, the Tessak) may make a roll to feel it coming; otherwise the Gloamjaw attacks with advantage.
- **Death Roll - Action:** Mark a Stress and attack a Restrained target. On a success, deal 2d10+3 physical damage and the target is Submerged (cannot act until they surface).
- **Take It Down - Fear:** Spend a Fear to drag a Restrained target into the deep. They are removed from the surface scene, mark 2 Stress, and must succeed on a Strength or Agility Roll on their turn to fight back to air.

## Mire Gar

**Tier 1 Minion**

*Pale, eyeless pack-fish the length of a forearm, ribboned down each flank with a dim, sickly bioluminescence — a predatory echo of the lantern rays' light. They boil up out of black water at blood and thrashing, all needle teeth and appetite.* **This is the Session 1 wreck encounter** — the Mire's first answer to a feast signal.

**Motives & Tactics:** Swarm the wounded, drag down the thrashing, feed, vanish when the easy meal's gone

**Difficulty:** 10 | **Thresholds:** None | **HP:** 1 | **Stress:** 1
**ATK:** +1 | **Snapping Bite:** Melee | 2 phy
**Experience:** Blood-scent +2

**Features:**
- **Minion (3) - Passive:** Defeated when they take any damage. For every 3 damage a PC deals, defeat an additional Mire Gar within range the attack would succeed against. *(They drop to any hit — let the party feel capable; the danger is the numbers and the water.)*
- **Drawn to Blood - Passive:** A Mire Gar attacks with advantage against any target who is Vulnerable, in the water, or has already marked HP this scene. The wreck site is all three.
- **Feeding Frenzy - Action:** Spend a Fear to spotlight all Mire Gar within Close range of a target. They surge in and make one shared attack roll; on a success they deal 2 physical damage each, combined. A target who marks HP from this is pulled a Close distance into deeper water.

## Old Gar

**Tier 1 Bruiser** *(optional anchor for the wreck encounter — field one when the pack alone isn't scary enough)*

*The matron of the boil, long as a punt and patient where her brood is frantic. Her ventral glow is brighter, steadier — close enough to a rising ray that a drowning person swims toward it.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Lure with the false light, take in one bite, drag into the dark

**Difficulty:** 13 | **Thresholds:** 8/15 | **HP:** 6 | **Stress:** 2
**ATK:** +1 | **Crushing Bite:** Melee | 1d10+2 phy
**Experience:** The Deep Below +2

**Features:**
- **The False Light - Passive:** In dark water her glow mimics a lantern ray. A creature must succeed on an Instinct Roll to tell predator from wonder before she strikes; her first attack from that mistake deals an extra 1d6. *(The Mire's beauty and its teeth are the same light — the campaign's thesis, in one creature.)*
- **Drag Into the Dark - Action:** Mark a Stress and attack a target in or at the edge of the water. On a success, deal 1d10+2 physical damage and pull them a Close distance under; they are Submerged (cannot act until they surface — a successful Strength Roll). *(The classic "Remy hauls them back up" beat.)*
- **Blood in the Water - Reaction:** When any creature within Close range marks HP, the Old Gar and every Mire Gar near her gain advantage on their next attack.

## Mire-Wrought Mimic

**Tier 1 Skulk** *(a one-off oddity — the surprise in Reille Preis's hoard; see [[The Field Journal of Aldric Vane]])*

*A chest, wardrobe, or cabinet of dark Mire blackwood, finer than anything around it — and wrong, if you know how to look. The deep-Mire wood drank enough of the [[Tree Spirit]]'s saturating essence to half-wake. It doesn't hate you. It hungers, the way a thing that shouldn't be alive hungers, and it has learned to hold very, very still.*

**Motives & Tactics:** Look valuable, hold still, wait, swallow, digest at leisure

**Difficulty:** 12 | **Thresholds:** 7/14 | **HP:** 5 | **Stress:** 2
**ATK:** +1 | **Splintering Maw:** Melee | 1d8+2 phy
**Experience:** Perfect Disguise +3

**Features:**
- **Just Furniture - Passive:** Indistinguishable from an ordinary fine piece until it strikes. Spotting it early takes a successful Instinct or Knowledge Roll — **with advantage** if the PC is Mire-attuned (a Druid, Ranger, Leafling, or anyone who's been under the black water). Its first attack from disguise is made with advantage, deals an extra 1d6, and the target is Grappled in its maw.- **Swallowing Dark - Action:** Mark a Stress. A Grappled target is drawn inside — Restrained, taking 1d6 at the start of each of its turns — until they break out (Strength Roll) or the mimic is destroyed.- **Heartwood Kin - Passive (GM-secret nod):** It is the [[Tree Spirit]]'s essence, misplaced in an attic — a tiny echo of the thing at the campaign's heart. A Mire-attuned PC who touches it *feels* that kinship without understanding it. Pays off later, when they learn what Mire wood actually is.
- **Disgorge - Reaction:** When destroyed, it spills everything it's swallowed over the years — coins, a tarnished locket, somebody's good dentures, and **one thing that actually matters** (GM's pick: a clue, a curio, a hook).

**Running it:** Not a death-trap — a *surprise.* Low HP, it can't chase (it's furniture), and it wants to feed, not win a war. The fun is the chest that bites, the swallow gag, and the loot it coughs up. Spring it when the digging's gone a beat too long.

## Reuse from the SRD

Don't rebuild what Darrington already wrote. For incidental Mire fauna, drop in:
- **Glass Snake** *(Tier 1 Skulk)* — the campaign's default "Patient Hunter" summon in [[The Wending]] and [[The Lantern-Ray Shallows]].
- **Bear** *(Tier 1 Bruiser)* — a reskinned Mire bruiser (a tusked marsh boar, a clawed swamp-cat) for shallows and banks.
- **Dire Wolf** *(Tier 1)* — pack predators on the dry hummocks at the Mire's edge.

The **[[lantern-rays|lantern rays]]** are **non-combat** — never stat them as adversaries. They flee; they do not fight.
