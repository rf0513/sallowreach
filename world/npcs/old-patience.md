# Old Patience

**Type:** Lesser Mire Spirit
**Location:** [[The Mire]] — the Still Pool, an unnamed break in the mangroves off the southwest Wending channel
**Tier:** 3 (not a combat encounter — tier reflects age and power, not tactical threat)

*The locals have a name for it passed down through the punt families: Old Patience. Not a name it chose. A name it accumulated, the way still water accumulates things.*

---

## Nature

Old Patience has been in this Mire longer than the city has been above it. It is not the [[Mangrove Spirit]]. It is not the [[Tree Spirit]]. It is something smaller and more local — a spirit of stillness, of deep water, of the particular quality of the Mire at its oldest and least disturbed. No agenda. A territory, a patience, and a set of protocols that predate anything the party has encountered.

The city of [[Sallowreach]] has been leaving it offerings for two hundred years without knowing it specifically exists. The offerings drift down the southwest channel and collect in the Still Pool. Old Patience receives them with the same still attention it gives everything. It does not distinguish between offerings meant for it and offerings meant for the Mangrove Spirit — it receives what comes. It is, technically, an honored guest of Sallowreach. The city just doesn't know its name.

It does not seek human company. Most humans it ignores entirely. The last living person it spoke to, Caspar Renne cannot find in his records — he has references to the Still Pool in punt family oral histories, a single first-hand account from three generations ago, and nothing since.

**The party is different.** The tether marks them. They went under the Mire's black water and came up changed — carrying spirit-water marking that anything with spirit-sight reads immediately. They are, technically, of the Mire now. Old Patience has been watching them since they arrived in the city. They are unusual enough to be worth examining.

It has also been feeling, for some time, a pressure in the deep water. Something very large moving toward Sallowreach with intention. This has made Old Patience unsettled in a way it does not have a name for.

---

## The Hook — Lenne

**Lenne** is nine years old, granddaughter of the matriarch of the oldest outer boardwalk holdout family — the one with forty years of tide journals. She went to the family's Mire-edge shrine that morning to leave an offering. She didn't come back.

This is not unusual in the way that missing children in the Mire are usually not unusual — the family knows the Mire, they know the risks, they know the outer boardwalks are being swallowed. But the shrine is close. She knows the route. She's done it a dozen times. Her grandmother is not panicking outwardly. She is very, very still.

The family has already sent word to [[The Guide]]. The Guide will ask the party to come.

---

## Finding the Still Pool

Lenne is at the Still Pool. Unharmed. Sitting at the edge of the water with her offering still in her hands — a small twist of dried Mire grass and a bright button she'd been saving — looking at the water with the specific expression of someone who sat down for a moment and forgot to stand up again.

She is not entranced, not hurt, not touched by anything hostile. The pool is just very still, and she sat down at its edge and the stillness got into her, and she didn't know how to break it.

Old Patience had been mildly examining her — a Sallowreach child with a sincere offering, more present than most of what drifts down the channel. When the party arrives with tether marks, the examination redirects. Lenne blinks. Looks around. *"Oh,"* she says, confused and slightly embarrassed. *"How did it get so late?"*

She will tell the party, on the way back, that she saw something while she was sitting there. A single image, very clear, like looking through glass: *two lights close together, almost touching but not quite.* She doesn't know what it means. She's not frightened by it. It was, she says, the most beautiful thing she's ever seen.

[**GM note:** [[Delphine]] will recognize this description immediately. It matches her vision exactly — *two lights moving toward each other across immense distance, a suspended chord finally resolving.* Connect these in whatever scene makes the most narrative impact.]

---

## Appearance

After Lenne is found and clearly safe, the Guide keeps the punt still. Waits.

The bioluminescence in the pool begins to organize. Not glow more brightly — *organize.* Patterns resolve into something with direction and attention. The light arranges into a shape that suggests a face the way a cloud suggests a face: once you see it, you cannot unsee it.

It is only ever in the water. Never above it.

The temperature drops slightly. Not cold. Still.

---

## The Protocols

Old Patience does not explain its rules. Violation results not in violence but in withdrawal — and the Mire becoming a harder place afterward. The bioluminescence stops. Channels that were readable become confusing. The sense of being watched without anything being interested.

The Guide knows these. A party member with strong Instinct might intuit them. Otherwise they discover through play.

**1. Wait.** Do not speak first. It will speak when it decides to. Initiating tells it the party doesn't understand what it is, and it will wait them out indefinitely.

**2. Do not lie.** Not a moral rule — a practical one. It knows. The conversation stops, and the liar receives a quality of attention that is very uncomfortable to be on the receiving end of.

**3. The offering rule.** If it offers something — information, an object, an experience — decline the first time (courtesy), accept the second (genuine exchange), never receive a third offer (it has decided the party is not worth the trouble).

**4. When it is done, it is done.** The light disperses. Do not follow. Do not call after it.

---

## What It Communicates

Old Patience communicates in something between language and impression. The Guide translates about half; the rest arrives directly. A party member who is quiet and still gets more than one who is talking.

**The first thing:** *"You carry two marks. The city's mark and the water's mark. You are the first in a long time."* — It has seen tethered people before. Not recently. If asked what happened to the last ones: *"They found what they were looking for."* It will not clarify. The implication should make the party uneasy, and the GM should leave it that way.

**The second thing:** *"Something in the deep water has been answering something in the city for longer than I have attended to it. It is very close now. It makes the water feel like waiting."* — The Mangrove Spirit's approach, described from the inside by something that lives in the same water. It does not know their names or their story. It knows the pressure of something enormous moving with purpose.

**The third thing** it shows rather than says: the bioluminescence in the pool briefly arranges into a view of the Mire from above — every channel, every pool, the lines of current and movement. The southwest direction is visibly, unmistakably brighter. Everything flows toward Sallowreach from there. Has been flowing that way. The pattern is not subtle once you can see the whole thing.

Then the light disperses.

---

## The Reward

Old Patience offers something on the second offering — after the protocols are followed correctly.

It offers **partial relief of the tether.**

It is made of the same spirit-water magic that created the tether. It understands the mark. What it gives is not a cure — the underlying binding is still unresolved — but it softens. The agony that meets the party at the Mire's boundary becomes quieter, the wall less sharp. They can go further toward the edge before the resistance becomes impassable. Not out. But closer to out than before.

This is the first concrete sign that the tether *can* change. That it is not simply a permanent condition. That something in the Mire has the ability to touch it. The full cure is still out there, still requires the work — but this tells the party they are not chasing nothing.

The Guide has no framework for what just happened. They will not speak for a while.

---

## After the Encounter

When the party returns Lenne to her grandmother, the matriarch will look at them differently. She has lived on the outer boardwalk her whole life. She knows what the Still Pool is. She knows no one goes there.

She will not forget this.

---

## Connections

- [[Caspar Renne]] has the pool referenced in three oral history transcriptions and one first-hand account. He has never been there and would have considerable feelings about the party having been. The name "Old Patience" appears in his oldest punt family documents — he can confirm it, add context, and be visibly envious.
- [[Delphine]] will recognize Lenne's description of the two lights. This should happen in a scene, not offhand.
- The Mangrove Spirit will know the party has spoken with Old Patience. It will not comment on this. The party will be able to tell.

## GM Notes

**When:** Mid-Act 2, as the party prepares their push toward the Mangrove Spirit. Lenne's disappearance is the trigger.

**Tone:** The quietest scene in the campaign. Long pauses. The Guide barely breathing. The Mire feeling genuinely ancient for the first time.

**If a protocol is violated:** Old Patience withdraws immediately. The party found the child — that part is complete. But the encounter is over. They missed the tether relief. They missed the Mire-from-above vision. The southwest pattern they'll have to piece together from the tide journals alone, which takes longer. This is a real consequence, not a punishment.

**On the previous tethered people:** Leave *"they found what they were looking for"* unexplained. Do not invent a story for it. Let the party decide whether that's hope or warning. It is possibly both.
