## 🎲 The Swim — how the rolls work *(kills the DC confusion)*

The swim's danger is **the water, not the gar's teeth.**
- Call **environmental action rolls vs the current** — Agility/Strength, **Difficulty 15–16** (hard for Tier 1). These are *not* attacks against gar.
- **Failure ≠ damage.** Failure = **mark Stress, you gain Fear, the gar close, the PC slips toward deeper water** (→ Vulnerable → risk Submerged).
- **Gar die to any hit** (minions, Diff 10) — striking back *feels* good. The dread is the numbers + the cold.
- **One PC falls behind** (the dice, or Isotta — she owns the payoff). Build real "I'm going to drown" dread (Read-Aloud Beat 6).
- **Spring the rays (Beat 7) BEFORE any death roll lands.** Rays scatter the gar instantly = fight over. Don't grind.

## 🎲 Daggerheart Basics *(if you blank mid-session)*

*Verified against the [official SRD](https://daggerheartsrd.com/rules/). Daggerheart is **not** D&D — these differ, so read once.*

- **Action roll:** player rolls **2d12 Duality Dice** (one Hope, one Fear) + trait vs the **Difficulty** you set. **Total ≥ Difficulty = success.**
- **Hope vs Fear** *(whichever die is higher colors it):* **Hope die higher →** player gains a **Hope**. **Fear die higher →** *you* gain a **Fear** — even on a success ("you succeed, but…"). **Matching dice = critical success** (auto-succeed, +Hope, clear a Stress, crit damage).
- **Advantage = +1d6 added, NOT a reroll.** Roll one extra d6 and **add** it (disadvantage subtracts one). They cancel 1-for-1; never more than one net die. *(Every "advantage" in the blocks below means exactly this.)*
- **Damage → HP via Thresholds (listed X/Y = Major/Severe):** below X = mark **1 HP** · ≥ X = **2 HP** · ≥ Y = **3 HP**. *(Thresholds 7/14, hit for 9 → mark 2 HP.)*
- **Your Fear:** gain it when players roll with Fear; **spend it anytime** to make/enhance a GM move or trigger a **Fear** feature (e.g. the gar's Feeding Frenzy). Cap 12, carries between sessions.

## 🩹 Conditions — verified rulings *(✅ = official SRD wording)*

| Condition | Ruling for play |
|---|---|
| **Vulnerable** ✅ | All rolls **targeting** the creature have **advantage** (+1d6). *(PC in deep/dark water = Vulnerable.)* |
| **Restrained** ✅ | **Can't move**, but **can still act** from where they are. Escape per the feature (Str/Finesse roll, or deal the listed damage to net/line/bola). |
| **Hidden** ✅ | Rolls **against** a Hidden creature have **disadvantage**; lost the moment they're seen, move into view, or attack. |
| **Grappled** *(feature-defined — SRD allows this)* | Not a core condition; treat as **Restrained + physically held**, and the adversary **drags you** when it moves (the harpoon-line). Same escape options. |
| **Submerged** *(feature-defined — SRD allows this)* | Dragged **under & out of the surface scene; cannot act.** On your turn, spend your action: **Str or Agility vs Diff 15** to surface; while under you're also **Vulnerable**. **⚠ Session-1 safety: dread, not death** — the rays/Remy guarantee a surface before anyone dies. |
| **Resistance** ✅ | **Halve** the damage **before** comparing to thresholds. *(Only the Tier-2 Guard uses it — not a Session-1 concern.)* |

## ⚔️ Encounter A — The Swim *(Read-Aloud 5–7)*

**Budget ~2–4 BP** (start ~3 gar/PC, add by blood). Both encounters deliberately **under-budget** — they're half-drowned and Remy is the net.

### MIRE GAR — Tier 1 **Minion**
**Diff 10** · HP 1 · Stress 1 · **ATK +1, Snapping Bite (Melee) 2 phy**
- **Minion (3):** defeated by **any damage**; for every **3 damage** a PC deals, drop **another** gar in range.
- **Drawn to Blood:** **advantage** vs a target who is Vulnerable, in the water, or already marked HP. *(The wreck is all three.)*
- **Feeding Frenzy — Fear:** spotlight all gar in Close of a target → one shared attack, **2 phy each combined**; a target who marks HP is **pulled Close into deeper water**.

### OLD GAR — Tier 1 **Bruiser** *(optional anchor — field if the pack alone isn't scary)*
**Diff 13** · Th **8/15** · HP 6 · Stress 2 · **ATK +1, Crushing Bite 1d10+2**
- **The False Light:** glow mimics a ray — **Instinct roll** to tell predator from wonder; her first strike from that mistake **+1d6**.
- **Drag Into the Dark — Action:** Stress + attack; hit = 1d10+2 and target **Submerged**.
- **Blood in the Water — Reaction:** when anyone Close marks HP, Old Gar + nearby gar get **advantage** on their next attack.

> **End on the rays.** They scatter the boil instantly (predators respect the light — canon). Don't roll death.

## ⚔️ Encounter B — The Thing on the Bank *(Read-Aloud 9)*

**Budget:** 1 Scout (2 BP) + optionally one more gar group via *Sound the Channel*. **Do not field a second scout.** Run the man-eater Tessak as a **predator, not a victim** — no NPC explains the clan yet.

### TESSAK SCOUT — Tier 1 **Skulk**
**Diff 12** · Th **7/14** · HP 6 · Stress 3
**ATK +1** · **Barbed Harpoon (Close) 1d8+2** · **Weighted Bola (Far) 1d6 + Restrained**
- **Of the Deep Channels:** never Lost, ignores terrain/current, stays submerged at will — **advantage on nearly everything in water.**
- **Haul It Home — Action:** Stress + attack Close; hit = 1d8+2 and target **Grappled** on the line. **Each of its turns it drags the Grappled target Close toward deep water.** Escape: Str/Finesse roll, or an ally deals **6 dmg to the line.** *(The horror is the dragging-off, not the bite.)*
- **Food That Walks:** always works the **most wounded/isolated** target; first attack each scene vs such a target = **advantage**.
- **Sound the Channel — Fear:** trill summons a fresh **Mire Gar** group at the blood.
- **Slip the Net — Reaction:** on marking HP it may submerge + move Close, vanishing. **It does not die for one meal** — if costly, it **flees, with its catch if it has one.**

> **Remy is afraid of it** — the Mire's biggest believer goes cold. *"Off the bank. Now. Quiet. They felt the wreck same as the gar did, and they don't scatter."*
> **Track two flags:** did it **escape**? did it escape **with someone**? If it gets away clean, **the clan + [[Gault]] now know outsiders survived.** Its abandoned **salvage boat = the party's ride to town** ([[session-01-read-aloud#10 · The Crossing|Beat 10]]).

## 🔥 Fear Menu — what to spend it on tonight

*You'll **bank Fear fast** across two fights (every player Fear-roll = +1). New GMs hoard it — don't. **A Session 1 that ends near full Fear under-used the engine.** Spend it for **dread and complication, not cheap damage** — remember the swim is "no deaths," the rays are the scripted out. Verified uses ([SRD](https://daggerheartsrd.com/rules/core-gm-mechanics/)): spend Fear to **make an extra/enhanced GM move**, **spotlight an additional adversary**, or **trigger a Fear feature.***

**⚙️ Fear features already on your blocks:**
- **Mire Gar — Feeding Frenzy:** all gar in Close make one shared bite, **pull the victim into deeper water.** *(The swim's escalator.)*
- **Tessak Scout — Sound the Channel:** trill summons a **fresh gar boil** at the blood. *(Don't field a 2nd scout — field more teeth.)*

**🎭 Narrative spends (official GM moves → tonight's flavor):**
- **Shift the environment** → the current sweeps the fallen-behind PC another Close into the dark; the wreck **groans and a section collapses,** splitting the party from shore.
- **Make a PC mark Stress** → the cold reaches their chest, limbs stop answering — *this is how it happens* (Beat 6 drowning dread).
- **Capture someone important** → enhance the scout's **Haul It Home:** drag its catch fully **under, out of the scene for a round.** The campaign's signature horror — a person vanishing toward the black.
- **Signal an imminent off-screen threat** → a **second wake** moves against the current. *More* is coming. (You never have to field it — the dread is free.)
- **Reveal an unwelcome danger** → the **Old Gar's false light swells like a rising ray** — they swim *toward* the predator. The Mire's beauty and its teeth, the same glow.
- **Use a PC's backstory** → for the primed PC, **the Turn stirs** — the deep glow, the iron-and-green taste (plant it deeper); or **Isotta's** spirit-murmur sharpens into a near-voice.
- **Make an NPC act on motive** → **Remy's competence cracks into open fear** at the Tessak — wordless proof of how bad this is.
- **Spotlight an additional adversary** → during the swim, a **second gar group surges the same turn** (extra Fear per extra adversary).

> **Rule of thumb:** if a beat feels safe and you want a knot in the players' stomachs — spend a Fear and pick a move above. If a PC is genuinely about to die — *don't*; spring the rays instead.

## 🗝️ Secrets & Clues *(reveal ~half; carry the rest)*

1. The black water did something to all of them — **every road out ends the same:** resistance, agony, back where they started.
2. Sallowreach is reachable only by hours of shifting channels — **no road in or out.** That's why it's never been conquered.
3. **The Mire is growing** — boardwalks clear two seasons ago are choked; the SW edge is losing ground.
4. Once a week **the whole city celebrates,** without fail; missing it already *feels wrong in the body.*
5. The city calls the swamp's presences **honored guests** — you welcome, you don't command.
6. Remy was a passenger too; **amphibious,** found them by following the rays to the shallows.
7. **The healer looks at the group a beat too long** — like she recognizes something she can't name.
8. The weekly lanterns are **the exact shape & color of the rays** that lit the water as they drowned. *(Plant quietly.)*
9. The wizard maintaining the **SW salt-ward is behind** on his work; the edge families have noticed. *(Background hum.)*
10. **PC-personalized:** someone back home is starting to notice a PC never arrived. **Tenson's is live** — [[Magistra Silence]] was told the ship was lost, refuses to believe it, her first letter is coming. ⟦Fletcher / Isotta: drop in their names.⟧
