# Oswin Cray

**Role:** Mages Guild ward maintainer
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — the Maintenance House, ward perimeter (Mire-edge, south side)
**Faction:** [[Mages Guild]]
**Tier:** 2

---

## Appearance

Mid-fifties, round-shouldered in the specific way of someone who was once properly upright and stopped bothering. He wears his Guild robes — grey and blue, the institutional colors of a mid-rank field practitioner — but the hem has been shortened twice because the Mire kept wicking up into the fabric and he couldn't face the paperwork to requisition new ones. The Guild signet ring on his right hand is real silver. The fingernails on his left hand are bitten to the quick.

His face is pleasant when he's performing pleasantness: a ready smile, good teeth, eyes that engage. When he forgets to perform — staring out at the Mire, doing the maintenance calculations in his head for the thousandth time — his expression goes somewhere flat and very tired. He blinks too much in bright light. He's not sleeping well.

He smells faintly of salt. You do, eventually, here.

---

## Personality & Mannerisms

Oswin Cray went to a good school. He knows it, in the way people know things they've stopped being able to use. He's articulate, educated, reasonably funny when not grinding under the weight of his own disaster, and almost physically hungry for conversation with anyone who can hold a complex thought.

When the party first meets him — especially if they are visibly worldly, educated, or simply polite — he will brighten in a way that is immediately pitiable and immediately endearing. He will offer tea. He will ask where they're from. He will ask what they do. He will ask follow-up questions. He has been talking to Sallowreach locals for four years and they are perfectly pleasant and have absolutely nothing to say to him about anything he cares about.

**He talks too much.** Not out of malice — out of loneliness and the habit of a man who fills silences before they can fill him. He'll start a sentence, realize it's gone long, say *"no, you don't need to know all that"* and then tell you all that anyway. He makes self-deprecating jokes about the posting with exactly the forced cheer of someone who has made the same joke many times and stopped finding it funny.

**He is genuinely knowledgeable.** Ward mechanics, Mire behavior, the history of the posting (what records he has of it) — this is the one area where he still has authority, and he will hold it with both hands. He explains well. He's done it to himself so many times in the dark that teaching it to someone new feels almost like relief.

**He does not lie well.** His face does something when a topic gets close to Marcel, or the money, or the ward budget. He changes the subject with a pivot so hard it practically audible. He's not practiced at deception — he's practiced at avoidance, which is different and less effective.

**He still gambles.** Not as much. He told himself: just once a week, just to have somewhere to go, just to feel something for an hour. He will not bring this up.

---

## The Ward

The ward is not a circle. It never was. The Mire expands from the southwest — everyone in Sallowreach knows this, everyone can see it — so the ward is a salt barrier along the southwest face of the city: a maintained wall, not a perimeter. Oswin reinforces it at precise intervals. He checks the southwest quadrant twice a week minimum because the pressure there is constant. The other sides get attention when needed, which is rarely.

This matters now because the wall is visibly thinner than it was a year ago. The outer boardwalk families on the southwest edge have noticed. The city has filed two formal inquiries with the Mages Guild about the ward's performance. Oswin has responded to both with technically accurate but strategically incomplete reports. The wall is still standing. He has not mentioned that it is running on roughly sixty percent of its intended maintenance budget.

The Mire's accelerating growth is not *only* Oswin's fault — the Mangrove Spirit's longing would be intensifying regardless. But the wall should be slowing it more than it is. Part of what the outer boardwalks are losing is on him.

## Background

Appointed to the Sallowreach posting four years ago after a decade of competent, unremarkable service to the Guild's Western Field Office. The posting was pitched to him as six months to a year — long enough to assess the Mire situation, write a proper maintenance report, and hand off to someone more junior. The six months became indefinite. The maintenance report became the job. No one at the Guild has explained why this posting requires a credentialed practitioner; the answer, if anyone ever looked, is that it always has and no one wants to change a system that works.

The ward itself is not complicated work. Salt traces reinforce at three-day intervals. The southwest perimeter requires twice-weekly attention — the spirit always pushes from that direction, for reasons Oswin has noted in his logs but not investigated. The Celebration serves some maintenance function he can't quite reverse-engineer (the ward always feels more settled the morning after; he's attributed this to something in the acoustics and moved on). He is technically proficient. He does his job. Mostly.

The gambling started in year two. Marcel Dray's establishment was the only place in the city that felt like anything he recognized — clean, ordered, governed by comprehensible rules. He was very good at cards, briefly, and then he wasn't. The debt accumulated with the specific speed of debts that are being chased.

In any other context, Marcel would have had him hurt, or worse. But Guild credentials are a hard wall, and Marcel is practical. He found the embezzlement instead — maintenance funds redirected through fabricated "research expenses," a paper trail Oswin had told himself was well-hidden. It is not well-hidden. Marcel has it. Oswin knows Marcel has it. The arrangement is unspoken but perfectly understood: Oswin stays, keeps the ward running, says nothing, and Marcel doesn't deliver the evidence to the Guild.

He is not a bad person. He is a weak person who got in too deep and then made it worse and then made it worse again and is now living inside the consequences. He would like very much to get out. He does not see how.

## What They Want

To leave. Not heroically — just to be done. He wants the posting to end, the debt to dissolve, the embezzlement to disappear, and to be back in a city with a real library and colleagues who know his name. He fantasizes about a small teaching post. Something uncomplicated.

What he will *settle for*: a day where nothing gets worse.

## What They're Hiding

- The embezzlement. Significant guild funds redirected over roughly eighteen months.
- The scale of the debt to [[Marcel Dray]].
- The ward has been underfunded. He skimmed the maintenance budget; the ward is not running at full capacity. The Mire's accelerating growth is not entirely the spirits — some of it is him.
- He still gambles.

He does **not** know about the binding. He does **not** know what the Celebration actually does. He hasn't done the research because doing the research would mean caring about the posting, and he stopped letting himself do that.

### The Evidence (In His Office)

The embezzlement paperwork is in Oswin's office at the Maintenance House — not well hidden, because he never expected anyone to look. A locked iron document box in the lower desk drawer holds two years of fabricated research expense receipts, a ledger with two columns (official and actual), and one foolish letter to a Guild administrator explaining a "methodology change" in maintenance budgeting that would not survive scrutiny. The box key is on his person. The lock is not complicated. [VERIFY: appropriate Finesse or Intuition roll difficulty for Tier 1 — suggest Difficulty 14]

This means the party does not need Marcel to source the evidence. They could find it themselves — and Marcel doesn't need to know that. Whether they hand it to Marcel, give it back to Oswin, take it to the Guild directly, or destroy it is entirely on them.

### If He Realizes the Party Intends to Expose Him

He will try to burn the evidence first. Not a plan — a panic. He'll go for the document box, and if the party is between him and it, he will fight. Not to hurt anyone. Not to kill anyone. He is constitutionally lawful and he knows assaulting Guild-adjacent strangers is a category of problem he cannot survive. But he will shove, block, plead, use a minor ward-push [VERIFY: appropriate Tier 2 utility move — suggest a Presence or Knowledge action to create a magical obstacle, not a damage roll], and put his body between the party and that box.

If he loses — if the evidence is taken from him — he goes very quiet. He will cooperate with whatever comes next. He has nothing left to fight for once the box is gone.

## What They Know (Useful to the Party)

- Full technical knowledge of the ward: what the salt does, why it has to be reset at intervals, why the southwest perimeter is always the problem.
- **The direction:** the spirit has been moving toward Sallowreach from the southwest for as long as records exist. "Not random. Something in the city has its attention. I've noted it every quarter for four years. The previous maintainers noted it too. No one's ever figured out what."
- Institutional records for the past fifty years (pre-Vane records have a gap he's noticed and not pursued).
- The lumber arrangement: he signs the quarterly shipment paperwork. He'll explain it without embarrassment — *"Tribute, essentially. The timber's worth it. Extraordinary stuff. I've never seen anything like it anywhere else."*
- [[The First Maintainer's Journal|Aldric Vane's original field journal]] is missing from his archive. He'll mention it as a mild frustration if asked about early records: *"There's a gap — Vane's original notes. First maintainer, two hundred years ago, obviously the most interesting source. Gone. Misfiled somewhere during a transition, probably."* He has not investigated. He has not wanted to pull on that thread.

## Key Relationships

- [[Marcel Dray]] — holds the debt and the evidence; Oswin is terrified of him in the bone-deep way of someone who has no move left and knows it
- [[Mages Guild]] — simultaneously his protection and his cage; they can't touch him while he's credentialed, but if the embezzlement surfaces, the credentials go first
- The party — if they are kind to him, he will respond to it. This will be a problem.

---

## What's At Stake For Oswin

His future runs through what the party decides to do with the embezzlement evidence — and they hold it whether or not Marcel ever frames it as a deal.

If they deliver the evidence to the guild in a way that gets him removed, Oswin is fired, his credentials are revoked, and Marcel collects. Marcel will tell the party this plainly if asked. The wizard doesn't know this is happening until it happens.

If the party ever tells Oswin what Marcel is asking them to do, he will beg. He will not be theatrical about it. He will just — go very still, and then say *"Please don't."* And mean it completely.

The party may find another way. They may not. Either way, Oswin Cray's fate is in their hands, and they will know it.

## The Gambling Invitation

If the party has been kind to him — good conversation, genuine interest, no obvious threat — Oswin will invite them to play cards. He'll frame it lightly: *"The locals play, but it's a different game, I've never quite gotten the hang of it. I have a deck from home if you — no, it's fine if not."* He wants the company more than the game.

He is good at cards. Not supernaturally good — just trained good, the residual skill of someone who was once very up before becoming very down. He will not cheat. He will win more often than he loses, and he'll be quietly delighted about it.

He will not mention Marcel. Not because he's protecting Marcel — because he's protecting himself. He knows that table is where his life went wrong and he is not going to hand a group of strangers the thread that unravels it. If pressed — *"You play at the Pale Room?"* — he will say, pleasantly, that he finds it a bit rich for his tastes these days, and change the subject.

## Dialogue Hooks

**On first meeting, if the party is polite:**
*"Oh — you're the ones from the shipwreck. I heard. Come in, please, sit down. I was just — yes, come in. Are you hurt? I have some things — nothing medicinal, the locals are better for that, but — do you want tea? I know it sounds strange but I actually have decent tea, I had a case sent from the city when I arrived and I've been rationing it terribly, but — sit. Please."*

**On the posting, when comfortable:**
*"Four years. It was supposed to be one. I've stopped asking when it ends because the honest answer is 'when we say so' and — well. The salt doesn't walk itself."* [small laugh that doesn't land] *"The work is fine. It's fine. Very repetitive. But fine."*

**On the Mire, the direction question:**
*"The southwest perimeter — yes, I need to check it twice a week minimum. The others I can do every three days. It's always from the southwest. Always. There's something inside the city that it's moving toward, I'm almost certain of it. I've written it up every quarter for four years. No one at the Guild has ever asked a follow-up question."* [beat] *"I've stopped being surprised by that."*

**On Marcel (if his name is raised):**
*"I — yes, I know who he is. Everyone knows who he is."* [something closes in his face] *"Is there something else I can help you with?"*
