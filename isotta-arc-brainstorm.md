## The big idea
Isotta's player is the most diligent at the table, and her character is canonically the "deepest lore conduit" who documents everything. So she becomes the one who slowly unravels the campaign's central mystery: the ritual, the trap, and the bound Tree Spirit. Her personal quest and the main quest are the same quest.

## Why it fits — the 200-year lineage
Three diligent observers chased this mystery and all hit the same wall:
- **Aldric Vane** spent 11 years proving the Mangrove Spirit is pulled toward something inside the city, and died never identifying it.
- **Caspar Renne** has spent 40 years on the same question, stuck, working from summaries.
- **Delphine** feels the thing in the water and can't name it.

Each could only observe from the living side. Isotta is the first who can perceive the spirit side, because she's a soul tied in the wrong shape. She's the next observer in that line and the first one equipped to finish the work.

## How the personal quest and the main quest become one
1. **Method.** Her cure is "a parley with the source, not an exorcism" (her file). That's also the humane resolution to the Tree Spirit: a parley that releases what's bound rather than smashing the Celebration and triggering the breach. Curing herself is how she and the party learn the one technique the finale needs.
2. **Mirror.** She's a small binding with a face, like the tethered party but more literal. Every time she understands her own condition better, she's reading the Tree Spirit's.

## The spine (working back from the ending)
- **Finale:** she runs the parley. She's the one who can cross to where the spirit is bound and conduct its release the way she learned to conduct her own. Resolving the great binding also lifts the shared tether, so her cure and the party's land on the same act. Her climax and the campaign climax are one scene.
- **Act 2:** lead investigator. Documents the Turn (feels it hardest), chases the chants, digs Vane's journal out of the Reille hoard, brings it to Caspar with a theory. Trains the spirit side with Vael (Basile is her first real parley). Goes after the Fishing Spear (her cure and the finale's tool). Her casebook becomes the account Vane and Caspar never had: written from inside the veil.
- **Act 1 / now:** the opening quest below. Delphine already pointed her at Caspar; the salt wall gives her the firsthand experience that sends her there with a question instead of just a referral.

## Opening quest — "What's Behind the Salt"
*This also solves the salt wall never getting introduced in Session 2: instead of a throwaway establishing shot, the wall becomes the doorway to her whole arc.*

### Part 1 — The Still Cup
She comes in asking about ghosts, spirits, and the veil. Vael answers straight (none of this is the secret):
- A spirit world sits beside this one, split by a veil that's thin across the Mire and worn to nothing in the deep channels. Things cross constantly.
- Spirits aren't good or evil. They're people from somewhere else.
- Small ones can wear a human face and live a quiet life here. (Seeds Basile.)

Then the white eye, and he tells her from his side what Delphine told her from hers: she didn't cross over, she was tied here, which makes her a curse and the most sensitive instrument in the city. He's felt a spot on the southwest edge where the veil has worn through, worse every month for a year, and the living can't stand in it. She can.

He teaches her one move first: how to listen to something bigger than her without coming loose from her own name. This is the seed of the skill that eventually breaks her curse.
- **Roll:** Knowledge or Instinct as he teaches. Success banks a grounding technique she can invoke at the wall for advantage or a Hope.

He walks her to the wall but stops at the edge of what he can stand. The last stretch is hers alone.

### Part 2 — The Wall

**Beat 1: The approach.**
> The wall is bigger than the stories. A long rampart of grey salt, taller than a stilt-house, crusted and re-packed over two hundred years, running off into the haze both ways. This stretch is losing. The southwest face is soft and weeping, green pushing up through the cracks, and a family is quietly pulling the windows out of a house the water already owns.

- **The human cost.** Front 3 with a face. Optionally Oswin is here, too tired to study the thing he maintains.
- **The salt works on her.** She's spirit-stuff, and the wall was raised to push spirit-stuff out. The nearer the salt, the more it drags at her edges. The thing that keeps the city safe is the same thing that would shove her out, which puts her on the wrong side of its protection, same as whatever presses on the far side.
- **Roll:** Instinct to read the line; a hit finds the worn point where the veil's gone to gauze. Pushing up to the salt costs 1–2 Stress. [VERIFY vs your ward rules]

**Beat 2: The reach (centerpiece).**
- **Roll (the big one):** Instinct + *Connects with Spirits* vs **Difficulty 15** [VERIFY]. Spend Hope to add the Experience; the grounding move gives advantage if banked. The deeper she pushes, the more she learns and the more it costs. Let her choose how far.

**Beat 3: Contact.**
> Something on the far side feels the opening and leans toward it. It is enormous. It presses on you the way deep water presses on your ears, except the pressure is grief, old and patient and bottomless. It isn't trying to get through the wall. It's reaching past you, through the city, toward something held deep in the middle of it. For one breath you feel both ends at once: the vast reaching thing out here in the dark, and a small fixed point buried in the city, the two of them straining toward each other and never quite touching, like a long-held note waiting to land.

The shape of the whole campaign, with no names attached. Something outside is reaching for something bound inside. First casebook entry. (It's also a fragment of the vision Delphine carries and won't share: two lights, the chord.)

**Beat 4: She isn't the only one drawn here.**
The thin spot pulls the dead like a window pulls moths. Another lingers at the wall (a drowned poler, or someone whose home the Mire already took) and has been close to the big thing far longer. Her first parley.
- **Roll:** Presence to reach it without spooking it. A hit earns a clue in its muddled words: the big thing doesn't want the city, it wants "the one they keep," and it's been getting louder for years. ("The one they keep" points at the prisoner without naming it.)

**Beat 5: The pull (her clock).**
Reaching that deep, the grief finds her own and the worn veil works her loose.
- **Roll:** Presence Reaction Roll vs **Difficulty 14** [VERIFY], or she can choose to pull back.
- On Fear or an over-reach: heavy Stress, her outline flickers, she half-crosses for a moment. The slipping clock ticks, and the grounding move is how she hauls back.
- **Choice:** pull out and keep what she has, or push for one more piece and risk another slip. This is the reach-without-drowning lesson in live play, the muscle her cure will need.

**Beat 6: Back to Vael, and the threads.**
She returns with the shape, the dread, and three live leads:
- The pull comes from the southwest and aims at the heart → drives her to Caspar, pays off when she later reads Vane's "it will always be the southwest."
- She needs to learn to reach without coming apart → Vael's training, the Basile quest.
- The wall is failing and she felt the why behind it → Oswin, and the Ward Breach clock, now also her clock.
- "The one they keep" → the question she carries into the archive.

### GM notes for the quest
- **Guardrail:** she feels the shape, never the answer. No Tree Spirit, no Celebration-as-cage, no love story, no founders. Direction and dread only.
- **Dice in one scene:** read the line (Instinct), the reach (Connects with Spirits), the parley (Presence), the pull (Reaction). Four real rolls with Hope/Fear stakes — the built-in fix for the two-rolls-all-night problem, in the flavor this player wants.
- **Fronts touched:** 1 (grief, firsthand), 2 (the wall thinning), 3 (the displaced family). Advance the wall a notch off the contact.
- **The salt-and-her detail** is worth keeping forever: the city's protection rejects what she is.
