# Marcel Dray

**Role:** Head of Sallowreach's underground economy
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — The Pale Room and private quarters (location TBD)
**Faction:** Independent (his own organization)
**Tier:** 3

---

## Appearance

Tall, lean, unhurried. Dressed in clothes whose quality is immediately apparent without being flashy — nothing that announces wealth, everything that confirms it on closer inspection. He listens more than he speaks.

His hands are cold. Not cool — cold. The kind of cold that doesn't belong to a living body. When he shakes your hand you feel it immediately, and the cold spreads up your wrist before he lets go. He doesn't apologize for it. He doesn't explain it. In eleven years, no one in Sallowreach has asked him about it to his face.

He doesn't sweat. In the bayou humidity, where everyone is always damp, Marcel Dray is always dry. His breath doesn't fog in cold air. Food and drink pass his lips at social occasions; whether he requires them is unclear. He moves through the world like a man who has already settled all his accounts with it.

When something displeases him, he goes very still and very quiet. This is more frightening than anger.

## Personality & Mannerisms

Complete respect for the people he's dealing with — he listens, he doesn't interrupt, he says what he means. He finds people who moralize at him tiresome. He finds people who are honest about wanting something refreshing. He does not raise his voice. He does not threaten dramatically. He simply describes what will happen, plainly, and it does.

## Background

The Empire executed Marcel Dray twelve years ago for crimes they've since classified. The method was official and thorough. He was pronounced dead. He did not stay dead.

What happened in between is something Marcel doesn't discuss — not from trauma, but because he finds the question uninteresting. He died. He came back. He came to Sallowreach because it's as far from imperial jurisdiction as you can get without leaving the continent, and because legally he's already dead, which makes extradition complicated. He arrived with nothing and built his operation from a single card table in the back of a distillery. He has been here eleven years.

## What He Wants

To collect his debt from [[The Wizard]] — which he cannot do as long as the wizard holds Mages Guild protection. He needs the wizard disgraced and fired, not killed; once guild protection is revoked, the debt becomes collectable by his own means.

He has been waiting for the right instrument. The tethered party, desperate and motivated and clean of any connection to his operation, are exactly that.

## What He's Hiding

That once guild protection is revoked, the wizard's debt becomes immediately collectable. By any means. Marcel will confirm this plainly if asked directly. He considers it the party's responsibility to ask the right questions. He does not volunteer it.

## The Operation

**The Pale Room** — invitation-only underground casino. White tablecloths, low light, exceptional service. The games are fair — Marcel runs fair games because rigged games create enemies and fair games create addicts. [[The Wizard]] has lost a significant sum here over three years.

**The Mire Spirits** — bootleg distillation using Mire ingredients that can't legally be exported. The resulting liquors are extraordinary and distributed throughout the city, exported quietly through sympathetic punt operators.

**Information** — his real operation. Everything that happens in Sallowreach eventually reaches Marcel. He doesn't sell information cheaply, but he doesn't hoard it either.

**The Courtesy** — businesses in certain districts pay a monthly sum for which nothing bad happens to them. The courtesy is reasonable, the protection is real, and Marcel has never had to demonstrate the alternative.

**What he doesn't do:** traffic in people. This is his line, absolute. It's one of the things that makes the city's relationship with him complicated.

## Key Relationships

- [[The Wizard]] — debtor; currently untouchable; Marcel's primary irritation
- [[Sallowreach]] — his home; he participates in the Celebration every week, always near the back; contributes to communal feast tables generously and anonymously

## The Deal

Marcel's price for [[The Fishing Spear]]: deliver the wizard's embezzlement evidence to the Mages Guild in a way that results in the wizard's removal and revocation of guild protection.

He has the evidence. He cannot use it himself without revealing he's been monitoring a guild wizard's finances. The party are his clean instrument.

*"Deliver the embezzlement evidence to the guild in a way that results in his removal, and the spear is yours."*

He honors deals. This is non-negotiable — his reputation depends on it. If the party delivers, the spear is theirs, no complications.

If the party tries to steal the spear rather than deal for it, Marcel will know within hours and be disappointed rather than angry. Disappointed is worse.

## Dialogue Hook

He opens with the party's names — already knows them, doesn't explain how. Offers something to drink. Asks what they need, not what they want. Listens to the whole answer before speaking.

## Stat Block

*Marcel Dray does not fight the party. If it comes to that, the campaign has gone wrong somewhere. He has people; he will simply not be present.*
