# The Pale Room

**Tier 1 Social**

*"White tablecloths, low light, a hand laid gently on your shoulder to take your coat — and the certain knowledge that everything you say in this room reaches the man with the cold hands before you've finished saying it."*

[[Marcel Dray]]'s invitation-only underground casino, run from somewhere beneath a working district nobody can quite place afterward. The games are fair — Marcel runs fair games because rigged games make enemies and fair games make addicts. The service is exceptional, the liquor is [[Marcel Dray|Mire Spirits]] you cannot buy anywhere legal, and the real product is not on any table. The real product is what walks in the door inside people's heads. This is the city's information economy with a velvet rope across it: the place where debts are made, secrets are spent like chips, and deals are struck across green felt by people who would never be seen agreeing to anything in daylight. The challenge here is never your sword. It is your composure, your leverage, and how much you are willing to owe.

**Impulses:** Make everything collectable, reward nerve and punish tells, let the room watch, turn want into debt.

**Difficulty:** 12

**Potential Adversaries:** None, by design — the Pale Room is a Social environment, and violence here is a failure of the room, not a feature of it. [[Marcel Dray]] is present as a Tier 3 presence and **does not fight** — if it comes to that, he is simply no longer in the room. His people enforce courtesy without ever raising their voices: a floor host who knows your debts to the copper, and a pair of unhurried [[sallowreach-roster#Pale Room Enforcer|Pale Room Enforcers]] who escort, never strike. Reach for them only to walk someone out.

## Features

- **Everything Reaches Marcel - Passive:** The Pale Room is built to be read, and to read you back. A PC who works the floor — buying a drink, losing gracefully, listening at the right elbow — may make an Instinct Roll (or Presence, if charming a confidence loose) to learn what the room knows. On a success with Hope, they learn a true and useful secret cleanly (a debt someone can't pay, who [[Caius Maren|the imperial man]] has been dining with, that [[Oswin Cray|the ward wizard]] still plays here once a week and is into the house deep). On a success with Fear, the secret is real but the room noticed them fishing — the cost is a marker: Marcel's floor now *knows their interest*, and Marcel hears about it before the night is out. On a failure, mark 2 Stress — the room is a mirror and they have just shown it more than they learned. [VERIFY: Daggerheart Action Roll, Instinct/Presence trait]

- **The Fair Game - Action:** Marcel's tables are games of nerve and skill, dealt straight. When a PC sits to play for real stakes — coin, a favor, a piece of information they need — run a **Progress Countdown (4)** of hands. Each hand is an Action Roll (the PC's choice of Finesse for the cards or Presence for the bluff); fill the countdown to win the stakes, and the table respects you afterward in ways money can't buy. But the house is patient: on any roll **with Fear**, the PC may *stay in* by raising — taking on debt to keep their seat — or fold and lose what's already on the felt. The game is honest. That is exactly why it is dangerous: nobody made them keep playing. [VERIFY: Progress Countdown; stakes/debt are narrative, GM-set]

- **Composure Under a Cold Eye - Passive:** When [[Marcel Dray]] turns his attention on a PC — and he will, plainly, without hurry — the chill of him is a physical thing. He does not threaten. He describes what will happen, quietly, and it does. Any PC negotiating with Marcel, lying to him, or trying to read him makes the attempt under pressure: mark 1 Stress to hold their nerve before the roll, or take disadvantage on it. He finds people who moralize at him tiresome and people who are honest about wanting something refreshing — flattery and bluster cost extra here; candor is the only currency that buys a discount. [VERIFY: Stress, disadvantage]

- **The House Always Listens - Action:** The GM may, at any time, have a host glide up and *offer* — a better table, a private room, a drink that loosens tongues, an introduction to exactly the person the party needs. Every courtesy is real and every courtesy is also an account being opened. Accepting is noticed. Refusing is noticed. The Pale Room never says no to a guest; it simply remembers, and adds it to the ledger that is the whole point of the place.

- **The Marker Comes Due - Reaction:** Spend a Fear to call a debt at the worst possible moment — and never with a blade. A host appears at a PC's elbow mid-sentence with a quiet figure owed and a warmer smile than the situation deserves; a sealed envelope arrives at the table holding a secret the party thought buried, slid face-down across the felt; or two enforcers materialize to *courteously* escort someone out — an ally, a mark, [[Oswin Cray|the wizard if he is playing tonight]] — before the party is finished with them. The escalation is social to the bone: composure shattered, leverage flipped, a deal collapsed or forced. No one is hurt. That is what makes it land. [VERIFY: Fear spend]

- **Marcel Honors the Deal - Reaction:** If the party comes to Marcel with the right question — *be his instrument* ([[The World in Motion#Front 7 — The Holstered Gun|Front 7]]) — he hands over what he holds, no second ask, and the felt becomes a contract. He opens with their names, already knows them, offers something to drink, and asks what they *need*, not what they want. He honors deals absolutely; his reputation is the whole of his power. But he will confirm the collectable truth only if asked it directly — he considers it the party's responsibility to ask the right questions, and he does not volunteer that once [[Oswin Cray|the wizard's]] guild protection is gone, the debt becomes collectable by any means. [VERIFY: this is a roleplay beat, not a roll — gate any persuasion against Marcel behind genuine candor, not a Presence check the party can simply pass]

## Feature Questions

- *What does each PC want badly enough to sit down at a fair table they know they can't beat — and what will they raise instead of folding?*
- *When the room reads a PC back, which buried thing does it surface — and does Marcel already have it on the ledger?*
- *Marcel opens with their names. Whose name does he say with the most weight, and what does that tell the party he already knows?*
- *If a marker comes due tonight, who gets walked out — and is it someone the party still needed in the room?*

## Running This

Run the Pale Room as the most civilized place in [[Sallowreach]] and the most dangerous, in the same breath. Nothing here is hostile; everything here is an account. Let the courtesy be genuine — the coat taken, the perfect drink, the fair deal — because the trap is not deceit, it is comfort, and the slow accumulation of being owed. Keep [[Marcel Dray]] still and quiet; his power is that he never performs it. He does not raise his voice, he does not threaten, he describes — and when something displeases him he simply goes more still, which is worse than anger. **He never fights here, full stop;** every escalation you reach for must be social — a debt called, a secret surfaced, an enforcer's gentle hand on a shoulder — never a blade. If [[Oswin Cray]] is at a table when the party arrives, you are holding the whole gambling thread in one room ([[The World in Motion#Front 7 — The Holstered Gun|Front 7]]): the wizard who lost his life one fair hand at a time, and the man patiently waiting for a clean instrument to make that debt collectable. Let the party feel how easy it would be to become regulars. That is the room working as designed.
