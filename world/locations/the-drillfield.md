# The Drillfield

**Type:** Training ground / city guard muster
**Region:** [[Sallowreach]] — southwest district, two boardwalks in from the salt wall
**Controlled By:** City Guard, southwest perimeter command — [[Sable Crue]]

*Tenson's first stop. A place where people who work with their bodies gather, sweat, and — because they're guards on the edge that's losing ground — talk about exactly the thing Tenson was sent to investigate.*

---

## Description
The Drillfield is the largest continuous flat surface in the southwest district, which in a city built on stilts over black water is its own minor miracle: a broad deck of close-laid, salt-greyed timber, lashed and pegged and re-leveled so many times that no two planks quite agree on the horizon. Recruits run its length at dawn. The air smells of sweat, rope, wet wood, and — always, this close to the wall — the faint mineral bite of salt.

It is not a parade ground. Sallowreach has no army to parade; what it has is a perimeter guard, a few dozen strong, whose job is less *war* than *vigilance* — watching the southwest face, hauling salvage when the water takes a home, walking the relocation orders door to door because somebody has to and it shouldn't be a stranger. The Drillfield trains people for that. The combat drills are real but secondary. The real curriculum is endurance, water-rescue, and the specific calm you need to tell a family their boardwalk is condemned.

You can see the wall from here. That's the thing nobody mentions and everybody notices. Two boardwalks southwest, past the last occupied houses, the salt barrier runs grey and crusted along the Mire's edge — and from the Drillfield you can watch, over months, the green press closer. Recruits drill with their backs to it on purpose. It doesn't help.

## Key Features
- **The Deck** — the main training surface: running lanes, sparring circles worn pale, racks of practice arms and rescue gear (poles, lines, floats — more rescue than weapon, which tells you what the job actually is). [[Sable Crue|Sable]] runs the morning drill themselves and turns it, somehow, into the best hour of the guards' day.
- **The Watch Post** — a raised platform at the southwest corner with a clear sightline to the wall. Manned in shifts. The logbook here is the *informal* record — what the guards actually see, day to day — and it does not always match what gets filed upward.
- **The Long Bench** — under the eave where off-shift guards eat (often something Sable cooked) and talk. The single best place in the southwest district to hear, unfiltered, what the people on the edge actually think is happening to their city. Gossip, grievance, and the occasional load-bearing fact.
- **The Kit Room** — a cramped store of gear and the perimeter's paper: duty rosters, incident reports, requisition records, filed correspondence. Nobody guards it jealously; it's just *boring,* which is its own kind of lock. Get Sable to wave you in and the whole wall's paper history is yours to read — including the one memo nobody clever has looked at.

## NPCs Present
- **[[Sable Crue]]** — sergeant of the perimeter guard; brawny, sunny, full of life, completely at peace being average; the happiest person posted to a "hated" city. Win them on the field (not with the seal) and they're Tenson's man on the inside — the door to the records, the rooms, and the people. *(They're sitting on the Caius memo and have no idea, because reading it closely is "a brain job." That's Tenson's win to find.)*
- Perimeter guards (background NPCs) — overworked, underfunded, increasingly angry on behalf of the families they keep relocating; good sources of ground-level texture and rumor *(run with [[improv-ammo]] if you need a face fast)*

## Tensions & Hooks
- **The wall is visible and it is losing.** Anyone who trains here watches the Mire advance. The guards know, in their bodies, that the official line ("performing within acceptable parameters") is a lie — they just don't know *whose* lie or *why.* Tenson can give that frustration a direction.
- **The relocation orders muster from here.** When a southwest home is condemned (Front 3 — see [[world-in-motion]]), it's these guards who post the notice and haul the salvage. They hate it. It's radicalizing them slowly, which feeds the city's widening fracture (Front 4).
- **The evidence is here, unread.** The bureaucratic half of the central case sits in the Kit Room — including the Caius memo. The lock isn't a lock; it's that no one here finds paperwork interesting. Tenson needs Sable's say-so to get at it, and Tenson's the one who'll understand what they're holding. *(See [[Sable Crue]] — "the man on the inside.")*
- **A body that won't sit still.** For Tenson personally, the Drillfield is temptation and trap: the old life — discipline, drill, the easy respect of people who work with their hands — reaches for them here, right when they're trying to become someone who keeps books instead. And Sable is standing in the middle of it, living proof that the old life can be a *happy* one — which is its own kind of challenge to Tenson's whole plan.

## Secrets
- The Watch Post's informal logbook and the reports filed up the chain **diverge** — the guards have recorded encroachment events that never made it into the official record. Someone, somewhere up the line, is smoothing the numbers. *(GM: this is degradation + denial — Oswin's neglect and Caius's interference both leave fingerprints here, though the guards can't tell the two apart.)*
- The Caius memo is real and it's *right there* in the filed correspondence — a denial of Mages Guild ward funding, signed by an imperial *cultural liaison,* which makes no sense. Sable has handled it, read the top line, and filed it, because parsing it is "a brain job." It has been sitting in plain sight waiting for someone who'd actually read it. That someone is Tenson.
- One of the long-serving guards grew up on a boardwalk that's already gone under — relocated two years ago. He doesn't talk about it. He's the one who volunteers, every time, to post the orders, because he can't stand the thought of a stranger doing it. *(A ready-made face for Front 3, if you want one.)*

## Dungeon Alchemist Notes
A large open-air timber deck over water, southwest-facing, with the salt wall visible at the edge of the map for atmosphere. Build it as a wide flat platform: running lanes and sparring circles in the open center, a raised watch platform at one corner, a covered eave with benches and a small enclosed kit room along one side. Salt-grey, weathered wood; rescue gear racked more prominently than weapons. For mood, the southwest horizon should read as *encroaching green* — the Mire pressing at the frame. No battle encounter expected by default; this is a social, information, and recruitment space — though it's a plausible site for a training-bout scene, or for a wall-breach crisis later in the campaign.
