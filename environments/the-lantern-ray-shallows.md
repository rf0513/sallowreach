# The Lantern-Ray Shallows

**Tier 1 Exploration**

> *"Stop punting. Stop talking. Just look — the whole channel's breathing blue, and every one of those lights is a creature that has never once in its life had a reason to be afraid of you."*

**Type:** Exploration — a broad, shallow basin off the southwest [[The Wending|Wending]] where the [[lantern-rays]] rise at dusk and the black water fills with living light. This is one of the most beautiful places in [[The Mire]], and the party should fall in love before the environment ever asks anything of them. Drop it in when the party is on the water near dusk, when [[Remy Duval]] wants to show them something, or when the trail of nets and harvested remains leads here. Reconcile navigation with [[The Wending]] and ground the creatures and the harvest side-plot in [[lantern-rays]].

**Impulses:** Fill the dark with wonder, reward stillness and patience, tempt the taking hand, remember what restraint costs the one who keeps it.

**Difficulty:** 11

**Potential Adversaries:** [[Gault]] (Mire Extractor, Tier 2 Solo) or his nets worked from a screen of reeds; the [[sallowreach-roster#Tessak Diver|Tessak]] under Drevath, in the water at multiple angles, doing *tessavrak* with shame they will not voice; opportunist poachers in Gault's pay; a glass snake (SRD) or [[mire-bestiary#Bloodmidge Swarm|biting swarm]] drawn by blood and disturbance once the light goes out.

## Features

**Ray-Light - Passive:** At dusk the school rises and the basin lights from below — slow blue-green pulses traveling through the water like a single heartbeat. While the rays are schooling, the Shallows are **brightly and beautifully lit**: navigation here is automatic, sightlines are long, and the party reads the water, the reeds, and each other with ease. Any roll to *see* — to spot the nets, the harvested remains, the shape moving in the deeper channel, the way a school is smaller than it should be — is made with **advantage** [VERIFY: advantage on perception-type rolls per frame]. The light is also legible the way it was the night the ship went down: follow the rays and you find the shallow water and the way to shore. *This benefit lasts only while the school holds. Put it out (see The Light Goes Out) and the basin becomes black water and guesswork.*

**What the Rays Are - Passive:** A PC who stills themselves and watches — a successful **Instinct Roll** [VERIFY: trait and Action Roll mechanics per frame] — reads more than beauty in the school. On a **roll with Hope**, they grasp something true about the Mire's deeper ecology: the rays are not decoration but the engine of it, stirring the deep channels, carrying the spores that grow the algae and moss and channel fungi the city's whole alchemical trade quietly depends on. On a **roll with Fear**, the truth arrives sideways and unwelcome — the school is *thinner* than the water can explain, and whatever is keeping these creatures alive is being spent faster than it returns. [[Remy Duval]], present, simply knows: he counts, counts again, and goes quiet in a specific way.

**The Taking Hand - Action:** The rays approach. They hover at arm's length, unhurried, unafraid, regarding the party with small dark lateral eyes — and they are worth a fortune. Their bioluminescent organs yield an oil alchemical institutions pay extraordinary sums for, and a still ray at arm's length is the easiest money in the Mire. A PC may simply *take* one; nothing stops them, and the GM should let the choice be entirely theirs. Taking a little is survival, and the Mire has always given that freely. **Taking beyond the season is** ***tessavrak***, and the Mire keeps its own ledger: each harvest past the first is a tick on a hidden **Progress Countdown (4)** the party never sees. The rays do not punish. They simply begin, that very dusk, to *leave* — the school pulls toward deeper water, the light recedes, and the Shallows dim around the ones still reaching. *This is the campaign's restraint lesson made into a lever, not a lecture. Offer the temptation honestly. Let them learn what the Tessak already know — or refuse to.*

**The School Turns - Reaction:** When blood goes into the water — a ray killed, a net hauled, a fight begun — the school responds the way it responded the night of the shipwreck: it *rises and flees*, away from the disturbance, toward shallower water and shore. The light that was spread across the whole basin gathers and streams away in a single direction. For one breath it is the most beautiful thing the party has ever seen. Then it is gone, and so is the Ray-Light passive: advantage ends, sightlines collapse, and whatever the party is standing in the middle of, they are now standing in it in the dark.

**The Light Goes Out - Action:** *(Fear-gated escalation.)* Spend a Fear when the Countdown fills, when the party spills blood here, or when the disturbance crosses a line the Mire will not abide: the school does not merely flee — it *vanishes*, and the Shallows go fully, suddenly black. Each PC marks 1 Stress as the wonder snuffs out around them. Worse, the disturbance has *called* something — blood and thrashing in the dark draw a Mire predator (a glass snake [VERIFY: SRD], a biting swarm, or one of [[Gault]]'s crew who heard the splashing) within Close range, and it takes the spotlight immediately. The basin that was the safest, most beautiful water in the Mire is now the most dangerous, and the party did that. [[The Mangrove Spirit|the deep Mire]] notices, briefly, the way it notices everything taken without restraint — never aimed at them, only *through* them.

**The Tessak in the Water - Action:** Spend a Fear to reveal that the party is not alone here. The [[Tessak]] are working the basin — figures in the water at multiple angles, fast and quiet, [[Drevath]] meeting any approach at the water's edge. They are not aggressive and they are not innocent; they are doing what they have to do for children who are dying, and they know exactly what *tessavrak* costs. This is a conversation, not a fight, unless the party makes it one. [[Gault]]'s waterproofed containers are stacked nearby, and they are rigged to sink if the camp is raided. He thought of that eight years ago.

## Feature Questions

*Which PC is the first to reach for a ray — and is it greed, need, curiosity, or the simple fact that no one is stopping them?*

*Who in the party recognizes this water — the one who opened their eyes in the black the night the ship went down and found a light hovering there?*

*When the school turns and flees, what does each PC understand in that moment that they couldn't have been told?*

*If [[Remy Duval]] is here, what does he count, and what does he decide not to say out loud?*

## Running This

**Beauty first. Always.** Run the first minutes of the Shallows as pure wonder — the rising school, the synchronized pulse, a single ray hovering at arm's length to decide whether the party is food. Make them fall in love before any feature does anything mechanical. If [[Remy Duval]] is along, this is his Steve Irwin moment: hushed, overcome, naming individuals he's known for twenty years. The *Ray-Light* passive is doing real work even when it feels like flavor — the basin is genuinely the safest, clearest water in the Mire while the school holds, and that safety is what makes the rest land.

**The Taking Hand is the whole point — so don't telegraph it.** Present the temptation with a flat, honest face: the ray is right there, it's worth a fortune, and nothing in the world stops the player from taking it. *Taking a little is fine* — survival always has been. The lesson is not "don't touch," it's *tessavrak*, taking beyond the season, and the only way to teach it is to let the table choose how far the hand reaches. The hidden Countdown is your dial, not their punishment; the consequence is the rays *leaving*, the light *receding*, the wonder withdrawing of its own accord. That's the Mire keeping its ledger, and it ties straight to the campaign's spine — a city that has never run out of anything, learning in miniature that something finite can be taken past the point of return. The [[Tessak]] have a word for this. Sallowreach doesn't. Let the party feel which side of that they're on.

**Spend Fear to make a choice cost something.** *The Light Goes Out* and *The Tessak in the Water* are your Fear levers — reach for them when a scene needs teeth: the dusk the party finally catches the operation, the moment a PC's harvest tips the basin, the encounter where wonder turns to a fight nobody wanted. Snuffing the light is the sharpest beat the environment has; use it once, deliberately, and let the dark and what answers it do the rest. Tie a heavy harvest here to the collapse timeline in [[lantern-rays]] — Remy notices first, the Consortium notices later, and the city never connects it to these creatures without the party's help.
