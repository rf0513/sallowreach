# Session 2 — Read-Aloud Blurbs

*Spoken, not written — paced like you're coming up with it at the table. Read it or riff off it. One image per beat. The load-bearing ones: the curse in daylight (2), Delphine's reframe (3), the four doors (6–9), and the echo (10). Full prep in [[session-02-prep]]; the one-pager in [[session-02-cheatsheet]].*

---

### 1 · The First Morning
> You wake up dry. That's the first thing — you're *dry,* and warm, and there's a blanket on you that somebody put there, and for one whole second you don't remember any of it.
> Light's coming in low and green through a shutter. Somewhere under you the whole building is creaking — gentle, like a boat — because the whole *city* is a boat, sort of, sixty thousand people on stilts over black water. And it's already loud out there. Hammering. Somebody singing a price. A drum, way off — somebody practicing, you'll learn, for a thing that happens every week. Fish and woodsmoke and frying spice, right in through the window.
> It smells, honestly, like the best morning of your life.
> And then it comes back. The water. The cold. The hand that closed around something behind your ribs and didn't let go. And you remember — *oh.* Right. We can't leave.

*GM — let the warmth land for a real beat before the memory does. The contrast is the whole point: the city is going to be good to them, and they are trapped in it. Don't rush to Delphine; let someone speak first.*

### 2 · The Edge *(the curse, in daylight)*
> [when a PC goes to the edge of the boards, where the channel opens toward open water]
> In the dark last night you could tell yourself it was shock. The cold. Nearly drowning'll do funny things to a body.
> But it's morning now. Sun's up. You're warm and you're fed and your head is clear — and you take one step toward the open water, toward *out,* toward away — and it's still there. A wall that isn't a wall. Resistance, like wading into a current that's all directions at once, and then — past that — something that isn't pain but is *worse* than pain, a kind of full-body *no.*
> And you stop. Of course you stop. And there's no mark on you. Nothing torn, nothing bleeding, nothing to show anybody. Just you, and the water, and the open channel — and the absolute certainty, in daylight, that it is not going to let you go.

*GM — this is the curse confirmed: in the cold light, no adrenaline to blame. **No marks** — that's the eerie part; a wound would at least make sense. Everyone tries it once. Let them feel it as a group or one at a time, however it falls.*

### 3 · The Knot *(Delphine)*
> Remy walks you to a stilt-house in the Thornside quarter — door's unlocked, herbs hanging from every rafter, smells like beeswax and brackish water and something green underneath. And there's a woman, sixties, grey hair cropped close, hands like she's been working forty years — and before any of you say a word she points at a stool.
> [Delphine] *"Sit. All of you — sit down. You're carrying it up in your shoulders, and that's doing you no good at all."*
> She gets in close. Quiet. Puts two fingers on the wrist of whoever's nearest — and for just half a second her eyes slide off to the left of you, to nothing, and she goes very still. Then she's back, like it never happened.
> [Delphine] *"Hm. Well — you're not sick. Nothing to flush, nothing torn. This isn't poison."* [a beat] *"It's a knot. You went under, and the water tied you to this place. ...It feels like—"*
> [and she stops herself. Flat. Decides not to.]
> [Delphine] *"No. I'm not about to go cutting at a thing I can't see both ends of — that's how you make spirit-water worse, not better. But I know what it* is. *And I know who might know more. Caspar Renne — the museum, up in the old quarter. Tell him I sent you; he'll actually listen."*
> [and she means every word of it. Then, softer:] *"...Come back if it changes. Come back if it doesn't, and you just want to sit somewhere that isn't trying to kill you."*

*GM — blunt-but-warm, never a lecture. **Reveal:** it's a relational knot, not a wound; she can't cure it; spirit-water; go to Caspar. **Withhold:** the vision (two lights, the resolving chord) and the Celebration-rhyme — she* stops *before "it feels like the—". Never finish it. If a player catches the eye-drift: "Old habit. The water's loud today."*

### 4 · The Invitation
> Somebody finds you. Cuts neat through the crowd — and you clock him before he reaches you, because he's *wrong* for here: pressed, clean, dry as a bone in all this wet heat, like the humidity got a memo to leave him alone. Imperial. He gives you a little bow that costs him nothing.
> [aide] *"Liaison Maren sends his compliments. And this."*
> And he hands over a card. Heavy cream paper, thick as a shingle, sealed in red wax with the imperial mark pressed into it. He doesn't wait for an answer. He's already turning to go.
> [open it. read it:]
> > *"To the survivors of the Mire — it is my sincere hope this finds you warm, dry, and on the mend. I am told the swamp does not often give back what it takes; that you are here at all is remarkable, and I should very much like to welcome you properly. Dine with me this evening, at the Attaché Quarters. Nothing elaborate — only the best this extraordinary city can set on a table, and the company of people who, like you, did not expect to find themselves in Sallowreach. — C. Maren, Cultural Liaison to the Empire."*
> Warm. Gracious. Perfect, really. And something about it — you couldn't say what — sits a half-degree off, like a painting hung very slightly crooked. Then the feeling's gone, and it's just a kind invitation to a good dinner.

*GM — the menace is sub-perceptual. He arranged the wreck; he didn't expect survivors; he wants their testimony in writing. **None of that shows.** Plant the hair-thin wrongness and move on. If you want a corroborating whisper later: a market voice — "The imperial one's been asking after that shipwreck. Buys you dinner just to ask."*

### 5 · The City Takes a Breath *(into open play)*
> So. You've got a day. And a city you've never seen, that already seems to have decided it likes you.
> Step out onto the boardwalks and it just — opens up. Walkways stacked on walkways, up into the smoke and the wash-lines strung house to house. Punts sliding down the canal-streets below you, poles dripping, somebody calling rates in a trade-cant half these people speak without thinking. The whole place tilted maybe half a degree off true, and not one soul minding. Green light coming up off the water. Cook-smoke you can *taste.*
> And everywhere you go, somebody's catching your eye, nodding, like — *oh, you're the ones. The ones the swamp gave back.* Nobody's cold to you. If anything it's the opposite, and you can't quite figure out what you did to earn it.
> [then, gentle, to the table:] Where do you go first?

*GM — this is the hand-off to the sandbox. Each PC has a place they came here for. Let them split; cut between them in short laps (see [[session-02-prep#How to Run a Split Party|the prep]]). Pass every transition through the market — a free bowl, a warm rumor — and actually hand out the Stress/Hope. Five-things-you-notice banks in [[improv-ammo]] §3.*

### 6 · The Ranging House *(Fletcher)*
> The sign over the door is just the Guild's mark — a knotted cord over crossed arrows — and below it, nothing. Either you know what this place is or you've got no business in it.
> Inside it hits you: rendered fat, solvent, river-mud worked so deep into the threshold no scrubbing'll ever reach it. Map tables. Specimen boards. A wall of shed skins, each one tagged in a careful hand. And in the corner, a woman who hasn't looked up yet, working at a table buried in channel-maps inked three colours deep.
> You start to reach for your journal — for the castings, the trail you've walked the length of the world — and she's already dragging her own map across the table to meet you.
> [Brin, not looking at the skin — looking at *you*] *"Two-headed pattern. Southwest junction. Three months old, give or take."* [now she looks up] *"I've got four more of these in a drawer. What I don't have is a reason to trust the man holding the fifth. So. Convince me you want this thing dead because it's killing what shouldn't be killed — and not because of how it'll feel to be the one who finally did it."*

*GM — Brin does the same work Fletcher does, by a stricter creed, and won't trust him on sight. She'll gate the deep intel behind a test — "the Haunt of Thornside." Set it up tonight; run it solo next session unless there's time. Watch his hands when he says it isn't revenge.*

### 7 · The Verdant Margin *(the Witch)*
> It's a garden, somehow — out here, on the literal edge, where the city's last cultivated dirt meets the black water. Three platforms of it, climbing. Turned earth and damp wood and something sweet that keeps changing as you move — blooming one bed, fermenting the next. Down a tide-wet ladder there's a lower platform where the plants grow in directions the light does not explain, and three beds that *glow,* faint and blue-green, even now in daylight.
> There's a tall figure at a greenhouse bench, broad as a door, soft-eyed — a firbolg, except there's a crust of something pale and barnacled creeping up one forearm, and a fringe of fine green where hair ought to be, and they do not seem to have noticed or to care. Something's bubbling in their hand that they've clearly forgotten they're holding.
> [Orna, not looking up] *"You're after something specific. Everyone who finds the Margin is."* [a swirl, a sniff, the beaker set down wrong] *"Most of them want the impossible ones — the reagents that shouldn't work. Those I have."*
> [now they look at you. and the wrong light moves in their eyes.] *"You, though. You want the impossible* done *to you. ...Different shelf. Sit, sit — no, not* there, *that's alive."*

*GM — the breadcrumb is the prize here: somewhere in this scene Orna glances and says "You're not sick. You're* **bound.** *No elixir touches that — wrong department. You want a—" and trails off, gone, three thoughts away. Never finish it. The mirror (a self thrown away on purpose vs. a self stolen) does the emotional work. The glowing beds are a Turn clue — let a Witch elbow-deep in the soil catch the pulse; don't explain it.*

### 8 · The Still Cup *(Isotta)*
> The name's carved right into the door-frame, not hung above it — like the place can't be bothered to advertise. Inside: dark wood, good light at every table, and a *quiet* that isn't empty, just — settled. Best coffee in the city, they say, and the city has opinions about coffee.
> The owner clocks you the second you're through the door. Tall, fifty-ish, an old grey tattoo running the whole sleeve of one arm. And the eyes — one's dark and quick and warm, and the other is milky-white and very, very still. The white one drifts, just slightly, to a point past your shoulder. The brown one stays right on you.
> [Vael, setting a cup down in front of you — no ceremony] *"I've served a few like you. Died in the transformation, came back still attached. The Order says that's not supposed to survive."* [a beat] *"But you're not quite* that *either, are you. Sit. Drink."*
> [and a little nod toward the corner — where an old, old man is laying out dominoes, alone, gentle, smiling at nobody] *"There's a man over there I think you ought to meet. And then — you and I are going to talk about what you actually* are."

*GM — once the white eye has her, the line that reframes everything: "The ones I see came across on their own. You didn't. Someone* **did this to you,** *child. That's not a haunting. That's a* **curse.**" *Relief and grief at once — the Order trained her to hunt what she is, and here's a master telling her she was the victim. Basile (the slipping spirit) is "Old Basile," her teacher-quest. Vael never names the deep thing or the Celebration.*

### 9 · The Drillfield *(Tenson)*
> Biggest flat deck in the district — which in a stilt-city is its own small miracle — salt-grey planks pegged and re-leveled so many times no two of them quite agree where the horizon is. Sweat, rope, wet wood, and under it all the mineral bite of salt, because the *wall's* right there: two boardwalks southwest, grey and crusted along the Mire's edge, and you can see — even standing here — the green pressing up against it. The recruits drill with their backs to it. On purpose, you think.
> You come in with your seal out and your questions ready, and the big sergeant running the morning drill takes one look at the seal and grins like you've handed him a present.
> [Sable] *"From the* Citadel! *Look at you. We don't get many of those who'll still get in the mud."* [a practice stave comes spinning at your chest before you can finish a sentence] *"Tell you what. You do the morning drill with my lot — then I'll tell you anything you want to know. Deal?"* [already turning back to the field, delighted] *"Don't* answer. *Just catch."*

*GM — the seal opens nothing; cleverness opens nothing. Sable reads people by how they show up, sweat, and treat the person next to them. The win is "Sweat First" — Tenson's buried sports-medicine eye saving a washing-out recruit, Perrin. The records (and the unread Caius memo) come* after *the friendship, not tonight. Sable is the happy life Tenson's terrified of — play it as a question, never mockery.*

### 10 · The Word *(the echo — reconvergence)*
> [end of the day — Remy's table, a market bench, the walk to the dinner. Let them swap what they found out. And let it land on its own:]
> The healer said it was a *knot.* Said you weren't hurt — you were *tied.*
> The alchemist in the garden barely looked up. Said: *you're not sick, you're* bound.
> The old hunter with the white eye said it plainest of all. Said: *somebody did this to you. You weren't haunted. You were* tied.
> Three people. Three rooms. None of them talking to each other. And every one of them reached for the same word — like it was just *sitting there,* the obvious word, the only word — and not one of them could tell you what it is you're bound *to.*

*GM — **do not** have an NPC point this out. Let a* player *catch it. This is the [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|Turn]] clue-chain breathing — recognition, weeks ahead of the click. If nobody clocks it tonight, it compounds. And hold the line: nobody in this city can connect any of it to the Celebration yet.*

### 11 · The Embassy *(optional capstone — the dinner)*
> The Attaché Quarters are the nicest rooms in a building full of people who'd rather be anywhere else — and you can tell the moment you're inside that everything in here came from somewhere else too. His own furniture, shipped in. Arranged just so. The desk faces the door. He always knows who's coming in.
> And the food — the food is *extraordinary,* even by this city's standards, which you're starting to understand is saying something. He's got the best of everything and he's not shy about it.
> [Caius, warm, glass raised, and he means it] *"I'm glad you survived. I mean that — I've read three incident reports out of this Mire in two years, and not one of them had survivors. Sit. Please. Tell me everything."*
> [and somewhere around the second course, easy, almost offhand:] *"Can I ask — where exactly did she go down? What did the water* feel *like, at the end? How long were you under?"*
> [and he listens. God, he listens. Like every word matters. Like he's going to remember all of it.]

*GM — he is going to remember all of it. He's collecting testimony for an annexation file and the ask ("would you put it in writing?") comes later in the meal — framed as helping future travelers. Run him pleasant and one degree wrong; the menace is sub-perceptual. Full scene and his lines in [[Caius Maren#The Dinner|his file]]. Run it tonight, or cliffhang here and open Session 3 at the table.*
