## 🧦 Chausson — the sock spirit *(Remy's house)*

The party noticed Remy's house is drowning in socks though none of them wear any. The truth, played straight by the one man in the city who'd treat a sock goblin with total reverence:

**Remy, delighted you asked** *(say this):*
> *"Oh! That's Chausson."* [like it's obvious] *"He's the house spirit. Lives in the walls, mostly. Bottom of the linen chest. ...You don't* see *him — nobody sees him — but you leave a sock out at night, and by morning it's gone, and there's a little* something *left in its place. Pebble. A button. Once a whole pearl, swear on my boat — well." [his face falls about the boat for half a second, then bright again] "ANYWAY."*
> *"No no — only ever the* one *sock. Never the pair. Drives my sister mad. But that's a guest, isn't it? You don't ask a guest his business. You just leave the man some socks."*
> [gesturing at the piles] *"...I may have overdone the socks."*

**What Chausson is** *(keep it light, never deep):*
- A tiny, shy domestic Mire spirit. Takes **one sock, never the pair**, and leaves a small "trade" — a smooth pebble, a bottle cap, a single button. A polite little burglar.
- Nobody in the house wears socks (Remy's a barefoot frog-man), so the place is **drowning in socks Remy leaves out as courtesy** — plus neighbors gift socks "for Chausson" as a housewarming joke that everyone half-means.
- Local belief: a house with a sock spirit **never floods bad.** Total nonsense. Remy believes it completely.
- The honored-guest gag: the whole city reveres vast unknowable spirits with grave etiquette — and Remy gives a sock gremlin the *exact same sincerity.* That's the joke.

**The "he likes you" beat:** Whoever's been complaining about the socks reaches into their boot/pocket and finds — a single **smooth blue river-pebble**, warm, where a sock used to be. Remy gasps, genuinely moved: *"He took to you already! Oh, that's* rare. *You're in, friend. You're properly in."*

**Recurring runner:** Chausson keeps "trading" with the party all campaign — a tiny offscreen gremlin leaving pebbles and buttons, occasionally a *weirdly useful* trinket. If anyone actually wears socks (Tenson?), they're the prime target. Never seen. Always one sock lighter.

## 🧵 Madame Vivienne — the dressmaker who reads you like a confession

*They wanted to go clothes shopping for the embassy dinner. Don't make it shopping — make it a **fitting with someone who dresses your soul, not your body.** Four backstory mirrors and a lot of laughs.*

A cramped, glorious shop on a tailors' boardwalk, bolts of impossible Mire-cloth to the rafters. Measuring tape round her neck she *never uses* — she eyeballs you and is never wrong. Pins in her mouth, opinions about everyone, calls people by colors (*"you — oyster-grey — in the corner"*). Theatrical, brisk, warm underneath.
> *"No no no — don't tell me what you want. People never know. I look at you, I tell you who you* are, *and then I make you* wear *it. Sit. Or stand. Turn around. ...Ah. Oh, you're* interesting."

**The hook:** she doesn't take orders, she takes *readings.* Each PC gets sized up and handed a garment that means something — the player's cue to push back, lean in, or reveal.

**Per-PC fittings** *(the fun + the mirror):*

- **🍃 Leavy** *(the best/funniest one)* — a shapeshifter is a *dream* client. *"Oh, you're EASY — you can be any shape you like, so let's pick the* finest." Run a quick try-on montage, she critiques each face like outfits — then, softer, the whole Witch question in one line: *"...but which one is* you, *darling? You keep changing the hanger. I can't dress a person who isn't home."* **Item:** a coat cut to drape on *any* frame she wears — *"so you needn't buy a new one every time you change your mind about being someone."*

- **🪓 Tenson** — built like a tank, nothing fits across the shoulders; she takes it as a *personal duel.* Payoff: a charcoal waistcoat with **seven inner pockets** — *"for a man who carries his work"* — and for once he looks like the respectable *clerk* he dreams of being, not the muscle. Quietly, secretly thrilled. (Comedy bonus: he winces at the price; budding-bureaucrat brain can't help itself.)

- **🐈‍⬛ Isotta** — she **casts no reflection in the mirror.** Vivienne doesn't blink (you don't comment on a guest's nature here) — just mutters, works around it, pins keep slipping off her, the cloth goes *cold.* Ends up in deep blues and silver that make her look — for once — *present.* *"There. Now they'll have to actually look at you."* (Cat-folk fitting gag: tail-hole, ear-room, *"hold still, I'm not losing a thumb to those."*)

- **🐸 Fletcher** — a frog ranger who'd rather die than look soft. She wins him with **function:** a sharp dark coat with a hidden journal-pocket and a concealed sheath — *"a gentleman of your profession needn't go* unarmed, *only un-obvious."* **Phobia gag:** she offers a fashionable **snakeskin belt** → he recoils hard → she swaps to eel-leather without missing a beat: *"...noted."*

**The twist that makes it matter** *(and plants a seed):* she clocks *where* they're going. *"Dining with the imperial man tonight? Hmf."* She dresses them in **Sallowreach** finery on purpose, not imperial fashion: *"You'll not go looking like you're hoping he* likes *you. You'll go looking like this city already decided you're* hers. *Big difference, darling. He'll feel it."* — armor of belonging, quietly anti-Caius. (Tiny clue that the city and the Empire are not friends.)

**They're broke — pick one, don't let it stall:**
- **Credit/rep:** *"You're the ones the swamp gave back. Your coin's no good to me yet — pay when the Mire lets you. And it will. One way or another."* (warm, faintly ominous)
- **Barter for RP:** *"Tell me something* true *about yourselves and we'll call it square."* (each PC pays in a confession = more backstory)
- **Remy sent word:** a bolt already set aside — *"He said you'd come. Said feed them and dress them. So."*

**Fabric flavor to sprinkle:** lantern-silk (hems glow faint after dark), tide-cloth (shifts color like the channels), blackwood buttons *(GM wink: the Tree Spirit's wood — no one knows)*, eel-leather, spider-silk shawls, river mother-of-pearl.

*(Vivienne's a natural recurring NPC — Leavy's disguises, street rumors, embassy-fashion intel.)*

## 🚶 Aimless wandering — what the city shows you

*A PC drifted with no goal. Wandering should reward curiosity — drop 2–3, see which one they walk toward, let that become the scene. All read-aloud ready, morning-after-the-Celebration flavor.*

**🤍 Warm & alive**
- **The cleanup feast.** A communal table being scrubbed down from last night's Celebration — a woman catches your eye and presses a heel of bread and a sticky golden **lantern-fruit** into your hands: *"You didn't get any last night, did you. Here. Don't argue."*
- **The domino war.** Two old men playing on an overturned crate, arguing in rapid trade-cant, *neither one looking at the board.* They pause to assess you, decide you're alright, and go right back to it.
- **The canal jam.** A punt traffic snarl in a narrow channel below — cheerful, profane negotiating — and a kid leaping boat to boat to hand-deliver a covered pot of something that smells incredible.

**✨ Wondrous** *(the bayou showing off)*
- **Light under the boards.** In the shadow between stilts where the water's blackest, the surface still glows faint blue-green — last night's light, or the Mire breathing. A child lies on their belly trailing a finger through it, making it swirl like ink.
- **Fritters that glow.** A cart of **lantern-root fritters** glowing pale gold *before* they hit the oil. The vendor flips you one: *"First one's free. Then you're mine forever."*
- **The heron nobody disturbs.** A heron the size of a man, dead-still on a piling, watching the canal. People give it room — and one passerby murmurs a polite *good morning* to it without breaking stride.
- **The runaway lantern.** A single Celebration lantern that never made it out to the Mire, drifting down the channel still lit, turning slow circles — and a small girl chasing it along the boards with a net, *scolding it* like a pet that got loose.

**🌑 Uncanny** *(light, eerie, no danger)*
- **The offering door.** A door with fresh green Mire-grass knotted to the handle and a little bowl of rice on the threshold. The house behind it is dark. Everyone steps *over* the threshold, never on it.
- **The boardwalk that ends.** A walkway that just… stops, mid-plank, where black roots have shoved up through the timber and split it. Someone's hung a lantern and a hand-lettered sign: **NOT THIS WAY NO MORE.** *(GM: the Mire growing — the city losing ground.)*
- **The reflection that lags.** You pass a cafe window and catch the room behind you — and for half a second, the old man at the corner table *casts no reflection,* though he's plainly sitting there. Then he does. He smiles at you like he knows you saw. *(GM: a small spirit passing as human. Free, eerie, never explained.)*
- **The three-second hush.** Every insect — every cricket, every fly — goes silent at once for three full seconds, then starts again. A local touches two fingers to their lips and keeps walking. *(GM: an honored guest passing through.)*

**🧵 Threads** *(drop if you want them tugging a string)*
- **Third notice.** A relocation notice nailed to a stilt-house door on a sagging boardwalk — the water's taken the floor, the family's still inside, a neighbor quietly moving their pots to higher boards. *(the failing southwest edge)*
- **The drummer at the water's edge.** Out past the last boards, a young woman drumming to the open channel — and the glow *answers her,* pulsing under the rhythm. A little crowd, half-delighted, half-nervous to be caught enjoying it. *(Fen — quiet Celebration/Turn seed; don't explain.)*
- **The homesick queue.** A hand-painted sign: **THE LONG ROOM — WORD SENT ANYWHERE, EVEN WHERE YOU CAN'T GO.** A line of people waiting to send letters out. *(A quiet gut-punch for someone who now literally can't leave.)*
- **Dock gossip.** Two dockhands, low: *"…the imperial one. Bought a whole table breakfast just to ask about that wreck."* *"Lonely, maybe."* *"…Maybe."* *(Caius, simmering.)*

**If they keep drifting with no bite:** the city adopts them anyway — someone learns their name, presses food on them, asks after the arm they didn't say was hurt. Hand them **1d4 Stress / Hope** and let the warmth be the scene. That's Sallowreach.

## 🧠 Sinope — the philosopher who wants nothing

*Perfect NPC for a wanderer: the one person in this city who wants **nothing** from them. Every other NPC has an angle; he just thinks they're interesting and likes being useful.*

**The entrance** *(read aloud):*
> Third pillar past the rice guild's loading dock, there's a covered alcove — bedroll, a cooking pot, a stack of borrowed books — and a lean, weathered man sprawled in it with the horizontal ease of someone who made peace with the ground years ago. He's mid-sentence to nobody in particular as you pass, and then his eyes land on *you,* and sharpen.
> *"You. Yes, you. You're one of the ones the swamp kept, aren't you."* [he sits up, genuinely delighted, like you're a puzzle box] *"Good. Sit. I have questions, and everyone else here only has condolences."*

**Play him in 10 seconds:** a brilliant, broke, unbothered philosopher who argues to find out if *you've* thought about it — never to win. Flat delivery, long pauses, occasionally and precisely funny. Not cruel: the point is always the *question.* He doesn't laugh at his own jokes — he watches to see if you got it.

**His hook — the tether as a puzzle** *(works on anyone):*
> *"Everyone says 'how terrible.' Useless. Tell me precisely: where is the sensation? Not metaphorically — physically. What direction does it pull? Does the threshold move over time? Have you* mapped *it?"*

It's annoying. It's also the most genuinely useful thing anyone's asked them — he treats the curse like something *knowable,* which is its own quiet hope.

**Aim it at whoever's standing there:**
- **Leavy** *(his best one):* *"You wear another's face perfectly, I'm told. So — in what sense are you not them, while you wear it? Where does 'you' actually reside, if not the face?"* (Her exact wound, handed to her as philosophy.)
- **Isotta:** *"A ghost in the Order that hunts ghosts. So which are you — the hunter, or the quarry? There's a line. Have you found it? I'd very much like to know where you put it."*
- **Fletcher:** *"You call it a job, not revenge. Fine. Define the difference. Precisely. ...Take your time. Most people can't."*
- **Tenson:** *"You'd rather keep books than carry a shield, and you're ashamed of it. Why? Who decided the ledger was the lesser life? Name them. I'd like to disagree with them specifically."*

**The seed to plant** *(don't underline):* if the Celebration comes up, his skeptic's line — and he's *right* in a way he can't know:
> *"Same words every week. Same torches, same positions. Nobody knows what language the oldest chants are even in — I checked; Caspar can't place the dialect. That's not faith. That's inertia in better costumes."*

**GM notes:**
- **He wants nothing.** Let that land — he becomes their agenda-free friend and their door into the whole street-level city (he knows everyone on the outer boardwalks).
- **Tells, for later:** his posture snaps to "recognition, not deference" when real power enters a room; his right hand curls where a signet ring used to sit. *(He's a missing noble heir — only matters if a PC turns out to be from his home region. Don't push it; just plant the hand.)*
- **If anyone ever uses his real name:** half a second of stillness. *"...Where did you hear that."* *(No name assigned yet — file it.)*

## 🏹 Fletcher's quest: "The Haunt of Thornside" *(full design)*

*Brin won't hand a stranger-with-a-vendetta the deep-channel intel on his snake — so instead of the amphisbaena, she gives him a **test,** and how he handles it is the whole answer. (The prep files stub this; here's the full thing.)*

**Her offer** *(say this):*
> *"There's a thing loose in the Thornside boardwalks. Killing pets, getting into pantries, scaring people badly. Neighborhood's screaming for it dead and somebody's about to do something stupid. You want me to take you into the deep Mire after your two-headed friend? Show me how you handle* this *first. I'll hear about how you do it."*

**What it actually is** *(what Fletcher uncovers by tracking):*
- Not a demon — an **amber civet:** a sleek, ring-tailed nocturnal climber from the **dry uplands.** Invasive. Doesn't belong in a swamp at all.
- It's here because it **escaped a smuggler's crate** — cage-sores, a clipped claw, half-starved, panicking in an environment it can't survive. It's not hunting Thornside; it's **terrified and trapped.**
- It's trafficked **alive** for the amber musk in its glands (a prized perfume fixative) — caged and milked so rich people elsewhere can smell expensive.
- The trail leads to a holding cache in the **night berths at the Mooring** (where the wardens look the other way). A crate broke; the civet got loose. **And there are *other* crates** — some holding caged *Mire* creatures with the marks of a deep-channel poacher. **One cache, two directions:** a fence named **Cauvin** shipping the civet *in,* and someone's Mire haul going *out.*

**What Brin is *actually* judging** *(this is the test):*
1. Did he kill **cleanly and only because it had to be done** — an invasive genuinely can't be loosed into the Mire — **without cruelty, without grandstanding, without playing to the bounty?**
2. Did he **protect the Thornside people** (and the native creatures) doing it?
3. **Did he chase the *cause*?** The trophy was the civet. The real quarry is the **pipeline.** A glory-hunter takes the kill and the credit and goes home. A balance-keeper follows the trail to the crates and asks *who's running them.*

**Outcomes:**
- **Pass** (clean kill, no show, chased the cause): Brin trusts him — visibly, for keeps — hands over the amphisbaena intel (the nest at the **Double Knot,** southwest deep channels), and gains an ally against the poaching she's fought alone. She stops seeing her dead mentee when she looks at him.
- **Fail** (goes glory-hunter): coldly courteous, withholds the deep intel; he earns it another way later. Recoverable, but the door clicks shut.

**Reward (not coin): Thornside Amber.** If he harvests the musk-gland *cleanly* (using all of a kill he had to make — the opposite of the poachers), he gets a few doses: smear it on and **beasts read you as *wrong* and overlook you** for a scene (or advantage to avoid a Mire predator). Doubles as a scent-lure — quietly perfect for the amphisbaena hunt later.

**Notes:**
- **Combat:** the civet is a **Tier 1 Skulk that wants to flee, not fight** — ~Difficulty 12, low damage, all evasion. The scene is *cornering it without butchering it,* reading that it's scared rather than savage. **Not a damage race.**
- **The thematic gut-punch:** the terrifying thing in the dark turns out to be **frightened, trafficked, and far from home** — the campaign's whole thesis in miniature. And those other crates plant **Gault** (the main villain) early, via Fletcher's hands.
- **Knife-twist option (heavy, optional):** make the civet a **mother with a hidden litter.** Now "invasive, must be removed" collides with "…and the young?"

*Runnable solo (or one tag-along) so it doesn't drag the party into the deep Mire. Set it up now and play it next session, or run it tonight if Fletcher's hungry for it.*

## 🌿 Leavy at the Verdant Margin — Orna & "Persona Non Grata" *(full scene)*

*The big one for Leavy. Establish Orna, drop the breadcrumb that recolors Leavy's whole quest, hand off the errand. Play Orna **delighted and three thoughts ahead.***

**Arrival** *(read aloud):*
> A garden, somehow — out here on the literal edge, where the city's last cultivated dirt meets the black water. Three platforms climbing, turned earth, something sweet that keeps changing as you move — blooming one bed, fermenting the next. Down a tide-wet ladder there's a lower platform where plants grow in directions the light doesn't explain, and three beds that *glow* faint blue-green even now in daylight.
> At a greenhouse bench: a tall, broad firbolg, soft-eyed — except a crust of pale barnacle is creeping up one forearm, and a fringe of fine green grows where hair should be, and they don't seem to have noticed or to care. Something's bubbling in a hand they've clearly forgotten they're holding.
> *(not looking up)* "You're after something specific. Everyone who finds the Margin is."

**Play Orna in 10 seconds:** a giddy genius, mid-dissolve into a swamp-thing **on purpose,** ten thoughts ahead of you, who answers the question you'll have in twenty minutes and then wanders off. Warm, vague, exhausting, *fascinated* by everything — including, immediately, Leavy.

**The opening — she *sees* her** *(the gold):*
> *(a swirl, a sniff, the beaker set down wrong)* "Most who find me want the impossible reagents — the ones that shouldn't work. Those I have." *(now the wrong light moves in their eyes, and they actually look at you)* "You, though. You want the impossible done *to* you. Different shelf entirely."

Then — the breadcrumb that changes Leavy's whole quest. Say it, then **trail off and don't finish it:**
> *"You're not sick, you know. I can tell from here. You're* **bound.** *No elixir touches that — wrong department, wrong shelf, wrong* everything. *What you'd want is a—"* *(already three thoughts away, peering into a jar)* *"...hm. Sit, sit. No — not* there, *that's alive."*

*(That's the **second** time an expert's said "bound" — after Delphine. Let it land. Leavy's been chasing a cure-potion across the world, and the best alchemist alive just told her she's looking on the wrong shelf. She does **not** finish the sentence — it points toward a thing that breaks bindings, for much later.)*

**The mirror** *(the ache, let it breathe):* Orna is Leavy's **exact inverse,** and Leavy will feel it —
- Orna is throwing her *self* away one experiment at a time and calls it research. Leavy had her self **stolen** and would give anything to get it back.
- The cruelest one: **Leavy documents everything** (the formulary — because she knows what it is to lose what was never written down), while Orna — who could fill libraries — writes **nothing,** and is letting the most valuable mind in the region wash out untranscribed. *That* will be unbearable to Leavy. That's the bond.
- Orna clocks the shapeshifting in one careless glance — and doesn't care, in the kindest way: *"Oh, you can wear other faces. Useful. Doesn't help, does it. ...No. The face was never the problem."*

**The hook — "Persona Non Grata."** Orna's out of a reagent and dumps the errand on Leavy like it just became her problem:
> *"Actually — you can be useful. I'm out of gloamcap distillate, and I can't get more. The Consortium keeps it. They won't see me."* [a beat, breezy] *"Long story. Boring story. Tell them it's for the garden."*

It is **not** a boring story (Orna got expelled — a self-test gone wrong on the premises). The keeper of the stores is **Magister Auguste Lorne,** still furious and secretly grieving the genius they lost. Saying Orna's name opens the door *and* cuts — *"You work with* Tessant?" Leavy can play it as a serious alchemist (she has the immaculate formulary Orna never kept — the front door), barter, charm, or shapeshift in and lift it (risky). **Optional price:** Lorne will only release it if Leavy can do what no one's managed — get Orna to **write down a recipe.**

**Seeds in the soil** *(don't explain):*
- **The glowing beds pulse on Celebration nights — *nearly* in time with the city's drums.** Orna's watched it for years, never recorded it, has half a theory she never finished. A **documentarian elbow-deep in that bed might catch the pattern Orna can't hold.** *(Turn clue — plant, never assemble.)*
- Orna's been quietly **moving her prized specimens uphill, away from the water,** and won't say why. *(The Mire's winning here, faster than it should.)*

**Push Leavy on** *(RP prompts):*
- *"The face was never the problem"* — so what **is?** What does she actually want back?
- Watching Orna throw herself away — does it disgust her, or terrify her, because it's the choice she'd kill to reverse?
- *"You write down every step. They write down nothing. Why does that bother you so much?"*
