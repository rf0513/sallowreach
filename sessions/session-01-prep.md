# Session 1 Prep — Black Water

*Lazy DM prep for the campaign's first session. Scaffolded pre–Session Zero: everything not keyed to specific PCs is written; the **⟦FILL FROM SESSION ZERO⟧** slots take the four private answers and drop them straight into play. The job of Session 1 is **arrival and wonder** — the shipwreck, the tether, and letting Sallowreach start to love them. Do not rush to plot. See [[world-in-motion|The World in Motion]] for what's quietly turning underneath, and [[STATUS]] for the build state.*

---

## Run Sheet & Pacing *(the session at a glance)*

*The spine, in order. **At the table, run from [[session-01-cheatsheet|the cheat-sheet]]** (this prose is the deep version). Read-aloud for each beat lives in [[first-words]] (cold open) + [[session-01-read-aloud]] (beats 1–12). Rough clocks for a ~3.5–4 hr table — **session-1 friction is real** (rules teaching, finding voices, settling in), so this is a **full session**, possibly ambitious. Plan for overrun, not underfill.*

| # | Beat | ~Min | Watch |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Housekeeping + Session-Zero recap | 10–15 | |
| 1 | **Tavern** — Remy's spirit-story → **Gault cameo** → character intros → board | 40–60 | Character intros are the big variable |
| 2 | **The water** — Remy banter, Celebration plant | ~10 | |
| 3 | **The explosion** — Remy thrown, the tether takes | ~5 | |
| 4 | **Swim — gar in the dark → drowning → rays' rescue** | 25–35 | Run it **scary**; rays are the scripted out — **no deaths** |

**Verdict: a complete session** — calm → catastrophe → survival → a threat with a face → arrival. **Time lever:** run Beat 4 fast (minions, a chase to shore) to bank clock for Beats 5–6. **Graceful endings, best→worst:** inside the gates (warmth + tether + Caius's dinner invite) › mid-crossing cliffhanger (Sallowreach rising from the dark) › on the bank (the realization they can't leave). **If they're fast** (~2.5 hr): roll into town — [[Delphine]], [[The Outer Market|the table that adopts you]], first honored-guest beat — and still close on the [[Caius Maren|Caius]] dinner invitation.

## Review the Characters

*Session Zero is done. The party (full sheets in `players/`): [[fletcher|Fletcher]] (Ranger/Wayfinder, Ribbit), [[the-witch|The Witch — name TBD]] (Witch/Hedge, Clank shapeshifter), [[isotta|Isotta]] (Bloodhunter/Ghost Slayer, Katari — a ghost), [[tenson|Tenson]] (Guardian/Stalwart, Faun).*

**⚠ Framing note — this party was sailing TO Sallowreach.** Unlike the scaffolded assumption (PCs en route to somewhere *else*, just passing through), every one of these four had **Sallowreach itself as the destination.** They arrive at the place they were trying to reach — and find they can never leave it. ## Character Intros — Entrance Hooks & The Board

*The tavern (Beat 1) is your **biggest time variable** and the new players' first test of voice. Give each PC a way *in* so intros build momentum instead of "so… who are you?" Framing: all four **booked passage TO Sallowreach on [[Remy Duval|Remy's]] boat** — strangers sharing a hull. Each gets: why they're on this boat, an opening image, and a tiny spotlight.*

- **[[fletcher|Fletcher]] — the one who booked with a *purpose.*** Already half-working: checking gear, asking Remy whether the SW channels still shed the skin he's tracking. *Open on* him cleaning a blade or pressing a sample. *Spotlight:* his Swamp Specialist eye reads the herons/water as Remy poles out (Beat 2) — first to clock the rays moving "like a herd that knows the safe channels." *Prompt:* "You came a long way for the thing that ate your town's future. What's the first thing you check boarding a stranger's boat?"
- **[[the-witch|The Witch]] — the passenger who doesn't eat.** Clank body, sits a little apart; the stew goes cold, untouched, and someone notices. Chasing the rumor the charged water breaks curses. *Open on* the small exclusion — a child stares, a passenger shifts away — against Remy's easy, unbothered welcome. *Prompt:* "You've crossed the world on a rumor. What does hope look like on a face that can't move?"
- **[[isotta|Isotta]] — feels the place before they arrive.** A chill that isn't the wind; the sense of being watched from the water. Came *because* the veil is thin here. *Open on* her reacting to something none of the others feel. *Prompt:* "You died once. The dead are louder here than anywhere. What's the first thing you 'hear'?"
- **[[tenson|Tenson]] — the ordinary one, openly out of his depth and loving it.** Sealed letter from [[Magistra Silence|Silence]], a personal ledger, practical questions for Remy about tariffs and the salt-wall. The audience-surrogate awe. *Open on* him fussing documents dry. *Prompt:* "You wanted a quieter life with numbers in it. What did you pack that says so?"

### The Board *(early agency — optional, cuttable)*
A notice-board by Remy's bar gives players something to **do** during the tavern beat instead of waiting for the plot. The pins double as soft hooks toward each PC's first stop:
- **"HUNTERS' GUILD — bounties posted, channels mapped. Ask at the Ranging House."** → Fletcher / [[Brin Sevarre]]
- **"GROWERS WANTED — the Verdant Margin beds need hands that won't flinch at strange soil."** → the Witch / [[Professor Orna Tessant]]
- **"THE STILL CUP — quiet rooms, strong cups, no questions."** → Isotta / [[Vael Miroux]]
- **"EDGE FAMILIES PETITION THE WARD-KEEPER — third notice."** → the salt-wall front / Tenson's audit
- *Color:* a smudged note about a missing punt; a child's drawing of a lantern festival; a polite warning not to whistle on the water.

**Gault, if they engage:** a PC can choose to approach or watch [[adversaries/gault|Gault]] — a real, low-stakes choice that hands them agency and plants him. Remy steers them off (*"We don't do business with Gault"*) but doesn't physically stop them. Curiosity costs nothing yet; it pays off in the Mire.

> **Why this section exists:** the cold open is ~40 minutes of wonder before the first die roll (the swim). The Board + a Gault approach give new players one thing to **choose** early — so they're participants before the boat explodes, not an audience.

## Strong Start

**Open underwater, mid-sinking. No "you board the ship." Begin in the dark and the cold.**

> The hull is already gone above you. Black water, absolute and freezing, and no way to know which way is up. Something large groans and breaks apart in the dark nearby. Your lungs are starting to argue. You went under with strangers — passengers on the same doomed vessel, no one you know — and now there is only the cold and the not-knowing-which-way.
>
> **Then the water lights up.**
>
> Below you, rising: blue-green light, pulsing in slow waves, spreading outward — a school of [[lantern-rays|lantern rays]] disturbed by the wreck, fleeing toward the shallows. They don't touch you. They move past you, around you, *upward* — and their direction is legible. Their light is enough to see the surface. Enough to see which way to swim.

**🌑 Plant The Turn (do not explain it).** *Underneath* the rays' wonder, the water is doing something else — this is the seed of the campaign's biggest recognition reveal ([[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|The Turn]]). Layer it beneath the light as they go under: the black water goes *glass-still* for a breath; a single slow glow swells up from far below, deeper than the rays; the taste of **iron and crushed green** floods their mouths (yes, they swallowed it — Remy will scold them); and each feels a low pressure, *a vast hand slowly closing.* **That** is the moment the tether takes. They'll remember the rays — you're quietly planting the rest, to pay off weeks later at a Celebration. It is a Celebration night; they have no way to know that.

Give the chosen PC the personal beat: they open their eyes and a single ray is *right there,* hovering, regarding them with an unhurried dark gaze. They follow it up. They're alive because they followed the light. (Don't explain it. The campaign will return to it.)

**Then the light leaves.** The rays scatter — they flee a half-second before the party understands why — the warm glow drains out of the water, and in the dark that's left, something else is coming. **The wreck is a dinner bell.** Blood and thrashing have called the [[mire-bestiary#Mire Gar|Mire Gar]]: pale, eyeless pack-hunters ribboned with a sickly mimic-glow, boiling up out of the black water at the swimmers. **The fight is the swim to shore** — the Mire showing its teeth in the campaign's first ten minutes. [[Remy Duval]] is already in the water, amphibious and fast, hauling the half-drowned toward the shallows and driving gar off with a boat-hook — the party's first sight of how good he is when things go wrong. *(Encounter and scaling under [[#Potential Adversaries|Potential Adversaries]] below.)*

**They drag themselves onto a muddy bank**, coughing silt and gar-blood, alive in the surprised way that means they nearly weren't — strangers gasping too close together, the wreck scattered behind them, and through the mangroves: rooflines, cook-fire smoke, the distant sound of hammering. Sallowreach.

**They are not safe yet — the bank is the second beat.** Something in the reeds has been waiting out the gar: a **Tessak scout** (the deep-channel clan's people-eater, drawn to the wreck like everything else) breaks from the mud and goes for whoever's most spent, trying to **drag them off the bank toward deep water — to take home.** This is the campaign's first threat with a *face and a hunger,* and the moment the Mire stops being weather. *(Run it per **Encounter B** below.)*

**[[Remy Duval]] reaches them in the middle of it** — he was on the ship too, lived because he's amphibious, followed the rays in to find them, and now he comes up the bank fast to drive the scout off with a boat-hook and a shout. He's good at it. He's also **afraid** in a way they haven't seen — the Mire's biggest believer going cold and quiet at the deep-channel clan. *"Off the bank. Now. Quiet."* Establish his competence and his fear in the same breath.

**The tether reveal** comes *after* — once the scout's slipped back under and the bank goes still — when the first of them tries to leave. A wall that isn't there — resistance, then something worse — and they end up back where they started. Everyone tries once. Let them work out together what they already feel: *they cannot leave the Mire.* (See [[sallowreach-frame|the frame]] → The Tether. No marks, no symptom — purely the relationship between them and the world.)

**Then Remy gets them moving.** He doesn't make a big deal of the distance thing. Not yet. He offers to get them dry, fed, and to the healer. *The only direction that makes sense is through him.*

## Landing Each PC — Per-PC Wonder & Pathos Beats

*The table wants **wonder and pathos** above all (see Review the Characters). The cold open delivers shared wonder; this is where you make sure **no player goes the whole arrival without their own thread being touched.** One beat each — small, concrete, advancing **no plot.** Drop them in the gasping-on-the-bank stretch, the Wending punt-ride, or the healer's rooms, wherever the moment breathes. The waterlogged items (below) are the props for most of these — this is how you **play** them instead of listing them.*

- **[[fletcher|Fletcher]] — the trail outlived the drowning.** On the bank, still retching silt, he checks for his **field journal before he checks himself** — the pressed shed-skin samples bleeding at the edges but still legible. The hunt survived; barely. *Pathos:* a frog who just nearly drowned in black water, and the first thing he reaches for is the dead clutch he's avenging. *Wonder, layered with unease:* his **Swamp Specialist** eye reads the rays moving in formation like a herd that knows the safe channels — and it bothers him that he can't name what's *wrong* with this water. (Don't explain it. That's The Turn, planting.)

- **[[the-witch|The Witch]] — the wreck doesn't let them belong either.** *Pathos, the quiet cruelty:* a Clank body doesn't drown the way flesh does — they walk out of the black water **not coughing,** unmistakably *other,* while the living retch and shake beside them. The exclusion they sailed here to escape follows them out of the wreck in the first ten minutes. The **formulary** survives, but the ink has run on **exactly the pages that mattered most** — the maker's own hand, smeared past reading. *(GM truth: it's their hand. Seed the wrongness whisper-quiet.)*

- **[[isotta|Isotta]] — a second second chance, and the water is crowded.** She owns the **"followed the light up"** beat (the ray hovering, regarding her — see Strong Start); make it ache: she has **already died once,** and a lantern ray handing the ghost *another* reprieve is almost more than the moment can hold. *Wonder + dread, hers alone:* as the thin-veil-sensitive one, the Mire's water is **not empty** — she gets the **first spirit-impressions of anyone,** a murmur of presences just under hearing, the certainty she's arrived somewhere the dead are close and not at rest. She came because the veil is thin here. She had no idea *how* thin.

- **[[tenson|Tenson]] — the audit survived; the seal still holds.** *Pathos:* he fishes out **[[Magistra Silence|Silence]]'s sealed letter of introduction** and his personal ledger — both water-ruined, both kept; the wax **seal still intact.** The bid for a different life, soaked and looking pointless, and he can't bring himself to let it sink. The accountant who now literally **cannot leave to file his report.** *Wonder, as audience-surrogate:* he's the most ordinary of the four — no curse, just brawn and a ledger — so let the Mire's strangeness land on **him** as the clean, open-mouthed awe. He's the one who says it out loud: *"What* is *this place?"*

## The Turn — Clue Chain *(the slow-burn reveal)*

*The party's tether and the [[The Celebration|Celebration]] are the same force — see [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|The Turn]]. They don't get told; they **recognize** it. Seed these in order; spring the click at the second Celebration. **Recognition ≠ understanding** — even after the click, they don't know it's a prison.*

1. **Seed — the wreck (this session):** plant The Turn as its own horrible beat the moment they go under — [[session-01-read-aloud#4 · The Cold Hand (the curse takes — see GM note)|Read-Aloud · Beat 4, The Cold Hand]]. They feel the tether *take:* the glass-still water, the deep swell, the iron-and-green taste, the closing hand. (Distinct from the rays' rescue light in Beat 7 — the deep glow here is the binding.)
2. **Remy, after:** *"You went under on a Celebration night. The water was singing — I felt it from the surface, stronger than I ever have."* Timing + "unusually active." He does **not** read it as the ritual's *doing.*
3. **Tether twinges:** small, deniable moments — a PC's curse *stirs* near a boardwalk drum, near [[Fen]] playing, near charged water. Priming, not proof.
4. **Fen's water:** the bioluminescence answers her drum — *"only with my arrangement."* A PC who felt The Turn gets a flicker. Bridges **rhythm → the water charging.**
5. **[[Delphine]]:** senses the tether *rhymes* with something the city does every week. Cryptic; she can't name it.
6. **First Celebration:** they're swept up, at a feast table, away from the water. The Turn passes as background beauty. Maybe one PC half-notices and shrugs.
7. **The click — second Celebration:** primed, and positioned at the water's edge when the lanterns drop, they witness The Turn and feel the tether *answer.* *"It's the same. The thing that took us is… this."* Then **keep the prison / Tree Spirit / love story hidden** — those are the end-game, earned through the chants.

## Secrets & Clues

*Ten short truths. Abstract from discovery — drop each wherever it fits (a line from Remy, a thing they see, a feeling). Expect to reveal about half; carry the rest forward.*

1. The black water did something to all of them — and every road out of the Mire ends the same way: resistance, agony, back where they started.
2. Sallowreach is reachable only by hours of punting through shifting channels; there is no road in or out, and that's the whole reason it has never been conquered.
3. The Mire is growing — boardwalks clear two seasons ago are choked now with new roots, and the city's southwest edge is losing ground.
4. Once a week the entire city celebrates, without fail; even after a few days here, the thought of missing it feels wrong in the body.
5. The city doesn't name the swamp's presences — it calls them *honored guests,* and treats them with the etiquette you'd give a guest in your home: you welcome, you don't command.
6. Remy was a passenger too; he's amphibious, and he found the survivors in the dark by following the rays toward the shallows.
7. The healer they're taken to looks at the group a beat too long — like she recognizes something she can't name.
8. The lanterns the city floats on the water every week are the exact shape and color of the rays that lit the water as they drowned. *(Plant quietly; don't underline.)*
9. The wizard who maintains the salt-ward on the southwest edge is behind on his work, and the edge families have started to notice. *(Front 2/3 — background hum only.)*
10. **PC-personalized:** word travels slowly to a city with no road — but someone is already starting to notice a PC never arrived. **Tenson's is live now:** [[Magistra Silence]] has been told the ship was lost with no survivors, and refuses to believe it; her first letter is already coming. ⟦For Fletcher, the Witch, and Isotta: name the person back home and let the same dread seed in.⟧

*PC-personalized clues, ready to drop as you get names:*
- **Fletcher:** his town doesn't only mourn the clutch now — it mourns the hunter it sent after the thing that took it. ⟦town + a face (an elder? a clutch-parent?)⟧
- **Isotta:** the famous bloodhunter family she trained beside has heard nothing — and one of them knows the ritual she underwent *shouldn't* have left someone walking around. ⟦name the family member⟧
- **The Witch:** no one waits back home — the maker they're hunting is themselves *(GM truth, [[the-witch]])*. Their version of this clue points *inward:* the first faint wrongness that the trail they've chased across the world loops back to their own hand. Seed it whisper-quiet.

## Fantastic Locations

*Three pointed aspects each. The Mire is wondrous first, threatening second.*

- **The Bank / Wreck Site** — silt-choked survivors gasping in the shallows; the ship's debris and what didn't surface with it; the city's rooflines visible and impossibly close through the mangrove.
- **The Wending** *(approach to the city)* — black channels that shift and double back; bioluminescence sliding under the punt; Remy reading the water like a conversation he's having with an old friend. → [[The Wending]]

## Important NPCs

- **[[Remy Duval]]** — innkeeper, Mire guide, first ally; was on the ship. **Wants:** to bring them in, get them whole, and (he can't help it) to show them how magnificent the Mire is. **Voice:** warm, fast, reverent about the swamp — barely-contained Steve-Irwin energy. *"The rays were moving. The rays always know where the shallow water is."*
- **[[Delphine]]** *(the healer)* — where Remy takes them. **Wants:** to help, and to understand the things she sees that she can't explain. **Voice:** gentle, unhurried, watchful; gives them care and a long look. Holds back more than she offers.

## Potential Adversaries

**Two beats, in the water and at its edge — then warmth on land.** Session 1 establishes the Mire's faces: it is *wondrous* (the rays), *dangerous* in a mindless, animal way (the gar), and *dangerous in a way that has a face and a hunger* (the Tessak scout) — and the city that holds the cure is *warm* (the arrival). Put every tooth in the swamp; the town stays warm.

**Daggerheart balance (party of 4, level 1):** budget = (3 × PCs) + 2 = **14 Battle Points**; spend fewer for an easier fight. Minion group (= party size) = 1 pt; Standard/Skulk = 2 pts; Bruiser = 4 pts. *(Full primer lives wherever you keep GM notes — the short version: spend all 14 = balanced, and a lone adversary is always too easy, so wrap a named threat in minions.)* Both beats below are deliberately **under-budget** — they're half-drowned and Remy is the net.

### Encounter A — The Swim *(the gar → the rays' rescue · Read-Aloud Beats 5–7)* — [[mire-bestiary#Mire Gar|Mire Gar]] (Tier 1 Minions)
**Shape: peril first, wonder as rescue.** The gar hit them in the *dark,* before any rays. Run this one set-piece genuinely **scary** — then the rays arrive as the deus ex machina that saves them. (Read-aloud Beats 4–7.)
- **Make the swim hard.** Call Agility/Strength rolls at **~Difficulty 15–16** (a deliberately hard GM-set Difficulty for Tier 1) against the cold, the dark, and the current toward shore. Failures don't deal *killing* damage — they **pile Stress, hand you Fear, and let the gar close.** A PC in deep water is Vulnerable / risks being Submerged. Minions still drop to any hit, so striking back *feels* good — the dread is the water and the numbers, not the teeth.
- **Let one PC fall behind.** Whoever the dice single out — or **[[isotta|Isotta]]**, who owns the payoff (she's died once; a ray handing the ghost a *second* second chance is the image the campaign wants). Build real *"I'm going to drown"* dread on that PC (Beat 6 — let them believe it).
- **It's theater — no one dies here.** The rescue is *scripted.* Spring the rays (Beat 7) **before** any death roll lands. The rays **scatter the gar instantly** (predators respect that light — canon), light the way up, and hand the fallen-behind PC the *"followed the light up"* beat. **That's how the fight ends** — don't grind it once the rays are up.
- **Remy is the backup net** if the rays somehow aren't enough (amphibious, in the water) — but this time let the **rays** be the hero, not Remy.
- **Budget:** ~2–4 pts (start ~3 gar/PC, add by blood). The rays = the campaign's thesis in one image: the same Mire that grew the teeth grew the light.

### Encounter B — The Thing on the Bank *(the scout · Read-Aloud Beat 9)* — [[sallowreach-roster#Tessak Scout|Tessak Scout]] (Tier 1 Skulk)
They drag themselves onto the mud, gar-bloodied, and think they're *safe.* They aren't. A **Tessak scout** — an outrider of the deep-channel clan, the **people-eaters the city pretends aren't there** — has come to collect the meal the Mire delivered, and found some of the meat crawled off. ⚠ *See the* **man-eater Tessak canon note** *in the roster: they're predators, not victims, and `tessavrak` survives — don't soften the scout, and don't let an NPC explain the clan yet.*
- **The horror is the *dragging-off,* not the bite.** Its **Haul It Home** move grapples the most isolated or wounded PC and drags them by the harpoon-line back toward deep water — to *take home.* A PC vanishing toward the black is the "Remy hauls them back" beat again, now with intent. This is the moment the Mire stops being weather and becomes a *who.*
- **Budget:** 1 Scout (2 pts) + optionally one more gar group it calls with **Sound the Channel** (1 pt). Keep it light — it follows a fight. **Do not** field a second scout in session 1.
- **It does not die for one meal.** When it's hurt or the fight turns costly, it **slips under and flees** — *with* a captive if it has one (Remy's frantic to prevent that). **If it escapes clean, the clan and [[Gault]] now know outsiders survived the wreck** — a live Act 2 seed, not a loose end. Track whether it got away, and whether it got away *with someone.*
- **Remy is afraid of it** — and that's the point. The Mire's biggest believer goes cold and quiet around the deep-channel clan. *"Off the bank. Now. Quiet. They felt the wreck same as the gar did, and they don't scatter."* Even your safety net is scared — which tells the party exactly how serious this is, without a word of exposition.
- **It's also their ride.** The scout abandons its **low salvage boat** in the reeds (it flees by diving) — that's how the party reaches town, since Remy's boat died with the wreck. ## Running Notes

- **Pace for arrival, not plot.** The win condition for tonight is: they're invested in surviving together, curious about the Mire, and starting to feel the warmth of the city. The tether is the hook; the personal stakes (Session Zero) are what make it ache.
- **The tether binds them to the Mire, not to each other** — let them split, argue, and not-yet-trust. Don't skip to found family (see the player principle *These Strangers Aren't Your Friends Yet*).
- **Fronts simmering in the background** (do not foreground yet): the wall thinning on the southwest edge, the Mire's slow growth, Caius's interest in their testimony. See [[world-in-motion]].
- **GM — the explosion & the harbormaster (sabotage seed).** The boat didn't *fail* — it was **rigged.** [[Caius Maren|Caius]] arranged the wreck (he doesn't yet know anyone survived). The **manifest delay was the window:** the origin-port harbormaster stalled Remy ashore — complicit, or paid through imperial paperwork — while the charge was placed, putting them on the water at the Turn's peak. **For the party tonight this is a true, *bankable* clue, not a reveal:** all they have is *"the harbormaster held us up, then the boat blew."* The word *sabotage* dawns later; the trail (who ordered the stall → an imperial seal → Caius) is reachable from inside the Mire even though the origin port isn't — [[Tenson]] the auditor is the natural one to eventually read a stall-order back to its source. **Don't say "bomb" or "sabotage" aloud in session 1.** Let Remy be baffled and gutted about his boat.
- **Carry-forward:** unrevealed secrets roll into Session 2; the Caius dinner invitation is your strong-start hook for next time.
