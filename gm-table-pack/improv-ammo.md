## 3 · Sensory Banks — "Five Things You Notice"

*When you blank on "uh… what's it like there," read one line. The Mire is wondrous **and** dangerous; the city is warm.*

**The Mire, by day:** green light falling through the mangrove canopy · the punt's wake folding the black water shut behind you · brackish mud and crushed reeds, sharp in the heat · a heron going dead-still as you pass · somewhere off the channel, water moving that nothing is moving.

**The Mire, by night:** bioluminescence pulsing in patterns the wind doesn't explain · the absolute black *between* the glows · night-blooming flowers, sweet going on rotten · the moment the insects all stop at once · a channel that was not here this morning.

**The Boardwalks (the city):** stilts creaking under sixty thousand feet · cook-smoke and frying spice you can taste in the air · washing strung house-to-house above the water · a punt-street vendor singing a price · the whole city tilted half a degree, and no one minding.

**The Celebration:** drums you feel in your sternum before you hear them · old words in mouths that learned them by sound, not meaning · communal tables running the whole length of the street · light-lanterns lifting off the black water toward the Mire · a stranger taking your hand for a dance you don't know.

**The Mooring (docks):** rope, tar, fish, green water · polemen calling rates in a trade-cant half the city speaks without thinking · a chalkboard of prices that change with the weather · goods and a day-stale newspaper coming off a cargo flat · the open channel mouth — and the wall you can't cross past it.

**Inside (an inn, a home):** blackwood worn smooth by generations of hands · a kettle always near the boil · something Mire-caught and nameless mounted on the wall · the damp that never fully leaves the cloth · a bowl of something good pressed into your hands before you think to ask.

## 4 · Overheard in Sallowreach

*Drop one as background chatter, a vendor's aside, a drunk at the next table. They quietly point at the real threads. Key: **(T)** true · **(½)** half-right · **(F)** false, but it tells you something.*

1. *(T)* "The southwest wall's thinner than it was. My cousin's floor takes water at high tide now."
2. *(½)* "That guild man at the Maintenance House hasn't filed a proper report in a year. Loses at cards, they say."
3. *(T)* "[[Fen]] played at the water again. The lights *moved* for her. I saw it with my own eyes."
4. *(T)* "Don't take the Hinge this season. It's not where it was."
5. *(T)* *(quietly, after a glance around)* "That whole table's feast last Celebration? Came from the Pale Room. Anonymous. You didn't hear it from me."
6. *(T)* "The imperial one's been asking about that shipwreck. Buys you dinner just to ask."
7. *(½)* "My grandmother says the Mire's reaching for something. Now the roots are in my doorway."
8. *(T)* "There's a frog-man at the Mire's edge who takes outsiders into the swamp. Lost his boat in that wreck, poor soul."
9. *(T)* "The rays were thick as a road off the southeast last spring. Half as many now. Nobody says why."
10. *(½)* "They pulled a wrecker's net from where that ship went down. Funny place for a net."
11. *(F)* "The Celebration felt *off* this week. Flat. Like a string gone out of tune." — *(no one can prove it; everyone half-felt it)*
12. *(T)* "A man went into the deep Mire with the frog-guide three years back and never came out. Ruled an accident. They all are."
13. *(½)* "The lizardfolk haven't traded at the edge in months. Something's wrong in the deep channels."
14. *(T)* "You can send word out at [[The Long Room|Lucien's]]. Costs a fortune and the swamp eats half of it — but it leaves, even when you can't."
15. *(T)* "[[The Mooring|Sabine]] would charge the Mire itself a docking fee if she could find where to nail the sign."
16. *(T)* "*Honored guests,* they're calling those wreck survivors. The swamp caught them for a reason. The swamp always has a reason."

## 5 · What's It Taste Like — The Table

*Sallowreach is famous for its food, and the warmth is a hook, not a garnish. Make mouths water. **Mechanics:** a shared, unhurried meal clears 1d4 Stress or grants a Hope — the city feeding you is a real reward (see [[The Outer Market]]).*

1. **Black-water gumbo** — smoke, heat, and something green-bitter from the Mire you can't name and won't forget.
2. **Lantern-root fritters** — crisp, faintly sweet, glowing pale gold *before* they hit the oil. (A tuber. Not the rays.)
3. **Reedback crab** boiled in spirit-pepper — you'll cry and ask for more.
4. **Stilt-house bread**, torn hot, dragged through brown butter and river-salt.
5. **Brightfin grilled whole** over coals — flesh that flakes blue-pale and tastes of clean, deep water. *(Rare. A gift, when offered.)*
6. **Mire-greens** stewed slow with smoked eel until they go silken.
7. **Pepper-candied frog-leg skewers** off a boardwalk cart, three for a coin.
8. **Sour-mash hush cakes** — every family makes them differently and will argue about it.
9. **Channel-rice** with whatever the day's catch was, pressed on you by somebody's grandmother.
10. **Mire Spirits** — [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]]'s bootleg liquor, illegal to export; tastes of smoke, night-bloom, and faintly of regret.
11. **Blackwood-smoked catfish** — the wood gives it a depth no other smoke does. *(GM: the wood is the [[Tree Spirit]]'s. No one knows.)*
12. **Honeyed lantern-fruit**, served at the Celebration — sticky, gold, and gone too fast.
