# Delphine

**Role:** Healer, spiritual practitioner, community anchor
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — her practice occupies the ground floor of her stilted home in the Thornside quarter; the door is always unlocked during the day
**Faction:** Independent
**Tier:** 1

---

## Appearance

A Black woman in her early sixties with close-cropped grey hair and hands that have been working for forty years — knuckled, precise, calloused in specific places from specific tools. She dresses practically: good fabric, well-maintained, nothing decorative except a single braid of dried Mire grass worn around her left wrist like a bracelet. It's not a statement. She just forgot to take it off once and started leaving it.

Her eyes are dark and attentive. Occasionally they focus on something slightly to the left of what she's looking at — a half-second lag, like she's tracking something in peripheral vision. Most people don't notice. The ones who do don't ask.

She moves without hurry and takes up exactly the space she needs, no more. When she examines someone she gets very close and very quiet, and patients who've never met her before find themselves telling her things they didn't plan to say.

## Personality & Mannerisms

Plain-spoken to the point of bluntness, but never cruel about it. She'll tell you what's wrong with you, what she can do about it, what she can't, and what you should do next — in that order, without cushioning. The warmth is underneath the directness, not instead of it, and most people feel it even when the words are hard.

She has a dry wit that emerges sideways — a flat observation delivered after a pause that makes the whole room laugh. She does not laugh at her own jokes.

She is deeply irritated by people who treat her as a mystery. She is a healer. She heals. The rest of it is her business.

She complains about the humidity with genuine feeling while refusing to leave and never explaining the contradiction.

## The Practice

Delphine's practice has two layers that she doesn't fully separate, because she doesn't see a reason to.

**The herbalism** is the foundation: forty years of working with Mire plants, their properties, their interactions, the ways different preparations affect different bodies differently. She grows things in boxes on her upper boardwalk. She trades with the Mire-edge gatherers for what she can't grow. Her knowledge is the kind that took a lifetime to build and can't be extracted from her — she is the knowledge.

**The ritual dimension** sits alongside the medicine, not above it. When Delphine prepares a healing, she works in accordance with the spirit presence she's felt in this swamp her entire life. She leaves specific offerings at specific places. She speaks certain words over certain preparations. She understands this as respect — the same etiquette the whole city practices toward the [[Cosmology#The Honored Guests|honored guests]], but more deliberately, more precisely.

She does not claim to speak for the spirits. She does not claim to receive messages from them. She pays attention to them the way you pay attention to the weather — you don't control it, you don't worship it, you just don't ignore it.

Her home smells like drying herbs, beeswax, and the specific mineral quality of deep Mire water. Bundles hang from every available rafter. The examining table is scrubbed clean. The shelves are organized in a system she's never written down and doesn't need to.

## What She Wants

To understand her visions well enough to actually use them.

They've been coming since she was a girl — fragments, unbidden, usually triggered by touch. A smell that precedes something terrible by three days. A face she doesn't recognize in the moment of touching a stranger's hand. A sound like wood cracking that plays in her head for a week before she finds out what broke. She's learned to live around them the way you learn to live around a bad knee: she knows what triggers them, she's adjusted her habits, she doesn't pretend they're not there.

What she cannot do is interpret them reliably. The visions are not narratives. They're impressions — charged, specific, and opaque until after the thing has happened, at which point they become retrospectively obvious and useless. She has tried everything she knows. The visions come and go as they please. She has accepted this. She has not made peace with it.

## What She's Hiding

The visions. Not out of shame — out of pragmatism. People who know about them treat her differently. They want her to be an oracle. She's not an oracle. She's a healer who drowns in fragments of things she can't prevent.

She has also not told anyone that when she examined the tethered party — the moment she put her hands on the first of them to feel the shape of the binding — she got a flash: cold black water, two points of light moving toward each other across an immense distance, a sound like a chord that had been suspended for a very long time finally, finally resolving.

She doesn't know what it means. She knows it matters. She has been thinking about it since they left.

## Key Relationships

- [[The Old Stories Keeper]] — old friends; mutual respect that occasionally shades into mutual exasperation; the referral is genuine and personal
- [[Sallowreach]] — she has delivered half the city, treated the other half, and buried more than she'd like to count; she knows things about most families that she will take to her grave
- [[The Celebration]] — she participates with full sincerity, but something in the oldest chants has always troubled her in a way she can't articulate; she's been trying to name the feeling for twenty years

## Plot Function

**Act 1:** First stop. Examines the tether with hands-on physical assessment and Mire-spiritual intuition. Cannot cure it — says so immediately, without apology. Identifies it as spirit-water magic and gives the party the referral to the Old Stories Keeper. This is not a cold handoff: she's invested, and the party should feel that.

**Ongoing:** The party's medical resource and a safe person to return to. She will tell them hard truths without cruelty. She will not lie to them. She can be leaned on.

**The vision disclosure:** At some point — probably Act 2, when the party has earned it — she tells them about the flash she got when she examined them. She frames it carefully: *"I don't know what it means. I rarely do. But I've learned to mention the ones that feel like that one did."* Let the party make of it what they will.

## Dialogue Hook

She opens by not asking what's wrong — she looks at whoever is most visibly affected and says what she already sees:

*"Sit down. Both of you, actually — all of you. You're holding it in your shoulders and that's not helping."*

She examines without asking permission, which most patients find presumptuous for exactly the four seconds before they realize she already knows what she's doing.

If asked about the honored guests or spirit presence: *"I've lived here sixty-two years. Something's in that water. I stopped needing to argue about it around the time I was thirty."*

If asked whether she can cure the tether: *"No. But I know what it is, and I know who might know more. That's not nothing."*
