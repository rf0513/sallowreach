# The Long Room

**Type:** Business — magical message-sending parlor (the city's "telegraph room")
**Region:** [[Sallowreach]] — a narrow stilt-house on the Thornside boardwalk, not far from [[Delphine]]'s
**Controlled By:** [[#Lucien Aubert|Lucien Aubert]] — independent (the only wizard Sallowreach ever made)

*Your words can leave the Mire even though you can't. Sit in the chair, and the person waiting for you a thousand miles away hears you tonight. You stay here. The ache lives in this room.*

---

## Description

A long, narrow room that smells of cold iron and candle-smoke and the faint ozone tang magic leaves behind. One wall is given over to the **sending apparatus**: a slab of Mire-dense blackwood inlaid with a lattice of resonance-wire and a row of attunement-stones that hum at different pitches, each tuned to a distant relay. A single good chair faces it — worn soft, because everyone who sits in it stays a while. Ledgers everywhere, in a hand only Lucien can read. A kettle always on. The light is low and warm and the quiet has weight, the way a confessional has weight, because that is more or less what the room is.

People come here to reach the world they can't get to. They come to send word that they're alive, that the deal is on, that the baby came, that someone died. Lucien takes the words, sets the resonance, and sends them out over the black water to wherever there's a stone tuned to catch them. Then he makes you tea while you wait to see if anything comes back.

---

## How Sendings Work

*A light system — mostly narrative. Tune costs to your table; gold is GM-priced (see [[The Mooring]] for the economy). Mark any hard mechanic [VERIFY].*

- **Relay to relay, not person to person.** A sending travels to another attuned **relay-stone** — major cities, Guild hubs, some wealthy houses. From there it reaches the recipient by whatever's local: a runner, a notice, word of mouth. **If the person you want is somewhere with a relay, they hear you fast. If they're in a backwater, your words get as close as the nearest stone and then walk.** *(GM: this is the dial for each PC's Session Zero "someone waiting" — set how reachable they are. The unreachable ones ache the most.)*
- **The Mire fights the signal.** Sending *out* of the bayou is hard — the spirit-charged water and the [[Oswin Cray|salt-ward]] scatter the resonance. Lucien spent years learning to punch a clean line through, which is the whole reason he has a business. He is effectively the city's only independent voice to the outside. *(The Guild has its own official channel through the posting — bureaucratic, monitored, and not for the likes of the party.)*
- **It costs, and the cost is the point.** A sending runs **a handful or more**, scaling with distance and length; a far, wordy message can eat several handfuls. For a party scraping toward a boat at [[The Mooring]], "do I spend the boat money to tell my mother I'm alive?" is a real and cruel question. Receiving a reply is cheaper, but it isn't free, and Lucien doesn't extend much credit — kindly, but he doesn't.
- **It is not clean.** Sendings queue, garble, and sometimes simply don't land — atmospheric "static" off the Mire, a relay down, a stone out of tune. A message can arrive late, arrive wrong, or seem to arrive and never have. Lucien will tell you honestly whether he thinks it got through. He's usually right. Usually.

**The gut-punch, plainly:** the party can reach the people they were traveling toward. Words go; bodies don't. A reply can come — relief, or grief, or *"I'm coming to you"* (and now someone they love is making the miserable journey *into* the cage). Run this room for the ache it is.

## Lucien Aubert

**Role:** Message-sender; the only wizard Sallowreach ever produced
**Tier:** 2 *(he is not a fighter; he is a craftsman of a rare and quiet art)*

A spare, deliberate man past sixty, with ink-stained fingers and the stillness of someone who spends his days holding other people's words carefully. He dresses plainly, local, nothing Guild about him anymore. He speaks slowly because he has learned that words sent in haste are the ones people regret. The city regards him as a beloved oddity: *our* wizard, the boy who left and — stranger than leaving — **came back.**

### The Rarest Thing About Him
Sallowreach doesn't make wizards. Roughly nineteen in twenty Sallowreachers are multigenerational locals who never leave; magic here is the business of posted outsiders who hate the humidity. Lucien is the exception the city tells stories about — born on these boardwalks, showed a talent no one knew what to do with, left as a young man to train (Guild-schooled, then independent), saw cities the rest of the city will never see. And then he came home, to a stilt-house and a sending-room, and never explained it to anyone's satisfaction.

### What He Wants
To be left alone to carry the city's words, and — though he'd never say it — to not be so alone in it. He is the keeper of everyone's joys and sorrows and a party to none; he knows who's in love and who's in debt and who got the worst news of their life in his good chair, and he carries all of it, discreetly, by himself. He came back for connection and ended up the most quietly isolated man in a warm city.

### What He's Hiding
- **He keeps copies of everything.** Two hundred sendings a year for decades, archived in his unreadable hand — a private library of the city's whole inner life. He tells himself it's reverence. It's also a kind of hoarding, and a violation he doesn't examine.
- **The message he never sent**, or the one he sends every year to a relay he knows has been dead for a decade, hoping. *(Pick one; it's the crack in him.)*
- **The thing he won't look at.** He grew up *inside* the [[The Celebration|Celebration]] — chanted the old words before he could read — and he is the one person in Sallowreach with both halves of the knowledge: lived devotion *and* trained arcane literacy. He has never once turned his wizard's ear on the Celebration's resonance. He'll tell you it's because some things are sacred and you don't dissect them. The truth underneath: years ago he felt *something wrong* in the city's hum — a note that shouldn't be there — and it is part of why he left, and he has spent a lifetime not naming it. **He is a clue, not a shortcut.** His reverence (and his fear) keep him from connecting what only an outsider, who owes the Celebration nothing, can force him to see. If the party ever brings him the truth, he is devastated in a precise and terrible way: *he could have known. He chose not to.*

### The Mirror
He is the inverse of the tethered party, and he knows it the moment they walk in. They cannot leave and would give anything to. He could leave tomorrow — and chose to come back to the one place that is home. When a PC rages at the cage, Lucien is the man who can say, without cruelty, *"I could be gone by morning. I came back. I don't expect you to understand it. I'm not sure I do."* Let that sit.

### Key Relationships
- [[Oswin Cray]] — the city's two wizards, and they could not be more opposite: the miserable outsider trapped doing the ward badly, and the native who could do it and won't. The Guild would have loved a local who knows both the Mire and magic; Lucien refused the posting years ago and has never regretted it. He pities Oswin, a little. They do not socialize.
- [[Marcel Dray]] — a quiet, respectful distance. Marcel deals in secrets; Lucien is *trusted with* them. Marcel would pay a fortune for Lucien's archive and knows better than to ask — Lucien's absolute discretion is the only thing that makes the Long Room work, and Marcel respects a clean operation. *(If Lucien's discretion is ever broken or bought, it breaks him.)*
- [[Delphine]] — neighbors and quiet friends; two keepers of the city's private pain, hers in the body and his in words. They don't need to talk much.
- The party — clients first. If they're honest with him and gentle with the chair, he warms; he is starved for people who've *seen* the world he gave up.

### Dialogue Hooks
- *"Tell me what you want to say. Not the pretty version — the true one. I'll make it fit. I always make it fit."*
- *(setting the stones)* *"It'll reach Vethmoor by morning. Whether it reaches **her** — that depends on whether she's still where you think she is. The stone doesn't know her. It only knows the city."*
- *(on the Mire eating the signal)* *"Everything fights to leave this place, even a sentence. I've spent thirty years teaching words how to swim out. You'd think the swamp would take the hint about the rest of us."*
- *(the mirror, quietly)* *"I could leave tomorrow. I came back. Make of that what you will."*
- *(if pressed on the Celebration, a door closing)* *"I sing it like everyone sings it. I don't take it apart. Some things you leave whole."*

## Key Features
- **The Sending Wall** — the apparatus; the row of tuned stones, each a distant place that can hear you.
- **The Good Chair** — where the city sits to send and to wait. The emotional center of the room.
- **The Archive** — shelves of copied sendings in a hand only Lucien reads; a private history of Sallowreach's heart, and a quiet violation.

## Tensions & Hooks
- A PC sends word to whoever's waiting (Session Zero) — and the cost competes directly with the [[The Mooring|boat fund]]. The first real "money or love" squeeze.
- A reply changes everything: relief, grief, a demand, or *"I'm coming"* — pulling an outside NPC toward the Mire.
- Lucien is the only person in the city who could, in theory, read the Celebration's magic — and the only one who refuses to. A long-game thread toward the central truth.
- His archive is a treasure and a liability — to [[Marcel Dray|Marcel]], to anyone with a secret in it, to the party if they ever need something only Lucien's copies remember.

## Secrets
- Lucien felt the "wrong note" in the city's resonance long ago and left partly because of it. He has never said so.
- The Guild offered him the ward posting that went to [[Oswin Cray]]. He turned it down. *(Worth deciding what that means about how much he suspects.)*
- Somewhere in the archive is a sending that matters to the campaign — a message about the wreck, the wizard, the Mire, or one of the party's own people. The Long Room remembers what the city has tried to forget.

## Dungeon Alchemist Notes
A long, narrow stilt-house interior: one wall of arcane apparatus (glowing inlaid lattice, a shelf of softly-lit stones), a single worn armchair facing it, a cluttered writing desk, floor-to-ceiling shelves of ledgers, a kettle on a small stove, low warm candlelight. Intimate, one-room scene. Cozy-uncanny, not threatening.
