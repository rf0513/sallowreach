# The Celebration

**Tier 1 Event** *(strong Social texture)*

*"Sixty thousand people, one city, one held breath of music — and when the light-lanterns lift toward the black water, you will want to belong to this so badly it frightens you."*

The weekly act of hospitality to the [[Cosmology#The Honored Guests|honored guests]]. Music before dawn in the outer districts, building through the day until by evening the whole of [[Sallowreach]] is in motion: chanting in old words, dancing in patterns that vary by family and lineage, feasting at communal tables that run the length of the thoroughfares, and at the end, the light-lanterns released over the Mire — an open door, a weekly welcome. Run it for what it is. The devotion is sincere, the joy is real, and the city means every word.

*(GM SECRET — the buried truth: the Celebration is a binding ritual that imprisons the [[Tree Spirit]]. The old-dialect chant, the torch positions, and the second-movement drum pattern are the mechanism — and per [[The World in Motion#The Interlock — The Engine Under Everything|the Interlock]], it is the same clock as [[Oswin Cray|the wizard's]] salt-ward. Anything that disrupts the Celebration frays the binding **and** weakens the southwest wall the next morning. See **The Hidden Layer** features below. They stay offline until the party knows the truth.)*

## THE SURFACE
### What everyone experiences

**Impulses:** Welcome the stranger, hold what matters, let nothing change, pull the lonely into the warmth.

**Difficulty:** 11

**Potential Adversaries:** None, by design — this is a Social event, not a fight. If violence erupts (a brawl at the feast tables, an arrest, a panic), reach for [[sallowreach-roster#Celebration Marshal|Accord stewards and Adaline's marshals]] to *restore order, not to kill.* [[Caius Maren|Caius]]'s [[sallowreach-roster#Imperial Attaché Guard|imperial guards]] may shadow the party through the crowd. Save [[Gault]] and Mire threats for the swamp — they have no place here.

### Features

- **The Pull of Belonging - Passive:** A PC who lets themselves be swept in — learning a family dance, taking a seat at a stranger's table, mouthing the old words — may make a Presence Roll to be folded into the city for the evening. On a success with Hope, the city *opens:* an old-family matron adopts them for the night and three secrets below become available to them. With Fear, the welcome is real but watchful (learn one secret; Adaline or her people clock that this stranger is *trying*, and remember it). On a failure, mark 1 Stress — the joy is so total it aches, and the ache is the question *what am I cut off from?* [VERIFY: Daggerheart Action Roll + Presence trait]. Three things the city offers up to those it takes in: (1) which families keep the drums, and which keep them *jealously;* (2) that the form has not changed in two hundred years, not one torch, not one word; (3) that newcomers are sometimes called honored guests too — said with warmth, and no idea of the irony.
- **The Whole City Moves - Passive:** There is no edge of this event to stand outside of. Every roll a PC makes to *observe coldly,* analyze, or hold themselves apart from the Celebration is made with disadvantage; every roll to *participate* — dance, feast, chant, carry a lantern — is made with advantage. The Celebration rewards surrender and punishes the clinical eye. [VERIFY: advantage/disadvantage from environmental stance]
- **A Seat at the Table - Action:** The GM seats the party beside someone the world wants them to love — an outer-boardwalk family whose floor is ankle-deep at high water ([[The World in Motion#Front 3 — The Edge Erodes|Front 3]]), a posting-hater who has stopped wanting to go home, [[Fen]] playing a defiant uninvited set three tables down. They are fed the best food they have ever eaten and asked, gently, *what were you doing on that ship?* The scene is warm. It is also the city deciding what the party is. Refusing the seat is *noticed.*
- **The Lantern Release - Action:** At the turn of the evening the canal-streets go quiet and sixty thousand lanterns lift toward the Mire at once. Any PC holding a lantern makes an Instinct Roll as they let it go. On a success, a clean moment of belonging — recover a Stress, this is the city at its most beautiful. On a failure with Fear, the lantern *catches the wind wrong,* drifting back against the current toward the releaser, and for half a breath the PC feels something out on the black water turn its vast attention through them toward the city's heart ([[The World in Motion#Front 1 — The Grief Rises|the Grief]]). They don't know what it was. They will not forget it. [VERIFY: Instinct Roll, Stress recovery]
- **You Should Have Been Here - Reaction:** When a PC tries to leave the Celebration early, skip it, or treat it as a thing to get through, the city answers — not with hostility, with *absence.* They mark 1 Stress and cannot recover Stress or Hope until the next scene; missing it feels wrong in the body, the way [[Sallowreach]] promised it would. [VERIFY: Stress/Hope economy]
- **The City Decides You Belong - Action:** Spend a Fear to make the welcome *total* and binding. An NPC the party has been keeping at arm's length — an edge-family elder, a grieving widow, a child who hands a PC the family drum — pulls a chosen PC fully into the ritual: a role to play, old words put in their mouth, a place that is now *theirs.* The GM may clear a Fear-gated complication by spending it here instead, because now the party has something to lose. This is the warmth the campaign weaponizes. Let it land before anything is asked of them.

## THE HIDDEN LAYER
### GM-SECRET — the binding beneath the welcome

*These features do not exist for the players until they understand what the Celebration actually does. Until then, run only **The Surface.** Once the party knows — the old words are a binding spell, the second-movement drum pattern is load-bearing, [[Fen]]'s rejected arrangement is the wrong note that frays it — the same beautiful event becomes a machine they can read, and a lever they can pull. Present these as **levers, not an ending.** Whether the party ever disrupts the Celebration, and whether they should, is entirely theirs. Honor [[The World in Motion#The Interlock — The Engine Under Everything|the Interlock]]: every fray here thins the southwest wall by morning.*

**Hidden Impulses:** Hold the great presence, drown the love story in joy, let no one translate the welcome, punish variation.

### Secret Features

- **The Load-Bearing Element - Passive (secret):** A PC who knows the truth and watches the ritual with new eyes may run a Progress Countdown (4) across one or more Celebrations to map the binding — cross-referencing the old words ([[Caspar Renne]]'s transcription), the torch positions ([[Adaline Roux]]'s inherited knowledge), and the second-movement drum pattern (the one [[Fen]] broke by accident). Each segment requires a roll *and* a piece of knowledge from a different keeper — none of whom can assemble it alone. Complete it and the party knows precisely which eight bars hold a two-hundred-year prison shut, and exactly how to fray them. [VERIFY: Progress Countdown mechanic]
- **The Wrong Note - Action (secret):** A performer who plays the second-movement deviation — [[Fen]], or a Bard who has learned it — frays the binding in public, with sixty thousand witnesses and not one of them understanding what they heard. The chant stutters. The bioluminescence on the canal-water shivers and answers the new rhythm. **The Interlock fires:** the next morning the southwest wall hums briefly solid, then sits a measurable notch thinner ([[The World in Motion#Front 2 — The Wall Thins|Front 2]] advances). A frayed-but-unbroken binding is *more volatile* than a sealed one — phenomena bloom, the lumber dulls at the edges, the Celebration starts to *feel wrong,* and the old guard blames the variation, which is true in exactly the way they don't mean.
- **Attend It Knowing - Passive (secret):** Once the party knows, the Celebration's central feature becomes the *watching.* Sixty thousand people in genuine, devoted, beautiful joy, holding a prisoner and calling it a welcome — and the party standing among them, knowing. A PC who participates in full knowledge marks 2 Stress and must answer, in character, *what do you owe the people you love, who are doing harm they never chose?* This is not a hazard to beat. It is the scene the whole campaign was built toward. Let it breathe. [VERIFY: Stress]
- **The Second Movement - Action/Reaction (secret):** If the party deliberately disrupts the load-bearing eight bars — Fen on the public stage, or a PC's own performance, or by silencing the drum-keepers — the binding *cracks.* Run it as a Progress Countdown the party fills to fray the binding while the city, the Accord, and Adaline's marshals try to *restore the form* (a Consequence Countdown against them). The black water answers, lanterns drift the wrong way en masse toward the city's heart not the Mire, and somewhere the [[Tree Spirit]] feels the wall of its cage go thin. Whatever the party intends, the southwest wall pays for it by dawn. [VERIFY: opposed/Consequence Countdown]
- **What the Mire Has Been Waiting For - Action (secret):** Spend a Fear when the binding is actively fraying to let the Grief *reach through the gap.* The [[Mangrove Spirit]]'s attention floods the Celebration for one impossible moment — the lanterns hang motionless in the air, the chant dies in sixty thousand throats at once, and the longing pours in not as an enemy but as a thing *searching.* Every creature present must make a Presence Reaction Roll or be Overwhelmed by secondhand grief that is not theirs [VERIFY: Reaction Roll, an appropriate condition — mark Stress / lose the spotlight]. The city will tell stories about this night for as long as it stands. The party will understand, finally and wordlessly, that the swamp's growth was never hunger. It was a love story, and they are standing in the gap of it.
- **You Don't Understand What You're Doing - Reaction (secret):** Spend a Fear when the party frays the binding *without grasping the Interlock* — disrupting the Celebration to free a spirit, or simply to win, while ignoring the wall. The cost arrives the next morning, off-screen and human: a southwest edge-family's home goes under at high water, a relocation order no one can rescind, [[Caius Maren|Caius]] filing the loss as document N+1. The party did a true thing and a costed thing in the same gesture. Make them watch the bill come due on someone with a face.

## Feature Questions

*Use these to seed hooks as the party moves through the Celebration — surface or hidden.*

- *Which NPC does the city seat beside the party tonight — and what does that person want from these strangers the swamp caught?*
- *Whose family role is one of the party offered — a drum, a torch, a serving procession — and what does it cost the family that gives it up to an outsider?*
- *When a PC feels the vast attention turn through them at the lantern release, what in their own forgotten past does the longing rhyme with?*
- *If the binding frays tonight, whose home is ankle-deep by morning — and is it someone the party has already learned to love?*

## The Turn — Recognition Beat

*GM. This is where the party's curse comes home — see [[Cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|The Turn]] and the clue chain in [[session-01-prep]]. Stage it deliberately; it's a scripted beat, not a Feature.*

At the height of the evening — chant peaking, light-lanterns released onto the canal water — **the water Turns:** the same phenomenon that took the party in open water the night they wrecked. Glass-still for a breath, a slow underglow swelling up, the taste of iron and crushed green in the air, the pressure of a vast hand closing. A **tethered PC feels their curse answer it.**

- **First Celebration:** *don't* let it land. They're swept up in feast and dance (the Surface features do this for you), seated away from the water, overwhelmed. The Turn passes as beauty; maybe one PC half-notices.
- **Second Celebration (the click):** once the clue chain has primed them, put a PC at the water's edge as the lanterns drop. The Turn comes; the tether answers; the half-memory surfaces — *"our curse and this ritual are the same force."* Let them sit in it. **Confirm nothing more.** They now *suspect* the Celebration took them. They do **not** know it's a prison, that a [[Tree Spirit]] is held, or that the growth is grief — those are the end-game, earned through the chants. Never have an NPC say it.

## Running This

Run **The Surface** first, for weeks of real-time table play if you can — let the city love the party before they have any reason to suspect it. Keep the two layers strictly separate: until the party has translated the old words and connected [[Fen]]'s wrong note to the binding, the Hidden Features simply do not exist, and the Celebration is nothing but the most beautiful night in Sallowreach. Once the truth lands, do not flip the place into a villain — the devotion stays sincere, which is exactly what makes attending it in full knowledge the campaign's best gut-punch. Above all, honor [[The World in Motion#The Interlock — The Engine Under Everything|the Interlock]] every single time the binding frays: the wall is thinner by dawn, and somewhere on the southwest edge a family with a name pays for it.
