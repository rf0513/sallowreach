# Session 1 — Read-Aloud Blurbs

*Spoken, not written — paced like you're coming up with it at the table. Read it or riff off it. One image per beat. The load-bearing ones: Remy's fear (3), the drowning (6) → **the rays as rescue** (7), the hunger (9).*

---

### 1 · The Tavern
> Okay — you're in the tavern. Warm, low light, somebody's murdering a fiddle in the corner. Smells like fish stew and wet rope. Out past the window, the black water's just... lapping at the stilts. Real patient.
>
> You've got time to kill — Remy's boat's hung up at the dock — so you end up watching the man at the end of the bar. Lean. Still. Sun-dark, hands scarred in a dozen places. He's not drinking. He's settling a delivery: a row of sealed clay jars on the bar, and in one of them — something's still faintly glowing. Dying, but glowing.
> A little kid wanders over, nose right up to the glass, eyes like saucers. The man doesn't even look down. Just slides the jar a few inches out of reach. *"Don't smudge the glass. It's spoken for."*
> The merchant counts coins into his palm. He counts them again himself, slower. Then pockets it, gathers his jars, and walks out — doesn't hurry, doesn't look at a soul, like the room isn't even there.
> [Remy, low, watching the door swing shut] *"...That's Gault. We don't do business with Gault."*
>
> And then he's bright again, clapping the stew off his hands — *"Right, that's everyone! Up we go, up we go, we're hours behind as it is. Harbormaster decided my manifest needed reading. Twice. So — we're chasing the light now. Come on."*

*GM (optional deep-cut): the jar's glow catches his eyes and holds a half-second too long — the only tell, here, of what eight years in the charged water has done to him. Don't point at it. It pays off in the Mire, in the dark, when his eyes do it again. ([[adversaries/gault|Gault]])*

### 2 · Out on the Water
> He poles you out into the channels, easy, like he's done it a thousand times — pointing out herons as they lift off the reeds. City lights shrinking behind you. Quiet. Just the Mire and the drip off his pole. Kind of beautiful, honestly. And it's yours.
> [Remy, half to himself, with a glance back at the glow on the horizon] *"Tonight's the Celebration, see. Back home. Whole city out on the water — lanterns, the music, the lot. First one I'll have missed in... huh. Don't think I ever have."* [a beat — then bright again] *"...Anyway. You just don't miss it, is all. You don't. We'll cut the Wending, make up the time."*

### 3 · The Explosion
> And then the whole world just — comes apart.
> Heat. This roar. The boat goes to splinters right under your feet. And the last thing you see before you go under — it's Remy. Thrown clean off his own deck. And on his face, for the first time? Real fear.

### 4 · The Cold Hand *(the curse takes — see GM note)*
> You go under — and for one breath, everything stops.
> The water goes glass-still. Dead silent. And way down below you, something huge turns over in the dark — a slow swell of light, rising and fading, deeper than anything should be.
> And then it reaches you. A pressure, closing around you — *through* you — like a vast, slow hand shutting. Iron and crushed green flood your mouth. And something *takes hold,* somewhere behind your ribs, in a place you don't have a name for. It doesn't hurt. It's worse than hurt. It's *wrong,* and it's *inside* you now — and every animal part of you knows at once: it is not letting go.
> Then it breaks. And it's just black water and cold and no idea which way's up. The boat's gone. Your chest is already burning.

*GM — this is **The Turn**: the tether taking hold (they've gone under during a Celebration, the binding at full song — [[cosmology#The Turn & The Tether|Cosmology]]). **Plant the fingerprint exactly** — glass-still water, the deep swell, iron-and-crushed-green, the closing hand — because it **recurs at the Celebration** and clicks the recognition reveal later. **Never name it.** Not "curse," not "the Celebration." Tonight they only know something is in them and it's wrong. The deep glow here is the binding, **not** the rays — keep it ominous and separate from the rescue light in Beat 7.*

### 5 · The Creatures
> And then something brushes your leg.
> They come up out of the black underneath you — pale, eyeless, fast, little sick lights running down their sides, and teeth. So many teeth. The whole wreck rang like a dinner bell, and they *came.*
> Now it's just the dark and the cold and the teeth, and somewhere out there — maybe — the shore. *Swim.*

*GM — this is the scary part. **Make the rolls hard** (Agility/Strength to swim, ~Difficulty 15–16 [VERIFY]). Pile on Stress, take your Fear, let the gar close — and let **one PC fall behind** (whoever the dice single out, or [[isotta|Isotta]] — she owns the payoff). Build real drowning dread. **But no one dies here** — the rays are the scripted rescue; spring them (Beat 7) before any death roll lands. The gar **scatter the instant the rays rise,** so that's how the fight ends — don't grind it.*

### 6 · Going Under
> [for the one who fell behind — the one the water's winning against]
> Your arms aren't really answering anymore. The cold's gotten right into your chest. The others' splashing is getting further off — or maybe you're just sinking, it's hard to tell now. Hard to tell anything.
> And some quiet, far-away part of you goes: *oh. So this is how it happens.*
> [let it land. let them believe it.]

### 7 · The Rays
> And then the water lights up.
> Underneath you — way down in the deep — a glow. Blue-green, swelling, *rising.* Hundreds of them, pouring up out of the dark like the whole swamp drew a breath and breathed out light. Lantern rays.
> And the teeth just... scatter. The pale things flee the glow, gone in a blink — because even they know better.
> [to the one who was drowning] And one of them rises right up to your face. Close enough to touch. And it just holds there — looking at you, with this slow, dark, patient eye. Not afraid of you. Like it came *for* you.
> Then it turns, easy, and starts up toward the air. Showing you the way.
> ...You follow the light. You're alive because you followed the light.

### 8 · Land
> You haul yourself up onto mud. Coughing up silt, blood, the whole — you're alive, but barely. Just a bunch of strangers gasping next to each other.
> And through the mangroves, you can see it. Rooftops. Cookfire smoke. Somewhere, a hammer knocking. Land.

### 9 · The Lizardfolk
> And the reeds part.
> Something stands up out of the mud — and you realize it's been there the whole time, just waiting. Tall. Scaled. Dead still. Harpoon hanging in one hand. And it looks at you... the way you'd look at dinner. No anger in it. None. It's just hungry — and patient about it.
> And it looks down the line of you, picks the one who looks worst, and starts walking.

### 10 · The Crossing
> [Once the scout's down or driven off — it slips under and vanishes, leaving its boat behind.]
> Remy's the one who finds it: half-tucked in the reeds where the thing beached. A boat. Low, flat, hide and salvaged wood lashed together. A nasty little craft — and it's been used for nasty work. You don't look too close at what's in the bottom of it.
> Remy blows out a breath. *"...Well. She floats. Beggars and choosers."* He waves you in, takes up the pole. *"Keep your hands inside the hull. Stay low. And whatever you see out there on the water tonight — you let it be."*
> And he poles you off the bank, the last slow stretch toward the lights. Toward Sallowreach.

### 11 · Sallowreach
> And then the channel opens up — and there it is.
> A whole city, built up out of the black water on ten thousand stilts. Boardwalks and lantern-light stacked all the way up into the dark — smoke, and music, and the smell of cooking drifting out across the water to meet you. Sixty thousand people. And not one road in or out. Just the water you came in on.
> And the water's *full* of light. Lanterns — hundreds of them — drifting loose on the channels, soft and blue-green, set out for the Celebration. [for whoever followed the rays up tonight: they're the same shape. The same colour. You know that shape.]
> Remy, quiet beside you, watching the last of them drift past — *"...Made it back for the end of it, anyway."*

### 12 · The Boardwalk
> Remy ties off at a public landing and more or less herds you up onto the boards. And the city just... takes you in.
> You're soaked, bleeding, half-dead on your feet — and before you've even got your balance, somebody's pressed a hot bowl into your hands. Somebody else throws a dry blanket round your shoulders. A woman you have never met in your life is already asking after the arm you didn't tell anyone you'd hurt.
> Nobody asks who you are. Nobody asks if you can pay. They just feed you. Like it's the most obvious thing in the world.
> [Remy, gentle, like he knows exactly what it's about to do to you] *"Welcome to Sallowreach. ...Let 'em. It's what they do."*
