# Session Zero

*For the GM. See [[sallowreach-frame]] for the player-facing version of these questions.*

---

## What Session Zero Needs to Accomplish

1. Players understand the world and tone (use [[sallowreach-frame]] — pitch, overview, player principles)
2. Each player privately gives the GM their four answers (see below)
3. The GM has everything needed to tie the tether's urgency to each character personally
4. Table safety tools discussed and established

---

## Private GM Questions (Each Player, Before or During Session Zero)

Ask these individually — not at the table. The answers inform how the GM personalizes Act 1 pressure.

**1. Where were you going when you boarded that ship?**
Be specific. Not "seeking adventure" — the actual destination. A city, a person, a place. This is what the tether has interrupted.

**2. Why does it matter that you get there?**
What happens if you don't make it? What's the consequence — to you, to someone you care about, to something you're responsible for?

**3. Who knows you were on that ship?**
Who is expecting you somewhere and will eventually notice you haven't arrived? Give them a name and a relationship.

**4. At least one NPC outside of Sallowreach who matters to your character.**
Family, mentor, employer, rival, old friend. Name and one-line relationship. These become the faces of the urgency the tether creates.

---

## Table Questions (Open Discussion)

- What drew you to a bayou/swamp setting? What parts of that aesthetic excite you most?
- The city of Sallowreach is warm and welcoming — how do you feel about spending significant time in a social, community-focused environment between expeditions into the Mire?
- This campaign is designed to have moments of genuine warmth AND emotional gut-punches. Is the table comfortable with both?
- Safety tools: what do we want in place? (Lines, veils, X-card, open door policy — establish before play begins)

---

## What the GM Does With the Answers

Each PC has somewhere they were supposed to be and someone waiting for them. These become pressure points throughout Act 1:
- A letter arrives for the PC (forwarded to Sallowreach after the ship was reported lost) — from the person who was expecting them
- An NPC in Sallowreach has a connection to the PC's destination and asks about it innocently
- Word reaches Sallowreach that the PC's absence has had consequences — the thing they were supposed to prevent has started happening

The tether is the hard rule — they cannot leave the Mire. The personal stakes are what make that rule feel like more than a mechanic.
