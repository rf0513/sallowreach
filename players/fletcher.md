# Fletcher

**Class:** Ranger — Wayfinder subclass
**Ancestry:** Ribbit
**Community:** Wildborne
**Tier:** 1

## Experiences
- *Eye For Danger* — "Very diligent when it comes to watching out for predators and other hazardous creatures"
- *Swamp Specialist* — "Knows a lot about safely navigating swampy terrain"

## The Shape of Him
Fletcher is a professional without a passion, and he has made his peace with that — or thinks he has. He became a ranger because it was what was expected, the path everyone who knew him agreed made sense, the one that fit on paper. He's good at it. He doesn't love it. He *enjoys* it, which is a quieter and more complicated thing, and the distinction has never cost him a night's sleep. There's a version of this frog who works a competent career, retires, and is mildly content the whole way through, and that prospect doesn't trouble him the way it probably should.

Which is exactly why the campaign should reach into his chest and find the one thing he *does* love, and squeeze. A man who's decided not to care deeply about anything is a man the world hasn't found the right lever for yet. Sallowreach is a city very good at being loved. Watch what happens to the professional when a place — and people — get under the detachment.

There is one crack in the composure, and it is not small: **Fletcher is viscerally, helplessly terrified of snakes.** A frog who can't be in a room with one. He does not joke about it. He does not bring it up. And he has come to the Mire to hunt one.

## What He Wants
An **amphisbaena** — a two-headed snake — came up out of the channels and into his town's nursery, and ate the clutch. Not livestock. Not stores. The **eggs.** For a Ribbit, that is not a pest problem; that is the next generation, the whole future of a spawning-pool, gone in a season. Fletcher's people count their continuance in clutches, and a clutch was devoured by the exact thing his body has been screaming at him to flee since before he could walk.

He has been tracking it ever since by the only sign it leaves: **shed skin.** Two-headed, a scale pattern he'd know in the dark, a trail of castings that leads into the Mire and does not come back out. He will not say the word *revenge.* What he'll say is that it's a job, that the creature is a confirmed hazard, that it falls squarely within his professional remit. Watch his hands when he says it.

**First stop in Sallowreach:** the Hunters' Guild at [[The Ranging House]]. The Mire is the amphisbaena's habitat; someone who works these channels has crossed its sign. He's looking for [[Brin Sevarre]] before he knows her name.

## The Hunt Points at the Heart
*(GM — this is why Fletcher is wired into the campaign and not beside it.)* The amphisbaena nests in the **southwest deep channels** — the same direction the [[Mangrove Spirit]] presses, the same water [[Oswin Cray]]'s failing wall holds back, the same depth the cure lives in. His private hunt drags the party *toward the campaign's center,* and the deepest stretch of it is gated behind a [[The Mooring|boat they can't yet afford]]. His grief and the party's tether want the same channels.

And the Mire is **growing.** Roots choke last season's clearings; channels shift and double back. The castings he's been following are being swallowed as fast as he can read them. It's not a doom clock — it's the quiet, awful pressure of a trail going cold in real time, the one thing he lets himself feel urgent about.

## What He's Carrying
His field journal — three years of professional observation: hazard notes, predator patterns, the careful taxonomy of a man who organizes fear into data. Pressed flat in the back, the shed-skin samples, annotated, the trail he's been walking the length of the world to follow. It is the most personal object he owns and he treats it like ammunition: precisely, and without sentiment he'll admit to. He'd lose a hand before the journal.

## Key Relationships
- [[Brin Sevarre]] — the Hunters' Guild's balance-keeper at [[The Ranging House]]; the first professional who's actually *seen* his quarry's sign — and who won't trust him on sight. She does his work by a stricter creed (cull for balance, never glory) and makes him prove he isn't a glory hunter (the in-town trust-test, "the Haunt of Thornside") before she'll hand over the deep intel. Earn her and she's a true ally; the amphisbaena is *her* problem too (it's invasive)
- The amphisbaena — out past the [[The Wending|Wending]] in the deep southwest, in water locals don't work
- **Home** *(needs a name from Fletcher)* — a spawning-town that is grieving a clutch and a hunter both; someone there will eventually wonder why he never sent word

## The Tether
Fletcher came to do a job and leave — the second half was always the plan. The tether deletes it. The snake is still in the Mire, there's no deadline anymore, and somehow the *absence* of a deadline is worse than the snake. A professional with no exit, in the one place his work might actually mean something to him. He should hate how much that starts to matter.

## Notes for the GM
- **Play the snake-fear straight.** Don't milk it for comedy. When the amphisbaena finally surfaces, the frog who hunts it should be genuinely, bodily afraid — and do it anyway. That's the character in one image.
- **Validate the competence, then find the lever.** He *is* good. Reward it mechanically and narratively — and let the city, not the snake, be the thing that teaches him he can still love something. The "doesn't love the job" stance is the door BLM tells us to walk through.
- **The Ribbit resonance is load-bearing.** A frog-person whose people's spawn were eaten by a serpent is mythic, not quirky. Lean on eggs, water, continuance.
- **His solo in-town arc is [[Brin Sevarre]]'s trust-test** ("the Haunt of Thornside") — runs Fletcher alone (or one tag-along) without dragging the party into the deep Mire, tests whether his fire is discipline or glory, and teaches the campaign's thesis in miniature (*the monster is a displaced victim*). It also plants [[Gault]], the main villain. Full design in Brin's file.
- **Don't let him kill the snake early.** The nest is deep, gated behind the boat and the Wending, in the territory where everything else is also true. Make him earn the channels.
- **Backstory gut-punch available:** word from home. The town didn't only lose its clutch — it lost the hunter it sent. A letter that arrives *after* the ship was reported lost (see [[session-01-prep]]) lands hard on a man who measures himself in jobs completed.
