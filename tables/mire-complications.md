# Mire Complications

*The swamp's content pool — what to draw when travel through the [[The Mire|Mire]] goes wrong. Two tables: the **Wending** (the known channels, where the world still behaves) and the **Deep Mire** (where reality bends and the attention turns toward you).*

---

## How & When to Draw

These plug into the travel loop defined in the [[Sallowreach (Campaign Frame)|frame]] and run by the [[The Wending]] and [[The Deep Mire]] environments:

- A party navigating the Mire makes an Instinct Roll [VERIFY: trait/Action Roll]. On a **Fear** result, or when the party becomes **Lost**, draw a complication.
- While **Lost**, the party can't complete a Long Rest, and the GM may **spend 1 Fear per hour Lost** for a Mire encounter — that's a draw. [VERIFY: Long Rest interaction]
- A draw can *be* an environment Feature's Fear-spend escalation (e.g. the Wending's *Something Surfaces*, the Deep Mire's *Mire Complications*). Don't double-charge: one Fear, one event.
- **Roll or choose.** Pick the entry that fits the moment over the die when you have an instinct for it.

**The deeper principle:** out here there are no NPCs, but [[Sallowreach (Campaign Frame)|the swamp still has a face]]. The Wending complications are the *living swamp* — dangerous, beautiful, indifferent. The Deep Mire complications are secretly [[The World in Motion#Front 1 — The Grief Rises|Front 1]] delivered as foreshadowing: the party feels the [[Mangrove Spirit]]'s attention, its searching, its *longing,* long before anyone can name it. Played right, they'll realize only in hindsight that the swamp was reaching for them the whole time. Lead with wonder as often as threat.

*Categories (for spread, not rules): **Hazard · Attrition · Navigation · Wonder · Attention.***

## The Wending — Known Channels (d12)

*The everyday swamp. Dangerous, but the world behaves. Mostly Tier 1.*

| d12 | Complication |
|---|---|
| 1 | **The Sinking Hour** *(Attrition)* — The water rises without rain, swallowing the boards you stood on a breath ago. Lose a piece of gear to the black water, or mark 2 Stress hauling it free. |
| 2 | **Holed Hull** *(Attrition/Hazard)* — A submerged snag opens the punt below the waterline. Bail and patch on a Progress Countdown (3) [VERIFY] or beach on the nearest root-island and lose the hour. |
| 3 | **Biting Season** *(Hazard)* — Stinging midges, or worse, leeches that don't let go. Each PC makes an Agility or Strength Reaction Roll [VERIFY] or marks 1 Stress and is Vulnerable until they can stop and clear them. |
| 4 | **The Channel That Closed** *(Navigation)* — A route you've used before is choked with roots that weren't there last season ([[The World in Motion#Front 1 — The Grief Rises|the growth]]). Reroute and lose time, or cut through on a Strength roll and mark Stress. |
| 5 | **Fogfall** *(Navigation/Wonder)* — A white mist drops in minutes, swallowing every landmark and sound. The next navigation roll is at disadvantage [VERIFY]; the silence inside it is the loveliest, loneliest thing any of them has felt. |
| 6 | **Bloomwater** *(Wonder)* — The channel lights up green-gold with an algal bloom, thick enough to row through. Beautiful and disorienting — the glow hides what's under it. A dropped or trailing hand comes back stained for a week. |
| 7 | **The Toll** *(Attention/social)* — Punt-pirates or a lone channel-runner who knows a waterway you don't [VERIFY: SRD Bandit]. They want payment — coin, cargo, or a favor owed. They'd rather be paid than fight. |
| 8 | **Rot Underfoot** *(Hazard)* — An old boardwalk, a fishing platform, a landmark stump gives way underfoot. A PC makes an Agility Reaction Roll [VERIFY] or drops into the water — Vulnerable, and whatever they were carrying is now wet or gone. |
| 9 | **The Generous Channel** *(Navigation/lever)* — A wider, faster current opens up, clearly the quicker way. It is. It also pulls a notch deeper than you meant to go — toward the unknown channels. Take the speed, or fight back to the route you trust? |
| 10 | **Carrion Light** *(Wonder/eerie)* — Something dead in the shallows, lit from inside by [[lantern-rays]] feeding, glowing like a lantern. Lovely until you understand it. A clue, if a PC looks closely, about what didn't survive this water. |
| 11 | **The Wending Shifts** *(Navigation)* — A channel that should be here simply isn't; a new one runs where solid root stood last week. Any map or memorized route is now wrong. Advance toward Lost unless someone reads the *current* instead of the chart. |
| 12 | **Something Watching** *(Attention, mild)* — The unmistakable feeling of being noticed. No threat comes. Each PC marks 1 Stress, or presses into the feeling instead of away and catches a single true detail about the deeper Mire. The first taste of what waits past the Wending. |

## The Deep Mire — Unknown Channels (d12)

*Past the known channels, where compasses spin and the [[Mangrove Spirit]]'s attention is directed, not diffuse. Reality bends. Mostly Tier 2. Being Lost here is worse — and these are the ones that, in hindsight, were the swamp speaking.*

| d12 | Complication |
|---|---|
| 1 | **The Channel Closes Behind You** *(Navigation)* — The way back is choked with new roots that weren't there an hour ago. Returning the way you came is no longer an option. The Mire has decided you're going forward. |
| 2 | **The Light Leads** *(Navigation/lever)* — Bioluminescence brightens along one route and dims along all others. Following it clears the next navigation roll's disadvantage — but it leads deeper, toward [[The Mangrove Spirit's Heart|the Heart]]. The easy way is always inward. |
| 3 | **The Water Remembers** *(Attention)* — A PC sees, just under the surface, a drowned thing wearing their own face — an echo of the wreck, the black water, [[The Tether|the tether]]. Mark 1 Stress, or mark 2 and surface with a flicker of true insight about the spirit. |
| 4 | **Wrong Animals** *(Attention/clue)* — Creatures that should flee don't; creatures that shouldn't be here are. A heron holds your gaze too long. If [[Gault]] is near, the wildlife is avoiding *him* — and that's a tell the party can learn to read. |
| 5 | **The Drowned Bell** *(Attention)* — From under the water: a sound like a struck bell, or a voice, or your own name in a mouth that isn't yours. No source. No threat. Each PC marks 1 Stress — not from fear, from the recognition of being *noticed* by something vast. |
| 6 | **Compass-Death** *(Navigation)* — Every instrument fails at once; needles spin, the sun is wrong, downstream runs uphill. Navigation rolls are at disadvantage and no Experience about wayfinding applies [VERIFY] until the party stops trusting their tools and starts trusting the water. |
| 7 | **The Hour That Repeats** *(Navigation/eerie)* — You pass the same split tree, the same heron, the same drift of lanterns — twice. Time is folding here. The Lost condition worsens (a second success is needed to break free) and no one can shake the sense that the swamp is in no hurry at all. |
| 8 | **The Reaching Roots** *(Hazard/tender)* — Roots slide across the punt and around an ankle — slowly, deliberately, *gently.* A PC is Restrained [VERIFY], but nothing squeezes, nothing harms; it is the grip of something that wants to *hold,* not hurt. Freeing yourself is easy. Understanding what just touched you is not. |
| 9 | **The Garden of Wrecks** *(Wonder/clue)* — A still backwater full of the boats the Mire has taken over the years, furred with moss and light, arranged almost tenderly. Somewhere in here is the party's own wreck. A place to learn how long this has been happening — and to whom. |
| 10 | **It Mistakes You** *(Attention — the core reframe)* — For one impossible moment the vast attention settles fully on a single PC, as if they were the thing it has been searching for across two centuries. Presence Reaction Roll [VERIFY] or be Overwhelmed (lose the spotlight / mark Stress) by a longing that is not theirs and is bigger than they are. This is the love story, felt before it's understood. |
| 11 | **The Bloom of Faces** *(Attention/personal)* — Pale flowers, or fungi, opened in the shapes of faces the party left behind — the person each of them was traveling toward when the ship went down. Each affected PC marks 1 Stress. *(Lean on the personal tether stakes once PCs are known.)* |
| 12 | **The Deep Goes Quiet** *(Escalation)* — Every sound stops. No insects, no water, no wind. The light steadies. You are very close to [[The Mangrove Spirit's Heart|the Heart]] now, whether you meant to come or not. The GM may spend a Fear to let the journey arrive there. |

## Growing This Table

Add entries as play surfaces them. Good Deep Mire complications share a quality: the threat and the wonder are the *same thing,* and the swamp never reads as malicious — only vast, patient, and searching. When in doubt, ask: *how does the grief show itself here, to people who can't yet know it's grief?* See [[The World in Motion#Why Only the Party Can Bend This|why the party is the only one who can read the whole board]], [[Cosmology]] for the truth underneath, and the [[The Deep Mire]] / [[The Wending]] environments for how draws integrate with Features.
