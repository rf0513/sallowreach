# The Black Water

**Tier 1 · Event** — *the swim after the wreck. Session 1's first real scene.*

> The antagonist of this scene is **not the gar.** It's the swamp. The Black Water is hungry and it is *patient* — it doesn't pull hard, it doesn't need to. It wants to turn you away from each other in the fog, make you spend every breath just to move ten feet, and quietly close over your head. The gar are just the moment it shows teeth. **Run the water. The gar are texture.**

---

## When do I reach for this card?

When the spotlight swings to **you** — a player **fails**, **rolls with Fear**, or you **spend a Fear**. Instead of "a gar bites you," you pick one of the swamp's verbs below and *the water takes its turn.* You never run out of moves, because the place itself is a move.

**Difficulty 15** *(this water is hard on purpose — they're half-drowned)* · **Safety: no deaths tonight.** The rays are the scripted out; spring them before any death roll lands.

---

## Running the dice — the two-clock race

> ⚠️ **No initiative, no turn order.** You describe the danger, someone reacts, they roll, and the dice decide what happens *and who goes next.* Tell the table: *"This is dangerous now, so we're rolling — but there's no turn order. Just tell me what you do."*

The scene is a race between two clocks. Put both on an index card where the table can see them:

> 🟢 **REACH SHORE** — Progress, fill **6** — *they swim out under their own power* (the earned ending)
> 🔴 **THE WATER HAS YOU** — Consequence, fill **6** — *they go under and the rays rescue them* (the wonder ending)

**Whichever fills first ends the scene. Both endings are safe. Both are beautiful.** The dice just decide whether tonight's story is *"we survived"* or *"something saved us."*

### The loop

```
1. YOU frame the swamp pushing on ONE pc → end on "What do you do?"
2. PLAYER describes their action
3. YOU name the trait + say "Difficulty 15"
4. PLAYER rolls 2d12 + trait  (one Hope die, one Fear die)
5. READ THE DICE TWICE:
      a) Total ≥ 15? → success / failure
      b) Which die is higher? → Hope (player) or Fear (you)
6. TICK THE CLOCKS (table below)
7. NARRATE with a verb, pass the spotlight
8. Either clock at 6? → that ending fires. Otherwise → back to 1.
```

### Which trait?

| They want to… | Trait |
|---|---|
| Swim hard, dodge, dive under, strike fast | **Agility** |
| Power through the current, haul an ally, shove a gar | **Strength** |
| Find the shore, find each other, read the water | **Instinct** |
| Rally someone, shout them calm, keep a PC from panicking | **Presence** |

### What each roll does

| They rolled… | What it means | 🟢 Shore | 🔴 Water | Spotlight |
|---|---|:--:|:--:|---|
| **Crit** (matching numbers) | Nail it, **+1 Hope, clear a Stress** | **+3** | +0 | They keep going |
| **Success + Hope** | Do it cleanly, **+1 Hope** | **+2** | +0 | Players pick who's next |
| **Success + Fear** | Do it **but the water takes something.** You **+1 Fear** | **+1** | **+1** | You take a soft verb, then frame next PC |
| **Fail + Hope** | Doesn't work, **+1 Hope.** Gentle complication | +0 | **+2** | You, softly |
| **Fail + Fear** | Doesn't work, **the swamp surges.** You **+1 Fear** | +0 | **+3** | You take a **hard** verb (Bite / Drown / Separate) |

**The knife's edge:** *Success + Fear* ticks **both** clocks. *"You make it three more feet toward the light… but the cold's deeper now."* Winning and losing in one breath — lean on it.

### How fast does it go?

- 🟢 **Reach Shore (6):** 3 clean successes, or ~4 mixed rolls.
- 🔴 **Water Has You (6):** 2 Fear-failures, or ~3 fails.
- Across 4 players, the whole scene is usually **5–7 rolls** — everyone rolls once or twice.

**Tuning knob:** longer/scarier swim → raise 🔴 to **8**. Quick, merciful rescue → drop 🔴 to **4**. Leave 🟢 at 6 and the swamp's whole mood lives in that one number.

> **Not every action needs a roll**, and the rays can still come early if a player's genuinely drowning *for real* (not having fun). The clocks are a backstop, not a cage.

## The swamp's five verbs

Pick one when it's your turn. Say the line. Hand the choice back.

### 🌀 SEPARATE — *"it turns you away from the others"*
> *"The current already has a hand on you. It's not pulling hard — it doesn't need to. It's just turning you. So gently you almost don't feel it. The others' voices are further than they were a second ago."*

Mechanically: move the PC a range band away from the group, alone. No roll needed — it just *happens* on a Fear.

### 🌫️ BLIND — *"the fog took the shore"*
> *"You swim hard toward shore. And the shore that was on your left is on your right now. The fog turned you around completely and you didn't feel it happen."*

Mechanically: attacks/rolls beyond **Very Close** have **disadvantage**. They can't find each other or the bank without spending an action to get bearings (Instinct, Diff 15).

### 🫁 DROWN — *"the cold is in your chest"*
> *"The cold's in your chest now. Your arms are getting heavy. [start the Breath clock] Every breath from here costs you something."*

Mechanically: this is the **face of the 🔴 Water Has You clock filling.** As it climbs past halfway, narrate the cold and the heaviness on whichever PC is most isolated — make the table *watch the clock* and feel it coming. When 🔴 hits 6, this PC is the one who goes under → **Relent.** *(Want a personal version for a single dragged-under PC outside this scene? Use a standalone Breath Countdown (3): ticks 1 per action, at 0 they mark Stress per action.)*

### 🦷 BITE — *"the water answers blood"*  ← the gar live here
> *"Something brushes your leg in the dark. Then another. The blood in the water called them, and they are not afraid of you at all."*

Mechanically: blood/splashing/a Fear roll → a **Mire Gar** group surfaces at Close. Use the [[mire-bestiary#Mire Gar|Mire Gar minion line]] (Diff 10, HP 1, die to any hit, 2 phy). They're the swamp's teeth — **let the PCs kill them easily.** Striking back should *feel good*; the dread is the water, not the bite.

### ✨ RELENT — *"the light comes for you"*  ← the way the scene ends
> *"And then — under you, around you — the water fills with light. Slow blue-green, like the swamp exhaling. The gar scatter from it instantly. And the light is moving, all of it, in one direction. Toward shallow water. Toward shore. It wants you to follow."*

Mechanically: the [[lantern-rays|lantern-rays]] rise, scatter every gar, and stream toward the bank. Fight over. **This is your guaranteed out** — spring it the instant a PC is in real trouble, *before* any death roll. The Mire that wanted to swallow them is the same Mire that lights them home. Both faces, one water.

## The move that makes it land

When the water takes something, **make the player name what it cost them.** Don't narrate it — hand it over:

> *"You're going under. The black's closing over your head. In that one second — what's the thing you think of? Not who. What."*

Now the swamp isn't a hazard. It's pulling **character** out of them. That's the whole reason to run it as an environment instead of a monster.

---

## Run sheet for tonight

1. **Open on the want.** Before any dice: the world is gone, the water is warm as blood, the fog is a closed lid, and *it has time for you.* Let them feel watched. Then draw both clocks on a card where they can see them.
2. **First roll, first Fear → SEPARATE** the most isolated PC (Isotta owns this — it's her payoff). Tick 🔴.
3. **Let them push back, then BITE** once so they get to swing at something and win. Killing a gar is a clean success → ticks 🟢.
4. **As 🔴 climbs past halfway → DROWN** the most isolated PC. Narrate the cold and the clock out loud so the table watches it fill.
5. **Whichever clock hits 6 → RELENT** (rays). 🟢 first = they reach the shallows themselves, gasping, and the rays rise like a reward. 🔴 first = someone goes under and the light comes for them. Don't grind — fire the ending the instant a clock lands.

*Two faces, two clocks, five verbs, one scene. That's the whole environment.*
