# Vael Miroux

**Role:** Owner of [[The Still Cup]]; retired Order of the Ghost Slayer; Sallowreach's quiet authority on the spirits who walk among people
**Location:** [[Sallowreach]] — [[The Still Cup]]
**Faction:** Order of the Ghost Slayer (retired, and privately heretical about it)
**Tier:** 2

*Isotta's anchor and first true teacher about what she is. The one person in the city who looks at a ghost wearing her own face and isn't surprised — because Vael has spent a decade watching far stranger things order coffee and pay their tab.*

---

## What Vael Knows About the World *(the lens — and the campaign's spirit metaphysics)*
There is a **spirit world.** It sits alongside this one, and between them is a veil — thick in most places, **thin in the Mire,** worn to gauze in the deep channels. Spirits cross. Always have. They come in every shape, size, and degree of power, from things that could drown a city to things barely strong enough to keep a candle lit — and, crucially, **they are not good or evil by nature.** A spirit is a *person* from somewhere else, with wants and a temperament, no more uniformly kind or cruel than the people you already know.

The small ones — spirits of modest power — can put on a human seeming and **walk this world as one of us.** Most who do it aren't plotting anything. They're curious, or lonely, or in love with something here, and they slip into a human life the way you'd slip into a warm bath. You have almost certainly met one and never known. Vael has met dozens. Vael serves several of them coffee.

This is not secret lore, exactly — the city *knows* it shares its home with [[Cosmology#The Honored Guests|honored guests]] and treats them with that careful etiquette. What Vael has that the city doesn't is the ability to **see which of the neighbors aren't human** — and a creed about what to do about it: *nothing, unless they're doing harm.* You don't slay a thing for the crime of existing near you. That conviction is most of why Vael is *retired.*

## Appearance
Mid-fifties, tall, the kept muscle of someone who trained hard and chose not to let it go. The Order's full sleeve of grey ritual ink runs up the left arm, last mark included. Sleeve rolled up; they want it seen.

The eye is the thing. One is dark brown, quick, warm. The other is **milky-white and very still** — eleven years ago, hunting deep in the Mire, Vael came face to face with a spirit at the worn edge of the veil and it *looked back.* Ever since, that eye sees across: a spirit's true shape under its borrowed one, the seam where a human seeming is stitched on, the faint wronglight on someone who isn't a someone. Asked about it, Vael says *"Occupational,"* and refills your cup.

## Personality & Mannerisms
Calm the way deep water is calm — not empty, just holding very still. They listen far more than they speak; when they know a thing they're precise and generous, and when they don't they say so flat and never let a guess pass for a fact. Dry humor that surfaces as a single sentence at the bottom of a long silence; it takes new people a few visits to start catching it. The coffee is genuinely the best in a city with opinions about coffee, and they make every cup like it matters, because they've decided it does.

## What They Want
To understand **what's straining the veil.** For months it's been wrong — spirits agitated, more crossings, the small quiet ones who've passed as human for years suddenly *slipping.* Something is pulling at the boundary, and Vael has watched it long enough to be moving from *attentive* toward *alarmed.* (They're right to. It's the rising grief, the binding, the whole hidden engine — but Vael can't see that far. See below.)

## What They're Hiding *(GM — keep Vael a fragment-holder)*
Eleven years ago, deep in the Mire, the white eye showed Vael something it has never been able to fully resolve: *something vast, and old, and* held — *bound* out in the dark water, far past any spirit they've met since. They felt it notice them. They withdrew. They've filed no report in eleven years, because what would they even write? They assume "a great spirit, caught by the Mire." They do **not** connect it to the city, the Celebration, or any deliberate act — and the white eye, so sharp on the small spirits next door, simply *cannot bring that one into focus.* It's masked by something bigger than Vael's sight. *(The Tree Spirit's binding stays end-game — see [[Cosmology#The Recognition Reveal — what they can know, and when|the reveal rules]]. Never let Vael name it.)*

## What They Know (useful to Isotta)
- **The spirit world, the veil, crossing, anchoring** — the whole working cosmology, taught plainly to someone who's ready. Isotta, a Ghost Slayer who *Connects with Spirits,* is more than ready.
- **A spirit is held to this world by an anchor** — an attachment, a will, an unfinished thing, a love. Lose the anchor and the veil takes you back. *Understanding what anchors a spirit is the first step to understanding what anchors Isotta.*
- **The cure for her condition is a parley, not an exorcism.** *"Exorcism kills what you are. What you'd need is to* talk *to whatever's holding you, and get it to let go."* Points, without Vael knowing it, straight at the campaign's center and the spear-path *(route her [[Delphine]] → [[Caspar Renne]] → the deep Mire, same chain as the spear)*.
- **The hard truth about Isotta herself, once the white eye reads her:** she is *not* a spirit who crossed over. She's a human who was **bound here in a spirit's shape** — *tied,* not *crossed.* *"The ones I see came across on their own. You didn't. Someone* did *this to you, child. That's not a haunting. That's a* curse." This confirms her ghost-state is a curse (and a binding the spear can break), and marks her as an anomaly even to the one person who'd know.

## The Quest — "Old Basile" *(Isotta; in town; the clean showcase of spirits-among-us)*
Among the Still Cup's regulars is **Basile** — an old man who's played dominoes in the corner for as long as anyone can remember. *Longer,* if you press it: the grandparents of the current regulars remember him looking exactly the same. Everyone's honorary grandfather, gentle, beloved, harmless. He is a **small spirit** who crossed the veil generations ago, fell helplessly in love with the warmth of an ordinary human life — the coffee, the gossip, the slow afternoons — and simply *stayed,* anchored by that love. Vael has known for years and never said a word, because Basile does no harm and that is the whole of Vael's creed.

**The hook:** Basile is **slipping.** The straining veil is dragging at him; his anchor is failing. The tells are small and eerie and clean — he casts no reflection in the cup's polished urn some mornings; his shadow falls the wrong way; he forgets which decade it is, calls a regular by their dead grandmother's name, sets out a domino that isn't there. **Isotta notices first** — her ghost-sight and *Connects with Spirits* read him as *kin,* as something standing where she stands, between. She brings it to Vael, and Vael — deciding she's ready, and kin — opens the door: confirms what Basile is, and asks her to help, because *she's* the one built to.

**What it teaches Isotta (the point):**
- **The cosmology, made flesh.** A spirit has been sitting in her favorite chair the whole time. Spirits-among-us stop being theory.
- **Anchoring.** To help Basile she has to find and shore up (or release) his anchor — and learning how a spirit is held to this world is learning the mechanics of how *she* is held. His situation is a working model of hers.
- **Her first real parley.** With Vael coaching, Isotta talks to Basile *spirit-to-spirit* — the exact skill her own cure will one day require. The quest is training wheels for the thing that frees her.
- **A mirror, and a fork in it.** Basile is a *spirit who chose to be human and is losing it.* Isotta is *a human cursed into a spirit and wants it back.* Two beings on opposite banks of the same veil, both clinging to humanity. What she does for him shows her a version of her own possible ending.

**Resolution (no clean answer — each teaches her something):**
- **Re-anchor him** — find what could hold him here again (a renewed bond, a purpose, someone who'll *know* him) and help him stay. She learns what it costs to keep a spirit in the world.
- **Help him cross, gently** — a peaceful return through the veil; Isotta witnesses a *good* crossing, what release looks like instead of destruction. (This is the version of "cure" she may one day be offered for herself: not undone, but *let go.*)
- **Fail / refuse** — the veil tears him back, or he dissolves at his table mid-game, and the regulars never understand why the old man simply stopped coming. A hard lesson in what's at stake when an anchor fails — including, someday, hers.

*(Scale: in-town, runnable solo for Isotta with Vael as guide and Basile as the heart of it; social/investigation/parley, not a dungeon. Optional deeper thread: Basile's *original* anchor was a specific person he followed across, long dead — grief as the thing that held him — which rhymes with the campaign's whole love-and-binding spine.)*

## Key Relationships
- [[Isotta]] — the bound ghost who walks in reading, to the white eye, as something the veil already has its hand on; Vael recognizes her instantly, takes her in (*"Sit down. Coffee first."*), and becomes her teacher in what she is
- [[Delphine]] — careful mutual trust; two people who each carry something they can't fully explain and have quietly agreed some things in Sallowreach deserve attention, not panic
- The Order of the Ghost Slayer — Vael keeps contact and hosts the Thursday gatherings, but is privately heretical: the Order tends to *slay;* Vael has learned most spirits don't need slaying, and won't hunt again
- **Basile** — a spirit Vael has protected with silence for years; the quest above

## Dialogue Hook
A cup set down in front of her, no ceremony:
*"I've served a few like you. Died in the transformation, came back still attached. The Order says that's not supposed to survive."* The white eye drifts to something past her shoulder, then the brown one finds her. *"But you're not quite that either, are you. Sit. Drink. There's a man in the corner I think you ought to meet — and then you and I are going to talk about what you actually are."*
